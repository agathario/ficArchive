Agatha freezes, her soul leaving her body on the spot. She stares at the open door from where she stands, livid. “What,” She whispers hoarsely. “The fuck.”

Her brain does several things at once, none of them helpful. Why does Rae know that song? Why is it playing out loud? Is she making videos? Who is she with? What is happening in this household?

Agatha’s heart rate spikes. She spins on her heel and marches down the staircase, calling for, “RIO.”

She finds Rio in the kitchen, calmly chopping vegetables. “Rio,” Agatha says again, storming in. “We need to talk. Pronto.”

Rio looks up, startled. “You’re awake.”

“That is not the point.”

Rio sets the knife down carefully and glances her way with puzzled looks. “Did you feel worse?”

“No.”

“Better?”

“Yes.”

“Okay—”

“Your daughter is making videos. Tiktoks, I think.” Agatha blurts.

Rio blinks. “Yes.”

“With a friend.” Agatha adds, pointing emphatically back toward the hallway.

“Yes.”

“To Walk ’Em Like a Dog.”

Rio pauses and Agatha sees the way Rio tries to suppress her laughter. “…Okay.”

Agatha stares at her in disbelief. “That’s it? Okay?”

“What would you like me to do?” Rio shrugs. “Confiscate the phone? Ground her? Stage an intervention?”

“She’s sixteen!”

“Yes, I am aware.” Rio purses her lips. “Were they doing anything unsafe? Anything more dangerous than listening to that music?”

“I didn’t stay long enough to find out, obviously.” Agatha admits. “I value my sanity.”

Rio hides a smile by reaching for the pot on the stove. “They’re just making tiktoks.”

“To that song.”

Rio raises an eyebrow. “You seem very invested.”

“I am concerned.” Agatha insists. “Isn’t she too young for whatever, pray tell, she is doing?”

Rio turns to face her fully now. “You should take a sit.”

“I don’t—”

“Sit.” Rio repeats firmly, pulling out a chair.

Agatha hesitates but she does what she’s told anyway, folding her arms like a petulant cat.

Rio slides a glass of water toward her. “You’re still sick.”

“I am recovering.”

“Okay, if you say so.” Rio nods. “Rae’s friend is staying for a few hours. They’re supervised with doors open. No nonsense, although I should have to talk with her for using the sound you heard.”

Agatha eyes her. “You’re far too lax about this. I’d assume you’d be otherwise.”

“Nah, I trust my kid.” Rio says simply.

That lands heavier than Agatha expects. She looks away. “Well, I don’t trust the internet.”

“That’s fair.” Rio agrees. “I monitor her social media accounts so she doesn’t post or see anything inappropriate.”

Agatha sighs, rubbing her temple. “I thought she was.. I don’t know. Studying? Memorizing lines for fun? Rotting in bed?”

“She feels better. Besides, this is her version of fun. I just let her.” Rio finishes plating the soup and sets it down in front of Agatha with unmistakable finality. “Eat.”

“I’m not a child.” Agatha whispers, but she still picks up the spoon, taking one spoonful. 

Rio watches her with arms crossed and a sheepish grin. “You know..” She begins, “For someone who keeps insisting she’s fine, you sure let me feed you a lot of soup.”

“I’m humoring you.” Agatha replies, sniffing. “For morale.”

“Mhm. Sure. That explains why you’re already halfway through the bowl?”

Agatha pauses mid-bite, scowls, then pointedly takes another spoonful. That action made Rio laugh. 

“Who’s with Rae?” Agatha finally asks, squinting toward the hallway as if the answer might physically emerge from it. “Who is possibly with her that she’s making tiktoks to that sound with?”

“America.” Rio answers easily.

Agatha's forehead creases. “America.”

“Yes.”

“The country?”

“The girl.”

“A girl..” Agatha repeats faintly.

“Uh-huh.” Rio smiles, completely unbothered, leaning her hip against the counter. “I think she’s courting Rae.”

Agatha’s soul almost left her body for the second time that day. “COURTING WHAT?” 

Rio winces slightly. “Indoor voice.”

Agatha ignores her. “Rae.. she’s—is she really?” She gestures vaguely with both hands, words failing her. “She's into girls?”

Rio hums.“Yes.”

Whoever or whatever Rae ends up liking, Agatha knows, with a certainty that she’d accept and support it. 

Rae’s femininity catches her off guard a little not because it’s wrong, but because it’s so intentional. However, Agatha doesn’t read it as a stereotype, she reads it as a choice. Rae knows who she is and she’s proud to know her daughter wears it comfortably.

Hearing that she likes girls barely registers as a shock. Agatha is just glad Rio takes it the same way, no questions asked as if it's the most simplest thing in the world.

“America lives close by. They’ve been friends since what? Grade school?” Rio supplies.

“She would..” 

“She would what?”

“Be the kind of girl who pulls the slow-burn friends-to-lovers nonsense.” Agatha continues. “That’s Rae’s brand.”

Rio laughs, nodding. “It kind of is.”

“And Rae’s making her wait?”

“Absolutely.” Rio confirms. “Rae’s stubborn. I don’t know how long she’ll keep America in the ‘just friends’ zone, but the girl’s patient. Persistent, even.”

Agatha hums, thoughtful. “Good.”

Rio glances at her. “Good?”

“Yes. It does build character.” She pauses upon realizing something, “Hey, Rio?”

“Yes?”

“I’m not meddling..” Agatha says quickly, which is usually how you know she is. “But have you, I don’t know, had the talk with Rae?”

Rio considers her. “Which is?”

“The birds and the bees.” Agatha clarifies. “Sex talk.”

“Yes, I have, actually.”

“And?”

“She handled it like a champ.” Rio shrugs. “She asked smart questions and if I remember correctly, she only rolled her eyes at me twice.”

“That tracks.”

“I gave her options, resources, boundaries—all that stuff. She told me she values her first time and isn’t rushing into anything.”

Agatha narrows her eyes. “Really?”

“Really.” Rio says without hesitation. “I also take precautions. Just like I mentioned earlier, the door always stays open when she has someone over. I also track her location when we’re not together so that's an added bonus.”

“Hence the tiktok trauma.” Agatha sighs, dragging a hand down her face. “Honestly? I feel better.”

“Do you?”

“Yes. I’ve heard kids act out when they’re deprived.”

Her own mother has always defined sexual intimacy as sins so severe they might as well be murder. Such desires rot your soul and send you screaming into hell if you so much as thought about them for too long. Being told otherwise didn’t soften Agatha. In fact, it did the opposite.

It made her curious. She was reckless in the way only a person raised on fear can be. She had to learn about biology and her options through television or educational pamphlets she'd seen around.

Rio nods sympathetically. “My parents did the same to me when I was younger. They were very honest and thorough. They even scared me half to death with pregnancy horror stories.”

“And?”

“I didn’t lose my virginity until I reached my twenties.”

Agatha smirks. “So high school was just making out?”

“Pretty much.”

“I lost mine when I was seventeen.” Agatha shares. “Well, as far as someone else's fingers go.”

Right on cue, Rae appears in the kitchen with her mystery friend in tow.

America looks exactly like the kind of kid who never learned to shrink herself. She has dark hair and wears a worn leather jacket slung over a plain tee. She takes one look at Agatha, bundled in borrowed clothes—and stops dead.

“Agatha Harkness! Rae wasn't kidding when she said you're here.” The girl moves forward, her eyes wide with excitement. “Hi! I’m America Chavez, Rae’s friend.” 

At least the kid has manners. Good for her.

“Hi. I’m Agatha.” She repeats, as if the name hasn’t already detonated in the room.

“It’s really nice to meet you.” America’s grin flickers into something softer. “My mother is a huge fan of your filmography. We watch some of them together.”

“My thank you, hon.” Agatha’s flattered. “Would you like an autograph for your mother?”

Rae watches this exchange with visible dread and speaks before America does. “Why are you still here?” She asks flatly, eyes on Agatha. 

Agatha scoffs, turning to her now. “Eager much?”

“You were supposed to leave.” Rae presses. “Like, hours ago.”

Rio, from the counter, offers gently, “She wasn’t feeling well, my love.”

“I’m feeling better now.” Agatha corrects reflexively because she refuses to be perceived as weak by a sixteen-year-old who inherited her attitude.

America glances between them as if she’s watching a tennis match. “You guys talk like—”

“Don’t finish that sentence,” Rae warns her.

Agatha bats her eyelashes in a mocking way. “Like family?”

Rae’s glare could peel paint. “No.”

America chuckles anyway, hands shoved into her jacket pockets. She looks comfortable here and she seems nice. That's a good thing. Agatha doesn't know what she knows about Rae being her biological daughter but if Rae did tell her, then that means she trusts her friend enough.

Rae turns toward the pantry. “We’re just grabbing snacks.”

“Disappointing.” Agatha mutters. “I was looking forward to spending more time with you.”

The teens disappear down the short hallway, voices trailing behind them. Rio silently watches her from across the kitchen, shrugging to herself.

A few minutes later, footsteps return alongside the crinkle of snack wrappers. Rae reappears with a bag of chips under one arm while America is balancing a handful of chocolate bars and a few canned drinks.

“Okay,” Rae stops short again. “You’re still here. Why?”

Agatha doesn’t even look up. “I might just stay another night.Who knows?”

“Please don't.” Rae frowns.

America speaks, getting herself in the middle. “It was really nice meeting you.”

Agatha meets her gaze. “Be a good influence to her, I think she really needs a role model.”

America nods without hesitation. “Always.”

“Stop talking to her.” Rae groans once more.

“Truly, your hospitality is unmatched.”

Rae shoots her a look. “You’re annoying.”

“And you’re a kid.” Agatha shoots back easily. “We all have our burdens.”

“Raina.” Rio says, calm but unmistakably firm. She doesn’t raise her voice, she never needs to. “That’s enough. That’s not how we talk to guests.”

Rae mutters something under her breath that sounds suspiciously like whatever. She adjusts the grip on the snacks and jerks her head toward the hallway. “Come on.”

America hesitates for half a second, glancing back with an apologetic little smile before following Rae. Their footsteps fade as they head back up to Rae’s wing.

Clearly Rae's not in the mood for a conversation so Agatha will try again next time. 

Agatha watches them go, then clicks her tongue. “She’s delightful.”

Rio sighs. “I’m sorry for that. I’ll make sure to speak with her.”

Agatha doesn't reply as she shifts in her seat, rolls her shoulders once, testing. The ache in her muscles is still there but it's no longer unbearable. Additionally, her head feels clearer. Her nose is still stuffy to no one's surprise, but she’s survived worse on far less.

“I’m better.” Agatha says, already pushing herself upright. “I really should leave now.”

Rio doesn’t respond right away. She steps closer instead, hand pressing gently to Agatha’s forehead before Agatha can protest.

Agatha freezes. “I didn’t consent to this examination.”

“Hush. Humor me.” Her hand is grounding as she frowns just a little. “You’re still hot.”

“I’m always hot. I maintain my figure through an active lifestyle and curated diet.”

“You are, but that's not the kind of hot I was referring to.” Rio smiles.  “I can drive you home.”

Agatha waves the suggestion off immediately. “No. Absolutely not.”

“Agatha—”

“I can drive.” She insists. “I’m not incapacitated and you’ve already done more than enough.”

Rio studies her, searching for cracks. “You’re sure?”

Agatha meets her gaze, steady. “I am.”

A beat passes. Then Rio nods, reluctantly. “Alright then.”

She gathers her things from the guestroom quickly without lingering any second longer. Downstairs, Rio is waiting by the door. They step outside together, the rain now a soft mist that clings to the environment. Westview feels almost polite this morning in contrast to the previous night.

Rio walks her to the car parked in the garage, keeping pace without crowding her. Agatha reaches the door, one hand on the handle, and feels Rio’s presence beside her. Close enough to feel, distant enough that she can pretend she isn’t noticing.

“Well,” Agatha breaks the ice. “This is where you tell me to text you when I get home.”

Rio’s amusement becomes evident. “Text me when you get home.”

“Predictable.”

“And you’ll do it anyway.” Rio doesn’t hesitate this time around.

She steps in, closes the distance, and presses a gentle peck to Agatha’s cheek. It’s enough to make Agatha’s breathing catch in the most irritating way.

Rio pulls back just far enough to look at her. “Drive safe.”

Agatha stares at her for a beat too long, then scoffs, flustered and annoyed at herself for it. “You know..” She says, opening the car door. “For someone so gentle, you’re alarmingly good at following orders.”

Rio winks. “Funny. I was thinking the same thing about you.”

Agatha pauses, one foot already inside the car. She glances back over her shoulder, smirking dangerously. 

“Oh?” She drawls. “Careful, Chef. Keep that up and people might get the wrong idea.”

Rio just lifts her brows, curious “What idea would that be?”

“That I tell you to jump..” Agatha grins, “And you’ll say how high.”

Rio taps the roof of the car once, a playful smile on her lips. “Text me.” She reiterates, closing the door herself.

Agatha smirks, leaning slightly out of the window as she starts the engine. “Thanks for the shelter, the food, and for putting up with me being a germ factory.”

“Anytime.” Rio replies, shrugging as if it’s no big deal, though the glint in her eyes says otherwise.

Agatha rolls the window up just a bit, her voice low. “Don’t let it get to your head.”

Rio laughs. “Too late.”

Agatha shakes her head with a small, reluctant grin and pulls out of the garage. She glances in the rearview mirror, catching Rio’s wave one last time. 

Somehow, she already knows she has a reason to come back or see the other woman soon. She’s still wearing Rio’s clothes, and she’s definitely not ready to stop thinking about her until then.
