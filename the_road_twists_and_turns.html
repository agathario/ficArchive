Summary

After Lilia Calderu flips the Tower card over, Agatha and Rio's fight is interrupted by a summons. A divine summons from Rio's father. Leaving the Road, Agatha and Rio have to pay the Underworld a visit. What do they learn once they are there?

Notes

Was anyone asking for an AU where there are some gods around? I have no idea. But the idea came to me and I couldn't stop thinking about it.

The lore from the comics and Greek myths is used hard and put away wet, so if you're a purest, you might want to look away now.

It picks up in the middle of Rio and Agatha's fight after Lilla dies.

Chapter 1

"... You call what you did special treatment?" Agatha asked with a mix of pain and anger in her eyes but also something else. There was a longing for the woman - no the entity because Agatha learned long ago that Rio Vidal might look like a woman but she could never be reduced to something so utterly human as that - to make all this pain that had twisted and turned inside every inch of her to go away.

Rio opened her mouth to offer a snappy retort but with a tilt of her head her mouth slammed shut. The venom covered barb had been right there on the tip of her tongue but with a single pull from somewhere far beyond this damned road the venom faded.

"We need to go," Rio said in a monotone voice. Her expression almost blank with just a slight undercurrent of confusion.

"Go?" Agatha scoffed.

"My father is summoning us. Both of us," Rio answered knowing that her answer would offer no clarification. For all the time they spent together, for all the conversations they had shared deeply personal details, the subject of Rio's familial connections had never come up.

"Father?" Agatha asked in utter confusion just as Rio expected. “Us? You have parents?”

“Of course. Immortal beings fuck too, Agatha," Rio said, slightly elongating the word 'fuck' just to try to get some sense of control back.

"I'm not going anywhere with you," Agatha said with a growing sense of anger that whatever tit for tat they were in the middle of had been shut down. She needed a fight. She craved it. And Rio had been the greatest sparring partner she could ever ask for.

"He is not someone you keep waiting when he summons," Rio said. "And Mother is there too. People think she's the sweet one but she has a steel spine that might very well..." She trailed off. "You don't need a lesson on my family tree right now. We need to go."

Agatha turned and walked over to a fallen tree and sat down. "Oh I think if you want me to go anywhere with you, I'm going to at least need the Reader's Digest version," she said with a quirk of her eyebrow.

Rio's jaw tightened. "The cosmos writes and rewrites itself. Gods are fact and fiction wrapped up in the same bow. The land beyond this one, the underworld, is ruled by a pair of entities that have styled themselves after part of the Greek Pantheon," she explained as she tried to unravel stories too old for even her to wade through to find a conclusion.

"You're telling me your parents are Hades and Persephone?" Agatha asked with a look of utter disbelief on her face.

"I'm telling you that is the story they have created for the last few thousand years. What came before doesn't matter anymore because they have rewritten the fabric of time and space to make it true. They do that sometimes when they get bored," Rio sighed as she tried to fight the urge to go get a human cigarette. It wasn't like Death could get lung cancer after all.

A dozen questions raced through Agatha's mind at this new information but the look in Rio's eyes told her that asking questions would lead to more frustration and to somehow less answers.

"And your father, Hades, is summoning us?" Agatha asked with disbelief like Rio had somehow grown a second head. No. If Rio had grown a second head that would make more sense than what she was being told now.

"Yes," Rio answered. "And if we keep him waiting much longer he might send someone to collect us."

"Collect?" Agatha asked with trepidation.

"Don't ask," Rio said with a hint of something akin to fear in her eyes.

"Right," Agatha said with a roll of her eyes. "Fine, I don't care if this makes sense or if you're setting up some trap to put my soul in a vase for the next thousand years. If your father is summoning us then let's leave this farce of a road and go visit. I always wanted to meet the in-laws."

The bonding ritual they did under a blood moon hundreds of years ago was still in full effect and made her words technically true. Still, they forced Rio to have to prevent herself from wincing at the familiarity of the term in-laws. That hit too close to where her black heart rested.

"Take my hand," Rio said in a somber tone. "Don't let go until I tell you it's okay."

"If this is a plot just to hold my hand Rio, I will find a way to cause you pain," Agatha said in warning even as her own fingers laced between Rio's.

"You've always been able to do that," Rio said before she pulled out the knife in her belt.

"What's that for?" Agatha asked as she took a half step away.

"This," Rio said simply.

Rio used the knife to cut a hole in the fabric of time and space and open a portal to the underworld. Sparks of sickly green glowed at the edges as the portal widened enough for the pair to slip through. Once the knife was sheathed again, Rio turned to Agatha.

"Ready?"

"Not one bit."

Rio nodded as she tugged Agatha through the portal without another word.

The thing that surprised Agatha the most about the underworld was how much light there was. She didn't have to squint to make out small bits of rock or try and find Rio at her side. As far as her eyes could see was bathed in a golden light.

"People think the underworld is dark and damp and depressed but it's not," Rio said as if she read Agatha's thoughts. In reality she just read her expression.

Besides them there was an infinite line of souls waiting to cross a river that was wider than she had ever seen before. Men, women and most painfully children stood in a queue together as a barge loaded up with a group of souls to bring them across the choppy water. Once they departed the line would move forward as the next group prepared.

"Here," Rio said as she held out a worn copper coin. "When we get to the barge you'll need to give it to Charon. No one rides for free."

"Even you?" Agatha asked with a forced tone.

"Even me if I materialize on this side of the river," Rio confirmed as they started walking beside the line toward the barge. Death got to cut the line after all.

The closer to the river they were the more Agatha could make out the other side. There were three massive islands with one in the center several times later than the other two islands - for lack of a better world Agatha thought to herself - floating in a sea of mist. The only part that seemed to really be on solid land was a path up to a large stone castle. The type of place that if it were up to her, she would sooner run from than head toward.

"My Lady," Charon called before bowing his head when the pair cut across the line of souls who all gave Rio a wide berth.

"Charon," Rio said with a bow of her head. "Daddy's calling his favorite child home and I brought the Mrs."

Without another word, Agatha and Rio disembarked the barge and headed up a path that led straight to the front of the castle.

"They are the different afterlives," Rio said when she turned her head and caught Agatha looking at the floating islands.

"Different afterlives?" Agatha questioned.

"Souls are judged and sent where they belong," Rio explained. "The big one in the middle in that soft white light is where most people go. A place for the mostly good, a little bad, and very human to end up. The one on the left bathed in golden light is where the very good, where heroes and those truly worthy, end up. And the one on the right that is darker than the rest is where the very worst spend their afterlife. Not even I visit there if I can help it."

Agatha's mind flashed to Nicky and where he would be. Her mind churned at the idea the island she would be cast to was likely very different from her son. She had no doubt if her soul was judged it wouldn't be bathed in golden light or even soft white. Nicky would be in the golden light, she was sure of it.

"Good to know I won't have you visiting after I meet my untimely end. A long, long, long time from now," Agatha forced out.

Rio just shook her head at the self deprecation. Judging souls wasn't her job and she forced herself to remember that. Whatever happened when Agatha died, all she had to do was get her here. Then fate and the natural order would take over. No fuss, no muss. Even as she thought it she knew it was a lie. There would be both fuss and muss when that day finally came.

After passing through the large stone entryway and walking down a hallway made of the same oily black stone they came to a large set of bog oak doors.

"Look, Agatha," Rio started as they stood in front of the closed doors to the throne room. "I don't know what is going to happen in there. I've never been summoned with a human I knew like I know you."

"You're telling me I'm the first woman you've brought home?" Agatha joked to try and find some sense of control.

"I'm telling you I will protect you as best I can but the forces at play might be very well out of even my control," Rio said seriously. "My parents are more powerful than you can understand."

"Got it," Agatha said as she turned more serious at the pleading look in Rio's eyes.

Without prompting from other of them, the massive doors swung open. The pillars inside of the throne room lighting it brighter than anywhere they had been so far down here. It was like high noon.

"Come forward, daughter and Ms. Harkness," A woman's voice called. The voice was like a spring rain on a tin roof. A comforting sound that would make lesser witches let their guard down.

"Show time," Rio whispered as they headed inside.

Seated before the pair at the end of the room were two figures with several hooked figures on either side of them.

The woman who beckoned them forward had hair the color of chestnuts, eyes as green as the finest emeralds, tanned skin and even seated Agatha could tell somehow the type of figure that would turn anyone's head.

Next to her, the lord of this realm, had a beard and head of hair as deep brown as freshly tilled soil. His eyes were stern and very nearly the same dark brown as his hair with flecks of gold contrasting the deep brown. He looked as fit as his wife, though Agatha cared much less about his fitness.

Agatha nearly let out a gasp when a massive, menacing looking three headed dog lifted the middle of the three heads that had been resting on the floor near his master.

"Pay Cerberus no mind," Hades commanded. "He is not used to mortals who have life still before them visiting us."

The mention of Agatha still having life before her relaxed Rio a lot more than Agatha.

"Not that I don't enjoy being summoned here," Rio said to her father. "But what would you like?" She asked in as sharp of a tone as she dared use with him.

"So quick to ask without a hug for your mother or a kiss to your father's cheek?" Hades asked. "Have you forgotten your manors?"

Rio fought to keep from rolling her eyes. She left Agatha's side to walk up the steps to the thrones to do as her father commanded. She hugged Persephone when she stood. The hug was a genuine reflection of the affection between the goddess of spring and nature and the original green witch.

Once the hug broke, Rio bent down to give her seated father a kiss on the cheek. She held his hand for a moment. Their relationship often had been one of deep affection even if she was more likely to push her luck than some of her siblings. Always one to test the limits even from the first moments of her creation.

"Now," Rio said when she was back to Agatha's side. "Why are we here?"

"There are two matters before us today," Hades answered. "The first is the matter of Ms. Harkness' immortality."

"Her what?"

"My what?"
