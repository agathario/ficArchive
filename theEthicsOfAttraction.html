Academic Disharmony

Agatha Harkness had developed a reputation for being an unshakable, no-nonsense professor. Her tall, graceful frame stood out in every room she entered, a force to be reckoned with. Her silver-streaked dark hair was always neatly pulled into a bun, yet a few strands would often escape, framing her face in a way that only enhanced her sharp features. There was a striking elegance to her—her tailored blazers and pressed trousers hinting at her strict professionalism—but also something undeniably alluring. Her piercing blue eyes, set beneath arched brows, could silence even the most disruptive students with a single glance, and yet, when she smiled (which was rare), the faintest hint of warmth seeped through her tough exterior.

Agatha didn't just command the room; she owned it, moving with the kind of precision that made you think every step, every look, was calculated. She was the kind of professor who didn’t tolerate nonsense, and everyone in the room knew it. Everyone, except Rio.

Rio Vidal—brilliant and utterly infuriating—was the exact opposite. Where Agatha was controlled, Rio was wild, all rough edges and mischievous smiles. She never seemed to sit still, her dark hair (always just messy enough to look like she didn’t care but in a way that suited her perfectly) falling into her deep brown eyes as she lounged in her seat. Every time she spoke, it was with a sarcastic drawl that made it sound like nothing was serious. But behind those half-lidded eyes and slouched posture, Rio was watching everything.

"Miss Vidal," Agatha began, glancing up from her desk as she collected papers. "I assume you have something insightful to add to today’s discussion on modern ethical dilemmas?"

Rio smirked, leaning back further in her chair, arms crossed over her chest in a way that made her shirt ride up just slightly, revealing the toned sliver of her midriff. "Insightful? I thought I was just here to look pretty, Professor."

Agatha's gaze didn't waver. She simply lifted an eyebrow, unimpressed but vaguely amused. "Unfortunately for you, I'm not interested in students with nothing but good looks to offer."

Rio's smirk widened. "So you’re admitting I’m good-looking?"

Agatha exhaled through her nose—an almost imperceptible reaction. "That’s your takeaway from all of this?" She turned her back to the class, walking toward the whiteboard, the sound of her heels clicking with authority. “Perhaps next time, try focusing on the material rather than whatever it is you think you're accomplishing."

Rio watched her, eyes flicking over the curve of Agatha’s back as she moved, appreciating the way her blazer hugged her waist just enough to hint at the figure beneath. Rio wasn’t subtle. She never was. She knew she was baiting Agatha, and that was the fun of it.

“I’m focusing,” Rio drawled, her voice low and playful. “Just not on what you want me to.”

Agatha didn’t turn around, but Rio caught the slightest hesitation in her stride. Something about that pleased her. Maybe, just maybe, the professor wasn’t as immune to her as she let on.

When Agatha finally did face her, her expression was cool, composed, but her eyes flashed with something that Rio couldn’t quite place. “Perhaps you’d care to demonstrate your focus in a paper, Miss Vidal. I’m sure you’re aware that your essays are due at the end of the week.”

A challenge. Rio grinned.

“I’ve got it handled,” she said, leaning forward just enough for her voice to take on a more serious tone, though her eyes sparkled with mischief. “I’m sure you’ll be impressed.”

Agatha met her gaze and, for just a fraction of a second, something softened in her eyes. She knew Rio wasn’t all talk. As much as the girl infuriated her with her constant teasing, Rio’s papers were always a stark contrast to her behavior in class—sharp, thoughtful, even brilliant at times.

But Agatha wasn’t about to let her know that.

“I look forward to reading it,” she said, her voice measured, though Rio could hear the faintest hint of something else—approval, maybe? It only fueled her further.

As class ended and students began to filter out, Rio hung back, lounging at her desk while the others left. Agatha was packing up her things, her movements precise, her gaze firmly on her bag. Rio took the opportunity to push her luck, as always.

“You know,” Rio said, her tone just a bit softer, almost intimate now that they were alone. “You could just admit it.”

Agatha didn’t look up. “Admit what?”

“That you think about me,” Rio said, standing up slowly, her steps purposeful as she approached the desk. She leaned over, close enough that Agatha could feel her presence without having to look. “Even when you don’t want to.”

Agatha’s hands froze for a moment, still hovering over the stack of papers she was organizing. For a split second, Rio thought she had finally cracked her, but then Agatha straightened, sliding the last of the papers into her bag. She turned to Rio, her expression unreadable.

“Miss Vidal,” Agatha said smoothly, her voice like silk, “if I thought about you, you’d know.”

With that, she brushed past Rio, leaving her standing there, watching her retreating form with a mixture of frustration and admiration.

“She’s good,” Rio muttered to herself, smirking despite the fact that Agatha had—once again—evaded her flirtations.

But Rio wasn’t one to give up easily. If anything, she enjoyed the chase.
