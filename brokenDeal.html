Broken Deal

The woman wasn’t surprised how he wasn’t ready to cross. Most likely he’d be roaming each continent in desperate attempts to find his brother for centuries to come. 

 

Selfishly, Rio couldn’t bother to waste another second thinking about the boy. Agatha’s words entered her mind so viciously. As if they could kill her themselves. Their deal was done, it was sealed, never again would things be the same. No more chasing Agatha, teasing her, trying to make amends any chance she could.

The pair were both so stubborn in their own ways. Rio swore it was a curse in itself they had to be that way. If only one could let loose, maybe the other would follow suit.

She studied how Agatha’s amazement turned into something different. The familiar slow wave of desire and lust taking a hold. She could only scoff, shaking her head.

“That voice of yours..” She smirked. “Only adds to it”

However, when she turned around, the overwhelming thoughts abruptly stopped. Her feet followed suit, eyes widening at the sight behind her.

“What happened to never wanting to see my face again?”

 

Agatha shrugged, scoffing (A fake scoff, Rio noted) with the gesture. 

 

“You must've trapped me here somehow, I can't leave” 

 

Rio tilted her head, eyes narrowing to see past the weak facade Agatha was wearing. She had to restrain a smirk over the pathetic lie.
