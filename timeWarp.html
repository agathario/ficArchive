Agatha was hung over. Just a little bit. 

Maybe she’d had too much to drink last night and maybe she’d made things even harder for herself by taking someone home. Kicking someone out was only fun if there wasn’t a splitting headache while she did it. 

Either way, hungover or not, Agatha was sure she’d find Billy’s voice just as grating. 

“Are you excited? This is your first day for Coven of Chaos 2 ! Do you think it’ll be hard to get back into character after all these years? I loved the original movie as a kid. I mean, I still love it now, but–”

“Shut up.” 

Agatha didn’t even need to look to know Billy was looking at her with that same, kicked puppy look. Sinking further into the leather seat, she adjusted her sunglasses and cradled her coffee to her chest. She was getting too old to be drinking the way she did last night, but it’d been a celebration. Nothing like getting wasted to kick off a sequel that took nearly twenty years to make happen.

Not that she’d ever admit it out loud, but Agatha had a soft spot for Coven of Chaos . It was her breakout role after a few smaller roles in college, solidifying her place in Hollywood as more than just her mother’s daughter. It had been a good distraction too, keeping her busy when all her mind wanted to do was think about something else.  

“Are you excited to see Lilia and Jen again?” Billy asked, clearly not understanding the need for quiet. He was such a goody two shoes. Agatha doubted he would ever have a drop of alcohol before he was legal. “When was the last time you all worked together?”

 “A TV show a few years ago,” Agatha said. Maybe if she answered him then he’d shut up.

“Do you ever wish you got to work with them more?”

Apparently she wasn’t going to get any peace and quiet any time soon.

“Yes,” Agatha said. If Billy was going to keep talking like this, he was only getting the bare minimum from her.

Agatha didn’t have many friends in the business. She’d made it a point to keep everyone at an arm’s distance, and while she wouldn’t really call Jen or Lilia a friend, they weren’t strangers either. Another thing she would never admit out loud was that she was actually looking forward to working with them again. They really weren’t anything more than coworkers, but Agatha knew both women took their work seriously, talented actresses in their own right. 

When the driver finally pulled up to the studio’s building, Agatha couldn’t help but feel relieved that today was more of a meeting than anything else. She couldn’t act to save her life right now. With a stifled groan, Agatha dragged herself out of her car and headed inside, Billy trailing after her still talking. One day she was going to tape that boy’s mouth shut.

The meeting today was taking place in a small conference room, long tables creating a large rectangle where everyone would be facing inwards. Agatha rolled her eyes. Great, now everyone would get prime angles to see just how shitty her hangover was. She circled the room, finding her name towards the center and front of the room, next to Lilia and a placard that simply read Director . 

Agatha furrowed her brows. That was one thing Agatha didn’t understand about this production, the secrecy behind the director. Her agent had been strangely skittish every time Agatha mentioned this, and even her mother had been evasive about the fact, which was even more suspicious considering she was an executive producer for the film. 

“I didn’t have much of a say,” Evanora had told her, looking faintly disgusted. “The studio wanted this director and this director alone.”

This only made Agatha all the more curious yet wary. The studio wanting this specific director so badly that they went against her mother said a lot about the director’s talent. Evanora disliking the director also said a lot, but Agatha wasn’t sure exactly what it said just yet. For all Agatha knew, Evanora just thought this director had looked at her wrong at an event, or maybe, this director went against all of her mother’s delicate sensibilities.  

Agatha had been slumped in her chair for about ten minutes when Jen arrived with a haughty jut of her chin. In response, Agatha wiggled her fingers in a sarcastic hello. Jen predictably rolled her eyes at her and sat one seat away from her, on the other side of Lilia’s chair. She was dressed impeccably as always, gold jewelry glinting even in the dim lighting of the room and dressed without a single crease. A stark difference to Agatha’s jeans and wrinkled button-down.

The two of them lasted a whole five minutes in silence before Agatha asked, “Hey, do you know who this hotshot director is supposed to be?”

“Wow, thank you so much for asking how I am, Agatha,” Jen said, making Agatha scoff. “It’s been, what? Five years? It means a lot for you to ask.” Then, as if she just processed what Agatha asked, she stared at her in bewilderment. “Wait, you don’t know?”

Agatha paused, studying Jen’s face. She looked genuinely surprised, and that only worsened the sinking feeling in her stomach. “You do.” It was a statement, not a question. 

“Yeah,” Jen nodded slowly. “I found out weeks ago.”

“What did you find out about weeks ago?” Lilia asked, sliding into her seat between them. Her curls were piled up high on her head, and her clothes were her usual hippie affair. Agatha barely held back a snort.

“Why can’t either of you have manners?” Jen huffed, but when Lilia just stared at her, she gave in quickly. “The director,” Jen explained. “Agatha doesn’t know who it is.”

Lilia raised an eyebrow. “You don’t?”

“We wouldn’t be having this conversation if I did,” Agatha snapped.

“That’s odd,” Lilia said, studying Agatha in a way that made her want to squirm. Instead, she settled for fidgeting with her now empty coffee cup. “She’s the reason I signed on to the project,” Lilia continued thoughtfully. “I thought she’d do right by the aesthetics and intentions of the first film.”

“She,” Agatha repeated.

Both Jen and Lilia gave her a look at this. “Of course,” Jen said, seemingly torn between concern and annoyance. “Do you really think we’d want to do this movie if it wasn’t directed by another woman?”

Agatha gave a small shrug. She’d signed on without knowing that fact. When it came down to it, nostalgia aside, the sequel seemed like easy money, and when both her agent and mother insisted she do it, Agatha agreed without a second thought. 

“Oh, here she is,” Jen said. “Your mystery director.” This was followed by a quiet hum of approval. “She’s even hotter in person.”

Agatha snorted, but when she looked up, she realized exactly why she was never told who the director would be. She never would’ve fucking signed on otherwise. 

Standing in the doorway, Rio Vidal smiled at her. “Hello, Agatha.”

“Don’t you have class right now?” Alice replied pointedly as she sat down next to her.

“Thought I could play my guitar,” Alice shrugged, but she wasn’t making eye contact, meaning she was being all self-conscious. 

Sighing, Rio said, “I’ll come with. I have nothing better to do.”

The two of them stayed out there for another hour with Alice working on an essay and Rio weaving together flowers into a crown. One girl caught her eye though.

At the same time though, no one sat anywhere near her, leaving a neat little circle of chairs empty around her. It never made much sense to Rio how a pretty girl with blue eyes like her didn’t have people flocking to her.

She didn’t look much like her mother.

Rio hummed but let the subject drop. She didn’t think much of Agatha Harkness again until later that week when she went to her college algebra class. Just like every class before, Agatha sat in the center of the seats with no one around her. 

Now that she knew who she was, Rio understood the condescending haughtiness the other girl seemed to exude, and she couldn’t help but smile at it. She knew girls like Agatha Harkness after years in private school. Well, years of getting kicked out of private schools.

Agatha looked like she wanted to say it was, but eventually, she shook her head and turned back around.

Even though she was following the professor, she clearly had no idea what she was doing, and her notes were a war zone of numbers and letters. This time, Rio expected the glare Agatha shot her, and she couldn’t help but smile. It was attractive on her, and it was also much more interesting than that weird, blank look she always wore. “Are you the professor?” Agatha asked her. Her voice was lower than Rio expected, not the high-pitched prissy pitch she associated with girls like Agatha. 

“No,” Rio said.

“Are you the TA?”

“No.”

Agatha sneered, “Then mind your business.”

Rio met her eyes for a long moment before she let out a quiet laugh. “Fine,” she shrugged. She shoved her notebook into her backpack and stood up, savoring the chance to look down on the other girl. “But when you finally accept that you need a tutor, let me know, Harkness.” 

With that, Rio strode to the front of the classroom to turn in her work. She watched as the professor checked over her work and answers. He nodded approvingly and told her to have a good day. Rio replied in kind, and when she looked up, she found Agatha still glaring at her, her pencils gripped tightly in her hand. 

Rio blew her a kiss as she walked out of class.

It looked like classes were going to be a lot more interesting from now on.
