Mismatch

Rio Vidal had one rule in life that she followed almost religiously: work enough to pay her bills and party hard enough to make a mess. At almost 30, she still hadn’t found anyone—man or woman—who made her want to abandon her friend’s company and the warm atmosphere of her favorite bar every Friday night.

In fact, Rio doubted such a person even existed.

So it was understandable the frustration she was feeling when, at 9:47 PM on a Friday, a notification from her friend popped up on her phone.

“WHERE ARE YOU????” Jen didn’t hesitate to emphasize the question.

Rio cast a tired glance at the stack of reports beside her, reaching almost at her shoulders, a mountain of obligations that seemed never to shrink. She worked at Harkness & Calderu, one of the most respected law firms in New York City, a place where dreams were shaped and often shattered.

Part of her life philosophy was shaped by that environment. For years, she watched associates bend over backward, sleepless nights trying to become partners in the firm, many with their aspirations falling short of expectations. As a paralegal, she decided not to waste her youth stuck in a cubicle.

Well, that was what she had in mind. Her boss, Agatha Harkness, who owned half the company and was known for her stiffness, certainly had other plans.

“The witch has me under her spell,” she quickly replied to her friend’s message, a wry smile forming on her lips.

“Witch” was an affectionate nickname Rio had given her boss, who clearly had no idea. And perhaps it was better that way.

On the other side of the office, through a glass door and under the only light on in the room, Agatha had her head buried in paperwork, as if the outside world didn’t exist. Even from behind several cubicles, Rio could see the woman’s silhouette.

“Vidal!” Agatha's voice reverberated through the room. “I need those reports right now.” She lifted her head, fixing her penetrating gaze on Rio.

“Then you can do it yourself,” Rio shot back, almost inaudibly, her determination growing. She gathered half of the reports she had finished, walked discreetly to her boss's office, pushed the door open, and gently dumped the stack on the desk. “I’m beyond my working hours.”

Agatha placed her hands on her head, showing impatience, and leaned over the desk to face Rio.

“I’ll pay you double for the overtime,” she offered, her tone impersonal as always.

“I don’t need the money.”

Agatha sized her up with an inquisitive, almost disdainful look.

“Are you sure?”

Rio rolled her eyes, indignation clear on her face. She hated Agatha’s condescending and elitist attitude toward other employees.

“I’m sure, and I’m also late for an appointment.”

The appointment in question was to get drunk on the highest-alcohol drink she could find at Road’s Bar, then dance around with the hottest person who crossed her path, and finally end the night on a high note, being carried home by her friends Jen and Alice.

She could still hear her boss shouting her name one last time, the voice echoing through the office, before crossing through the elevator doors.

Upon entering Road’s bar, a wave of warmth and laughter enveloped Rio like a cozy hug. With the music playing loudly, wide smiles and familiar faces made their way to Rio, and the everyday worries were left at the door. As soon as she spotted Jen and Alice sitting at a table near the bar, a broad smile spread across her face, wiping away any trace of accumulated stress.

“Finally!” Jen exclaimed, raising her beer glass as if toasting. The sparkle in her eyes was contagious. “You’re late, but better late than never!”

“Sorry, I was in an exciting meeting with the witch.” Rio rolled her eyes but couldn’t help laughing at the look of anticipation on her friends’ faces. “What did I miss?”

Jen, always the boldest of the group, leaned forward, almost bouncing in her seat with excitement.

“Oh, just had the brightest idea of our lives!” She gestured dramatically. “Remember that nerdy phase of mine when I created a profile on a literary social network?”

“You were convinced you’d find the love of your life there,” Rio replied disinterestedly, as the waiter handed her a large glass of beer, which she immediately began to drink.

“Look who I found there!” Jen turned her phone screen around, revealing a profile of a woman they all knew very well: Agatha Harkness!

Rio couldn’t contain her laughter.

“No way!” She grabbed her friend’s phone for a closer look; it was definitely her boss.

“Let’s create a fake profile and mess with Agatha!” Jen practically screamed with excitement.

Alice, the most reserved and dedicated of the trio, was clearly uncomfortable with the idea.

“I don’t know, girls. That’s not exactly ethical; Agatha is our boss.”

“Oh, come on, Alice!” Jen insisted, a mischievous glint in her eyes. “She needs a dose of lightness. Look how she buries herself in work all the time, we need to shake that witch up!”

Agatha’s hand remained there, affectionately, as if, for the first time, she were fully accepting what she felt and what was between them. It was no longer a game, nor a struggle. It was real.

The world outside could wait.
