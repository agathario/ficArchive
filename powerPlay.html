Summary

Veteran consultant Rio Vidal is called in to assist with a presidential campaign. Rio thought she’d seen and conquered all, until the candidate’s spouse proves to be more of a challenge than she’d anticipated— both professionally and personally.

An election AU that absolutely no one asked for.

PROLOGUE

December 4, 2023

“No.”

“I’m sorry?” Jen fired back incredulously.

“You heard me,” Rio said firmly but distantly as she gazed out the window. “I’m not interested.”

“You barely even heard the pitch,” Jen let out, her frustration getting the best of her. “I know this wasn’t what you expected when we called you in this morning; consider it a favor for Lilia, one she will repay generously when the time comes.”

Rio let out a groan, spun around and walked back towards the desk behind which Jen was sitting. Through the glass window behind her, the Washington Mall was covered in a thick layer of snow. Rio always thought DC looked more appealing during winter time, when temperatures dropped and only but the toughest runners and most determined tourists ventured outside. It was quiet, and a sight to admire.

“I’m a political consultant,” Rio said tersely, “a strategist, a spin-doctor, whatever you want to call me— just not a babysitter .”

Jen rolled her eyes, “You know that this job sometimes entails ushering family members through the election just as much as the candidates— and it requires even more tact and labor than dealing with the politicians themselves.”

Rio slumped on the chair across from her and looked through Senator Bohner’s file again. He had just been re-elected for a second term last year, so his presidential bid was most likely just an attempt to access a higher profile office than the one he currently had. Married, no children, Republican. Rio gagged. His track record was mediocre at best, and while Rio had had no issue in the past running campaigns for both sides of the aisle, she felt a pit in her stomach at the prospect of shoving Ralph-mother-fucking-Bohner’s face down the American people’s throats. The last thing the country needed was another self-serving white man with no moral compass making life and death decisions on behalf of his constituents. Still, Rio hesitated — the request had come from Lilia after all, who didn’t make brash decisions and certainly wouldn’t call in a favor unless it was important to her to get her best colleagues on the case. Especially if the job wasn’t directly involved with the candidate himself.

“Tell me about the wife,” Rio said defeatedly.

“Agatha Harkness, 48, equity partner at a BigLaw firm out in NoVa,” Jen said without batting an eye. “I’ve met her, she’s a total bitch,” she added with a wink, “clearly Lilia thought she’d be a handful, too, otherwise…” Jen gestured at Rio with her hands, fighting back a delighted smile. The pair had known each other for over a decade, and while outwardly projected an image of professionalism, in private there was nothing they enjoyed more than riling the other up like a pair of children competing for their parents’ attention.

Rio chose to ignore her, as she continued going through the folder until she came across a picture of the woman they’d just been discussing. It was a professional headshot, likely pulled from the law firm’s website. A stern-looking woman stared back at her, her bright blue eyes conveying a mixture of contempt and defiance for whoever had taken the photo, not a hint of a smile in sight. The kind of lawyer I would hire if I wanted to make sure someone cried on the stand , Rio mused. Yet underneath it all there was a certain sadness to her that struck a cord with Rio. This beautiful, powerful, rich woman was on the verge of being relentlessly dragged through the public eye so that her husband could score some points for his next career move. And knowing the other candidates who had already announced their run, it was shaping up to be a brutal fight, where old skeletons were all but guaranteed to come out, the benefit of the doubt scarce, and every misstep broadcast on the only thing more devastating than the 24-hour news cycle, social media. Lilia wanted to protect her as much as she could, that’s why she’d called in the big dogs.

“She needs someone sharp and savvy who can help her navigate the primaries,” Jen added, “an ally in her camp who isn’t on her husband’s payroll.”

Rio stared at Agatha’s eyes one more time before closing the dossier and setting it on the table. This was not how she had expected to spend the first couple of months of an election year, but there was something about this request that made her feel like this was where she needed to be.

“How does Ms. Harkness feel about getting a handler?” Rio asked curiously, although deep down she had a feeling she already knew the answer.

Jen grinned knowingly. “I’m sure she’ll love the idea after you break it to her,” she added with dark satisfaction, “as a matter of fact, that’s the first item on your agenda— that is, of course, if you’re agreeing to take her on.”

Rio face planted dramatically on Jen’s desk. She allowed herself a moment to gather her bearings, and when she straightened up, her face was resolute. She’d done much harder jobs than this before. How much more difficult could this be?

“Tell Lilia I’m in— and that however much she thinks she owes me, she better double it,” Rio said, putting the files in her messenger bag and making a beeline for the exit. Jen’s phone began ringing, and the conversation was left at that.

As the cold air hit her face as she exited the office building lobby, Rio wondered for the first time if she had actually bitten off more than she could chew.

Chapter 2

Rio woke up at the crack of dawn the next day to a text from Lilia

Gave A a heads up-- she's expecting you in her office today.

Rio stretched her arms and legs in bed, recalling her meeting with Jen yesterday while taking in her surroundings. The Coven, Lilia's consulting group, had booked her a suite at the Kimpton prior to her meeting, and it was now to become her temporary home until they hit the road with Bohner's campaign or called it quits-- whichever happened first. It was conveniently close to the Bohner-Harkness residence as well The Coven offices, but a long way from her destination today.

Padding barefoot towards the bathroom, she started making mental notes on all the things she needed to prepare ahead of her first encounter. Jen's assistant, Alice, had emailed Rio the preliminary opposition research the Coven had conducted on the couple for both of his Senate campaigns, along with notes and highlights from the previous campaign managers.

The first thing that stood out to Rio was how uninvolved Agatha had been in her husband's previous campaigns. There were a few pictures of her on the campaign trail, and a couple at donor events, but Agatha hadn't given a single interview, or speech, or introduction for Ralph at any of his fundraisers. She hadn't leveraged her rolodex for more donations, or contributed to the campaign in any meaningful way.

It was also exceedingly clear that that hadn't been an accident. There were dozens of offhand comments from senior campaign staff about her reluctance or outright unwillingness to cooperate with the campaign. A former communications director wrote in the post mortem for his first run that "Bohner could have greatly outperformed his divorced, openly gay rival among more conservative voters had he been able to get his wife to show an ounce of support for him in public" with another staffer during his re-election bid remarking "at this point, it's unclear if she would even show up to his funeral if he were to drop dead tomorrow." That one earned a chuckle out of Rio.

It had paid off for Agatha. Very little outside of her professional life at the law firm was known about her, and because Senate races didn't typically draw as much attention at a national stage, especially outside of swing states, she had managed to walk away unscathed from both campaigns. Even now, if you Googled her name, the first 20 results pertained to her legal work. The image search showed various professional headshots taken over the years.

Rio made herself a cup of coffee with the Keurig in her room while she continued to browse through the files. She hesitated before opening the oppo research. Typically she would go over it with the candidate's consent, but seeing as she was neither the candidate nor the one who had commissioned it, she decided she'd rather have as much information as possible before the meeting.

Having seen her fair share of oppo research in her lifetime, Harkness and Bohner's was... unremarkable. Which could mean one of two things: either both had led relatively quiet and private lives prior to his first run, devoid of scandal, or they'd done a damn good job at covering it up.

Bohner came from a large working class family in Ohio, went to state school for undergrad on a scholarship then Yale for law school (on a conservative Christian fellowship), where he met his future wife. Worked for 15 years as a litigation attorney before he was tapped by a conservative group as a potential candidate for an open seat in the Senate, which he won on his first try.

Agatha on the other hand was the only child of Ted and Evanora Harkness, a wealthy established family from Massachusetts, sole heiress to the family fortune. Her father passed when she was in her early 30s, and a small note at the bottom of the document pointed to the fact that she and her mother were estranged. Agatha had hopped around a couple of law firms before becoming a lateral hire at her current firm, where she made partner and head of practice.

Rio finished her coffee and closed her computer. So , she thought to herself, polar opposites . Ralph did everything he could to catch someone's attention to outgrow his working-class roots. Agatha used her family's wealth and position to keep a low profile and carry about her professional life.

Splashing some water on her face, Rio fixed her makeup and got dressed for the day. She made a quick call to the front desk so they could find her a driver to take her out to Agatha's firm in Virginia. She took one final look in the mirror before leaving the room, her messenger bag in tow, feeling more in control regarding the job than several hours prior.

The ride was relatively quiet, but by the time she walked through the lobby doors, a strange feeling of uneasiness had taken hold of her stomach. She tried to quiet it down by doing some breathing exercises in the elevator, but she was still on the edge when the receptionist told her to take a seat while she waited for Agatha to come get her. Rio had always taken pride in the level-headed, almost nonchalant image of herself she projected to others, but that version was nowhere to be found that morning.

After a couple of minutes of waiting, a skinny, dark-haired boy approached her. He looked very young, and his dark eyeliner and nervous excitement seemed completely out of place in the office environment.

"Are you Rio Vidal?" he asked quickly, and Rio nodded.

"Great! I'm Billy, by the way, Agatha's assistant. She's waiting for you in her office. Did I pronounce your name correctly? Is it Vee-dal or Vy-dal? When I asked Agatha she just gave me her usual death stare... you know the one... wait, you actually don't! Maybe I shouldn't have said that, can you pretend I never said that, please? My mom got me this job and I would really like to keep it so I can save enough to buy tickets to the Eras Tour for me and my boyfriend in Europe this summer... and you definitely didn't need to know all that, oh my God, I'm so sorry, it's just Agatha looked so nervous before I came out here and that made me nervous too and-"

"Billy," Rio said, stopping him in his tracks, "it's okay, take a deep breath for me."

Billy exhaled dramatically, after which he gave Rio a nervous grin. She returned his easy smile, and patted him on the back.

"Relax, kid, you're too young to be this stressed out."

"I know it's probably hard to believe after what you just witnessed, but I'm typically very calm and collected," Billy added sheepishly.

Rio gave him a wide grin this time. "Are you going to take me to the dragon's lair now?"

"Right, yes, sorry. She's not that bad, you know," he added. Billy led them down a corridor to one of the corner offices. Right outside the door was a small desk and matching chair, Billy's, Rio presumed. "I would probably just say she's misunderstood more than anything."

"Is that so?" Came a cool voice from behind them.

Rio and Billy turned around to see Agatha Harkness, a cup of freshly brewed tea in her right hand and a look of profound irritation on her face. Billy cowered and ran back to sit behind his desk, eyes downcast.

Agatha stood impossibly tall in front of Rio, her blue eyes searching for something on her, before she abruptly turned around and walked into her office without another word.

Chapter 4

Rio took an Uber back to her hotel to freshen up before her next meeting. Agatha had left her rattled. There was something about her, about her motivations, about her support for her husband that didn’t quite seem right to her. Her carefully constructed life could very easily come apart under public scrutiny, and she seemed to recognize that, at least to a certain extent. And yet, for some mysterious reason, she was going along with it instead of calling it quits. It was the kind of unconditional support that she’d only seen before in deeply committed couples— and by all accounts, Agatha and Ralph’s relationship appeared to be a lot more detached and independent. She was looking forward to observing them interact in private, perhaps that would yield some insight into their relationship dynamics.

Her phone buzzed in her bag, and he fished it out to find a text from an unknown number.

Rio smiled at her text in spite of herself and saved Agatha’s number. Rio worried at her lip, she knew better than to allow herself to give too much of herself to a campaign, from a personal standpoint. Emotions get heightened, the stakes feel impossibly high, and lines can get blurred in the late hours of the day on the campaign trail. Rio had seen too many of her colleagues make mistakes they’d come to regret. She needed to get a grip before she made some mistakes of her own. Rio wasn’t impressed; she was, however, surprised that they anticipated Bohner would outperform most of his opponents in Iowa and New Hampshire. If that were to be the case, Bohner would stay in the race past Super Tuesday. For now, it was all speculative.

“What’s your end game here? State? Veep?” Rio asked Bohner point blank.

Right, so literally anything that made him feel more powerful than he already does. Self-serving shit.

“Have you discussed this with Agatha?”

“She would rather I take any job that doesn’t involve her taking an office of her own, if it comes to that. And I will respect that.”

“Good,” Rio thought out loud.

“If that is all for the moment, I think we should call it a day, folks,” Bohner said, “Ms. Vidal, a word?”

They waited a couple of minutes for the room to clear before he spoke again.

“Look, I don’t know what you’ve heard, but I need you to understand I care about Agatha’s wishes.”

Rio found that hard to believe, especially after her conversation with her that very morning. She let him go on.

“We have found a way to make things work in the past and I’m hoping we can continue doing so in the future. We may want completely different things, but we’re good for each other. We’ve been married for almost 25 years, so as you can imagine this isn’t the first time we’ve disagreed on something. But I firmly believe we can both get what we want.”

“And what, in your opinion, does your wife want?” Rio challenged.

“The proximity to power, without getting her hands dirty.”

That answer stunned Rio. The woman she’d met hadn’t struck her as someone who would court political power for her own personal gain. If anything, she’d seemed like she relished the power she herself wielded in her professional life, independent of her husband. She’d gone through quite the trouble to distance herself from him politically in the past, and her legal work didn’t conflict with his work in the Senate. Rio raised an eyebrow at Bohner.

“Maybe not directly… but she craves the protection a powerful man in office can afford her,” he conceded, “and if in the process, I get the privilege to serve my country in a higher office… then I think it’s a win all around.”

Rio nodded, and Bohner offered her a ride back to their residence for their evening debriefing.

But Rio couldn’t help but wonder, what did Agatha need to be protected from?

Rio approached them, hoping to get Agatha on a taxi to the airport as soon as humanly possible.

“So inspiring.” Rio heard Cushing say to Agatha. The woman looked more stern than she’d ever seen her before.

“It really is shame you were put on administrative leave,” Cushing added, “you’ve always brought so much passion to your work.” There was something in the Senator’s voice that made Rio shiver. “Campaigns really do bring out the worst in people and turn your world upside down. At least this will all be over soon— hopefully, that is.”

“We’ll see about that,” Bohner said firmly, a practiced smile plastered on his face. “Darling, we should go find our people, they’re probably eager to debrief. A pleasure as always, Edward.”

“See you around,” Cushing replied, his eyes on Agatha.

Bohner led them to his dressing room and asked his team for a couple of minutes alone. Rio and Billy didn’t make a move towards the door.

“I’d like to have a word alone with my wife.”

“Billy, you go find the others, we’ll come meet you in a couple of minutes,” Rio instructed.

“By alone, I meant just the two of us,” Bohner said sternly.

“Rio can stay,” Agatha chimed in, her eyes distant.

“I really don’t think…”

“She knows.”

Rio did not know what they were referring to.

Bohner eyed his wife curiously. “She knows?”

“Yeah.”

Bohner turned to Rio and looked her up and down, searching for answers. Seemingly finding none, he turned back to Agatha.

“You heard what he said, about the story. It clearly scared him.”

“He didn’t seem scared. If anything, he seemed… ready for a fight,” Agatha retorted.

“It would certainly explain the timeline of his announcement. That and the fact that my polling after my speech went through the roof.”

“I’m sorry,” Rio jumped in, “I’m lost here.”

“I thought you said she knew?” Bohner asked pointedly.

“She knows about the abuse,” Agatha replied, tears in her eyes. “I didn’t… I wanted to tell you earlier.”

“Tell me what?” Rio’s heart was beating frantically, a mix of anticipation and dread.

“My dad’s friend… the one who—you know— it was Edward Cushing.”
