Summary

After a successful and -- mostly -- fun trip to Vegas, life is set to return to normal for the Harkness-Vidals. Well, as close to normal as could be given the fact that for the first time since meeting they've got a couple of someones to return home to.

With a brief respite, Rio very quickly realizes that Agatha is up to something, something... mischievous.

An abnormal road trip in the middle of the day plants a seed of growth for their life going forward and nurtures a part of Rio she hadn't known needed to bloom.

Oh, The Sunlit Haze

For the Harkness-Vidals, returning home after a vacation had always consisted of a set ritual. 

It was an arrival, an unpacking, and a very, very long nap of recovery. It had been the same every single year since they'd started the tradition.

This year?

This year, things were different. Alice had insisted that she could be the one to pick them up from the airport, but Agatha assured her they would be fine with an Uber and would see them once they landed.

So, as Rio shut the trunk of the rideshare and grabbed the handles of both suitcases to drag them up the driveway, she was not as surprised as she should have been when the front door swung open and Ace came bounding out to greet them, first running directly into Agatha's legs before tearing off toward Rio.

Rio knew she should have braced, should have tried to fortify her stance to prevent disaster, but it was useless. Ace leapt off the ground with force, knocking into her and breaking her balance. As she toppled backward over one of the suitcases, she was in stitches before she even hit the ground.

"Okay! I missed you too!" she managed through uncontainable laughter, but the way Ace began to lap at her face with wet kisses made it difficult to keep an even cadence.

Peripheral to the chaos, both Jen and Alice descended the front porch steps bearing grins. Cradled in Alice's arm was Zap who, for the moment, seemed distracted by the spiked dangle on her earring.

"Welcome home!" Alice greeted, a genuine grin lighting up her face. The moment she got close enough, Zap's nose twitched not once, but twice. The smallest litle turns of his head started alow, but became a little more frantic, as if he was searching for the source of a new smell, a familiar smell. The moment Alice stepped even closer, his attention snapped immediately to Agatha. The very second those beady little eyes landed on her, he quickly began to fight his way out of Alice's containment, clearly wanting Agatha.

"Come here, my boy," Agatha cooed at him, stepping forward to scoop him from Alice's grasp.

When she had a good hold on him, his relief became evident in the way he practically melted into her.

His little ekekekekeks were consistent and loud as his tiny hands grabbed and rummaged through Agatha's hair, as if he were looking for the reason she had been gone for so long. He was sniffing and huffing, nuzzling his face into her neck as he remembered what it was like to be held by her.

His safe place, his bond.

His Agatha.

Seeing the way he had missed her, it struck something in Rio; they had taken him in with little intention of keeping him and now? Now, Rio couldn't imagine life without him, and she knew Agatha couldn't, either.

The sounds he was making pulled instantly at Ace's attention, her head whipping up to identify the source of his anguish. When she realized that Agatha was the one holding him and that his anguish was actually excitement, she was quick to return to her task of pawing and licking at Rio's arms and face, despite Rio's weak protests; of course she could never really mean it.

In fact, those protests altogether waned when her eyes lifted again to the sight of Agatha, holding onto Zap while they both basked in the appreciation that absence had instilled in them. There was no questioning the fact that Zap had softened Agatha beyond measure, and if anyone else -- Jen especially -- tried to point it out, Agatha would have turned into a goddamn nightmare, but to Rio? 

To Rio, that was her whole life. 

It was Agatha, her impossibly stubborn and mischievous wife and Zap, the whole reason they now had something they could actually call a 'family.' It was the sight of them after a week apart and how clearly both hearts had grown fonder. The connection that was there between them, it was strong and had grown out of those first few weeks, those countless hours that Agatha had doted on him and fed him and taken care of him.

And then, it was also the dog that had flopped to her back between Rio's legs and wriggled around in desperate need of more attention.

"Hi gorgeous," Rio sweet talked her, leaning forward to rub at Ace's belly, causing her tail to thump heavily in delight. After a moment longer of showering her in scratches and pets, Rio's focus lifted to their friends, a bright grin on her face.

"They were mildly well behaved then? And no one let Zap play with matches, I see," she joked, her eyes wrinkling at the corners with her smile as she finally picked herself up off the ground to offer them each a hug of both greeting and gratitude.

"I thought about it," Alice chirped facetiously, smirking. "But... Yes. Well behaved, if not a little rowdy. And... We did invest an additional thirty dollars in blueberries... Jen really hated it when Ace would look at her with sad eyes because Zap got some and she didn't." Alice nudged her fiancée who was primarily fixated on Rio with a genuine enough smile, though Rio did not miss the way her eyes would occasionally flicker to Agatha. 

Agatha? 

She was none the wiser. In fact, if she would have caught Jen looking at her like that, she would have snapped right out of the softened state she had fallen into the moment she had pulled the little raccoon into her arms and raised her hackles with no hesitation.

Yes, Agatha could be soft. And apparently, missing home had caused her to forget that she had an audience she often tried not to be perceived by. Alice would have been fine, but for the moment -- and thankfully -- it seemed as though Agatha had forgotten Jen's existence. 

With Rio having departed from her spot on the ground, an oh-so-neglected Ace was back on the hunt for someone else to pay attention to her. With a quickness, she zipped toward Agatha -- who had been a bit better at bracing herself than Rio had been -- as she shifted Zap to one arm in preparation. Her hand flew out, carefully catching Ace before she could launch.

"Ace, sit!" she ordered firmly.

Immediately, the dog settled and plopped her hind end to the ground obediently, those big brown eyes peering up at her mother. 

Above her, Agatha had been stoic in delivering the command, but the moment Ace complied, Agatha's lips pulled wide into a grin and she leaned down to offer heavy and commendatory rubs to Ace's head and ears. 

"That's a good girl," she cooed at her. Concurrently, Zap had started to wriggle around again, this time reaching for Ace.

His friend. His compatriot. His pal.

Understanding his need, Agatha set him down, only a couple of inches of space between him and the dog. Instantly, his attention snapped to Ace, his tiny little mitts grabbing and clutching at her fur to scale his way up her back as he chirped his excitement the entire time.

Life could be strange in a variety of negative ways, but right then, the best kind of strange unfolded in front of them in the form of their two animals and the bond they had woven between them.

Her hands lifted -- almost too high for the quickest second -- and adjusted the waist of her joggers, simply because she was not wearing a blazer that she could have tugged at with an air of defiance. "It really wasn't an inconvenience. They're so much fun. And I think it went a little further in convincing her to let me adopt a raccoon, too," she joked, lightly bumping her hip into her fiancée's.

His frantic clicks and chirps pulled at Rio's attention, causing her to head straight for him. Fastened into that little rut of her elbow on his back, he was content and calm beyond measure as Rio scratched at his belly.

"You're welcome, Agatha."

Alice's focus lifted to where Zap sat, perched in the highest level of the cat tree. "You behave, you mischievous little shit," she scolded teasingly, raising a pointed finger at him despite the fact that she was grinning.

With one more round of appreciative goodbyes, the two of them departed and the house was reclaimed once more under the Harkness-Vidal reign, not matter how jetlagged and exhausted they were.

Instead, she just laughed, leaning forward to press her lips to Rio's forehead.

"My love, I know that. I have a business plan already written up and it is inclusive of a moderately sized staff of trained professionals. And... I figured after we get our legs under us, we could accept volunteers, too."

What Agatha was showing her was that no matter how good, no matter how full their lives were, they could always be better.

And even more than that?

Agatha was proving that she would make it happen, every single time. There was no instance, no situation where Agatha was not going to strive to provide the ultimate and unending happiness she thought Rio deserved. 

They were still physically close, close enough that Rio's dark brown eyes were boring into Agatha's trying to parse out what exactly had motivated her into making such a sweet decision, but then she realized...

Did the reason even matter? 

What mattered was that her wife, who had an impressive job where she made insane amounts of money that had always enabled them to have a good day-to-day and took vacations where she absconded with thousands of dollars, would do anything and everything with that financial stability to just make Rio happy.

A soft breath escaped her as their lips parted, her eyes flickering open once more to behold the woman before her.

Her wife.

Her life.

Her Agatha.

Although Agatha chose to kiss her before she offered her reply, the smile she was wearing when she pulled away said so very many things before she even formed her response.

The moment Rio felt the panic bubble in her chest, her gaze cut to the side and she saw it on Agatha's face, too.

Loving one another.

Over and over again, in every single way.

It would be quiet in the way Rio picked up Agatha's favorite flowers after work every Friday. It would be quiet in the special edition Hot Wheels Agatha would buy Rio to add to her collection in her den.
