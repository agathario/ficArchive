Summary

When it comes to being Hollywood royalty, Agatha Harkness has clawed her way up from the bottom to make a name for herself in the film industry. With three Oscars to her name and a potential fourth on the horizon, it seemed to the rest of the world as though there was no slowing her down.

Working on a new project, Agatha finds herself in New Zealand to scout for locations. One afternoon after a particularly annoying meeting, Agatha's attention span is shot enough that common space-sharing courtesy vacates her mind.

At the sound of a sharp whistle across the water, Agatha's eyes find her for the first time.

And then, the fall.

Each chapter is a brief snapshot of Agatha's perspective throughout the course of the events that unfold in Here With You Now.

The pressure being applied directly to the center of the forehead by a lone pointer finger left the poised woman at the end of the fifteen foot mahogany boardroom table feeling like she was going to puncture a hole through her own skull.

This was the worst part of the job.

Agatha Harkness typically overpowered any room she walked into. She had a presence about her from her posture to the way the hair that fell to her lower back always whipped around behind her as she strode. She carried about her an aura of someone who had rightfully earned her place in the world and the spaces she chose to occupy. She had built her reputation from the ground up and any time she found herself in a boardroom with a bunch of men prattling on about budget constraints and practicalities of trying to shoot on location in certain spaces, she felt like she was going to lose every last shred of sanity she might have had left.

This was a dance she had done all too many times before, and it was one she was always tired of.

Agatha had been creating and directing films since she was in high school, putting on productions of timeless classics to help hone her craft. The older she got, the more her imagination seemed to blossom and the worlds that lived inside of her head begged to be visualized, commanded, cultivated. Creation was what Agatha knew to do best, and it was something that had given her quite a bit of wealth and status throughout the course of her twenty-five year career.

Out of the eight films she had led thus far in her career, she had secured nominations for six, won three, and was a few weeks away from possibly winning her fourth, which would put her at a fifty-fifty ratio of award-winning and ones that she'd put into the world because she'd needed them to be there. All of her films fit that criteria, really, but crowds could be hit or miss.

The art of human interaction was the one thing she tended to focus on the most. She liked to tell stories about the recesses of humanity, the parts people found difficult to talk about.

Agatha was a sucker for visuals to tell those stories, too; places to insert her characters that were not only beautiful, but had their own sense of intent, were a character in and of themselves.

Agatha had earned herself a reputation of asking for the most and accepting nothing less.

Sure, there were plenty of people that would have called her an unadulterated witch to be in the company of when she was in the throes of her creativity, but they also would admit in the same breath that it was something to be respected, no matter the distaste it left in its wake. Agatha was an absolute machine; she never stopped.

If Agatha stopped? Well, then life caught up with her and that wasn't something she had ever been to keen on handling for the last ten years or so, and she had no intention of starting any time soon.

The jet setting lifestyle had been all she had known for decades and even though she was well aware that she was getting older, she had kept to the mindset that she would only find power in her age, and power was Agatha's most important currency.

The amount of power she had?

It left her wondering why she was sat at the head of that table fighting every urge she had not to break her own nose on it as some means of getting out of the meeting early.

Two chairs down to her right sat Regan Hipelius, the newest addition to the financial team. He had been lecturing her on making budget-friendly choices in regards to location for the last eight minutes. She had half a mind to hurl the remaining bit of her black coffee with a shot and a half of Jack in it directly at his head and that ridiculous haircut.

Who the fuck had hired this guy and why had she not been notified?

The studio, of course.

"Look, Regan. I know this is a whole new foray for you, and I don't really care what kind of accounting degree you have. I don't need you to lecture me on budgets," she snapped curtly, but he was quick with his rebuttal.

"The cost of the permits here alone are astronom--"

"Regan! I do not believe my pause was meant to elicit a response from you. I was taking a breath." Agatha spoke with authority, and she tended to weaponize it when she was in a room full of men who thought they were going to tell her how to move about any project she took on.

She lifted her coffee and tossed the rest of it back before nearly slamming the empty cup down on the table in front of her, her steely blue eyes flashing in warning as the man opened his mouth to speak again.

"I get it, Regan, I really do. You're new here. And you've been tasked with..." Both hands raised, her first two fingers out on each hand as she mimicked quotations around her next words, "'keeping me in line,' but if you ask literally any person in this room, they'd be more than happy to inform you that what I exceed in budget I do not hesitate to cover out of my own personal finances."

At this, the man's mouth slowly closed, his lips flat together beneath that gods-awful mustache.

"I know that you are undeniably here at the behest of the studio, and I respect that. But do not think for one second that you're going to waltz in here in a suit two sizes too small, unkempt and a little less than put together and assume that you're the one in control here." She leaned back in the chair, one leg folded over the other knee, her foot swinging around in circles that channeled her irritation into one place.

"Perhaps you should try to make friends instead of a name for yourself within the studio. The more you learn, the more useful you will be to both them as well as myself. On paper, you're only looking at numbers. Things change, schedules get pushed or compounded." She shifted, shimmying her shoulders before she settled back into her chair. "This is my battlefield, and I have traversed it many a time. I do not need you, fresh out of boot camp, to lecture me on what I am doing." Her elbows shifted to rest on the arms of the chair and her hands met, fingers weaving, directly over her middle.

It was a move that screamed power, but it was what Agatha did when she wanted to fortify her defenses. She refused to let a man see her buckle in any situation and she was not going to waver in front of this fresh-faced idiot who had no idea just how much he was going to need to learn if he was going to survive in her company.

"Point being, Regan, I am the captain of this ship and you are a deck hand. If you happen to prove useful, then you'll have a much easier time around here. Understood?"

Regan's dark brown eyes were wide and more than a little shocked that she had just spoken to him like that in front of six other people, but she quickly realized that the shock had come from the fact that no one had stepped in to defend him.

"Y-yes, Ms. Harkness."

Agatha wouldn't have said that people were scared of her, but she knew that she demanded the attention of those around her and required presence from them as well. Focus was imperative and drive was something she wanted anyone working on a project with her to have. It was the attention to detail, the desire to want to create something memorable that she sought out.

That was why he sat there; she had not picked him, the studio had. Should he have been her choice?

Well, if she had willingly selected him from a pool of candidates, she would have demanded in the same turn that she be submitted for a wellness evaluation.

Vision was the one thing Agatha had always had, and because of it, it felt impossible to keep company of those who looked at the world through a pinhole. There was so much out there to see and experience, so many things to absorb and take in. There were so many sides to the human condition and Agatha was often starved to explore the deeper parts of what it meant to walk this earth through so many different paths.

There were some, like her most recent, where she had stepped a bit out of the box. Shooting that one in Germany had been a pure thrill and the local reception to their presence had been incredible. They had been welcomed, been taken care of in their stay in a way she really had never expected.

This trip, though, she was facing a lot more pushback. She'd already been emailed several notes from her casting director about more studio interference and it was beginning to grate on her nerves. It was like they were trying to set her up for failure when she knew that this would be worth every single penny she wanted to sink into it. This was not her first time at this rodeo, or even her fifth. This would be her ninth project, and they were trying to wrestle it out from her grasp?

Absolutely not.

She knew what this was; this was a cash grab. The plot had sat well with the focus groups in polling and because of the higher ranking, the studio had gotten a relatively good idea of what it might look like once it hit the big screen.

If there were ever one thing in the world that Agatha positively loathed?

Fucking focus groups.

She, quite frankly, did not give a solitary fuck about a focus group. Sure, it might have been a mandated part of the industry, but she wasn't making films that were meant to be shoehorned into one category. To her, it felt like another attempt to stifle the creatives in their field that refused to stick to the cut and copy style of film making.

She'd been doing that for years, and it had never not panned out for her. Sure, some of her works had a bit of a harder time gaining tractions, but to carry the ratio she currently had? For a woman in the industry, it was impressive. Hell, for anyone in the industry. She was still in the process of climbing the ladder, wanting to reach those names like Kubrick and Leone.

Typically, she would have been told to stop at five, maybe six. And truthfully? She had been, by a handful of people. Had she listened? Well, then there would have only been one Oscar in her den instead of three.

Bending to the will of the world was not something Agatha Harkness knew how to do. She was too rooted in her sense of self to ever think for a moment that she should move out of the way for the rest of the world.

No, the world moved for Agatha Harkness.

Agatha's hands lifted from her middle, though her elbows were still planted and she instead brought them up, her fingertips meeting at a narrow point, her eyes sweeping the room.

"Is there anything else anyone would like to say?" She posed, that pointed glare darting from face to face.

Their windows for filing the permits there are a lot smaller and so we'll have to act fast." Dave was nothing if not informative when he knew he was going to be delivering less than desirable news to a woman he had worked under for nearly eighteen years.

Dave?

His hair was cut short but was also shaved on the sides. The salt and pepper that sat atop his head matched the beard that covered his entire jaw. He always had those ridiculous half-moon spectacles on that made him look like a member of a biker gang became a professor at Hogwarts.

More than Dave's looks was his sheer dedication to Agatha and her craft. Everything she did, he found a way to press it forward, a way to make sure there was a path that could be followed exactly to her specifications. Dave was a doer. Dave made things happen, no matter how hard Agatha was on him.

They'd had their gentler moments, though they were incredibly few and far between. In fact, there had only been about five of them in their entire time spent working together.

In those five instances, she had learned that Dave had been married for four years to his husband and they had two Corgis -- who, much to Agatha's disdain, were named Benny and June -- and they lived in Malibu, not too far from her own home, as it had turned out. She had never really ventured to dig much deeper than that; Dave had made it apparent that he was there as a proponent to her success and he would do what he needed to do to ensure that each vision she had, it was replicated with crystal clarity. That did not mean, for either of them, that they had to be friends.

Agatha respected Dave.

She respected him because he seemed to be the only person who could tolerate her bullshit for that long and not leave.

People always left.

But not Dave.

She knew it was because, at the end of the day, Agatha appreciated Dave's work ethic. It was probably why she was harder on him than anyone else; she knew he could take it, and that he would remain in his undying loyalty to her. In a way, there was a silent understanding between them that he didn't mind being the example for those who were getting their first glimpses of what life would look like on the S.S. Harkness.

"Well, then I suppose I better get my ass over there Monday morning to get good enough of an idea to understand where I want to take this. You'd be a doll and set that up for me, hm?" She posed, tilting her head at him in a manner that she knew would intimidate everyone in the room but Dave.

"I'll text you the details as soon as I have them."

There was always comfort to be found in just how damn efficient he was, and she was more thankful than she knew she ever told him. Maybe he would be due for a hefty Christmas bonus this year, far more extensive than the one the year before.

The thing about Agatha? She treated her people well when they treated her well. She knew it was a fucked up mentality with the authority she held as a result of her position in the industry, but she refused to engage with people who somehow got through interviews only to prove that they were in it for all of the wrong reasons.

Agatha wanted forward thinkers, people who didn't just think outside of the box, they stood on top of it to reach for whatever stars they could snag from the sky. Agatha wanted motivation, wanted the thirst for greatness to bleed out of those who were honored with the opportunity to have a hand in something that was created under her name.

Many gossip rags were all too eager to jump at the chance to post fabricated -- though there may have been a little truth to a couple of blurbs -- stories from disgruntled former employees who claimed mental anguish and emotional damage, but nothing had ever even made it to court. She'd settle for half what they probably deserved, make a condition of the money that they had to sign an NDA and then moved forward with her life.

Agatha hated another thing with a little more fervor than focus groups.

Agatha fucking hated quitters.

Giving up without exploring every single avenue in search for a viable answer to any problem? There had been literal days when Agatha had been up without sleep, trying to think herself out of a hole. She would pace, she would chug coffee, she would hit the drawing board again and again until that precise moment when she turned one piece just the right way that it fit and made sense of the bigger picture.

There was always an answer to be found, but it took effort and time, two things that people did not seem all that keen on these days. Agatha might have been a little old world in that respect, but if that was what it meant to keep on achieving greatness? She was never going to stop, no matter how many interns she sent packing along the way.

Suddenly, Agatha dropped her foot, the heel catching on the ground before her toes did. At the same time, she sat forward and smacked both palms flat to the table, taking one last sweeping glance across every other face in that boardroom.

"Well, gentlemen, if there's nothing else I can ease your qualms over, I believe this meeting has concluded. Oh, and Regan?" she called as she got to her feet, lifting her head to see him walking away as if he had not heard her. "Excuse me, Mister Hipelius!" she snapped, her eyes narrowing at the back of his head, his feet cementing to the ground before he turned around.

"Ma'am?" he posed, his tone tight and a little stressed. If she wasn't mistaken, she could hear the embarrassment, too.

"Word of the wise. If you want people to take you seriously, it happens a lot quicker when you take yourself seriously."

He gave a terse nod. "Yes ma'am."

Agatha's eyes never left that lanky frame as he nearly fought to keep from sprinting out of that room. She was pleased with herself, of course, because she knew that one way or another, people were going to get it. She did not tolerate anything else than what she expected, no matter how much that seemed to upset people.

Agatha had not gotten very far in her life by giving a fuck what other people wanted or expected of her. She had her own dreams, her own ambitions, and she would keep doing this until she was too old or her creative spark seemed to disappear.

For as long as Agatha knew how, she would keep pushing both herself and her work to new limits every single chance she got.

Everyone else vacated the room slowly, but as Agatha checked the time and stowed her phone in the pocket of her plum trousers, Dave remained.

"You don't have to actively scare the fucking life out of them, Agatha," he admonished gently as he slid his papers back into his folder, though he wasn't looking at her.

"Now, David, if you'll excuse me, I have all of Auckland to spend my next three days in. Perhaps I can find some trouble."

This got a rise out of the man who shook his head at her. "Agatha, the last thing you need is trouble. But... happy mischief to you, I suppose. Have a good night."

She had made it to the door but stopped, one hand on the doorframe as she turned around to face the lone remaining occupant.

"Buy yourself a nice dinner this evening. Put it on the company card."

"Thank you, Agatha," he offered back, his voice a bit too sing-song in a mocking kind of way for her tastes, but she did not push the matter and simply carried herself toward the elevators.

By the time she had hit the street, she was pulling up Wanda's contact number and pressing send to submit the call as she lifted the phone to her ear. Since she'd left Los Angeles, they tried to spend a bit of time together each evening to just touch base, to get a feel for how things were going on either side of the fence.

When she had taken on the most recent season of The Bachelorette, she had surely never been able to predict that they were going to cast the most closeted socialite in all of America.

One who, apparently, had a thing for cute redheads.

"Jesus Christ, neither did I. I thought I was going to have to sail that new accounting idiot through the window. Either that or hit him with what was left of my coffee. Ugh." Agatha exhaled a rough and frustrated grunt, but she was met with laughter from Wanda's end.

"Let me guess, the studio appointed babysitter?" Wanda offered back, though she'd known before she'd even had to ask.

"Of course. And then David informed me that we are on a much tighter deadline in regards to the location decision than I had initially thought. So, I'm headed to the wildlands in Auckland sometime Monday morning or early afternoon." Agatha had been walking the entire time, but she had no idea where she was going.

Fuck it. If she got lost, she had her phone and could find her way back to the hotel where she was staying.

"Of course not," Agatha offered back, but Wanda immediately announced that she had missed what she said, likely because her speaker had started to pick up the dispersed shouts in the background that Agatha was completely oblivious to.

"Are you headed back to the hotel now?" Wanda probed, though the anticipation in her tone was nearly palpable.

"Yeah, do you have your gin ready?"

Wanda laughed.

"It's almost like you know me or something," the other woman returned.

"I'm ready to be six shots deep while I hang over the edge of my hotel bed like a five year old because my lust for immaturity is strong after three hours in that fucking boardroom."

As Wanda began to speak, she was drowned out by another shout that Agatha had not caught the context of as someone whizzed by her.

Like a crack of thunder in the sky, something hit Agatha's ears, sharp and demanding of her attention. Wild blue eyes swept around, looking beyond the railing of the bridge for the source of the sound.

And then, they found it.

There was a woman on the deck of a houseboat, a light green tank top and a pair of shorts on her lithe frame. There were a pair of aviators over her eyes, but she was just far enough away that Agatha could not quite read her expression. She lowered the phone and placed both of her hands on the railing that was a little higher than her hips.

"Can I --?!" she had started, but the moment it started to leave her lips, the heard another shout as it got a lot closer. Half a second later, she felt the force of the biker that had been screaming at her. It almost knocked the wind out of her, but it did successfully knock her forward.

It felt like slow motion, the way she went from looking at the beautiful blue New Zealand sky, down over the skyline, and then straight into the water. As she toppled, her phone launched out of her hand somewhere feet away into the water. She thrashed around for a moment as she tried to right herself in the marina before she surfaced, her head whipping around wildly to locate the source of the sound.

They landed on her again, that woman.

"Is there something you wanted?!" Agatha shrieked as she began to swim over to where the woman was, noting that she had a ladder on the edge of the deck that she was in the process of letting down.

Agatha grabbed either side of it as she climbed up. Her eyes lifted, though, to see an awaiting palm to pull her the rest of the way out. Without a second's hesitation, she slid her hand inside of the woman's and finished removing herself from the water.

The woman finally spoke.

"You were walking in the bike path."

As the tether of consciousness began to reel her back in, Agatha identified several things that felt off even before she opened her eyes.

Firstly, whatever pillow was under her head was definitely not hers. The material was wrong and the firmness was lacking in the most headache-inducing way. Fingers flexed into it, but brushed something that was not the soft sheets of whatever hotel bed she was staying in. It felt like... furniture?

Secondly, there was a pleasant aroma wafting through the air, which Agatha was certain was not any kind of furnished breakfast provided by the hotel -- it smelled too good.

Lastly, the way the shirt on her torso was hugging her frame felt oddly uncomfortable, like it was a shirt that hadn't belonged to her. It was too tight in the places that Agatha usually liked to feel space and air, primarily her midsection. Agatha was a guarded human, and while some form of snug fabric should have felt like armor, instead it made her feel as though she were not in her own skin.

Slowly, with a disgruntled groan slipping through her lips unhindered, Agatha forced her eyelids up and open, blinking in the sight of her surroundings. For a moment, she was incredibly confused. Where the fuck was she?

The woman.

The post-it note Agatha had left between two flower pots.

The wine.

The dinner.

More wine.

The dryer.

There was admiration in fearlessness, Agatha thought.

The longer the two of them had sat on that deck, drinking their wine and eating that perfectly cooked steak, the harder it had been for Agatha to meet that bright green gaze. It made her stomach churn in a feeling she couldn't put a finger on. Agatha was not shy, she did not hide any bit of herself from anyone who looked in, but meeting that gaze across that table, those little candles flickering between them?

Still, she sat. She ate. She drank wine.

More wine. More conversation that flowed as easily as if they had known each other for years and were simply catching up after time apart. More deep thinking.
