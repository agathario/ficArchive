Summary

As teenagers, Rio Vidal and Agatha Harkness fell into the all-consuming throes of young love on Christmas Day, 2002. It was their first kiss and the first of the thousands of kisses they would share over the next ten years of their lives.

After years of masters and doctorate programs, their chosen career paths pulled them in opposing directions. Out of fear and misunderstanding, the relationship fell to pieces and left both parties irrevocably injured, never wanting to cross paths again.

On Christmas Eve, Rio finds herself stranded with her car stuck in a snow bank and a nine hour waitlist for a tow. With a couple of miles to walk in the frigid cold, Rio begins her trek only to believe that someone has arrived to save her.

When she turns around to view her knight, Agatha is the one that climbs out of the car.

How much can change in a decade? Maybe, the more important question when it came to Agatha and Rio was instead: How much more could stay the same?

Decemberism

December 25th, 2002.

1AM.

"If your dad wakes up and finds out we accidentally put half his rum in this eggnog, he's going to be pissed."

Of all the people to be concerned about breaking the rules, the last person who should have been voicing it was Agatha Harkness. Since the moment Rio had met her on her first day at a new school in the fifth grade, Agatha had never liked rules. She just didn't like being told what to do, and that defiance had always been such a defining trait.

For Rio Vidal, it was exactly what had tethered her to Agatha in the first place.

That first day in class, right after the teacher had placed Agatha in the seat directly to the left of Rio, it had been all too easy to spot the absolute indifference she had carried about her, even as a kid. Agatha hadn't cared what anyone thought about her, not other kids, not teachers, not even the principal on the few instances when she got sent to his office because of her inability to not argue with the teacher.

For being ten-years-old at the time, Agatha definitely had obtained a wild streak, and that vibrant energy was something Rio had been drawn to. They spent every minute at school together aside for when they were placed in different corners of the classroom to keep them from being a distraction to the rest of the kids.

It was the beginning of a pattern, one that wore on for years.

Rio always watched Agatha, just taking her in as she moved about life. The time ticked on, things started to change, but only physically. Agatha got a little bit taller, started to fill out more. Her hair kept growing and growing and growing. Rio had not missed the way Agatha had taken full advantage of the changes in her body. She wore lower cut tops, ones that hugged her middle a little closer, would jump at any chance to be in a bathing suit. Agatha knew what her body had become, and she had to see how the way Rio looked at her had started to change, too.

They kept playing this coy game of feigning disinterest, but there had been times that Rio had been caught up in whatever she was working on but would raise her gaze just in time to catch Agatha staring at her. She'd giggled and told her to take a picture every single time, but Agatha would simply roll her eyes in response and turn her attention back to whatever was in front of her.

They'd carried on like that for five years, this silent game of back and forth, like they'd been swinging a tetherball to and fro around the pole, but the weight at the end of the rope was the emotional tension that had started to build between them. As of late, Rio had been struggling to compartmentalize those feelings; it had been easy to dismiss it as teenage hormones per her parents' scolding, but Rio knew that wasn't it.

Her hormones weren't the reason her chest felt light almost every single time Agatha laughed. Her hormones had nothing to do with the fact that when she was upset and Agatha wrapped her arms around her, the smell of the perfume she wore seemed to slow Rio's heartbeat. Her hormones had little to do with the way her stomach felt every flutter of a butterfly's wings when Agatha took a drink of her hot chocolate and got whipped cream on her nose in a way that caused them both to delve into laughter.

There the two of them sat, fifteen years old with far too much rum in their eggnog at the base of the Christmas tree, in front of all of the gifts the Vidal family had purchased for each other, and for Agatha.

Rio's parents had already experienced the unpleasantries of meeting Agatha's mother and it was part of the reason they had always made it clear to Agatha that she was welcome in their home, that she had a safe place if she ever needed. And boy, did she.

It was Christmas Day, even if it was only one in the morning, and Agatha had climbed through Rio's window not ninety minutes earlier with a carton of eggnog in her hand that she proudly proclaimed she'd stolen from her fridge at home.

"It's been a shit year, I think he'll forgive us. Plus, it's Christmas. It's hard to be mad on Christmas." Rio's cheeks pushed up in her youthful grin as she held her mug up. "Merry Christmas, Agatha," she offered.

Those beautiful blue eyes lifted and met Rio's evenly, a sweeping grin crossing Agatha's face as she lifted her mug and tapped it softly to the side of Rio's. "Merry Christmas, Rio."

They drank their eggnog and poured over the wrapped gifts, trying to figure out what each package might hold. By the time they got to the bottom of their mugs, Agatha had been quick to refill them, though her hand was a little lighter when it came to the rum the second time around. The first one had been enough to do the trick, but what could a little more hurt?

They were reasonably buzzed, bordering on drunk, by the time they finished their second set of drinks. Agatha had been scanning those wrapped packages, but then she turned her attention back to Rio and the look on her face was a little... pained? Rio couldn't identify it, but she let it settle a moment before she decided to break the tension.

"What's the one thing you want this year that you really hope you get?" Rio posed, tilting her head adorably in question.

Agatha didn't answer right away. She just looked at Rio, staring. Rio could see as the gears began to turn behind those deep blue eyes, like Agatha had settled on an answer but wasn't content with it, or was too nervous to voice it.

"Oh, come on! Just tell me!" Rio pressed, giggling a little louder than she had meant to, clasping a hand over her mouth to stifle the sound.

What she had not been expecting was for Agatha to reach up and take hold of her wrist, however gently. It still threw her off, especially as Agatha pulled it away at the same time she shifted to her knees, scooting forward so that her other hand could raise to Rio's face, cheek fitting into cupped palm.

Rio's gaze lifted to Agatha there above her, holding her face like that, and before she could even bring the question to her lips, Agatha gave them something to do instead.

It was both lightning fast and yet so impossibly slow as she leaned down, wild and long hair falling over her shoulder as their lips met in a soft but slightly messy kiss, due only to the fact that they'd had more rum that was necessary to get two fifteen-year-olds drunk.

But what it did to Rio's brain, that kiss inebriated her far more than the rum had. Her view behind closed eyes was pure white, pure fire as it engulfed every sense she had. Her hand had raised, fingertips grazing the skin of Agatha's cheek in order to feel like she was contributing to something that was literally stealing the breath from her lungs. Agatha pulled away, a sweet and tender smile curling her lips.

"You, Rio. I want you to be mine. And I want to be yours."

That was it, the defining moment that had turned Rio's life in a new direction for the next decade of her life.

That was the moment Rio tied her soul to Agatha's in a way that she would never see coming for the rest of her life.

December 24th, 2024.

6PM.

"NINE HOURS? Are you kidding me?! That's three in the morning on CHRISTMAS!"

"Ma'am, I understand, but it is the holiday, and there's a backlog due to the conditions in your area. I do apologize, but that's all I have available."

Rio exhaled a frustrated grunt as she leaned forward, pressing her forehead to the top portion of her steering wheel, the phone still pressed to her ear, the customer service rep silent on the other end as they were surely awaiting for her to fly into some tirade about how bullshit it was that she couldn't get a tow until three in the morning.

A soft sigh passed through her lips and she leaned back into her seat, her lips twisting in silent apology before she could voice it.

"Look, I know it's not your fault. I'm just stuck and a good two miles away from the hotel I'm staying at, and it's eight degrees outside. I don't mean to take it out on you. I know you're probably pissed you're answering phones on Christmas Eve instead of being home with your family. So... thanks for being on the other end of the line, I guess."

This seemed to soothe the woman on the other side of that call, who exhaled an audible sigh of relief.

"Thanks for that, Ms. Vidal. I empathize with your predicament, I am just limited in what I can do."

"I understand. Just... I'll call back the day after tomorrow. There's no use in adding to the workload tomorrow and making it harder on myself to try and meet them. So... Thank you for trying. I hope you have a Merry Christmas."

"Thank you, Ms. Vidal. Happy Holidays to you as well."

Rio lowered the phone and cut the call, a sharp breath exhaling from her nose. What was she supposed to do now?

Her head tipped back, thudding into the headrest of the seat behind her. The urge was there to cry, but she was doing every possible thing she could to shove it down. She couldn't let herself get emotional in her current situation; that could wait until she somehow found a way to the hotel.

Coming back to Westview had not initially been in Rio's plans; the idea of leaving Cambridge to come back to New Jersey had been one she'd struggled with for weeks leading up to the holiday. Truthfully, though, it had been four years since she'd been to the cemetery where her parents were buried to bring them carnations and sit with them for a little while, just updating them on her life even though they were no longer around to listen.

It had been the last year of her doctorate program in Evolutionary Developmental Biology that Rio had received the call. It was a memory that she sometimes still lived in on the darker days when she was hurting beyond measure. It had been her fault, she'd told herself, because she hadn't been around.

Even if she would have been, would it have mattered? Would she have been with them the night they hit that patch of ice and slammed into an oncoming semi? And if she was, would it really have been better to die with them than to simply live in their memory?

Every single night after that call for the following two weeks, Agatha had pulled her into her arms, held her, brushed through her hair with fingertips. She tried to get Rio to eat, helped get her in and out of the shower. The depression had hit her hard, but Agatha had been right by her side to carry her through it.

Agatha had been Rio's rock, all while finishing her own program in Sound Engineering. It had been a trying time for the both of them, almost to a point where Rio had been ready to drop out, but Agatha had refused to accept that it was an option. She pushed Rio, did everything she could to find a way to help her, to get her to see that while she had suffered immense loss, she still had things to hold onto and look forward to.

Agatha had loved Rio in a way that Rio had never known was real, or possible. She had been there for Rio on the good days, the bad ones, and every single one in between. For ten long years they were all that one another needed to make it through the shit times. Rio had been convinced of it as she had come out on the other side of losing her parents with Agatha's hand in her own.

Six months later, it all changed.

Rio hadn't been ready.

"You're going to LA?! Like you're moving to LA?"

The panic in Rio's voice was thick and sharp, a true sense of not understanding what she was being told. Her eyes were darting back and forth over Agatha's face as if she was going to find the slightest give that told her this was a joke.

"I got an offer from Spellbound, Rio. Fucking Spellbound Records! This is huge, I can't just turn it down."

Rio's eyes were stinging at the backs with the promise of upset and angry tears, but she blinked them back despite herself. They'd graduated not three months earlier, making plans for the rest of their lives and now Agatha was looking her in the face and telling her that she was going to be moving to the other side of the fucking country?!

In a show of helplessness, Rio's weak voice cracked between them.

"What about me?"

Agatha's face had fallen slightly, but it was clear she was trying to keep up that air of resolve.

"Come with me."

Rio's jaw tightened at the suggestion.

"So I'm just supposed to give up the Biotech position at Rutgers? I'm just supposed to table all of the research I'm already neck deep in to skip across the country so you can fucking record music?!" Her voice was shrill as it filled the apartment around them, though the anger was beginning to weave into her words.

"You act like you can't find another research center!" Agatha shot back and it only caused Rio's features to pinch sharply in anger.

"Wow, Agatha, please continue to act like you haven't listened to a fucking word I've said the last year and a half I've been fighting to finish my doctorate and get into this program." She snapped her mouth shut, teeth clicking as a result of the action. "They're doing exactly the work I have trained myself to be vested in. My contribution could help make this world a better fucking place, and all you can say is 'go somewhere else.' Fuck you for that, honestly." Rio's words were loud, they were hot and downright pissed.

"You aren't God, Rio. You can't just wave your hands and save lives."

"No, Agatha, I'm not God. I'm a woman who has spent the last eight years of my life learning as much as I possibly can so that I can take that knowledge and collaborate with other academics on a path forward through medicine and biology that could end up saving lives, even in the distant future." She paused, taking in a long and deep breath, one that did nothing to stave off the hurt. "Am I stupid enough to think that we'll be able to biologically print a kidney by the time I retire? No, of course not. But if we can be even five steps closer to getting there, then I'll have served my fucking purpose."

Rio threw up her hands in surrender, the look on her face sliding into something that leaned toward utter disregard. "But sure, your career in the music industry is so much more important. So important that I should just say fuck-all to my own hard work to make you happy. I won't. So, pack your shit and get on that plane and live your happy little Hollywood life. I don't give a shit anymore, Agatha."

Agatha's expression had slipped from irritation to actual shock at hearing Rio speak to her like that. Her Rio who was always tender and had a fondness for the beautiful moments in life. Her Rio who caught spiders and put them outside. Her Rio who brought home a new bouquet of flowers every week to place in the vase on their dining room table. Her Rio that cooked breakfast in her underwear.

Rio wasn't Agatha's any longer.

"I'm going out. If you're not going to be gone by the time I'm back, I'll sleep on the couch in the office."

Rio had stomped out and slammed the door to their condo behind her, doing what she could to convince herself that she was not going to shatter into pieces until she knew that Agatha was gone for good.

That had lasted all of the forty minutes it took her to drive to the cemetery and place herself on the snowy ground at the foot of her parents' graves. She had cried, told them how it wasn't fair, how it crushed her that Agatha seemed to have no care for the dedication Rio had poured into her studies for years on end. She cried about not wanting Agatha to go, about wishing things could be different. She sobbed about how it felt to lose someone she'd been madly in love with since she was a teenager, for a decade of her life.

She'd sat, wet and cold, in the snow for hours. She would surely catch a cold within the next couple of days, but she hadn't cared.

Sure, Rio had the condo for now, but somehow she still felt... homeless.

Her roots had been so entangled with Agatha's in a way that was literally ripping Rio from the ground, pulling those roots from the hold Agatha's had on them.

Rio needed a new place to grow.

Dark eyes lifted to glance out of the front windshield, at the half-covered road that would eventually open up into Westview and a heavy sigh passed through Rio's lips.

The adrenaline from the initial trauma of the accident had worn off, but it had only left her feeling like her energy had been depleted. She had done what she could to remind herself that she was safe, that she was okay, and that she had not met the same fate her parents had. It had been difficult to talk herself down, but she had managed it in a way the last twelve years had taught her.

Rio had been her own worst enemy for months, maybe even years, after Agatha had left. It had taken a lot of time for her to stop acting like she was the reason Agatha had left. No, Agatha had chosen to leave because she wanted to follow her own goals. For those first years, Rio would have called her selfish without a second thought.

The older she got, the more Rio had come to realize that maybe they just hadn't been as compatible as they had thought they could be.

By the time Rio had landed the spot at the University of Cambridge and packed her bags, she hardly thought of Agatha anymore.

Those four years, they were filled with heavy loads of work and study, of parts and processes, but those four years had also brought with them a great deal of personal growth. Rio often took the train into London on the weekends, spent time in pubs where she would chat up cute women that would drag her back to their flats for a night or two of fun. She'd board that train again and delve right back into her research. It was a cycle that tended to repeat itself again and again for damn near the entirety of four years.

In those four years, though, she had never come home.

Not until that night.

As a result, she was sitting in her car, which had its nose lodged into a snow bank and she had no idea how she was supposed to make it the two remaining miles into town. The road outside had been practically dead, no one coming in or out.

Another irritated sigh formed as she realized that her only option was to walk.

After another moment of indignant stalling, Rio kicked the door open and climbed out of the car. As the door slammed shut behind her, her eyes lifted to take in one side of the street and swept down to the other side, completely empty.

She turned for the trunk and popped it open to pull out her suitcase, unlocking the handle so that she could drag it over the snow-covered asphalt. It had only been about a minute and she'd hardly put any distance between herself in the car when she saw her own shadow, one that was created by a distant casting of light. Light that seemed to get closer, making her shadow grow in size.

Rio shuffled toward the side so that the car could pass without risk of injury to herself, but instead the car slowed and then stopped.

At the sound of the door opening, Rio couldn't help but to turn around to see if someone was actually stopping to help her.

As the figure climbed out of the car, Rio's insides completely plummeted through the soles of her feet into the ground below her, only sinking further as the woman spoke.

"Rio? Is that you?"

Rio's lips pressed into a tight line, one that caused her teeth to sink into the insides so roughly that the taste of blood spilled out onto her tongue.

"Hello, Agatha," she managed dryly before she adjusted her coat and began to drag her suitcase again.

"Rio! Wait! You're... heading into town? Is that your car back there?" Agatha's voice was so sweet that it made Rio sick. She didn't want to be having this conversation in any universe, much less the one they were stood in at that moment.

"I am, and it is. I can't get a tow until tomorrow." She was still walking, but she had raised her voice enough to be heard.

She heard the car door slam again, but then there was the sound of crunching gravel and snow beneath the tires of the Explorer that Agatha was driving, surely a rental in the same way her own had been. As Agatha pulled up to her side, the passenger window was rolled down, but Rio fought not to look over.

"Rio, just let me take you into town. It's cold as hell and you've still got almost two miles to go. By the time the sun's fully down, you're going to freeze to death." Agatha's logic was sound, sure, but it was also pissing Rio off beyond measure that the only savior the universe could give her with the woman who had broken her heart nearly twelve years ago.

"You don't owe me any favors, Agatha," Rio bit back.

"Rio..." Agatha managed, her voice dropping softly. "Please, just let me get you where you're going safely and then you can forget I'm even in town."

"Like you even care," Rio mused, a sardonic laugh spilling out of dry lips.

At this, Agatha stomped on the brakes and threw the vehicle into park. The door was opening again and Agatha was out and scrambling around the front of the SUV to place herself in front of Rio, whose boots scraped the ground as she skidded to a stop.

"I do care, Rio. I care about you, and always will. I... sure, we parted angry, but that doesn't mean I haven't wondered how you've been doing at random points over the last decade." Agatha's words were a little pointed, but they were also sincere.

Rio wanted to push, even though that look in Agatha's eyes was making it hard.

"You could have reached out." It was a firm but simple statement, one that Rio intended to poke.

"I tried. A couple of years ago. You changed your number."

"I moved out of the country," Rio corrected.

Agatha's eyes widened slightly, as if this were an answer she truly had not been expecting. Rio could tell that she wanted to press, that she was interested. Her mouth opened to say something, but Rio started to push past her.

"Congrats on owning Spellbound now, by the way. Seems like you got what you wanted out of your life." Rio's words themselves were sharp, but the defeated tone in which they were delivered was more heartbreaking than it was encouraging.

Rio had thought, for a moment, that Agatha was going to let her go, but was shocked to instead feel Agatha's hand encircle her bicep, holding her in place.

"You can stay angry with me, Rio, that's fine. But, please, would you just let me take you into town?" Agatha was nearly begging, now, and Rio was inclined to believe that her concern primarily was Rio's safety and not getting a word in edgewise about what had ended them all of those years ago.

Rio blew a rough puff of air through her lips, those dark eyes cutting up to Agatha's blue ones. The moment they settled there, Rio felt like she could cry. She had missed those eyes far more than she had ever let herself admit and seeing them so close in the light of the setting winter sun felt like an absolute haymaker to the chest.

"Fine."

Agatha visibly relaxed, her posture slackening in a noticeable way at the white flag Rio seemed to be waving, if only for the moment.

"You can put your suitcase in the back," Agatha informed her, releasing her hold on Rio's arm. Only a beat passed between them before Agatha was moving, heading toward the rear of the vehicle to open the hatch. Rio met her around back and lifted her suitcase to lay it next to Agatha's.

Without another word, she departed toward the front and loaded herself into the passenger's seat. Her belt clicked into place and her eyes cast out the window to the snow covered field on the other side of it.

The car shook slightly as Agatha climbed back in and pulled her own door shut. Wordlessly, Agatha put the car into drive and began the trek toward town, though the silence that filled the car was damn near enough to choke the both of them.

It went on for about three minutes before Agatha couldn't seem to take it anymore.

"Where did you move to?"

Rio was quiet, debating on the inside of her skull if she was going to dignify Agatha's question with an answer. It was very clear that they were not going to be apologizing to each other, and Rio felt like asserting any fact about how she knew she'd made the right choice in telling Agatha to leave would have been beating a dead horse.

That didn't stop her from forming her honest reply in a not-so-timely fashion.

"Cambridge, England. I'm going back after the new year. The program I'm in has been making strides in the printing technique. We've been able to print hollow organs, like bladders and intestines, but the process is still being perfected to make them suitable for transplant."

Agatha's silence was not one of indifference, it was one of undeniable interest. She was quiet for a long moment as she searched through her mind for the right thing to say.

Twelve years had been put between them, and in that time Rio was literally making leaps in the art of saving lives.

"That's... Wow, Rio, that's really incredible. Truly, I mean that."

Agatha was being genuine, Rio knew. It was audible in that tone she chose, in the way her words were soft, affirming. It poked at that still tender spot in her chest that she was certain she would always have for Agatha, and in that moment it made her incredibly sad.

Her eyes were still cast out that window, lips twisting in her indecision to speak again, but she finally found her voice.

"Thank you. And I... Congrats on the Spellbound thing, really. I know how long it took you, and I know that it's what you'd always wanted. So... I'm p--" Rio's teeth clamped down on her tongue to stop her from saying what almost came out so easily: I'm proud of you.

"Good job," she managed to finish, though it almost pissed her off at how easy it was to want to celebrate in something so momentous in Agatha's life when Agatha could not have given a shit less about Rio's accomplishments.

Okay, that was unfair. Agatha's response to her explanation of why she had left had been real, and Rio could hear it. She hadn't been looking at Agatha, but if she had she was sure that she would have seen the pride there, too.

"Thank you," Agatha offered back softly. Rio could almost hear the way she opened her mouth to talk about it, but then decided against it, her teeth clicking softly as her jaw snapped shut again.

The next minutes wore on in silence, a silence Rio had started to find comfortable, but the comfort was suddenly gone in the blink of an eye as she felt warmth on the back of her hand that sat atop her thigh. By the time she shifted her head to look down, the feel of Agatha's hand wrapping around her own hit her before the sight did, and it caused her heart to leap and lodge somewhere in the middle of her throat.

Her initial reaction was to pull away, to yank her hand back so fast that it would startle the both of them.

She didn't. Instead, she stared down at their hands, Agatha's fingers curled around her palm and fingers in a gentle grip that wasn't demanding and wasn't tight enough that Rio couldn't remove it if she wanted to.

Rio's eyes lifted to Agatha's profile as she continued to drive.

"Agatha, what are you doing?" Rio asked lowly, though it wasn't a harsh question, just a confused one.

"I'm holding your hand," Agatha voiced back just as lowly.

"B-but why?" Rio asked, clearly confused.

Agatha did not immediately answer verbally, but her fingers did curl a little tighter against the flesh of Rio's palm.

"Because I miss you. And lying about the severity of which was only working as well as it could until the moment I saw you again."

Rio had been so angry when they had went their separate ways, so indignant toward the fact that Agatha had chosen music over the love they had. On the other side of that coin, hadn't Rio made the same choice? Hadn't she chosen to stay where she was for her own career? Maybe Rio had placed her own work on a pedestal, one that should have been more paramount to the wellbeing of the world than recording records.

Rio's self-righteousness in regards to her work had been the reason they had parted. Agatha had simply been chasing her dreams and she had not been willing to let anyone keep her from it, even if that person happened to be her long-time girlfriend.

In that moment, as Agatha gripped her hand a little tighter, Rio seemed to climb down from her high horse and realize that maybe it had not truly always been about her.

When she let herself admit it, she had missed Agatha painfully for nearly two years after their split. There were reminders of her everywhere, even when Rio finally moved out of that condo. It was in the smell of dark roast coffee, in the taste of cheap whiskey. She sometimes saw shades of Agatha's eyes in the midday sky. There were even times that she would walk down a crowded sidewalk and smell something that was so reminiscent of Agatha's perfume.

Right then, sitting in that car, she could smell it. It was the same perfume. It was light, but it was enough to fill Rio's senses. It was that scent of fresh apple, plum and bergamot. It smelled as good right then as it always had.

She managed to swallow her heart back down into its rightful place as she glanced at Agatha again who was still focused on the road ahead of them.

"Why?" Rio finally managed, though the single word sounded a bit strangled in its delivery.

"Why do I miss you?" Agatha asked back, her tone a little close to disbelief. When she realized that it was exactly what Rio was asking, her face fell a little and even though Rio couldn't see it full-on, she could tell that Agatha's brow knit together as she formed her answer. "Because even though I made the right choice for me, it was the wrong choice for us. I... I told myself that you were right, that your work was going to be important. But, I also realized that my work could be important, too, even if it was in a different way. Those realizations didn't make me fall out of love with you, it just made it harder to move on."

Rio wanted to scoff at this, but she bit it back. Before she could form a response, Agatha was speaking again.

"I miss you because I've never met anyone else like you. I've dated, I even got engaged at one point, but... I realized that every time I got into a serious relationship, I would always find things wrong with the other person. And it was because the things wrong about them were always the things that were right about you."

Rio could feel it, somewhere in the middle of Agatha speaking, as her eyes started to sting with the threat of tears.

Rio had spent years thinking about hearing Agatha talk to her like that. She had spent so many sleepless nights missing just how carefully Agatha chose her words, just how nuanced she could be when trying to talk about the things she clearly loved.

There was no way she still loved Rio.

Too much time had passed.

Too much had happened.

There Agatha sat, though, one hand on the top of the steering wheel and the other holding Rio's, and she was saying the things Rio could have only ached to hear before that moment.

Rio's eyes turned back out toward the window and she let the silence fall between them once more. It lasted only a few moments before Agatha spoke.

"Where do I need to take you?" she asked softly, though her hand had not moved and Rio had not made her move it.

"I got a room at the Pinewood Lodge," Rio informed her in a low tone. She hadn't expected the rip of laughter that flew from Agatha's lungs.

"No shit?" she asked, and Rio realized instantly why.

"That's where you're staying, too?"

Agatha's lips pressed, but she was still fighting a smile as she gave a nod.

"Well, isn't that just irony at its finest?" Rio managed in a soft grumble, picking at the hem of her coat with her free hand.

Immediately, the tears welled in Rio's eyes. After they had kissed that night, Rio had given Agatha her present early. That polaroid camera. It was the very first picture Agatha had taken with it.

She'd held it out with one hand and told Rio to kiss her so she could snap a photo.

They had been kids, and they had loved each other so much.

Now, they were adults and loved one another just as much as they always had.

Distance had pulled them apart, but what if it didn't have to?

Rio wiped at her face with one hand as she lifted the photo out of Agatha's palm with the other.

"You kept it all this time?"

"I put it in every car I drive, no matter where I am. I like to think you've always kept me safe."

That absolutely had Rio in tears again, reaching over to cup Agatha's cheek so that she could kiss her again.

"Promise me something," Rio requested softly and the look in Agatha's eyes communicated that she was listening. "Promise me that this is just another beginning. Promise me that you'll come see me, in the same way I promise I'll come see you. I... know that moving in together again isn't a feasible option... Just... Promise me that this isn't the alpha and the omega at the same time."

Agatha leaned in, gracing their lips together once more in a gesture of pure and raw sincerity. She pressed their foreheads together, the smile on her face audible as she spoke.

"I promise you, Rio, that this is the start. We choose where we go from here."
