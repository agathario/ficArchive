Chapter 1

"Mama."

It was a whisper.

"Mama?"

She had had this dream before. This nightmare.

"Mama, wake up."

She was nudged forcefully in her shoulder.

That was new.

That was touch. Touch was physical. Touch wasn't possible.

Agatha pressed her fingertips down. As they pushed into the softness of the mattress, she frowned, she was in her bed. In the same bed she had been in in Westview. How could she forget the lumpy mattress?

"Mama."

There was that impatient replica again. Agatha squinted her eyes open, keeping them narrow in case it was a trick. But no. It wasn't a trick. Stood in front of her, with a mop of brown hair falling over his little forehead was her son. Her boy. Her baby. 

“I know you said you wanted to sleep in, but I'm hungry and I wanted to make you breakfast but I can't reach the top cupboard where the Lucky Charms are.”

“Nicholas,” Agatha’s breath escaped her as she sat up. Her eyes searched for him, his face, even down to the dinosaur pyjamas he wore. His determined expression didn't change as he stood expectantly in front of her. His blue eyes they had shared were firm in their determination.

"I just wanted to make you breakfast in bed," he huffed. Agatha reached out almost hesitantly, afraid her hand would pass through him but instead it made contact with the soft strand of his hair. He was real. She pushed his hair from those determined eyes as she tried to form any words.

"That's very kind of you, Nicky," Agatha whispered. Afraid if she raised her voice in the slightest he would disappear. His name on her lips felt foreign. It had been so long since she had spoken his name aloud.

Nicky's cheeks bloomed as he blushed over his complexion. "So, are you getting up to come help?"

His impatience was intoxicating. Agatha shifted, suddenly aware that she was in nothing more than a lace slip. What the hell was going on?

"Why don't you get the bowls and milk ready?" Agatha suggested. "I'll be there in a minute."

"You're not going back to sleep," Nicky accused, his eyes narrowing as his arms folded.

"No," Agatha reassured softly, pushing her own hair from her face. It was still long and curled. Still getting in her way. "I won't go back to sleep."

Nicky turned, clearly accepting her words and made his way to the doorway of the bedroom. Agatha turned as he did and began looking around the bedroom. Her bed was the same green vine leaf duvet cover and her closet was still the same. It looked exactly as she had decorated it before going under Wanda's curse.

Wanda's curse.

Was that what this was?

Agatha wasn't so sure. Wanda was meant to be dead. Besides, Wanda didn't know about Nicky. No one did except-

"Mama?" Agatha turned back to Nicky as he stood in the doorway. His brow dipped impatiently.

"I'll be there in just a second," Agatha reassured as she moved to exit the bed. The seven year old took it and left the room. Peeling back the covers, Agatha scoured the room. Everything was exactly as she recalled it in every significant detail. Except her locket. Opening the clasp, she gasped. Her locket wasn't filled with his hair. It was empty.

Looking around, everything else was identical. With a sigh, she lifted the robe draped over the chair in the corner and made her way downstairs. Pulling it over her bare shoulders and tying the tie tightly, she braced herself at the foot of the stairs as an excited giggle passed through.

"Chocolate chip- No- Blueberry- No - Nutella!"

"How about all three?" Agatha's spine froze at the voice. Panic filled her. She couldn’t let her take Nicky again. 

She moved without thinking but halted at the doorway because sitting on the counter was Nicky, a wide grin on his face and Rio stood in her shirt and hair tied back, just like Agatha remembered.

"Mama, look! Mami's home!"

Agatha forced a fake smile. "I can see that."

"I missed you, Mami," Nicky admitted and Agatha watched Rio's entire being soften.

"I missed you too, buddy," Rio placed a soft kiss on his temple and Agatha couldn't help the emotion clog her throat. "Hey, why don't you do me a favour? Why don't you go get the cartoons ready and I'll begin making the mix huh?"

Like a flash, Nicky was gone, leaving them alone. Agatha immediately rounded on Rio, Rio who's gaze was hard and void of emotion. Just as she had been when they had met in London and again in the middle of the Atlantic upon the unsinkable ship. It had been rare to get a rise from Rio but that didn’t mean Agatha hadn’t tried. 

"What the hell is going on?"

Rio frowned back at her. "This wasn't you?"

"Do you really think I'm that much of a masochist?" Agatha hissed. Her anger rose by the second.

Rio's lips pouted as she tilted her head. "You know we were always pretty liberal."

"Not when it came to this." Agatha snapped. "Not when it came to him."

Rio's seriousness returned, her eyes darkening. "Are you lying to me?"

"Of course, I'm not," Agatha huffed. "I didn't do this."

Rio looked around slowly. Her eyes drifting over items, her head tilting this way and that. Eventually, she sighed. "It all feels real."

"But it can't be." Agatha whispered, her denial kicking in until one little glimmer of hope peaked through. "Can it?"

"I carried him through myself," Rio's dark gaze locked on hers. "I know he was at peace."

"But," Agatha's mind tried to catch up but all she could feel was the hand on her shoulder. The one he had pressed into her shoulder not ten minutes ago. "I felt him."

"So did I," Rio whispered. Looking around once more, the cosmic entity huffed and began pulling out a large bowl and whisk.

"What are you doing?"

"What does it look like?" Rio gestured to the eggs in her hand. "I'm making our son pancakes."

"We don't know what the hell is going on."

"I know that my son is in there and is real. Feels real, has a soul that I can sense," Rio hissed. "So, I'm going to do what I should have always done."

"And what's that," Agatha drawled.

"I'm going to be there for him."

And that was how Agatha stood at the dining room table with coffee in her hand as she flicked through the Westview Reporter. Half her gaze was trained hard on Nicky and Rio. His brow scrunched tight as he added the ingredients to all three pancake batters. Honestly, just because Rio was a cosmic entity that could make her form how she wished didn't mean Agatha could.

"All right," Rio praised. "Now let's see if we can flip them."

Agatha wasn't really sure who was having more fun making the pancakes, Nicky or the immortal being who somehow had spilled batter over the counter.

"Can I try, Mami?" Nicky asked, his eyes wide and hopeful.

"Sure-"

"No." Agatha snapped and it made both turn. Rio's brow drawn close, her lips immediately downturned in anger. But it was Nicky's confused pout that made her stutter. "Maybe, let Mami, do it first? It can take some skill."

Nicky accepted it, stepping out of the way and Rio's brow arched at hers in a smug win. It was infuriating.

"Alright here we go."

Rio's first attempt landed on her ceiling. The second on the floor.

"Maybe third times the charm," Nicky said hopefully. Rio at least smiled.

"Maybe it'll land on her head." That earned her a glare. 

Agatha watched from the corner of her eye as Rio’s concentration focused and attempted the task. She didn't expect Nicky to cheer and jumped when he did. His eyes alight with an excitement she hadn't seen in centuries. She dared to glance at Rio. Rio who watched with a mirrored expression. One of awe, like she had never heard anything like it. 

Agatha wondered if she had. She thought of their time together, just her and Nicky, and she found tears welling at the memories of them running around together, exploring the states. Constantly moving. Rio had barely been there for most of his life, it was harder then. There were more souls to ferry and collect. It meant she had to be in more places than one. But now? Now something had shifted.

It took another four attempts before Rio got the hang of the pancakes. As all seven year olds, he was bored, turning back to her and leaning into her side. Without thought, she pushed his hair from his forehead, earning a toothy smile up at her.

"Will we sit at the table for breakfast?" She asked like it was the most natural thing in the world. Nicky happily nodded, moving to set the table like it was what they did every Sunday morning. As Agatha reached for the paper a hint of silver caught her eye. She had a ring. Not just any ring, it was her ring. The one Rio had given her all those years ago. But it was on the wrong finger, or right finger, she supposed. With it was a simple silver band, one that sat perfectly on her ring finger.

She pulled her hands to herself as if she had burned and traced the two bands. She hadn't felt rings there in centuries. Not since before. Not since the decision to have Nicky was made. Not since Rio herself broke the rules.

"Mama, can we have orange juice?" Nicky pulled her from her thoughts and Agatha took a deep breath, transported back to this crazy reality she found herself in. Nicky stared patiently up at her as he waited and Agatha quickly nodded. Habitually, she moved to the giant fridge freezer that no longer had a dent in it as a consequence of Rio flinging her against it. Without thought, she pulled the carton from the fridge and poured three glasses. Her eyes caught Rio who watched her with a veiled look, one Agatha had seen before but couldn't put her finger on.

Shaking her head as she walked away from the dark eyes, she set the glasses down and settled beside Nicky, who grinned back up at her with the happiest smile she could only just recall from a different time. "So, what do you think about the pancakes?"

Nicky laughed at her as Rio set the large stack on the table. It may have been exactly 427 years since she had parented Nicky directly but something still stirred within her at the thought of all the sugar.

"They're gonna be great," Nicky grinned, holding his plate up to Rio who happily set two down. Agatha half expected her to settle at that but then there was a hand waiting expectantly for her own plate.

Agatha's jaw moved once, twice and found herself staring into that subtle smirk that had her more riled than she'd care to admit. Her jaw snapped shut as a brow arched and lips pursed. Pushing aside the flustered nature that Rio put her in, she pushed her plate into the taller woman's hand. It was brief, barely there, but as Rio's warm touch grazed Agatha's own, she felt herself tense involuntarily. She quickly pulled her hand back and put it in her lap. She wasn't ready to feel Rio, in any capacity. Setting the plate down, she dared to meet the dark eyes that settled next to her. Dark eyes didn't leave her own and Agatha shifted under the gaze. It stirred something within her. Something she had hidden away for a very, very long time. Something, she had tried not to let slip onto their journey on the road. The road-

A scrape of a knife on the plate made her turn. Nicky's brow was deep as he attempted to cut the pancake himself, his tongue sticking out one corner in concentration. Something endearing stirred in her as she asked. "You need assistance there?"

Nicky's blue eyes looked up at her wide and irritated. "Mama."

With a soft laugh, she reached over and like it was 1750 again cut the meal on his plate. Once she finished, she sat back and her eyes connected with Rio's, whose head tilted ever so slightly to one side.

"What's the plan?" Rio eventually broke the silence. Her question was pointedly directed at Agatha. 

"You need to help me with my Halloween costume, Mami."

That made Rio's brows shoot up. Her dark eyes falling on their son. "I do?"

Nicky looked almost apprehensive as he said, "You promised."

"I did?"

"You don't have to go work again do you?" Something fractured inside Agatha's chest and even Rio visibly crumbled.

"I'm not going anywhere," Rio reassured, reaching across the table to squeeze his arm. "I promise."

Nicky accepted the words as he focused on his plate.

"Did you decide on your costume?" It distracted Agatha, her mind drifting to the Maximoff Twins from a time before, their excited grins in their superhero costumes grinning up at her. 

"Mama, you said you'd help too." Nicky whined. "You didn't forget too?"

"I was just teasing," Agatha laughed off quickly.

"Well, if I'm going to be a wizard then you shouldn't tease me, I can put you under a spell!"

Agatha felt something in her panic, something deep in her gut and she shared a laugh with Rio who looked back at her with as much confusion as she felt.

"A wizard huh? Not a witch?" Agatha pressed a little.

"Johnny Malkin's says boys are wizards and girls are witches," Nicky pouted.

"Well, that's not true at all," Rio jumped in, leaning forward a little eagerly as she rested her forearms on the table. "I know plenty of boy witches. They're just warlocks."

"Not wizards?" Nicky's brow scrunched.

"Nah," Agatha reassured, brushing his hair from his eyes. "Wizards need help to tap into their magic. Witches and Warlocks are born with it."

"What kind of help?"

"Normally with rings or other kinds of items, it comes to us witches naturally." Agatha explained softly, it made Nicky ponder a moment before he nodded.

"I think I get it," Nicky said, moving back to his pancakes.

"So, um, what specifics do you need with the costume?" Rio asked, sipping the orange juice in front of her. It gave Agatha a moment to really take in the scene around them. They were literally playing house.

"I need help making a broom," Nicky said, shovelling the last of the pancake on his now chocolate covered face.

"A B-Broom?" Rio stuttered. Agatha's brow arched. Rio had never stuttered in her long life.

"Yeah," Nicky nodded. The underlying 'Obviously' was there. "You and Moma had brooms in the older days."

"Well that was a bit different," Rio protested weakly. "There weren't cars or bikes or well, any decent mode of transport."

"Exactly," Nicky nodded. "If I'm going to be Wizard or or Warlock, then I need a broom."

Rio turned to Agatha for help. With the best sickly sweet voice she could muster, she grinned. "You heard your son, he needs a broom."

It was certainly one thing teasing Rio about their son being on a broom, but it was a completely different matter seeing it in action. As soon as they started to clear up, Nicky off to wash the gross sweet treats of his face, she grabbed Rio's arm. She knew the result would be Rio grabbing her own wrist and spinning her, pinning her to the counter with inhuman strength.

"You are not putting my baby on a broom."

Rio softened, "I have no intention of sticking him on a broom."

Agatha tried to step back but Rio was close, the smell of cut grass in the rain. The hint of pine in her nose made it almost intoxicating. "We-We need to work out what this is."

Rio's eyes drifted lower, to Agatha's lips. "What do you remember?"

Agatha's breathing felt hard, her nerves fraying and her need to escape higher. Agatha swallowed. "We were at the end. I wanted my prize."

"And?" Rio pressed, her nose brushing Agatha's.

"I kissed you," Agatha recalled. "I tried to take-"

Rio's finger shot up pressing a finger to her lips silencing any recollection. Agatha’s brow knitted. It didn't make sense.

"Mama?" Nicky's voice called.

"Yeah, baby?" Agatha asked back, she didn't miss the way Rio's head tilted, listening but her finger traced patterns on her jaw line that had Agatha's heart rate doubled.

"I need help," Nicky's insistence immediately made Agatha give in. She pushed past Rio before stopping at the foot of the stairs.

"This conversation isn't over."

Nicky's emergency was his outfit for Halloween. "You know most people don't start getting ready for All Hallows Eve until nightfall."

"You need to help me stitch it Mama."

"Stitch it?" Agatha's jaw dropped.

"Yeah," Nicky nodded, "It needs to be all floaty."

"Floaty?"

"And dark." Nicky added holding up the black cloth.

"Right," Agatha nodded. She took the fabric from him and turned. Hopefully where ever the hell they had ended up she might have a sliver or magic.

Once everyone was showered and dressed, Agatha began to take stock of her new realm. The house was exactly as she had fantasised about with Rio. But it was in Westview, it was that idiot mans house she’d commandeered when she had tried to steal Wanda's powers.

Setting the bunch of fabric on the dining room table, she sat, rolled her shoulders and with practised ease she flexed her fingers summoning her power from deep within. Nothing happened. Stretching her neck, she tried once more, her concentration on the flow of it through her veins, the desire of her power to assist her in her need. Nothing.

Agatha huffed, her frustration etching out. The thunder of the stairs revealed Nicky in his jeans and t-shirt, a hoodie draped over his shoulders and arms full of cardboard.

"What's all that for?" Agatha couldn't stop herself, she didn't want to miss a moment of this.

"It's for my broom." He practically vibrated with excitement. As if on queue, Rio descended the stairs in a simple black sweater, rolled to her elbows. Her jeans and shoes made Agatha's breath hitched. It wasn't something she had ever expected to see.

"On his bike," Rio added, her hand curling around his shoulder as she stopped beside him.

Agatha stood, slowly approaching them, as if she got too close Rio might snatch him away once more. "I see and where are you going to be riding-" Nicky scowled and she quickly corrected course. "Sorry, flying off to?"

"Mami says that the appropriate prot-protocols are to fly together," Nicky recited with wide blue eyes. It reminded Agatha of the road. Of their ritual escaping the Seven.

The next words out her mouth tasted like ash. "And she's right. Which means we don't want you going anywhere without both of us."

Nicky rolled his eyes. His huff clearly indicated that he thought  his mother was being over reactive. But it was Rio that made her lookup with wide eyes.

"Nicholas."

"Course, Mama."

"Why don't you get your bike ready?" Rio suggested, far more softly than her previously warning tone and waved him in the direction of the back yard. Without another thought, Nicky bounced off out the backdoor and Rio rounded on her.

"I can't go anywhere, Agatha."

It immediately put her on the backfoot. "What do you mean?"

"I don't have my normal power."

"So you have no power?" Agatha practically choked on her words. Rio completely defenceless was something else entirely.

"No, I have my normal, Death powers." Rio clarified. "But I have nothing else. I can't teleport, I can't shift out of this form. I can feel it, but it's not there."

"Interesting," Agatha folded her arms over one another and tapped a finger to her lips. "So if I was to stab you, would you die?"

"You know that's not how being cosmic works."

"I don't know how it works because you never told me," Agatha bit out. Her anger rose with every word.

"It's not exactly something I can put into words for a human to understand," Rio snapped and Agatha felt victorious. It was always hard to get Rio to crack. But now, without the protection of her powers, she was cracking and it showed in the downward tilt of her lips. "Do you?"

"Do you think I would tell you if I did?" Agatha arched a brow as she rested against the door jam.

"Agatha," Rio took a step towards her but Agatha only lifted her chin.

"No," She said with a purse of her lips. "I don't have anything."

"Not even a spark?" Rio pressed, stepping closer. She took Agatha's hand almost forcefully and Agatha ripped it from calloused hands.

"No, nothing." Agatha huffed. As she did, she watched Rio place her hands on her hips and huff. Dark hair shielding her face. "Not exactly as much of a walk in the park as you thought being mortal?"

She couldn't help the goading but Rio's jaw clenched as she looked back at Agatha. Her nostrils flared and her fingers ticked in the impatient way she got. Agatha saw it. The faint glint of a black band on Rio's finger. It made her stomach twist.

Rio's mouth opened to likely lash back at Agatha but as she did, the backdoor swung open. "Mami! Come on! I need the scissors and tape!"

Rio's look of death softened as she turned with a forced smile. "Of course, let's go," She said, walking to the kitchen with practised ease and collecting what they needed.

With a slam of the door, Agatha was left in silence. With a sigh, she pushed from the frame and looked around. She was in some kind of alternate world, she was sure of it. The house wasn't as dark as it had been, it was still all dark woods but the couch was light and far less man child that Bohner had. On the walls were photos of Nicky she knew couldn't have existed and her breath caught in her throat as she traced them. One of him at a lake, smiling at the camera. Another with him on Rio's shoulders laughing. One of him as a baby on the mantle. Another of Rio and Agatha stood in the shadows but there was no mistaking their silhouettes with their foreheads pressed together. On the stairs, there was more. Nicky picking out a pumpkin, him holding Senor Scratchy. Another of Agatha pregnant. A photo of the three of them at the top of a hill.

Tears rolled freely down her cheeks as she lifted a hand to trace them. They hadn't had this life. They never had. Who was cruel enough to give it to them now?

Chapter 2

 

Agatha hated manual labour. In fact, she hated it so much she was ready to burn the damn fabric in front of her. The only thing stopping her was the excited look Nicky had given her when he pushed the costume into her hands. So, once she had gotten over the heartbreak of the photos that surrounded her, she collected herself, deciding she would investigate later once Nicky was asleep. 

After stabbing herself for what felt like the 700th time with the needle and thread, she held the costume up. It wasn't her finest for sure but it wasn't awful. 

The backdoor slammed open as Nicky raced in, his eyes alight. “That looks awesome.”

Agatha’s heart grew about four sizes at his words. “You think?” 

“Yeah!” He rushed to her side as he eyed the material. “I’m going to look so cool.” 

“You sure are, bud,” Rio chuckled as she stepped in, clapping the boots on the mat as she did. Like it was second nature. 

Nicky grinned and bounced off to his room. Rambling something to himself about Senor Sratchy needing his own costume. Agatha slightly winced for her familiar.

"Since when do you know how to sew?" Rio began as she stepped further into the kitchen.

Agatha glowered openly at the woman. "Since I was thirteen."

Rio's brow arched at the hostility before her smirk that Agatha was growing to have a serious love hate relationship with. "It's a long time to remember a skill like that."

"Well, when you have my mother as a teacher it's literally beaten into you."

Rio's eyes softened as she stepped closer, her voice dropping so only they could hear. "Have you worked out what this is yet?"

"No clue." Agatha sneered as she looked around her home once more. "Anything outside?"

"You mean in the greenhouse with rare and exotic plants only a green witch could keep alive?" Rio said and Agatha glared. She didn't need the snide remarks. "No. Just a soccer ball, Nicky's bike and standard garden items."

"You're not planning to tie me to the trellis again are you?"

Rio's grin was practically feral as she leaned close to her. "Only if you ask for it, baby."

Agatha huffed, pushing past the witch and into the kitchen. She needed coffee or wine or bourbon or anything to get her through this.

"So what could it be?" Rio asked, looking around. "Another hex?"

"I doubt that could hold you," Agatha countered. "An illusion?"

"I already told you, I don't have all my power." Rio said, her irritation showing.

"A curse?"

"Curses don't happen like this," Rio looked around. "You and I both know that."

Agatha huffed. "Well then what could it be?"

She turned to Rio who shook her head, her dark eyes landing on the photos on the wall. She watched the witch walk and take in the fantasy Agatha knew they both had. Some part of her was pleased to watch the pain flicker across Rio's face. The way her head tilted at the photo of Agatha heavily pregnant made her turn.

"I remember you like this," Rio muttered. Agatha stood taller, her walls flying up as fast as she could make them. "You were so beautiful carrying him."

"If you mean scrambling in the woods in labour," Agatha snarked but Rio turned, her dark eyes soft and it pierced Agatha to the spot.

"I used to listen, when you thought I wasn't there, to the stories you would tell him in your belly about our adventures."

Agatha could remember it. She remembered it like it was yesterday. "Don't." She didn't want to recall any of it, just to have it all slip away.

Rio's gaze dropped and she turned back to the living room, she stepped around, Nicky's toys scattered the floor as the TV played.

"What about something else?" Agatha distracted. "Another realm?"

Rio shook her head. "No, we're not allowed to jump across the multi-verse."

"You mean-"

"That there's other version of our lives?" Rio continued. "Yes, but there's billions of them, and we wouldn't be in another one so easily. I certainly wouldn't."

"Why not?" Agatha couldn't help her curiosity.

Rio laughed, "because I'd be stepping on another Death's yard. I have my job. My realm in our universe. Another version of Death has theirs."

Agatha chewed on the information before she huffed. "Great, so we're still no further forward."

"Maybe we play it out?" Rio suggested with a shrug as she settled on the chair she frequented while Agatha was under Wanda's curse.

"Play it out?"

"Yeah," Rio leant backwards. Her face and voice softened. "This is the life we dreamed of my love."

"Don't call me that."

Rio's features hardened. "You can't stand there and tell me part of you doesn't want this? Doesn't want to watch him grow up?"

"It's cruel, Rio." Agatha choked. "I gave up on that dream when you took him."

"I told you I could only give you more time." Rio growled. "I broke so many rules for you Agatha."

"No you didn't," Agatha scoffed. "You toyed with me. You gave me what I thought we wanted and then you snatched it away."

"I didn't have a choice." Rio was now standing, her hands twitching like they did when she was getting ready to fight, to draw on her power.

"You are literally life and death," Agatha laughed. "Of course you had a choice!"

"Stop!"

Both turned to find Nicky standing on the stairs. His brow drawn tight as he looked between them. His lower lip wobbled. "Don't fight."

Agatha's hands went to her hips. "Nicky-"

"We weren't fighting, bud," Rio softened, as she lowered herself to his height. "We were just having a disagreement."

"That's fighting," Nicky argued back and Agatha tried not to laugh at his pedantry.

"We didn't mean for you to hear us," Agatha smiled, pushing her hair behind her ear. "We're sorry."

Nicky nodded, slowly descending the stairs and stepping into Agatha's space, his arms wrapping around her waist as he buried his face into her hip. "I don't like it when you fight."

Agatha's knee's practically gave out as she slowly knelt and wrapped her arms around his thin frame. "I'm sorry."

"Don't do it again." Nicky's small order had her pressing her cheek to his hair and meeting dark eyes. An unspoken agreement fell between them.

"We won't," Agatha whispered. "We promise."

Rio nodded back at Agatha in silent agreement as she stepped forward and ran her fingers through his hair. "Promise, mijo "

Once Nicky was content that they were in fact telling the truth, he settled with Agatha on the couch, curled into her side while Rio made herself scarce. She hoped feeling the disappointment from their son for the first time might scare her off.

Agatha sat with one of his books as she listened to him read aloud for her. She had missed his voice, the little scrunch of his face as he concentrated hard. But it was peaceful and it was nice even as melancholy set over Agatha's bones when she reminded herself none of this was real. Maximoff might have been right with her twins. Maybe the bad guys could have a happy ending?

Of course their peacefulness was ruined by Rio slamming through the backdoor. Nicky jumped and instinctively, Agatha pushed him behind her, glaring at the intruder. Immediately, however, Agatha laughed. It spilled from her in probably the most undignified way she could imagine. Because practically spilling from her arms were three of the largest pumpkins Agatha had ever seen.

"If we're going to do this equinox properly," Rio announced as she set them on the table. "Then we're carving pumpkins."

Nicky was off the couch faster than she thought he could move. "They're huge!"

Rio grinned almost manically as she nodded. Agatha rolled her eyes as she set the book on the coffee table and stood.

"What do you think, Nicky?" Agatha tried to enthuse, she must admit she shared Lilia's feelings about Halloween. "What are you going to carve to keep the ghosts away?"

Nicky giggled and Agatha glanced up at Rio to watch her lip curl. She really did hate ghosts.

“I doubt the PTA wants to hear about the sinking of the Hindenburg,” Agatha teased as she straightened her shirt. 

“You mean they don’t want to hear about the anniversary gift you gave me?” 

“Who said it was for you?” 

“I doubt you did it for Hela?” Rio sipped the coffee in her hand with a slow smirk. 

“Please,” Agatha snorted. “I have better taste than that overdramatic Asguardian.” 

Rio chuckled as she leant back into the wooden bench, her elbow draped over the back, knees crossed as she took in the playground where Nicky ran happily. Agatha watched Rio, really watched her, the slight crease of her eyes, the way her jaw was raised with an air of superiority only Rio could manage. She watched dark eyes follow their son, the way she occasionally lifted a hand to wave as Nicky turned back to check they were where he left them. 

“Does it make you proud?” Agatha asked, forcing Rio’s eyes back on her. “To know that all this life is in balance.” 

Rio’s brow crinkled for a moment, her smirk falling before the mask was back in place. A small smile graced her face as she turned back to Nicky. “Does it make you happy knowing that you could potentially have your son forever if you left whatever this is alone?” 

“But it’s not natural, you said it yourself,” Agatha quietly countered, pushing dark locks from her face. 

“Maybe I don’t care,” Rio huffed. “Maybe this is what I want. What I always wanted.” 

“Rio-” 

“I know,” Rio snapped under her breath. “I know it's not real, or natural but sometimes when I sit and listen to his day all I can think about is how badly I missed out on all of this.” 

“You chose not to be there,” Agatha sneered. Her lip curling as anger licked hot in her chest. “You chose to not see him.” 

“I watched from a distance, Agatha.” Rio sighed. “I didn’t want you constantly hiding from me.”

Agatha let it drop, the pressure in the wound becoming too much. 

“He’s happy here,” Rio voiced after a long, painful moment. “He seems more energetic, content.” 

“There's indoor plumbing and medical care, what do you expect?” Agatha griped. Of course he would have been better off in this time. He could have lived, he could have had this perfect life that had been crafted. 

Rio chuckled low in her throat, “my point is-” 

“Your point is that you want to accept this,” Agatha snapped. “But what happens when it all gets ripped away again, Rio? What happens when Nicky is dead and I’m gone and you are alone again?” Agatha’s tirade spilled and erupted from a place she didn’t know she was capable of going. “Can you honestly say you would be happy knowing about what you could have had? I know what it's like to have my world ripped from me and I know I won’t handle that again.” 

Rio’s silent as her dark eyes are soft as she looks forward. Her face was blank and unreadable. “Do you think I didn’t grieve for Nicky?” 

Agatha watched as those dark eyes slowly and painstakingly turned to her. “Because I did, Agatha. I spent centuries seeing that little boy in every soul that was similar. Every boy that crossed into my realm I thought was him. But I had already committed the one crime I know I should never have done and it rips me apart every time I think of him. Lying, cold and dead in your arms.”

“Don’t-” 

“No,” Rio growled, turning to face her fully. Tears threatening to fall from dark lashes. “You seem to think I did this to you. But I did what I did because I had too. I wasn’t going to let him wander the plain between worlds just because you couldn’t cope with the reality that I had to take him and yes, it kills me to know that he was safe in my world but I can’t think of doing it again. I know I can’t take him or lose him to Death again. He was my son too and he never deserved to die but it's the natural order of things, Agatha and even I can’t cheat myself.” 

“So you’re burying your head in the sand?” 

“I’m enjoying the time I never got to spend with my son,” Rio’s words slip through clenched teeth. “I don’t want to miss anymore if I don’t have too.” 

“And what about when it all slips away, Rio?” 

“Then I’ll fight to get it back.” 

Agatha can feel the argument grow weary in her bones. “Rio-” 

“Mama!” Agatha doesn’t get to finish her caution as Nicky runs to them, his eyes wide and alight with excitement. His soft locks windswept from running. “Did you see?” 

“Of course we did,” Rio chuckled, soothing his dishevelled hair. “Some excellent climbing.” 

“I went so fast down the slide!” 

“You sure did,” Rio laughed at his energy and it was infectious. The happiness that seeped from their pores when they were with one another- who wouldn't want this? Could Agatha really blame Rio when deep down she wanted this too? 

“And then did- did you see-” Nicky's gasps came in short and sharp and Agatha shifted closer. 

“Nicky, slow down,” Agatha pleaded softly, her flashes to a memory of Nicky coughing made her go cold.

“I’m fine, Mama,” He wheezed and now her brow drew tight as she shifted closer. 

“Nicky,” Agatha warned as she held him against her knee. His breaths in short sharp gasps, with a pleading look she turned to Rio who had shifted closer, worry splashed across her face. 

“Nicky, just slow down ok?” Rio’s voice took that authoritarian tone that it took when things didn’t go to plan, when she was deeply concerned and she couldn’t hide it. 

Nicky’s breaths were struggling to come out in an even form now, his own brow drawn tight. 

Agatha’s panic rose and she turned to Rio, Rio who had equally turned to her with worry sketched across her face. “Rio? Rio, what’s wrong with him? Why can’t he breathe?” Agatha turned to Nicky, whose eyes were filled with panic and tears. 

“I’m calling an ambulance,” Rio announced in a tone Agatha swore she had never heard before. 

“Amb- What?” Agatha all but screeched, her body frozen, her limbs wouldn’t move. No- No this wasn’t happening. Not now. “Rio!” 

Rio turned her eyes dark. “He can’t breathe, Agatha, he needs a doctor.” 

“Mama?” Nicky’s wheezing panic snapped Agatha from her own panic. 

“It’s alright baby,” Agatha forced herself to smile. “We’re going to fix it, ok? We’re going to make it all better.”  

It didn’t take long, his breathing continued to fill her with fear and terror that she knew was happening, but then the paramedics were there and the people in the park were watching with wide eyes that made Agatha’s teeth on edge. She didn’t care as she followed them into the back of the ambulance and there was no question as Rio announced she would follow with the car. Agatha didn’t even look back. Her eyes were only for Nicky with that awful plastic over his nose, his forehead stricken with sweat and his eyes wide with fear. She tried, painfully, to reassure him, to tell him it would be alright but it was hard when her hands shook and her tears fell. 

“Mama?” Nicky’s wheezing voice made her force a smile but he only griped her hand tighter. “Where’s Mami?” 

“She’s coming Nicky, it’s going to be ok, try not to worry, they’re going to make everything better I promise.” Agatha was rambling, it wasn’t a promise she knew if she could even keep. She had been here before. She had watched him battle that fever and there was nothing she could do. Now, she was just as helpless. 

“Ma’am, we’re here,” She hated how the paramedic looked at her with those pity filled eyes. But before she could even snap, Nicky was whisked away. 

“Wait, Nicky!” 

“It’s alright,” A nurse, urgh she hated it, stepped in her way. “We’re going to run some tests, the doctor will update you soon.” 

Agatha swore under her breath as she began to pace. 

“Are there any allergies we need to be aware of?” 

“No- No, he’s just a boy, a normal boy,” Agatha felt her voice crack as she uttered those words. She’d uttered them before. 

“Alright,” There was that pitying smile again. “We’ll update you as soon as we can.” 

 She was on her fifth stride and plotting how to rip the nurses eyeballs out when Rio ran in. Her eyes wide and for the first time since waking up here. Murderous. “Where is he?” Rio growled at her. 

“They took him, they- they said they had to run tests,” Agatha gestured to the double doors her son had disappeared through. 

“And you’re still here?” 

“I want him to live, Rio!”

“So do I!” 

“So, let them do their jobs,” Agatha spat. Agatha froze, her next words tasting like venom “Unless you know something I don’t.” 

Rio spun and redirected all the anger at Agatha, her jaw set, her eyes wide and her nostrils flared. “I’m not here for that.” 

“Are you sure?” 

Rio didn’t justify her with an answer and instead went to the desk. Rapid Spanish ensued and Agatha pinched the bridge of her nose. Her head was pounding. 

“Agatha?” 

Agatha spun, never before happy to hear that tone of distaste in her life. 

“Jen!” 

Agatha walked quickly over to where Jen stood, pink scrubs and all. 

“Nicky- They- they took Nicky-” 

Jen visibly softened. “I know, come with me.”

Jen led her and Agatha didn’t even think as she followed. “He had an asthma attack, undiagnosed previously but that’s what it was.” 

Agatha nodded as they reached the small ward, filled with colourful animals on the wall. 

“Now before I go back and get the noted sociopath of your ex, I’m hoping to god you remember.” 

Agatha nearly fainted. 

“You- You aren’t under the spell?” 

“No, Agatha, I am not.” 

“H-How?” 

“I don’t know,” Jen squawked. God, Agatha wanted to ring her neck. “I was hoping you could tell me since the last thing I remember was unbinding myself and then I’m here, stuck in this hospital wandering empty halls until you turn up and it’s like an episode of Grey’s Anatomy in here.” 

What The Fuck.  

Agatha tried to regain her footing. Jen knew. Jen knew who she was. “Alright, alright.” Agatha grounded, holding her palm flat. “You remember, that’s- that’s good. So far it’s only you, Rio and I that can remember anything.” 

“What the hell does that mean?” 

“It’s a really long story,” Agatha sighed, pushing her hair from her face. “First things first, I need to see Nicky.” 

Agatha didn’t wait and turned, pushing through the doors to find Nicky propped up in the bed. The sight floored her. He was here. He was fine. That toothy grin smiled back at her and despite looking tiny in that hospital bed he was fine. 

“Dr Jen fixed me, Mama!” 

Agatha nearly crumbled to tears as she curled around him and kissed the crown of soft hair. He was here. He was still here. 

“She did,” Agatha bit back her sobs and as she did, she looked up to find Rio stepping into the room. Her hands shaking, her body trembling as dark eyes were still welled with tears. “He’s alright,” Agatha whispered and it earned her a shaky nod, one that had her stumbling to the bed and Agatha wrapped them both in her arms. Her family. That’s whatever this life had given her. Her family. The family she had never had. The one she had only briefly had with Nicky but with both Nicky and Rio, she felt complete. 

Maybe- Just maybe she could enjoy whatever this was.
