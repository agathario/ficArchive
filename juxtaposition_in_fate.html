Summary

Agatha Harkness gets invited to a wedding in her hometown, Westview, New Jersey—where her cheating ex still lives.
Determined to “win” the breakup, she ropes in a friend to pose as her date to the event.
Enter Rio Vidal: hot, chaotic, and very willing to fake-date for the weekend.

It’s supposed to be harmless, just for show. But between shared motel beds and moments that feel a little too real, the women start to wonder when exactly fake started feeling so real.

Of how none of this was supposed to be real… except it terrifyingly was.

Agatha exhaled slowly. Like she’d been holding her breath the whole time.

Rio leaned in first, brushing a quick kiss to Agatha’s cheek.

But that wasn’t enough.

“Boo!” someone yelled. “We said kiss!”

At that moment, Agatha swore she wanted the earth to swallow her whole. But all eyes were on them, and Rio—sweet, chaotic, incredibly committed Rio—was already turning toward her with that usual mischief in her eyes.

“Guess we’ve got to sell it,” she murmured.

Rio nodded almost imperceptibly, as if telling her it was okay. 

And then Agatha kissed her.

It wasn’t meant to be much. Just enough to shut everyone up. A show. A second.

But it changed as soon as their mouths met.

Rio kissed her back with more pressure than expected, one hand sliding from Agatha’s waist to her hip with purpose, pulling her closer. Her lips were warm, impossibly soft, and Agatha felt her whole body react—her heart suddenly thudding painfully hard, her skin alive everywhere they touched.

She moved almost unconsciously, hands finding the back of Rio’s neck, curling into her hair, tugging her in like she couldn’t get her close enough. Agatha opened her mouth without meaning to, and the kiss deepened.

Rio gasped, quiet and wrecked, and Agatha felt the sound vibrate through her.

The kiss burned slow and deep, but it wasn’t frantic. It wasn’t rushed. It was consuming. The kind of kiss that made time slip away. That made it impossible to remember where they were or why it had started in the first place.

Agatha kissed her like she didn’t know where she was. Like she forgot it was fake. Like it wasn’t the first time.

Like it wouldn’t be the last.

Rio kissed her back like she’d been waiting to find out what Agatha tasted like since the second she first saw her. Like she would never get enough of her.

By the time they reluctantly pulled apart the room had erupted into applause, but it sounded far away, almost as if happening underwater.

Agatha’s breath caught in her throat.

Rio’s lips were parted, red and a little kiss-swollen, her pupils blown wide. Her chest rose and fell fast and shallow, like she’d just run a freaking marathon.

They stared at each other but neither of them could utter a single word.

There was nothing to say that wouldn’t break whatever had just shifted between them. So they just stood there, caught in the moment of something neither of them could name yet. Of something raw and real and terrifyingly close to the surface.

And for the first time all night, the fake part of it was nowhere to be found.

And neither of them knew what the hell to do about it.
