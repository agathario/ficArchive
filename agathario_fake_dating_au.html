Agatha took out the single piece of tissue paper, crumpled it, and threw it at Rio’s face. It bounced and landed at her feet, but Rio remained stoic, staring at her unamused.

“Open your damn gift.”

Agatha reached back into the bag and pulled out the gift. It was a small, circular wooden keychain, stained a light honey color with a glossy finish. She ran her finger along the back of it and felt a slight indent, and when she turned it over, she saw it was actually an engraving. Agatha moved her thumb to the side to read it.

A + R

Their initials were burned into the wood in fancy cursive lettering.

“Light’s green,” Rio said, drawing Agatha’s attention away from the gift.

Agatha quickly tucked the keychain into the palm of her hand and accelerated as Rio kept talking.

“I bought that back at the beginning of the vacation, before either of us fell for the other. It was supposed to be a joke, like a fun little memento, but things have kind of changed since then so I guess it’s just a regular gift now.”

“It’s perfect,” Agatha smiled. She ran her hand down Rio’s forearm resting on the center console and intertwined their fingers, giving her a quick squeeze.

They stayed like this for a while, hands gently together, as Agatha drove down the winding back roads. Outside, snow capped trees blurred past the window, their sameness like visual white noise.

Every once in a while, Agatha would glance over to Rio and catch her mid yawn. Rio would try to stifle and hide it, but by the fourth or fifth yawn, Agatha gave her a look.

“Why don’t you take a little power nap?” she suggested.

Rio shrugged petulantly, fighting to keep her eyes open. “But I’m not tired,” she said, her speech slightly slurred.

Agatha smiled gently, her features softening. “No, of course you’re not.” She reached behind herself and grabbed her winter jacket hanging off the headrest, grateful she hadn’t stowed it away in her duffel bag. “You just want to rest your eyes, right?” She placed the jacket over Rio’s lap like a blanket. “Just for a little bit?”

“I know what you’re doing,” Rio mumbled as she snuggled under the jacket, tucking the inner fleece lining under her chin.

“I’m just taking care of my girlfriend,” Agatha said innocently.

Rio said nothing in response, her eyes slowly falling, the gentle hum of the engine drawing her to sleep. The sound of her breathing soon evened out to a steady cadence, and Agatha looked at her, the corner of her mouth curving up. She let out a quiet laugh through her nose and turned back to the road.

As she did, a pothole appeared out of nowhere. She swerved to avoid it, and Rio jostled in her seat.

“Huh? What happened?” she said blearily.

“Nothing, go back to sleep,” Agatha soothed.

She reached over and brushed Rio’s hair out of her face, gently petting the top of her head until Rio felt safe enough to close her eyes again. She mumbled something Agatha couldn’t make out as she drifted back to sleep, and Agatha turned her attention back to the road, taking care not to stir Rio with another pothole.

When another eventually appeared, she carefully maneuvered around it, and Rio stayed securely asleep. In the back of her mind, Agatha reminded herself this was a back road, but she couldn't help cursing the government for not fixing more of the potholes.

“Isn’t that the whole point of taxes?” she whispered to herself. “Filling these damn holes with more cement?”

But all she could do was keep driving, taking extra care not to wake Rio. As the miles stretched on, Agatha found it wasn’t particularly difficult, either. Almost all of her thoughts were already about Rio, and making sure she was comfortable was just another to add to the fray.

Slowly but surely, the miles remaining on the GPS ticked down, Agatha recognized the streets of Westview, and she could finally shut off that infernal automated voice.

Getting closer to Rio’s house, Agatha found she remembered the streets she had to turn on, even without the GPS guidance. Maybe she did have Rio’s address memorized after all. Not that she was about to tell Rio that.

The sound of tires against her gravel driveway woke Rio up, and she stretched in her seat, carding her hand through her hair.

Agatha watched her with a new sense of apprehension gnawing at the edge of her consciousness. Was she supposed to drop Rio off and leave? Did Rio want her to come in? How was she even supposed to ask that without sounding too forward?

Throwing her questions aside, Agatha said, “I’ll get your stuff.”

She got out of the car and unloaded Rio’s suitcase from the backseat. Agatha didn’t know if Rio wanted her to stay, but she’d be damned if she didn’t carry her luggage to the door like a proper gentleman.

“You didn’t have to do that,” Rio said, joining Agatha in the driveway. She tried to take her suitcase back, but Agatha didn’t let her.

“Yes I did.” Agatha cupped Rio’s cheek and planted a quick kiss. When she pulled away, she offered her arm to Rio. “Allow me to escort you to the door, my lady.”

“You’re too kind, my liege,” Rio said with her best British accent.

Agatha shook her head, trying not to break character at Rio’s terrible accent, and walked up the steps to Rio’s front door.

Rio fiddled with her keys until she found the right one, unlocking the door. It swung open and hit the doorstop with a gentle thud, and Agatha got her first glance into Rio’s home, into her life away from work and the chaos of Agatha’s schemes. Warm air floated onto the porch, bringing with it a homey, faintly cinnamon scent.

Agatha stood with Rio’s suitcase in front of her like a protective wall. She didn’t move forward or back, unsure which direction was right, settling instead to shift her weight side to side.

Rio tucked her keys in her pocket and took half a step into her house before pausing, glancing back at Agatha. It took her one look, less than a second, to soften her expression, understanding exactly where Agatha’s head was at.

“You don’t have to leave yet.”

Those six simple words brought Agatha out of her nervous spiral and curved her lips into a smile, her heart steadying. “I was hoping you’d say that.”

Rio’s hand brushed against Agatha’s, and Agatha intertwined their fingers without a second thought, instinctual. They stood on the porch for a moment longer. Rio looked inside and regarded her house like it was the first time she’d seen it.

“Can’t believe it’s been a week.”

“One of my crazier weeks, too,” Agatha said, bumping their shoulders together to nudge Rio inside.

“One of?” Rio asked incredulously. Agatha shrugged. “Yeah, well, we really botched the whole fake dating thing, didn’t we?”

“Yeah, we did,” Agatha laughed. “Completely ruined it by actually falling for each other.”

“Tragic. Truly tragic.” Rio pushed the door shut as they stepped inside. “Guess we’ll just have to keep pretending.”

“But to what end?” Agatha said dramatically.

“Forever?” Rio leaned into her, and Agatha’s chest tightened in the best way possible.

“Deal.”

She tilted Rio’s chin up to kiss her. When they parted, Rio’s smile was uncontainable, and Agatha knew there was no better ending to their vacation.
