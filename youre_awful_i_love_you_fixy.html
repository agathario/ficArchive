That achingly beautiful rose tint falls over everything. It’s love at first sight. Passionate pursuit. A resilience to obstacles that stop Rio from reaching her happiness, from offering the same happiness right back. 

No monsters play in pink skies. Only girls in love, chasing each other, scuffing their knees in rows upon rows of dark, dark roses, past ponds of water that glitter like the broken glass of balconies and shop windows. 

Try as she might, Rio can’t shake the pink. 

Perhaps she doesn’t even want to. 

It’s been hours. Six, maybe seven. Her body hurts. Her mind hurts. Her heart aches. 

It’s pathetic. And pink. 

Rio tries to picture Agatha in pink. 

Her Agatha, dressed in innocent blossoms?

Instead, she sees the heady flush of outrage and anger. Blushed skin shimmering with sweat. Blotchy cheeks stained with crocodile tears. 

There’s no innocence to be found in Agatha. 

And maybe it’s not quite so scary, Rio thinks, when the monster chases another monster. 

Maybe their shade of pink is the blood they draw from each other. 

Rio sighs. What does it mean to see your wrongs laid out behind you like bodies, and still not feel remorse? She would have, once upon a time. Or maybe she never wanted anything enough to willingly leave disaster in her wake. As long as she can reach her monster, the destruction hardly matters. 

She does miss Alice. 

Rio thinks about her now. Supportive until the very end, seeing Rio’s new silhouette before even Rio herself could, and still trying to help. Alice doesn’t feel like a body left behind. She feels like something that can be fixed. And Rio will fix it. Them. She’s not suddenly unfeeling. She feels more than ever, if anything. Her heart has simply grown to fit more, to pump more pink blood around her new hulking form. 

Fuck. Enough with the metaphors. The water stain is a prayer away from becoming sentient. 

Rio rolls over, hissing as her bad shoulder is jostled. 

The cell walls are too white. Bright tiles. Easier to clean. 

Rio never saw the inside of Agatha’s bathroom. What colour were her tiles?

Even now, even after tonight, even after the cuts on her wrists stopped smarting from the handcuffs slapped on by Agatha, Agatha, Agatha, Rio yearns. 

It’s mortifying. Humiliating. 

It’s permanent. 

She recalls a story in one of Billy’s manga comic things, left on the counter during a post-lunch lull. The male character vomited flowers. A love unrequited. 

At least Rio’s petals would be the prettiest shade of pink before she choked. 

But-

Agatha had called her baby. Had stroked her spine so sweetly, checked she was okay, spoke with warmth. 

So maybe she- 

But then she’d called the-

Never mind. Rio’s new home is white tiles, soon to be concrete and bars, if movies told the truth. She has no idea what sentence comes with a crime like stalking, and that’s if Agatha doesn’t divulge the rest. 

Rio made this bed, hammered together the wood and nails and stitched the sheets by hand. She’ll lie in it. She’ll have to. 

And she’ll think of pink skies and blue eyes. 

“Vidal.”

Had she fallen asleep? Rio blinks, the still too-bright walls coming into focus. The door is open, and a woman in uniform stands there, looking at her with a bemused expression. 

Pushing herself up onto her elbow, Rio grimaces, mindful of her other shoulder. The officer gives her a once-over. 

“Keep your hands to yourself, or you’re back in the cell with an assault charge,” the woman says, one eyebrow raised. “Got it?”

Rio nods. 

“Okay, let’s go.”

The metal bench creaks as Rio slowly stands, body stiff and sore. She must look confused. The officer takes that as a question, and answers. 

“You’re free to go.”

Rio stops.

Free. 

What?

Her heart pounds. 

Alice?

She’s Rio’s emergency contact. Do the cops have her phone? Maybe they called Alice, and she’s paid Rio’s bail. She’s probably standing outside now, waiting to tear Rio a new one and drag her back to her bar by the ear, and fuck, Rio will welcome it with open arms. 

She follows the officer, ready for the sight of Alice in her leather jacket and flame-tipped hair, ready to begin some kind of repentance. 

And she’ll repent, she will, and she’ll mend things between them, and then, then when night comes around again and Alice leaves Rio on the pull-out couch, Rio will get on her phone and pull up that wretched number and despite everything, despite the risk, she’ll call-

“Agatha.”

The name falls from Rio’s lips, rasping and quiet. 

Because Agatha is here. 

She’s framed by the station’s double doors. Early morning light streams through the glass, giving Agatha the most ethereal, backlit glow. 

“Hi, sweetheart,” Agatha says with a coy smile. “Sorry about all this. You’ll forgive me, right?”

Rio can only stare. 

Back there, in her cell, in her white tiled existence, Rio had sunk into something like acceptance. Acceptance of her new situation, of her new self, and of the way Agatha fits, and will now always fit, so neatly into her soul.

Staring at the woman before her, resplendent in slacks and sweater, Rio feels that same acceptance. That same sweet inevitability. 

“Ms Harkness,” a tall, broad officer says, “I have to stress again that this isn’t really the done way-”

“I told you,” Agatha cuts in, sharp and clear, “that I withdraw my accusation.”

“That doesn’t mean-”

“Vision,” Agatha says tiredly, pinching the bridge of her nose. “Listen. We can do this, if you really want to. We can do the whole rigmarole, fill out paperwork and take statements and do interviews and go to court, and Ms Vidal can stay incarcerated. We can do that. Would you like to do that?”

The officer purses his lips. He fixes Agatha with a severe look. 

“If you waste police time again-”

“Who said it was a waste?” Agatha smiles, all saccharine and snark. She nods her head in Rio’s direction without looking her way. “This one needed a time out.”

“She injured herself during arrest,” the man snaps. “The others say she tried to attack them like a woman possessed.”

And Agatha’s smile, so fake and sharp, slides into something more genuine. 

Blue eyes catch brown. 

“That’s my girl.”

The world stops moving for Rio. 

There’s more conversation. Agatha says something about an argument turned heated, a hasty and now much regretted phone call. The cop talks about forms to fill in, handing Agatha a pen. It all happens, happens right around Rio, who, for now, can only exist. 

Exist in Agatha’s orbit. 

Terrible. Hideous. Beautiful. Everything. 

You’re awful, Rio wants to say. I love you, she wants to shout. 

She keeps quiet. Takes it in. Wakes up when Agatha’s warm hand wraps around her tender wrist. 

“Let’s go,” Agatha says. And they go. 

Outside, the sun is just rising, and the sky is pink. 

“You’ll live with me,” Agatha tells her confidently. She glances to the side where Rio stands. Rio can count the freckles across her nose and cheeks, the lines that tell stories at the corners of her eyes. Agatha blinks, softens just slightly in a way that is, unbelievably, starting to become familiar. “If you’d like to.”

Rio replies, “I would,” her voice quiet from lack of use, from screaming bloody murder the night before. Moving in already? She doesn’t bother asking. Why would she?

She looks away, stares down at her bloodied jeans. She pulls at the denim in mild annoyance. “Jesus, Agatha. I cannot believe you did that. I could’ve gone to prison."

“I would’ve visited you,” Agatha shrugs, delight dancing in her eyes. 

Rio sighs. 

“No you wouldn’t.”

"Mm," she agrees, "probably not.”  

Rio scowls at her. It makes Agatha laugh. The sound is more captivating than the morning birdsong. 

Delicate fingers link with Rio’s own as they start to walk. Rio suddenly realises she’s never felt more at peace. 

“Rio?” Agatha asks. 

Rio looks at her and hums. This doesn’t feel real. Maybe she’s dead. 

The pink light of morning turns Agatha’s eyes lilac. She’s the most beautiful monster Rio’s ever seen, dead or not. 

“What would you do for me?”

A pause, heavy with thought. Rio looks up at their pink sky. 

“Probably anything you ask.”

When Rio glances back, Agatha’s smile has turned wicked. 

It’s breathtaking. 

“Oh,” Agatha sighs happily, “we’re gonna have fun.”
