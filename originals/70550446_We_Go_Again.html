January 15th, 2026:

This wasn’t fucking happening. There was absolutely no way Abby had read that notification right.

Arsenal in talks with FC Barcelona about a double deal concerning Vidal and Harkness after Barcelona financial crisis.

Her phone shut off as she stood in her front doorway, mouth agape. Fuck the men’s team for being £1 billion deep in debt. What business did Barcelona have not separating the finances of both teams? And fuck them again for prioritising the men and letting the women’s team go to shit.

Abby was already in hell. Off the back of their latest win against Real Madrid, her club had lost four of their most valuable players. And now? Now they might lose their best players.

Abby needed a gun.

“Abbs…are you going to come in?” Her mum asked, taking in her daughter frozen in their doorway.

“What’s the point?” Abby grumbled.

“What’s got your knickers in a twist?” Her mother laughed.

Abby turned her phone showing her mother the photoshopped images of Vidal and Harkness in red and white jerseys.

“This!” Abby cried.

***

This was fucking insanity.

Rio had just come back from her Euros injury, played in front of their fans for the first time in six months and scored two goals in the process. But unfortunately, she’d returned home the same night her house had been set on fire.

Wanda? Gone.

Monica? Gone.

Their two best defenders? Gone and gone.

Rio had never played for another club, never wanted to. She was supposed to retire at Barcelona. This was never supposed to…there had to be…Rio couldn’t fucking leave! This was her mother’s club, had been even when she’d lived in Puerto Rico and playing for Barcelona was just a dream. She never made it so, but Rio did.

“It wouldn’t be a bad decision, mija,” her father said, placing a mug in front of her. “Your career deserves better than Barcelona; they don’t value the women’s team in the way Arsenal does.”

Which, in hindsight, was true. It was undeniable that Arsenal’s management put just as much effort into the women’s team as the men’s with their ‘One club’ marketing campaign. Even then, it felt bigger than making money. Rio saw it in how both teams were showcased proudly around Emirates Stadium, how the players interacted with each other, how the heads of the clubs only ever spoke as a collective.

You’d never find that at Barcelona.

But Barcelona was home.

“What do you think mami would say?” She asked, didn’t meet her father’s gaze.

Silence filled the kitchen for a long moment before he said:

“I think she’d be upset about you leaving.”

Rio huffed.

“But she would encourage you to do so because you should only play where you are seen and respected.”

She lifted her head to see her father nodding, a small smile on his lips.

“Papi, I wouldn’t live in Spain anymore. I’ll no longer be a ten-minute drive away…you’ll be alone here,” Rio whispered.

“I can always come and visit. What happens to me should not affect your decision. I will support whatever your choice is.”

Rio bit her lip.

“What about Agatha?” Her dad spoke into the quiet. “Does she want to leave?”

Rio didn’t know.

Agatha could go home.

Of course she’d be upset about leaving Barcelona; this had been her club for four and a half years after all. It was just…fuck em. They didn’t give a shit about the women’s team and how their overspending on salaries would fuck with the entire club. La Liga F was light years behind the WSL anyway. Spain was hindering women’s football, and England were trying to skyrocket it. Why wouldn’t she want to go?

The only issue was the red.

When Agatha used to play in England, she was blue, only ever blue. A Chelsea girl through and through. She never wanted to play for Arsenal because her mother did, and she’d be damned if she followed in her footsteps.

But now her mother was dead.

The playing field had changed.

One of the Barcelona managers, Nico, pulled her aside after the Arsenal deal was announced and told her Chelsea was in talks about negotiations for her…just her.

Agatha hadn’t known how to feel. Club football was different to international football. On the world stage, Agatha wouldn’t bat an eyelash about going head-to-head with her girlfriend. They’d be at each other’s throats for a month every two years and call it a day. Different clubs meant all year, every year. Different clubs meant never playing with her girl again.

Rio always said she was a one-club woman, a loyal Culer till the end. Would she go down with the ship or jump off the edge and get to a life raft?

Time would tell.

Now two WSL clubs wanted her Barcelona twenty-two. Understandable, she was one of the best forwards in the world, but jesus did no one have any consideration for Abby’s heart? Abby would have to redecorate.

Agatha twisted her body, grey eyes locking with brown.

“Why? Can’t we just throw a coin?”

Rio deadpanned.

“You done?”

Agatha pulled a face. “You don’t want me to be comfortable for this very uncomfortable conversation? Jesus, what kind of girlfriend are you?”

And even though she didn’t want to, Rio laughed.

Agatha's lips lifted. “Okay, go on.”

“What do you want to do?” Rio started. “And I don’t mean us, I mean you.”

Agatha stared at her for so long that the silence felt like a threat, like carbon monoxide, slowly but surely suffocating whilst you’re none the wiser.

“Aggs-”

“I want to leave.”

Maybe Rio’s heart stopped. Maybe the world had ended. Maybe her ears were deceiving her.

“You…say that again.”

Agatha dropped her head. “I want to leave Barcelona, Rio.”

There was no maybe about this; Rio’s world had ended.

“Why?”

When her girlfriend looked up, her facial expression said it all: ‘You know why.’

“The management don’t care about us, Ri. We win trophy after trophy, sell out game after game, and it’s still not enough for them to listen to us. We asked the league to separate the men’s and women’s finances, they ignored us. We asked them to treat every part of the club with respect, they ignored us.” Agatha huffed. “Women’s football in England is taking the right steps to ensure its success, steps that aren’t being taken here, baby.”

Rio felt tears prick her eyes because Agatha was right. Their club had been rotting from the inside, and Rio was doing everything in her power to ignore it. As of right now, her girlfriend was refusing to let her do so.

“Might I add it’s gotten too easy. We’ve won the league ten times; don’t you miss the fight?”

“But Chelsea have won the WSL for six consecutive years in a row,” Rio noted.

Because there was also that. Two London clubs wanted Agatha compared to the one that wanted Rio. This wasn’t about jealousy, never would be; Rio could only ever be proud of her girlfriend. This was about whether Agatha would split them up or not.

“Wouldn’t that be easy too?”

Agatha tilted her head. “I never said anything about going to Chelsea.”

“But you’ve considered it though, right? You spent your entire football career there before you joined Barca.” Rio paused before adding, “To you that’s home like being here is mine.”

“I thought you didn’t want to make this about us,” Agatha pointed out.

Fuck.

Rio had, hadn’t she?

She was stupid to think she could separate the two. She tried anyway.

“No, you’re right. At the end of the day, it’s your decision and-”

“What about you?” Agatha butt in. “What do you want to do?”

Those tears began to swell again.

“We’re not on the same page, that’s all I’ll say,” Rio replied.

“About leaving Barca or me going to Chelsea?”

She swiped at a tear that fell. “All of it, Aggs. I don’t want to leave. I love this club; I love this city. I just got back on the pitch, I…” Rio choked.

Agatha immediately entered her space, resting a hand on her freshly recovered knee.

“I know you’re right. Arsenal is a good club. It would be a good move for me.”

Us, Rio wanted to say so desperately.

“But I just keep thinking about my mum.” About how I don’t think I’ll survive not being your teammate.

“I understand, sweetheart,” Agatha said.

Rio wiped a tear with her sleeve. “She’d only ever seen us playing in Barcelona jerseys and one of the last things she said to me was that she hoped we’d play for the club forever.”

Agatha nodded, squeezing soothingly.

“I just can’t help thinking she’d hate me for moving.”

Agatha furrowed her eyebrows. “Baby, no. Your mum didn’t have a bad bone in her body; she’d understand. She only ever wanted the best for you.”

Rio bit her lip as Agatha drew her into a hug.

“We don’t have to make any decisions tonight, okay?” Agatha said, running her fingers through her hair.

They settled into a comfortable silence, Rio’s sniffles being the only sound. Oh, how she’d never get tired of this, of having Agatha play with her hair, her scent filling her nose, her warmth cradling her body.

More tears fell.

If Agatha chose to leave and Rio stayed, she’d have to go months without seeing her girlfriend. Rio knew she wouldn’t be able to survive off that, and yet she found herself saying…

“We have to do what’s right for ourselves,” she mumbled into Agatha’s shoulder. “Our careers are bigger than us; they’re what we’ve worked our whole lives for. What’s a few years of being separate? We always have retirement.”

She felt Agatha’s body stiffen, felt her pull away until grey eyes locked onto her.

“Why do I feel like we’ve done this before?”

In retrospect, in a way, they had. Rio had strung a similar line of sentences together before the Euros 25 final.

She shrugged. “Football should always be bigger than us.”

Maybe if she said it aloud, she might actually believe it.

The Tottenham girls hung their heads as Alice shot off to the corner flag. Nothing could stop Agatha’s feet from following her, a grin firmly in place. How they deserved to be ahead again. How they deserved this win.

“Wu-Gulliver got lucky with that one, Fisk gets a hand on it and so very nearly saves it,” the commentator smiled. “Not sure she’ll care about that though.”

When Agatha reached the corner flag Alice was resting on it, her facial expression nonchalant. A tribute to Arsenal and Premier League legend Thierry Henry. A message. The corner flag celebration was just as powerful as Jen’s badge tap.

Agatha smiled.

Her best friends were Arsenal through and through. To play with people who would die for the badge was such a privilege, a reminder that they would give everything for their club and for their sake, so should she. This type of pride reminded her of what it felt like to play for Barcelona.

“The Arsenal away end love that!” The commentator yelled above the noise. “It’s four-three to The Arsenal.”

Only when the photographers stopped snapping away did Jen say:

“Well done babe!”

She drew Alice into a hug the same time Rio patted her on the head.

“Well done, Ali!”

“Thank you,” Alice grinned as the rest of the team surrounded her.

Eighty-third minute.

Tottenham’s number twenty-seven booted the ball into the Arsenal box. Agatha watched it the entire way, leaping into the air when it dropped down to her. She made contact, headed it away, but not far enough.

“Received by Silva outside the box.”

Rio swooped in, poking her foot at the ball to dispossess Giovana. She moved her body into Giovana’s path to prevent her from regaining it, was about to break away when Giovana shoved her and Rio lost her footing.

“This North London Derby is getting feisty once again.”

Giovana had the ball once more and Agatha knew Rio wasn’t going to let that happen. She bodied her out of the way with her shoulder, completely legal, most definitely allowed within the rules of football and yet-

The ref blew her whistle.

“…Erm? Tottenham free kick?”

“Are you fucking kidding me?” Agatha shrieked. “You’re having me on! There’s no way in fucking hell you-”

“Harkness,” the referee warned.

“Can you actually explain yourself though?” Rio snapped. “How is that a foul? I didn’t use my hands which she did last half.”

The referee said nothing, just walked away.

Agatha and Rio rolled their eyes simultaneously. This referee was taking the absolute piss.  

Patel stood over the ball, Jen stood behind the vanishing spray in front of her and Agatha, Rio and Alice stood in the box to make up the wall that would hopefully block the ball's path.

The whistle blared.

“Patel to take it.”

She booted the ball into the box.

“Riddler misses the header. Ball drops to Scott…it’s Angelina Scott to level the score!”

Rio’s head snapped to hers and Agatha actually had to step back. There was a war raging behind her girlfriend’s eyes and Agatha, quite honestly, feared for her life.

“How in the fuck?” Rio bit out.

Agatha shook her head, swirling her index finger in the air. “Another goal from us let’s go.”

Rio nodded, storming out of the box.

Ninety +2.

“Just two more minutes left of injury time and it’s still four all.”

“Arsenal corner.”

Alice stood at the corner arc as Arsenal and Spurs players huddled in the box. Giovana, of course, was an Agatha and Rio’s ass, pushing and shoving them at any given opportunity. If they had more time on their hands, Agatha was sure she would’ve shoved her back. She had to focus on bigger and better things.

“Cross comes in from Wu-Gulliver.”

“Mine!” Agatha shouted as she watched the ball soar through the air.

Pushing off the ground to make contact, she felt a weight dragging her back. Agatha yelped, flinging her arms in an attempt to steady herself. And then-

“Goal! Giovana Silva puts it in the back of her own net!”

Agatha hit the grass with a thud only for cheers and cackles to fill her ears. Giovana stood mortified, her hands over her mouth as she watched the ball bouncing in the goal.

“That is so unfortunate for Tottenham. I was wondering if the ref would allow Arsenal to retake their corner after Silva’s blatant foul on Harkness, but I’m sure they won’t be complaining now.”

“Wait…wait what happened?” Agatha asked as Rio helped her to her feet.

“Public enemy number one just gifted us a goal,” her girlfriend grinned.

Grey eyes locked with brown and Giovana bit her lip. It took Agatha all of three seconds to start cackling. Perhaps it was childish, but she threw in a finger point too.

“You headed the ball into your own net?” Agatha wheezed. “You…you…” she couldn’t finish her sentence.

“Oh, give it a rest,” Giovana muttered.

“No chance in hell,” Agatha snorted. “You are never living this down.”

“Gotta make sure it features in the post-match photo dump,” Rio chimed in.

“Oh, for sure!” Agatha beamed.

Giovana walked off with a scowl and Agatha wiped tears from her eyes.

“She’s won us the match,” Rio said.

And she had.

“Full-time here at the Tottenham Hotspur Stadium. It’s five-four to Arsenal with that own goal from Giovana Silva putting the Gunners ahead.”

A post-match interview. A likely place for Agatha to be considering this was her first match back after her three-match suspension. Valentina insisted and Agatha obliged under one condition…her girlfriend could do it with her.

The WSL Sky Sports presenter smiled at them as they approached her set-up. A camera, the Barclays WSL backdrop and mics.

“Rio, Agatha, lovely to see you.”

The girls nodded.

“Agatha, let’s start with you first. How does it feel to be back on the pitch after so long?”

“Yeah, it feels good,” Agatha began amongst the shuffles of the stands clearing out. “I’ve missed playing with my team and to make my comeback during a North London Derby is special.”

“And this is your first one, does it compare to what you’ve heard?” This was a question for both of them.

Rio took the lead. “Definitely. I think we both felt how passionate the team were about this fixture and we hope we showed up for them today.”

The interviewer grinned. “I think they’ll agree with your goal and assist contributions. How much do you two enjoy being in that nine, ten role together?"

Agatha answered this. “We love playing together and I think you can see that on the pitch. We just want to win, and I think that’s evident with the way we play.”

The interviewer nodded. “And Rio, I think it’s safe to say that your first few weeks at Arsenal didn’t go as smoothly as people expected.”

Agatha felt Rio tense beside her, laced their fingers together to calm her down.

“Would you say you’ve found your footing in the WSL yet?”

Agatha squeezed.

“I would like to think so. Playing at Barca is definitely an entirely different experience and there were things that took me a moment to get my head around, but I’m happy with the way I’ve been playing recently.”

The interviewer’s lips curled. “One last question before I let you go. There were some…questionable decisions made by the referee today. What are your thoughts on that?”

“The referees in the WSL need to do better,” Agatha said without missing a beat. “Some of the ref's decisions were abysmal and set women’s football back to a concerning degree.”

“It’s not okay that they are allowed to get away with this, especially when there is no VAR. I think referees need to make sure they are up for the task of refereeing before they agree to do so, especially during big matches,” Rio chimed in.

They would most likely get fined for chatting shit about the ref, but neither of them cared.

“Thank you, ladies,” the interviewer said.

“Thank you,” they replied in unison, walking towards the tunnels hand in hand.

“I bloody hate interviews,” Agatha muttered.

Rio laughed. “I know you do.”
