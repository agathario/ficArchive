“You know what?” Rio’s voice got high-pitched and stone cold. “Fuck off. Fuck you. You can leave.”

 

Agatha blinked at the bluntness. Usually it was her doing that. “Wait, what?”

 

“I can’t do this.” Rio stepped away from Agatha, further and further until their fingers wouldn’t brush if they’d both extended their arms. “You either need to buck up and take me on a fucking date or leave me alone.”

 

Agatha felt small. And like the world was crashing and burning. “Why are those the only options?”

 

“I’m not a machine!” Rio yelled. And then a defeated, “You said you weren’t going to hurt me and you lied. Lilia was right.”

 

Before Rio could say anything else that made Agatha’s brain feel like it was getting pricked with needle after needle, Agatha made up her mind and switched off the hurt. “Fine. Whatever. Just ripping the bandaid off the inevitable, yeah? You can find that smart girl your age and not even worry about fucking me over first.”

 

Rio’s eyes were red-rimmed. “Thanks for the last word. You can leave now.”

 

“Don’t have to tell me twice, hon.” Agatha made sure the slam the door on her way out. And sorry to Lilia, but she hoped she busted a hinge. 

 

It was two horrible and long and truly awful days later— forty-eight hours spent sitting around her house sleep-deprived and sulking because she’d called out of work since the thought of talking to people made her chest hurt— when Agatha heard a knock on the door. If she practically crashed into her furniture rushing to open it, that was nobody’s business but her own.

 

She was surprised to see it was Lilia, arms crossed. “What in the everloving fuck have you done?”
