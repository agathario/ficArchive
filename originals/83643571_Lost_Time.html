Now she’d practiced this in the mirror. It flowed easily, “Yeah briefly. She came running in to Rio’s room. Seemed nice enough. And she seemed,” Agatha coughed, even after practicing this would never come easy, “happy.”

“Now she’s here I bet Rio’s really upped her game to convince her to stay,” Alice said, “She said she took her to all the hot spots in town, Lukes Dinner, saw a band at The Pit, even went out to the outlook.” 

Agatha’s glass wobbled in her hand, her body flinching at the realisation of what Alice had just said before she had the warning to stop it. The outlook? No that’s fine. That doesn’t hurt at all. Because Agatha’s completely, definitely, slightly, on the way to being over Rio. What doesn’t help is the way her mind jumps to when her and Rio were last there. 

-

Agatha drives up the narrow uneven road, wincing as the car hits another pot hole, one hand loose on the wheel and the other holding onto Rio’s knee, rubbing slow circles absentmindedly on her outer thigh. 

Rio was spiralling. And after the day she just had Agatha was not surprised. The hourly texts had painted enough of a picture for her, increasingly frantic and misspelt, each one was a little more unhinged than the one before as things had gotten from bad to worse. 

It hadn’t helped that Rio had started her day by spilling her newly bought coffee down her white top before even entering the building. She then had to sit with the stain through meetings with her boss and meetings with her sources. Both of which had not yielded the responses she had been expecting. 

By the afternoon the messages had tipped from frustration into something more pained and sharper, leading to one particular text threatening whoever had designed the office printer. It was at that point that Agatha decided it was essential to intervene. 

To save Rio’s colleagues of course, not because seeing her in this much anxiety was quietly eating her up inside. 

She had picked Rio up from work without warning, earning a surprised and cautious look from Rio as she had gotten in the passenger seat. Despite her confusion, and the clear questions hovering behind her eyes, Rio merely turned forward and held them back. She trusted Agatha to take the lead and to know what she needed. She never had to say it herself.

After a moment of silence as Agatha started the car, Rio had begun her ramble, ranting about her boss, her colleagues, the source, the printer, and for reasons that only made sense to her anyone wearing eyeliner. And Agatha had let her for the entire 45 minute drive, offering an occasional hum or dry comment. 

Rio was still in her coffee stained work clothes, mid rant, “It just makes no sense. He tells us we are this close to something huge. Like something that could make front page,” She gestures wildly, “But just won’t tell me what the hell he knows.”

“It’s…”

Rio interrupts before Agatha can try and sympathise, “It’s annoying is what it is. I’ve been chatting to this source for three weeks and every time I think i’m getting somewhere he disappears.”

Out of the corner of her eye Agatha sees Rio tightly grasp her hair in her hand in frustration, “My editors telling me to trust the process. I swear to God if he tells me to be patient one more time I’m going to quit.” A sigh, “I just don’t think he understands that if this goes wrong I’m the one that takes the fall.”

The defeat in Rio’s voice is what makes Agatha turn to her properly. Rio is sat staring at the window, jaw tight and her fingers tapping restlessly on the door. 

“You won’t.” Agatha says certainly. Confidently. 

She takes the last turn, pulling into the small clearing at the top, then she cuts the engine. Agatha squeezes Rio’s leg one more time in reassurance, “Come on,” she turns to reach for the door, “Field trip.”

Agatha steps out of the car, breathing in the space. Behind her Rio follows.

They both meet in front of the cars bonnet, leaning back on it as Rio continues like the conversation was never broken, “He has the nerve to tell me all I need to do is circle back with the source.” Her voice is tighter now, the frustration has coiled in her gut. She has yet to fully turn and take in the view.

Agatha’s eyes are on the village stretched out below them as Rio continues. She can make out the narrow streets, still lit from the warmth of the streetlights. The windows glow, adding to the soft amber of the scene. This always makes whatever Agatha is dealing with feel manageable. Neat and ordered, like nothing could ever be considered overwhelming. 

Beyond the village, the landscape fades into a darker backdrop, swallowing the hills beyond. But above it. Above it the sky is clear, and open scattered with the stars staring back at the lives below. 

It’s the kind of view that usually does something to people, slowing them down and pulling them out of their own heads.

“Circle back to what? There is nothing to turn back to!” 

Agatha glances from the view to Rio.

Yeah. Not working.

Rio pushes herself off the bonnet and starts pacing in front of of Agatha, hand running through her hair. 

“I just feel like I’m missing something, and everyones laughing and in the know. God I sound - ”

“Invested.” Agatha validates.

“Obsessed,” Rio corrects, picking up speed in front of her. “This is how people fuck up. They get too close. They get too personal and stop seeing clearly then suddenly they’re reporting something that doesn’t actually have legs,” She exhales sharply, “I can’t afford that.”

Agatha had now pushed off the car to step closer to Rio’s orbit. Still caught in the spiral Rio doesn’t react, and Agatha doubts she even notices. 

“I just need - I just need it to be like solid.” She holds her hands out in front of her, as though holding something stable. “I need to not be chasing something abstract. Something that disappears as soon as anyone studies it properly. I can’t have built this on something that isn’t real. If it does - I can’t -“ Her voice quietens, faltering for a second, “Thats on me.”

Agatha watches her, really watches her. The pacing, the sharp edge to her voice, the way she runs out of breath, failing to pause. The view might be beautiful and grounding, but that’s not what Rio needs. 

And she’s still going, “I should go back tomorrow, I could - “

Agatha was bored of waiting for Rio to notice the view. She steps in, and kisses her decisively, cutting straight through the sentence mid-breath. A full stop where Rio won’t allow herself one. She swallows Rio’s surprised soft sound. For a second Rio remains tense in her arms, still caught up in the pattern of her thoughts. Then it shatters and she can feel it happen, the moment Rio stops thinking and instead starts responding. 

She follows the pull of Rio’s hands gripping onto her jacket and brings her palms up to Rio’s cheeks. 

Rio pulls away, eyes finally focused on something real. Agatha. Her voice was a little breathless as she asks, “What are you doing?”

Agatha leans in again, lightly brushing her lips agains Rio’s, smirking. “I’m distracting you.”

Rio doesn’t argue as she pulls her closer again reconnecting their mouths. She expects it to turn then, the familiar escalation involving an urgency that usually defines them. But it doesn’t and neither of them rush it.

It deepens instead. Slowly, drawn out lingering lips as they engage. Agatha’s thumb begins tracing patterns along Rio’s jaw as Rio’s hand lowers and rests on her side under her jacket, steady and present. 

Rio walks her slowly backwards to the car so she ends up leaning against the bonnet again, trapped between the car and Rio’s warmth. She tilts her head, just slightly to adjust the angle and Rio follows, her lips exploring rather than hurrying. 

Agatha loses track of how long its been. She becomes aware of how Rio exhales softly against her mouth and how her fingers curl into her shirt, how close they are without it feeling like a fight for power. 

When Agatha pulls back it’s not to end it, just to breathe it. She allows their lips to brush, unable to pull back completely. And Rio doesn’t rush to fill the silence like usual, instead she leans into it. She leans into the choice.

Rio pulls her in again, softly, lingering on it like she chooses to be in this moment as their lips meet. 

Shifting her hand from Rio’s jaw to below her ear, she could feel Rio’s pulse beneath her fingers, steady and grounding. 

And it feels solid. 

It throws Agatha off balance. This doesn’t feel like a distraction anymore, it feels like something else. 

She doesn’t like it. Or maybe she does. 

She pulls back, Rio’s lips trying to follow hers. There is enough space between the two for Agatha to look at her properly. Her expression is soft. Her eyes searching, not hungry and wild like Agatha is accustomed to, but open, and vulnerable. 

Rio bridges the space, not with her mouth but her forehead, resting on Agatha’s. “You’re a good distraction,” she murmurs. 

In an attempt to diffuse the situation, to deflect, Agatha jokes, “one of my many talents.” voice quieter than intended. 

The hand resting on Agatha’s side curl slightly into the fabric of her shirt, like she’s holding onto something she hasn’t figured out how to name, something precious. 

After a pause she carefully whispers into the night, “Agatha, what are we doing?”

Agatha feels it a moment too late, this shift from safe ground, to something else. Driven by her instinct to reframe and dodge, she lightly pushes Rio’s shoulders away, unable to move to create distance herself. She needed the space, the barrier. Immediately following Agatha’s instruction Rio took a step back, not far enough to be out of her orbit. Because Rio always would. She would do anything Agatha asked. 

The absence of Rio’s figure froze the air around her, she had to cross her arms over her chest in restraint to avoid reaching out for her warmth again.

“What do you mean?” Agatha asks, too casually, too innocently. 

Rio watches her knowingly, “this. Us.” She gestures between them, brushing Agatha gently.

Forcing a small unconvincing laugh, “We’re friends.” Rio raises her eyebrows at that. 

Sighing Agatha continues, “Okay friends…who enjoy sleeping together. Don’t overcomplicate it.” She rushes through the last sentence, the words coming slightly to easily. She is good at this, making things smaller than they are. 

Rio’s expression falters, subtle, yet Agatha see’s the hesitation that it is. That brown glint in her eyes dropping.

She carries on anyway, barreling through it because if she stopped now it would mean something else that she doesn’t want to dissect. “It works doesn't it? Why put pressure on it? Just this, us as we are.”

As we are. Very safe. 

Agatha’s eyes are darting between the view and Rio, unable to land on anything, the contrast of the beauty in the view and the pain she can’t pretend to unseen on Rio’s face. In front of her Rio was glancing to the floor, before she looked up, something muted in her expression, more guarded. 

“Yeah, yeah, right. Casual.”

The word falls in the air but Agatha nods nonetheless. Like that’s exactly what she wants. 

“Exactly.”

Rio lets out a breath, a sound similar to a laugh but it fails to tell the rest of her face. She steps back further, outside of Agatha’s reach, spinning around. Agatha tells herself that the sinking feeling in her chest is relief, ignoring the way that Rio is no longer looking at her. Ignores the way in which Rio’s softness has been replaced by something Agatha can no longer read. She ignores the persistent nagging thought in the back of her mind telling her that wasn’t what Rio was asking for.

She leans back against the car, lets her gaze drift over the village lights like nothing has changed. 

Beside her, Rio is finally looking at the view. 

After a moment of silence Rio murmurs, “Holy shit it’s gorgeous up here.”  

-

She hasn’t been back since. Part of her knows why. She’s afraid if she was to go back, she would start analysing the night differently. That, now she was older and wiser, she would finally read Rio’s expression. That she would finally see what wasn’t very well hidden in the first place. And she is certain that if she allows herself to see it properly, really see it, it would break her heart ten times over again. 

Jen, following on from Alice, “Well I’m not surprised she’s pulling out all the stops. I found her instagram, she’s hot. If anything, I’ll be trying to convince her to stay.”

Nudging Jens shoulder Alice putting on a half hearted glare that doesn’t quite hide her smile, “Hey!”

The both laugh easy and unbothered. But Agatha doesn’t. 

No Agatha goes very still, fingers turning white around her glass. The noise of the rest of the bar carries on but, to Agatha, it feels distant. She hadn’t prepared for these comments, the casual admiration, hadn’t thought about the fact her friends are probably going to like her. 

Before she steadies herself, before she can mask her expression, the door to the bar opens and all four women look up instinctively. The conversation between them staling for a moment.  

Into the bar walks Rio with a pace too fast for someone who is technically still meant to be recovering from her accident. Agatha could see the faint stiffness in her shoulder from across the room.

Nat came in behind her, laughing at something Rio was saying, hand brushing Rio’s lower back with a confidence it could only have from consistency.

Agatha’s grip tightens without thought. There was no way she could reinterpret or soften the edges of this, it was real and it was happening now. And for a brief second, she feels like she is back on that hillside, under the stars, standing just a fraction too far away, watching something she didn’t think she could lose, settle into someone else’s orbit. 

Rio spots the group quickly from across the room and makes her way over.

“Look who’s vertical.” Jen was sitting up straighter now as she greeted the newcomers. 

Sliding into the booth and pulling the hand of Nat to slide in next to her, Rio groaned, “Can we please not make this my identity.”

“Too late,” Jen said, “I had a look at your discharge notes - i’m impressed.”

Rio laughed, then turned to the table more seriously, “Right. Formalities. Okay you lot obviously know each other so that’s efficient,” she jokes before flourishing to Nat dramatically, “Nat, here is the coven of emotional damage that I have been subjected to for a decade.”

“Hey Nat! Jen” Jen raises her hand in acknowledgment, smile wide and welcoming. 

Alice between Jen and Agatha matches Jens warm smile, “Alice.”

“Nice to meet you guys.” 

“Lilia, charmed dear,” 

The introductions move smoothly, Nat finding the rhythm of the group easily. 

Rio gestures to Agatha opposite the table, with a quieter voice, “and Agatha.”

She didn’t really need to elaborate beyond that. 

She finally looks up to meet Nat’s eyes, “Hey we met briefly”

Smiling, Nat nods at her, “yeah, good to see you again. It’s nice to meet you all properly, I’ve heard…some things” she teases lightly. 

Rio clapped her hands once, cutting through the moment before it could settle. “Right guys, drinks? Drinks? I’m injured so I’m ordering irresponsibly.”

“Overridden.” Jen immediately ordered.

“Party pooper, you’re technically not my nurse.” Rio said with a mock frown across her face. She stands up, wincing slightly which Jen merely pointedly watches, and heads to the bar, Nat following behind. 

Upon Rio’s return to the table conversation had begun to continue. Rio had one arm draped loosely along Nat’s chair, leaning into her space with no performance. And Nat had a hand resting on Rio’s thigh, like it simply decided to stay there. It looked easier than anything had ever been between Rio and herself. 

Alice was in the middle of a story about a child who had been conducting a science experiments on the teachers, analysing their behaviours and marking them on it, when the door to the bar flew open. 

A gust of cold air entered the room, as did a flustered Wanda. 

“Hey. Hey, sorry I’m late!” Her breath was uneven, hair loosely pulled back, spilling out of a ponytail in a way that suggested a frantic day. “I swear I left on time, but one of the parents called me back to start a philosophical debate about their child and glitter glue.”

Alice laughed, “Ah the one who tried to argue that it was a cognitive development tool last week?”

Pointing at her directly, “Yes. That one. He sure is developing, developing quite the attention seeking streak.”

Lilia leaned back in her chair smiling fondly, “Did you survive?”

“Barely.” Wanda threw her bag down next to the chair between Nat and Agatha, and Agatha relished in the barrier it provided. She exhaled to the table, like she had been holding her breath since lunchtime. “Oh and also Tommy is now convinced that he can open doors with his mind power now, and Billy has decided he is offended by the idea of socks.”

Rio raises her hand straight up, deadpan expression as she states, “I am in full support of Billy.”

Agatha rolled her eyes, “yeah of course you would be.” She muttered, earning her a squinted glare from Rio across the table. 

Finally looking around the table, Wanda’s eyes land on Nat’s unfamiliar face. Her body paused at last, slowing down now she was settled and had assessed the situation. 

“Oh, hi”

Nat turned, smirk already composed, “Hey.”

There was an immediate switch as Wanda entered into her warm, school teacher, social butterfly mode, “I’m Wanda, you must be Nat.”

Offering Wanda a polite smile and quick nod, “Alice you mentioned you work at the same school - what age do you teach?”

“Yeah we do. I teach grade 3, which is basically controlled chaos.” Wanda pauses, eyes flicking up as she reconsiders, “actually I’m giving myself too much credit there - it’s just chaos. No control.”

Chuckles echo out of the group.

Rio leans forward, elbows perched on the table in front of her as she makes eye contact with Wanda, “respect.”

Wanda laughs, “You know, you would not survive five minutes in my classroom.” She told her directly, laced with a smile. 

Rio had the decency to look offended, “I survived getting hit by a car. I’m basically invincible.”

“It is not the same skill set.” Jen cut in with immediately. 

“Similar enough,” Rio insisted, eyes wide in fake outrage. 

Agatha, who had been quiet through the majority of this conversation, attention focused on her drink, finally participated. It came out flat, “Neither of those are skills one is luck.” She gestured to Wanda with the hand holding her glass, “The other is poor judgment.” She turns to look directly at Rio. 

Rio furrowed her brow in annoyance, and leant back in disbelief, “Why do we keep you around?”

“Well it’s against my will.”

Nat’s eyes flickered briefly to Agatha then back to Rio, as the others rolled their eyes, used to their teasing now. 

Wanda now turned her attention to Nat, friendly curiosity encouraging the conversation forward, “So Nat, how long are you in Westview?”

“Couple of weeks,” Nat answered, nodding her head slightly, “managed to get a little work break so thought I’d come and see Rio.”

Rio’s arm had made its way back to the back of Nat’s chair, she pulled Nat in to a jokey hug as she added proudly, “She came all the way here because she missed me.”

“I came because you were hit by a car.” Nat shot back. Everyone laughed at that, despite the fact they all knew it wasn’t the case. 

“How romantic,” Lilia exaggerated. 

And whilst Agatha tells herself, its just conversation, harmless and exactly what’s supposed to happen when someone new gets folded into a group like theres - Nat fits. 

She fits easily. Like she has always been here. She knows when to laugh, picking up on Lilia’s dry comments without missing a beat, answering Wanda’s questions with just enough detail to be interesting without dominating. Even Jen, who usually likes to initiate people with a level of bullying some might not consider appropriate, seems won over.

Throughout the conversation she watches how Nat leans in when someones talking, attentive naturally. Her genuine laugh and the way she brushes her shoulder against Rio’s without thinking. 

It’s objectively good.

Because this is what she wanted originally, for Rio to be with someone who meets her where she is. Someone easy. Someone open. Someone who doesn’t turn everything into a negotiation or deflection. Someone she doesn’t have to bend for. Someone she doesn’t have to work for. 

Someone who stays.

It’s nice that its all so easy. Because ease was never something she and Rio managed to figure out. Not like this. 

It’s nice. 

And its annoying her more than she wants to admit. 

“Okay wait,” Jen says, pinning towards Rio and Nat, “We’ve heard Rio’s versions of Peru, but I want the proper version.”

Nat didn’t hesitate before speaking, “I was there for a photography project. Documenting biodiversity in protected regions of South America.”

It was almost disarming the ease of which she answered with.

Alice’s eyes widen, “That sounds, actually very cool.”

Nat nodded, “It was.” She confirmed with no embellishment but just fact.

Rio grinned, “And I was there because of this story about illegal logging, but mainly trying to not get murdered by the mosquitoes the size of small birds.”

Nat’s hand rested gently on Rio’s laying on the table, “that is a bit of an exaggeration.” She corrects with a squeeze. 

Pouting, “See! This is what she does, ruins all my dramatic stories with boring facts.”

Whilst Agatha failed to comment, her mouth twitched before she could control it. 

“We ran into each near a research station. I was there to interview locals and she was there to take photos. We kept pretending we weren’t working around each other.”

Nat picked off right from where Rio left it, “She kept asking me questions about animal tracking, that she already clearly knew the answers to because she kept correcting me.”

Rio was just looking at Nat now, smiling wide, “I had to test you.”

“You were flirting.” Alice infers immediately and knowingly.

Tearing her gaze from Nat, Rio raises her eyebrows and looks at Alice with defensiveness, “I was not!”

“Oh you absolutely were.” Wanda’s comment made her the victim of Rio’s glare. 

Nat didn’t disagree just watched with amusement like she had already won this argument months ago. 

Rio gestured over the table, “Anyyyywayyy, we kept bumping into each other in different places.”

“We then thought it would be easier to just share the transport between the sites.”

Jen smiled at the pair, “Thats actually kind of sweet.”

“It was logistics,” Rio clarified quickly, unbelievingly.

“Fate.” Said Lilia. 

“Unreliable infrastructure,” Agatha, ever the pessimist chimed in. 

“All three can be true.” Nat smiling at the group. Her comment settled the group into a moment of quiet, or reflection and Agatha noticed it more than she wanted. 

Because it wasn’t just the story but it was the familiarity in which they told it. They way Rio had leaned towards Nat, the way Nat had adjusted subtly towards Rio when she spoke, like the space was already familiar enough to stop measuring. 

Rio breaks the silence, softer now, looking at Nat, “We didn’t actually you know plan anything. It just kept happening. Bumping into each other.”

“So when did it become.. you know.” Jen was watching the couple now with fondness. 

Rio shrugged slightly, “Define ‘you know’”

Jen gestured vaguely towards them, “The relationship. Thing. Girlfriend.”  

Agatha held back a wince at those words. Still afraid of them. Still bitter they never got to be used. 

Nat answered this time, “Eventually, we just stopped leaving it all undefined.”

The words land lightly and Agatha thinks thats the worst part about it. Nat puts no weight on them, just a simple word like the most natural conclusion.

The undefined is all she knows. The almost, the something that never quite becomes anything out loud. Her and Rio had set up camp there and refused to move when the blizzard came. They circled it and filled it with everything but the one thing that may have made it real. Things that meant something, until they pretended they didn’t. Conversations that delicately danced closer to honesty before one of them would stop the music.

The undefined had never been an accident. 

It had always been a choice. 

Agatha maintains her neutral expression, allowing the conversation to continue. Her grip tightens on the glass, thumb pressing into it before she forces a breath and her hand to loosen. 

We just stopped leaving it all undefined. 

Nat had said it like it had been simple.

She looks up hesitantly, eyes landing on Rio, who is looking at Nat. The gaze isn’t intense, it is just steady and comfortable. There is none of the questioning tone anymore, no visible restraint she is holding to avoid scaring her off and tipping into something too real. It is just something named.

Rio leans back, rubbing the back of her neck like the transparency is uncomfortable, “She followed me to a story in Chile and I think that was the moment it stopped being accidental.”

Agatha looked down from Rio again.

Jen, obvious to Agatha, let out a soft whistle, “wow, big moment.”

Wanda smiled, “I like this sappy version of Rio. The one who accidentally falls into romance whilst chasing dangerous stories around the world - its like a romance book.”

Rio laughs before visibly dramatically shaking, like the emotions were too much for her body to handle and she wants to move the conversation along, causing the others to laugh.

Alice, doing Rio a favour, asks lightly, “So you liking Westview? The plan is to move here right?”

There’s a slight pause, it’s brief enough that most people wouldn’t notice, and judging by Agatha’s glance around the table, no one else does. 

Nat tilts her head, quickly looking at Rio, “Maybe.” She says, “We’ve spoken about it.”

The table didn’t react beyond a few understanding nods. But Agatha was looking at Rio.

Her posture shifted, slightly, subtly, enough that made it invisible unless you knew her well. Something in her stilled, like she was waiting for the rest. Like she knew how this conversation went. 

Wanda, ever the gentle one, smiled to Nat, “yeah it must be hard, with your work. You both have to travel so much.”

Looking somewhat relieved she was being understood, Nat replies, “Yeah, its a part of it, we need to figure it out.”

Rio leaned forward, a little too quickly, “But I’m not travelling as much.” She said factually but lightly. Like the meaning was obvious. 

Turning towards her Nat simply said, “I know.”

And there it was again, that pause. 

It wasn’t disagreement per say. But not agreement either. Just a space between words. 

Agatha watched Rio. Her eyes trained on her. The way her knee bounced slightly under the table. The way her hand hovered near her glass. The way her attention flickered to Nat after every answer, checking where she stood. 

“I like travelling,” Nat said after a moment, as if continuing a thought she hadn’t finished, “I think id go a bit stir crazy if I had to stay here in one place.”

Rio’s shoulders had dropped a fraction, loosing the looseness she had maintained since she had entered the bar. Something dulled, like someone had turned the brightness down on her. 

She was shrinking in small, invisible increments. Not dramatically or even noticeably to others. You couldn’t label it but she was less jokey, less loose and less certain. 

Agatha watched as she glanced down to her hands to the table where they curl slightly as though she is grounding herself. Agatha watched her swallow something she didn’t say. Agatha watches her try again, softer now, treading more carefully into the discussion, “We can make it work.”

Nat was looking at her now, but not seeing what Agatha saw in plain sight. “I know. We can try.”

Try 

Rio nodded, fast as though it was something Nat could take back. Then, for the first time since they’d arrived, Rio didn’t interrupt the next moment. 

The conversation moves on, driven by Alice asking about Nat’s photography, but once Agatha had seen it she couldn’t move past. 

That flicker in Rio as she takes a slow sip of her drink, gaze steady and expression unreadable. 

Agatha takes a drink, she is trying, really trying to let it go. To accept the situation as it stands and stop reading into things that are no longer hers.

The point is Rio is happy and that is what Agatha told herself matters. But watching her now, the way she adjusts and accommodates, she notices, with an uncomfortably clarity, how someone who usually fills a room is suddenly trying to make herself smaller inside it. 

And how Nat, calm, and perfect and steady as she was, didn’t seem to realise she was doing it.
