In, out.

In.

 

Out.

Murderous rage, unfortunately, turns out to be a really good look on Agatha Harkness.

Rio blanches. Absolutely fucking not. Over her dead body. The truck might as well come back and hit her again instead of this. She tries to pull away, but Agatha’s hold on her wrist doesn’t falter.

Rio thinks she might die here, with Agatha leaning over her body to buckle the seatbelt, with the soft, dark waves of Agatha’s shiny, perfect hair brushing across her chest with every minute movement, with the scent of jasmine and amber and something woody wafting through the air, tempting Rio to lean forward and breathe deeper. 

 

It’s pure torture. 

 

It’s utter bliss. 

 

The buckle clips into place with a crisp click, and Agatha leans away.

“You can thank me by texting your boss,” Agatha replies, and then she’s shutting the door and making her way over to the driver’s side of the car. 

 

Rio sighs, allowing herself to sulk for approximately three seconds before she pulls open her bag and reaches for her phone to do exactly that.

Billy, appearing supremely unnerved by the vagueness of Rio’s sudden proclamation, sighs heavily through his nose.

 

“You’re going to tell my mom what, exactly?”

 

“That I like Agatha. And that I’m going to ask her out. Maybe she’ll have some advice for me,” Rio reasons, thinking back to her conversation with Billy on Thanksgiving. He’d made a great point. Once upon a time, Wanda had unexpectedly found love with someone almost a decade older than herself. There’s no way she’ll judge Rio for falling onto the same path. Right? Right.

 

“WHAT?! You want to—you what?!”

 

Billy’s trembling fingers find purchase along the edges of his half-eaten plate. It looks like he’s considering frisbeeing the entire thing at her head.

 

“I really like her, okay? We had lunch the other day and all I could thinking about was how badly I wanted to kiss her. I want to go back to her house and thank those fucking azaleas for looking like a good hiding spot that night. I want to bake cookies with her and not be all useless and bandaged next time. I want—” Rio falters a little at Billy’s wide-eyed, open-mouthed stare. 

 

“Her,” Rio finishes weakly. Then, firmer: “I want Agatha Harkness.”

 

“Okay. Well that, we already know, genius. It’s kind of obvious to anyone with working eyes. But telling my mom? She’s Agatha’s friend and she sucks at keeping secrets, bro. You should know this! Remember that one time she visited us on campus and accidentally spilled the beans about my crush… To my crush?”

 

Ooh. Good point. There’s been a strict moratorium on Wanda dropping by the school ever since that fateful day.

 

“Where is this even coming from?” Billy lets go of his plate in favor of picking up his fork again and jabbing it in her direction. “And don’t think I forgot about the part where the two of you had lunch together and you didn’t say anything to me and Alice about it until now.”

 

Rio blanches. Billy’s gaze turns shrewd. Knowing.

 

“… You had another hot sex dream, huh?”

 

Damn the entire freaky Maximoff bloodline.

 

Alice shuffles into the kitchen, rolling her shoulders and stretching her arms. She stifles a yawn with the back of her hand before speaking.

 

“Who had another hot sex dream?”

 

Damn the Wu-Gullivers too.

 

 

Work, at least, manages to provide Rio with ample distraction from her evil, horny thoughts.

 

Lena swings by to drop something off for Kara, who—rather predictably—reacts to her girlfriend’s presence like someone’s injected pure serotonin into her veins. Rio, while enjoying the starry-eyed and stuttering Kara Danvers show, decides to put a pin in rearranging the pastry display in favor of making Lena a mocha latte. She’s just starting to pour steamed milk over the espresso when the sound of her name pulls her attention away.

 

“Rio, do you think Professor Harkness would be open to writing letters of recommendation for her students?”

 

The tiny milk pitcher in Rio’s hand almost goes flying. Almost.

 

“Agatha?”

 

So much for working being a decent distraction. Lena blinks guilelessly back at her.

 

“Yeah. Kara said that the two of you are friends, so I figured you’d be able to tell me if asking for something like a recommendation for an internship would annoy her or not. I’d prefer to stay on her good side, y’know?” 

 

Kara shoots Rio the kind of pleading look that should be considered a serious threat to global security.

 

“Um, yeah,” Rio replies, thinking back to Agatha’s words from one of her earliest visits. She knows—from her own impression of the other girl as well as the way Kara constantly sings her praises—that Lena’s no slouch, academically or otherwise. “I think she’d be open to writing a recommendation for you. No harm in asking, right?”

 

Lena beams at her with a little more gratitude than Rio thinks she deserves for such a casual, noncommittal answer. Rio makes a mental note to prod Agatha into talking about her students more. She’s pretty sure that her assumption is correct, all things considered, but it wouldn’t hurt to be sure.

 

 

Rio gets the chance to see Agatha again towards the end of the week, spotting her hurrying across one of the smaller lawns on campus with nigh-inhuman grace, considering the ridiculous height of her impractical heels. There’s an uncharacteristic frown marring her features, a strain that Rio immediately feels pulled to address.

 

“Agatha, hey,” she calls out only after glancing around to make sure that they’re the only ones in earshot. A solid number of professors at the school allow students to address them casually, but Agatha’s not one of them and Rio would prefer not to advertise the fact that they’re on a first name basis if she can help it. “Everything okay?”

 

Agatha, who slowed to a stop as soon as she’d heard Rio’s voice and turned to see her approaching, shakes her head as Rio finishes closing the distance between them.

 

“It’s complicated. My firm needs me back in the city and the matter’s urgent enough that I doubt they’ll want me commuting back to Westview in the meantime. You didn’t get to meet him before, but I actually have a rabbit,” Agatha explains, and Rio’s jaw drops. A rabbit? The woman that’s got entire lecture halls fearing her name has a pet rabbit? Amazing. If she wasn’t half in love with Agatha already, Rio totally would be now. “He’s a little shy, so he usually stays in his playroom when I have guests over. I’ll have to ask Wanda to check on him—”

 

“I can do it.” Wait. Pause. What the hell did Rio just say? Did she just volunteer to hang out with Agatha’s rabbit? (Whoa there. Not like that. Brain, leave it alone.) With the exception of looking after her brother once they were both reasonably old enough to be left alone, Rio’s never been the nurturing kind. Besides, at least Nico had been human and therefore capable of speech. How’s a bunny supposed to tell her if anything’s wrong? The sheer, unfettered relief shining in Agatha’s eyes, however, keeps Rio from trying to walk back her hasty words. She tries for a convincing nod; the gesture more for herself than Agatha at this point. “What’s his name?”

 

Agatha’s cheeks turn a pretty, delicate shade of pink.

 

“Señor Scratchy.”

 

Rio actually ends up having to bend over as she laughs.

 

 

Rio follows Agatha home from campus in her own car in order to make their introductions right away.

 

“You’re welcome to hang here around as much as you like,” Agatha says as she presses a set of spare keys into the palm of Rio’s hand before hurrying down the same familiar hall towards her bedroom. Rio trails behind her, nodding whenever Agatha looks back. “I don’t want you to feel like you can only visit to check on Scratchy and that you have to leave as soon as you see he’s alright. If you wanna watch a movie, do your assignments, take a nap, eat, whatever, the house is yours. You can stay over as much or as little as you’d like. Either of the guest bedrooms are open to you and there are fresh sheets and towels in the linen closet at the end of the hall. Anything in the fridge or pantry is fair game too, okay? Seriously. You’re honestly doing me a huge favor. Please make yourself at home while you’re at it.”

 

Free rein to just exist in Agatha’s space for however long it takes her firm to put out whatever fire popped up in her absence? And she gets to befriend a cute rabbit? Oh, Rio’s so diabolically down for this.

 

Within ten minutes, Rio’s decently acquainted with the fuzzy, tri-colored apple of Agatha’s eye. Señor Scratchy, in an adorable display of devotion, even hops out of his well-appointed room and journeys over to the foyer as soon as he hears the sound of Agatha preparing to leave. Naturally, Rio follows.

 

“Be good for Rio,” Agatha murmurs to the bunny as she crouches down to scoop him up and nuzzles her chin against his fur. Rio’s pretty sure her dumb heart actually starts melting as she watches this happen. Señor Scratchy’s ears twitch contentedly as Agatha cuddles him close before setting him back down onto the floor. “I’ll see you soon.”

 

 

“U-Haul,” Alice and Billy eerily intone as one over the group FaceTime call that Rio had started in order to let them know what was going on. What are they, the annoying gay version of the twins from The Shining?

 

Rio scowls and slouches on the couch, disgruntled.

 

“I’d do the same thing for any one of you,” Rio insists. “It’s called being a good friend. You guys should try it sometime.”

 

Alice gives her the finger before speaking while Billy sticks out his tongue.

 

“Whatever, you simp. Are you even coming home tonight or did you want one of us to bring over some of your clothes and shit?”

 

Rio looks sideways towards her furry companion, who’d simply stared at her until she’d caved to his superior willpower and lifted him onto the plush cushion beside her.

 

“Um. Please pack all my favorite pajamas. You know the ones. And also some regular clothes for school.”

 

Billy sniffs dramatically.

 

“So this is what parenthood feels like.” His voice turns choked. “And you’re already leaving the nest.”

 

“Billiam freakin’ Baxiboff,” Rio booms. Señor Scratchy doesn’t so much as twitch at the rising volume. “I swear to God, I’m going to beat—”

 

Billy waves a silencing hand.

 

“Alright, alright. There’s no need for major threats of violence. I’ll head over as soon as Alice finishes going through all your things.”

 

An evil, chilling smile pulls at the corners of Alice’s mouth.

 

“Yeah, Rio. He’ll drive right over there as soon as I finish going through all your things.”

 

Then they hang up before Rio can cuss either of them out. 

 

Scratchy wiggles closer and starts snuffling curiously at her bare arm.

 

Great.

 

At least they’re off to a promising start.

 

 

On Sunday night, Rio wakes up to the strange sensation of the book balanced page-down on her stomach beginning to slide sideways.

 

She clumsily fumbles for it on instinct, sleepy and slow. A tiny, vague noise of distress issues itself from her throat as Rio paws at the cover. Her efforts are useless—the weight of the book vanishes, though no telling thud of it hitting the floor follows.

 

“It’s okay,” comes a voice that does a better job of jolting Rio awake than a bucket of ice water. “It’s just me.”

 

Rio’s eyes snap open to the welcome sight of Agatha standing in front of the coffee table, textbook in hand.

 

“You’re back.”

 

“Yeah. Things wrapped up pretty late but I couldn’t stand the thought of being stuck in the city overnight so I decided to make the drive. What are you doing out on the couch?”

 

Rio tilts her chin towards the book in Agatha’s hands.

 

“Assigned reading. I was hoping not being in an actual bed would help me stay awake.”

 

“A sound plan—in theory.” Agatha flashes her a sharply teasing grin. Rio’s stomach does a line of somersaults. Action, reaction. It’s practically Pavlovian at this point. “How’s Scratchy been behaving for you?”

 

“He’s been the perfect gentleman,” Rio assures her. Truthfully, the bunny’s a pretty chill guy. He loves being held, occasionally butts his head against the side of Rio’s foot or leg when he wants more food or attention, and keeps to his playroom more often than not despite having full run of the place, just as Agatha had said he would. All in all, Rio’s way more of a pet person now than she was a few days ago. She sits up, stretching slightly and rolling her neck as her joints protest the sudden movement. “Have you eaten anything?”

 

There go the cutely familiar crinkles in the corners of Agatha’s eyes as her smile turns fond. Rio doesn’t know what she wants to kiss more—the wrinkles, or Agatha’s mouth.

 

“I had a salad for lunch. You’re sweet for asking.”

 

Rio shakes her head.

 

“Just opportunistic. There’s some takeout in your fridge from earlier, let me heat you up a plate.”

 

Agatha’s free hand catches Rio around the wrist as she stands. Her grip is light, but to Rio it may as well be an adamantine net. She freezes in place.

 

“You don’t have to do that.” Agatha’s thumb sweeps soothingly across her skin. “I didn’t mean to wake you up.”

 

It takes several seconds for Rio to remember the entire English language.

 

“I want to, though. Let me?”

 

After a moment’s consideration, Agatha’s fingers fall away. Rio’s woman enough to admit, at least in the privacy of her own head, that she instantly misses the touch. (Also, this is probably definitely going to haunt her dreams.)

 

“Okay. Thank you, Rio.” Agatha’s gaze roves searchingly over the relaxed planes of Rio’s face. Whatever she finds makes the fine lines around her eyes deepen further as the already upturned corners of her lips twitch higher. “Will you eat with me?”

 

“Yeah,” Rio replies, easy as breathing. Was there ever any other answer?
