“I know.”

“I got everything I needed, Rio listen, I remembered everything-“

“Did you think I wouldn’t see you kissing her?”

Agatha blinks at her in disbelief.

“I didn’t-“ she shakes her head “she kissed me. How did you even-“

Rio feels her stomach lurching at the memory.

“-I don’t know, Agatha, it sure seemed like you were into it to me.”

Agatha’s eyes fill with tears. It’s a sight that before today would’ve made her feel nauseous and force her into action, taking Agatha’s face in her hands and wiping them gently. Now she just feels satisfied.

“How can you say that?” She chokes out. “I don’t want her! You know I don’t!”

Why is she lying so adamantly? Wanda had been talking about marrying her again. Agatha had clearly gone back to her. She saw it with her own eyes.

“Okay.” She says dryly.

“Rio, I came straight back here to tell you that I-“

Agatha’s lip wobbles. She looks terrified. Rio knows her better than she knows anyone - or at least she thought she did - and she’s never seen her look so vulnerable before.

“You what?” Rio spits back.

Agatha’s face steels, eyes brimming with tears, and she looks like she’s mustering everything inside of her to say what she does next.

“I love you.”

The numbness inside of Rio dissipates. In its place - blind, hot rage and devastation. Tears fall freely as she chokes out a sob that sounds a little like a laugh. Rio stands from the bed and runs her hands through her hair, tilting her head back to face the ceiling.

“Oh, you’re a sick fucking asshole you know that?” You don’t know what love is, Agatha. I’m not even sure you’re capable of it.”

Agatha backs up a step as though what Rio says actually physically wounds her.

“Rio-“ her voice comes out so, so small. “-I don’t understand.”

“Get the fuck out of my house!” She screams like a wounded animal. “I don’t ever want to see you again!”

Agatha’s face crumples and a tiny part of Rio, way, way deep in her wounded heart, wants to apologize. Wants to reach for her and tell her it’s okay. That she can be with Wanda and she’ll suck it up if that’s what she wants to do. That she can destroy Rio as much as she wants and Rio will just let her.

The part of her that would do anything for Agatha, even endure unimaginable pain.

Agatha turns and races down the stairs. Rio hears the front door slam and she’s alone again. Without Agatha, just like she was last year.

But now she knows what it would’ve been like to have her and it’s so, so much worse.

“Not talking seems to be the basis for every fight you’ve had with Rio so forgive me, but you’re not getting away with that this time.”
