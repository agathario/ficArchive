Striding through the front doors of the hospital, Agatha makes a beeline for the front desk, shaking off the slight chill from outside and being careful not to jostle the gift bags in her hands too badly, even though nothing in them is fragile. She smiles politely at the bespectacled woman sitting behind the desk. “Hi there, I’m here for Wanda Maximoff. Could you point me in the right direction?”

The woman taps a few times on her keyboard and then smiles up at her. “Those are some very popular babies this morning. Let me just get you a visitor’s badge and then you can head on up.” 

“Great, thank you.” She takes the sticker badge that the woman holds out to her, peeling it off the backing and slapping it on the bottom of her shirt. “Elevators?”

“Right over that way,” the woman says, gesturing off to the side of the lobby.

“Wonderful.” She flashes the woman one more smile and then moves quickly across the floor toward the bank of elevators. 

Her nerves have been amped up to an eleven ever since Vision called her many hours earlier to let her know that Wanda had gone into labor. She’s known for a while that Wanda only wanted her husband in the delivery room with her, something Agatha wasn’t going to argue with her about, so she’d willingly, albeit anxiously, waited at home curled up on the couch with Scratchy to help keep her as calm as possible. And she wasn’t even the one going through the labor itself. But she remembers the story Wanda’s mother told about her own pregnancy and labor and how there were a few complications during delivery that thankfully had no long-lasting effects. It’s all she could think about while waiting for the second call to announce a safe delivery of both babies.

She’d even completely ignored a call from her property manager about one of her tenants, which she knows she might regret later if it’s something serious, but she couldn’t be bothered to care about that. Not when her mind was so focused on her best friend and whatever pain she was enduring because of labor and then giving birth to twins. 

Stepping off the elevator onto the correct floor, she turns and heads down the hallway in the direction of the room Vision had relayed to her over the phone mere minutes ago to announce that both boys had been born perfectly happy and healthy. She’d been relieved beyond belief and definitely broke the speed limit on the way over. 

She comes to an abrupt stop just outside the room, brows furrowing slightly at the lone figure standing in front of her, two small gift bags clutched in her hands. “Rio?”

The woman in question spins toward her and gives a half smile. “Hey.”

Agatha moves closer slightly. “What are you doing out here?”

“Psyching myself up to meet the twins.”

Agatha snorts. “And how’s that going for you?”

Rio sighs heavily. “Not well. I love kids, want a couple of my own one day, but I’m still nervous as hell about meeting these babies specifically.”

“I know what you mean,” Agatha tells her honestly. “It might surprise you, but I’m not exactly a baby person. Yet I know that meeting these babies is going to be one of the most important moments of my life that I’m going to remember for a very long time and that they’ll probably ask me about one day. I want to be able to have something positive to tell them about it.”

The space grows quiet around them, save for the usual ambient noise of a maternity floor and the faint conversation coming from the other side of the door. The last thing Agatha wants to do is disturb whatever sweet family moment she knows is happening inside the room, but she’s also beyond eager to meet her nephews. She’ll never get another moment exactly like this one.

“I suppose we should go in, huh?” Rio finally speaks up after a long moment. 

Agatha nods. “Probably.” She glances at the other woman. “Do you want to go in together?”

Rio’s eyes go wide ever so slightly. “Really?”

Agatha closes and opens her mouth a few times. “Yeah. We were both planning on it anyway, right?”

“Right, yeah. Thanks.”

“Sure.” Agatha turns back toward the door and takes a deep breath, mentally preparing herself for the inevitability of meeting her best friend’s kids. This is the moment she’s been waiting almost nine months for. She didn’t even carry or give birth to them; she shouldn’t be this antsy. Right? 

Pushing through the door, Agatha steps inside with Rio following closely behind her, though her eyes are only fixed on the tired redhead sitting up in the bed at the other end of the room, a clear hospital-issued bassinet on one side of the bed. Vision is standing by the head of the bed, one twin cradled in his arms while the other is in Wanda’s, both boys wrapped up tightly in matching hospital-issued blankets and blue knit caps on their heads.

“I hope we’re not interrupting,” she practically whispers, inching closer across the floor, setting the gift bags down on her way; one for each boy filled with a couple of outfits, some toys, a few board books, and a brand new stuffed animal.

Wanda looks up and smiles brightly despite how tired she looks. “Hey, no, you’re not interrupting at all. We’ve actually been waiting for you to get here. Hey, Rio.”

“Hi.”

Agatha moves closer, heading straight for her best friend and pulling her into a side hug. She presses a lingering kiss to her hair. “I’m so proud of you, Wanda. So incredibly proud. Pietro and your parents would be too.”

“Thanks, Agatha. I know they would be. Pietro would probably already be trying to make them his little sidekicks,” she says with a quiet laugh. 

Agatha smiles. “I don’t doubt it.”

“You wanna meet your nephews now?”

“Absolutely.” She pulls back slightly and shifts her attention to the slumbering newborn in Wanda’s arms. “Well hello,” she whispers, already feeling the tears prickling at the back of her eyes. She reaches out to run a gentle finger down the side of the boy’s face. “And what’s your name?”

Wanda adjusts her hold on the baby just slightly. “This is Billy. Viz has Tommy.”

Agatha glances at the other baby and smiles. “Hi, Tommy. And hello, Billy,” she says, shifting her attention back to the baby in Wanda’s arms. “It’s so nice to finally meet you both. We’re gonna have a lot of fun together, I can promise you that.”

“Do you want to hold him?”

Agatha freezes, gaze shifting to her friend. “A-are you sure?”

“Of course. Sit next to me and I’ll pass him to you.” She shifts over a little on the bed and then pats the empty space. “I promise he’s not gonna bite.”

Agatha hesitates but then sits next to her friend, flexing her hands and then nodding. “Okay. Give me the kid.” She listens intently to Wanda’s instructions on how to hold Billy as he’s passed into her arms and she adjusts her hand placement accordingly. She sits ramrod straight, her entire body scared to move. The very last thing she needs is to drop such a precious being.

A gentle hand runs down her back. “You know you can lean back if you want to,” Wanda tells her, amusement clear in her voice. 

Agatha shakes her head. “Uh, no, I’m…I’m good right here. There’s less risk of dropping him if I don’t move even an inch.”

Wanda chuckles quietly. “Okay.” She leans closer and tugs gently on the blanket around her son’s face. “This is your Aunt Agatha, Billy,” she whispers. “She’s gonna be the one you go to when you want to find ways to drive me and your dad crazy.”

Agatha can’t help but laugh. “Oh, trust me, toots, I’ve got years of ammo ready and waiting to be used.”

“I’m sure you do.”

Chancing a glance, she turns her head to look at the other twin, eyes going wide when she finds him already nestled in Rio’s arms. The other woman is rocking gently from side to side, bouncing Tommy slightly. “H-how do you look so calm right now?”

Rio smiles at her and shrugs. “I told you I love kids. Plus, I babysat a lot in high school.”

“Of course you did.” She focuses her attention back on the baby in her arms, grateful that he’s still completely fast asleep and not making a sound. She’s perfectly fine with holding him while he’s like this, but the second he starts actually doing anything, she’s handing him right back. At least, while he’s still so small and fragile. Once he’s a little bit older and sturdier, she’ll have no problem holding onto him for a while.

“This is actually perfect that you’re both here right now,” Wanda says, breaking the silence, “because there’s something Viz and I want to ask you.”

“What is it?” Rio asks.

“Well, Agatha,” Wanda says with a small smile, “we’d like for you to be Billy’s godmother.”

Agatha’s eyes go wide. “What? You…what?”

“And Rio,” Vision continues, “we’d like for you to be Tommy’s godmother.”

Rio lets out a breath. “Whoa. ¿En serio?”

Agatha blinks, brows furrowing slightly. “Hang on, you speak Spanish?”

Rio glances at Vision briefly, then back to her. “Uh, yeah. All my life. Wait, did you seriously not know that? I’m not exactly shy about using it.”

“Honestly, I tend to tune you out a lot of the time. It’s nothing against you, though.”

Rio raises a brow. “Really? Because it kinda feels like it is.”

“Enough, both of you,” Wanda cuts in. “Listen, we know it’s a lot to ask, but Viz and I think you’re both more than up for the task. You’re our best friends, and we know you’re going to be around for our boys as often as possible.”

Agatha smiles down at baby Billy, her heart swelling in a way she’s not sure it ever has before. “I suppose I could take this one under my wing. He is pretty perfect already.” She looks up at her friend again, nodding. “Okay, I’ll do it.”

“So will I,” Rio says. “I’m about to be this kid’s favorite person.”

Agatha drops her gaze back to Billy one more time, finally shifting on the bed and leaning back, letting out a breath as she holds the baby closer to her. The tiniest of cracks forms in her heart in that moment, as she stares down at the sweet, innocent face of her best friend’s baby. She knows in an instant that she’s going to spend the rest of her life doing anything for this kid, and his brother, with zero hesitation. 

Well, except for…

“I hope you know I’m not changing any diapers,” she tells her best friend, her eyes never once leaving the beautiful baby in her arms. “Anything else, I will absolutely help you out with, but I draw the line at changing diapers.”

Wanda laughs. “Yeah, I figured as much. That’s what I have an amazing husband for. And we’ve already found someone to come over a few days a week to help me out once Viz goes back to work.”

Agatha looks up at her. “Oh, you did? Already? Who’d you end up picking?”

“Her name is America. She’s a sweet girl, graduated high school in June and is currently enrolled part-time at Westview Community. She’s very excited to help us out.”

“Well, that’s good. Only the absolute best for my godson. Isn’t that right, sweet boy?” 

“We should get a picture of you two,” Wanda tells her. “And of Rio and Tommy.”

“Maybe one of the four of them together,” Vision suggests. “I’ll grab my camera.”

Agatha shifts slightly, glancing up at the other woman holding Tommy. “Uh, should I come to you or are you gonna come here?”

“Might be easier if you come over here so we’re not both crowding Wanda’s bed.”

“Oh, hang on, don’t move just yet,” Vision says, wandering back over with his camera. “Agatha, let me get one of you and Billy first.” 

A little bit more confidence coursing through her, Agatha carefully adjusts her hold on Billy so he can be seen better and then smiles brightly. “I want a copy of that, by the way. Can’t have you two hogging all the memories.”

“Of course.” Vision turns toward his friend. “Rio, Tommy, you’re up.” He snaps a quick photo of the pair and then looks over his shoulder. “Alright, Agatha, get on over here.”

Taking a deep breath, Agatha stands from the bed and carefully shuffles over to where Rio is still standing, her hold on Billy tightening ever so slightly. She stops next to the other woman and lets out a breath of relief when she glances down to find Billy still asleep. She looks up at Vision. “Okay, better take it before something happens.”

Wanda rolls her eyes. “Stop it. Nothing’s going to happen.”

“It’s me, Wanda, something always happens. Not necessarily to me, but something always goes wrong.”

Rio has barely stepped foot into her apartment when her phone goes off in her pocket. She pushes the door closed with her foot and digs her phone out with one hand, carefully balancing everything else in the other. She smiles at the name on the screen (accompanied by an old photo of Vision from their college days that he begrudgingly lets her keep) and immediately answers the FaceTime call. 

She holds her phone up as the video connects, a very familiar sweet face filling up the entire screen. “Hey, buddy,” she says to the wide-awake infant. “Te extraño mucho. I hope you’re being good for your mom and dad.”

The camera turns, showing Vision’s face deeply frowning. “He’s a menace already at only a week old. Billy’s a bit more…docile, but Tommy is certainly already showing a propensity for chaos.”

Rio laughs. “See, that’s exactly what I like to hear. A little chaos never hurt anyone.”

Vision’s face falls. “Yes, it absolutely has. And you wouldn’t be so enthusiastic about it if you were the one having to take care of them all day, every day.”

“Then it’s a good thing I’ll never have to,” Rio quips. “You know I love those boys, but I need to fully prepare myself before becoming a parent.”

Vision sighs. “You can prepare yourself all you like, Rio, but when it eventually happens for you, you’ll still feel completely underprepared.”

Rio frowns slightly. “Everything okay? Do you two need anything?”

Vision shakes his head. “No, thank you. We’re…we’re good. It’s just been a lot, trying to navigate this new routine. And well, trying to settle into a new routine, actually. You think it’d be easier with two of us and two of them, but it’s actually quite a bit tougher.”

“I’m sorry.”

“Don’t be. I love being a dad,” he says with a small smile. “And I know once I get through all the exhaustion and wanting to pull my hair out, I’ll find that pure satisfaction everyone’s always talking about.”

“Well, I hope you find it sooner rather than later.”

He lets out a heavy breath, smile still in place. “As do I.”

Stepping up to the front of the Maximoff household, Agatha carefully balances the small stack of Tupperware in one hand and knocks on the door with the other. The faint sound of crying drifts through the wood and she smiles to herself, proud of her decision. Wanda may not have said as much to her over the phone, but she knows her best friend well enough by now to be able to read between the lines.

“It’s open!” the redhead in question shouts from inside the house. 

Twisting the knob, Agatha pushes the door open and steps inside, immediately finding her best friend sitting on the couch in her pajamas with her hair halfway up and one twin attached to an exposed boob. “You know, it’s probably smart to actually keep that thing locked.”

“Agatha,” Wanda breathes out, eyes slightly wide in surprise. “What are you doing here?”

“It’s Thanksgiving, dear,” she reminds her best friend. “And in case you’ve forgotten amidst the chaos of new motherhood, we haven’t missed a single one.”

Wanda glances down at the nursing baby, over to where Vision is bouncing the other twin in the kitchen, then back to Agatha. “Uh, sorry, Agatha, but that’s kind of the furthest thing from my mind right now.”

Agatha waves her off. “Of course it is, I wasn’t expecting you to cook some gourmet meal today. Besides, you know that’s not exactly our style.” She chooses that moment to present the stack of food she brought along, adding a little flourish to the presentation. “One mismatched Thanksgiving-ish meal, at your disposal.”

Wanda suddenly looks as if she could cry. “I love you.”

Agatha smiles softly. “I love you too. And to make the meal even better, I brought some disposable plates and utensils so you won’t have to do a single dish after.”

“Agatha, I could kiss you.”

“Ooh, promises, promises.” She laughs quietly and steps further into the house, leaning down to press a gentle kiss to her best friend’s forehead as she passes. She smiles down at the baby blinking innocently back up at her. “Hello, Tommy.”

Footsteps approach softly. “Agatha? What are you doing here?”

Agatha smiles up at the man, gaze immediately drifting down to the baby in his arms. “I brought you Thanksgiving. Now give me my godson and go start dishing us up some plates, would you?”

Wanda sighs. “Agatha…”

She looks back at her friend. “What? I said you wouldn’t have to worry about anything. Never said your husband wouldn’t.” She sets everything down on the nearest surface and then steps forward to carefully pull Billy into her arms, bouncing him slightly. “Hello, sweet boy.”

“I suppose I’ll just take these into the kitchen, then,” Vision mumbles, picking up the dishes and food and carrying them away.

“You know, you’ve gotten better at holding him,” Wanda remarks.

Agatha looks at her and smiles. “He’s a little bit bigger so I’m not quite as worried about breaking him now.”

“Well, for what it’s worth, it’s a good look on you.”

Agatha rolls her eyes. “Don’t start. Not today.”

Wanda holds up a hand. “Fine. I’ll save it for tomorrow. Right now, let’s enjoy our mismatched Thanksgiving-ish meal, and you can tell me all about what it’s like on the outside.”

Agatha laughs, gently patting Billy’s back when the baby starts to fuss. “Sounds like a plan.”

“Merry Christmas!” Agatha greets the small family as she makes her way into the house, two large gift bags in her hands and a grin on her face. She stops short in the doorway at the sight of the unexpected fourth figure. Her eyes narrow slightly. “What are you doing here?”

Rio laughs. “Merry Christmas to you too, Agatha.”

Agatha rolls her eyes and moves further into the house. “Don’t you have your own family you can bother today?”

“No, actually, I don't. They’re all dead.”

Agatha freezes in place, eyes wide. She spins to face the other woman, quickly searching her face for any sign of joking. But then she quickly realizes that’s not something people normally joke about. “Ah,” she says instead. “Well, join the club.” She sets the bags down by the tree and begins pulling out the various wrapped gifts and setting them out. “I didn’t get you anything, by the way,” she calls over her shoulder, “I had no idea you were here.”

Agatha turns around to face Rio. “Yes?”

“How can you afford all of those gifts? You don’t work.”

Rio frowns. “On our date you said you didn’t work.”

Agatha shakes her head. “Not exactly. You just assumed I didn’t and wouldn’t let me explain. I work, just not in the conventional sense.” She immediately crosses the room to take Billy into her arms and bounce him gently. She smiles down at the baby. “Your Auntie Agatha has been collecting rent from multiple properties for a very long time. That’s why she doesn’t have to have an office job.”

Mostly houses, but a few office-type spaces as well. And I have a wonderful property manager who saves me from a lot of headaches.”

Wanda laughs. “Oh, yes, we did indeed.” She takes a deep breath. “Now, once Viz and Tommy come downstairs, I think we should get the holiday truly started. It’s the boys’ first Christmas, I want everything to be perfect.”

Agatha smiles at her friend, continuing to gently bounce Billy. “It already is. You’ve got your husband and your boys. And me.” Her eyes flick over to Rio. “And I guess her too.”

Rio scoffs. “Gee, thanks.”

“This is the first of many Christmases for your boys, Wanda. And each one is going to be better than the last.”

“Where is she?” Agatha growls. “I was told she was here. So where is she?”

“Miss Harkness.”

Agatha whirls around to face a man with tight grey curls donning pale blue scrubs. “Are you the one who called me?”

The man shakes his head. “I’m not. But I’m the one who worked on your friend, my name is Jack Abbot. Please, come with me and I’ll take you to her.”

She wordlessly follows the man through a set of swinging double doors and down a long hallway. The usual hospital smells and sounds curl around her and she tries desperately to shake them off; she hates hospitals.

It doesn’t take too much longer, and then she’s led into a room where her best friend supposedly lies, fighting for her life. She’s not entirely sure if she’s ready for what she’ll find inside, but she knows that she has to, she needs to be there when Wanda wakes up. Or the opposite. But she really hopes the latter isn’t the case.

Her breath catches in her throat upon the sight she’s met with as soon as she crosses the threshold: Wanda lying motionless in a drab hospital bed, the gray blanket pulled up tightly under her arms, multiple bandages wrapped around different places on her arms, a larger one wrapped around her head, and various cuts and bruises littering her pale skin. It’s almost enough to make her lose what very little stomach contents she has left over from last night. This isn’t right. It’s so far from okay.

“W-what exactly…what happened?” she finds herself asking, her voice barely above a whisper. Whether it’s out of fear of disturbing what she hopes is just her friend sleeping to recover or worry that her voice won’t be steady if she speaks louder, she isn’t sure.

Doctor Abbot steps into her eye line. “From my understanding, she and her husband were rear-ended. Their car went off the road and rolled multiple times. Her husband was treated by a different team, but I handled Wanda’s care myself. As you can clearly see, she has multiple bruises and lacerations to her face and arms, as well as some on her legs. She also had some major internal bleeding from multiple broken ribs, which we were able to manage, but we did lose her a few times while we were operating. Additionally, there was some swelling in her brain, which we were able to relieve, but she is still very much in critical condition. The next twenty-four hours are crucial for her. If she makes it through, she has a very good chance of a slow but full recovery. But the journey after that won’t be easy by any means.”

Agatha swallows thickly, fighting against the tears that want to fall. “Do you know anything about Vision?” 

“I’m afraid I don’t. But I can speak to the doctor who treated him if you’d like.”

Agatha nods. “Please.”

Doctor Abbot nods, turning toward the door. “I’ll give you some time with her. Press the button behind the bed if you need anything.”

Agatha waits for the click of the door closing and then slowly makes her way across the floor, her heels echoing against the linoleum. An unsteady breath escapes past her lips as she gets to the bed, her hand shaking as she reaches out to gently graze her fingertips across her best friend’s hand.

“You’re gonna get through this, hun,” she tells her friend, praying the redhead can hear her in her injured state. “You have to pull through, okay? Your boys need you. And we both know I’m not gonna do well if you don’t make it through this. I’m a mess. But somehow you still find a way to be my friend. I already don’t have too many of those, I can’t afford to lose one now.”

She settles into the chair beside the bed, leaning forward and curling her hand around Wanda’s, continuing to fight back tears with each breath she takes. “I’m gonna be right here waiting for you to wake up, okay? You’ve got this, Wands, you’re so strong and brave. You’re gonna get through this. You’re gonna see your boys, and you’ll get to hug them and never let them go.”

Her breath catches at the reminder and she immediately leans back to whip out her phone. If Wanda and Vision were out without the boys, then there are only a small handful of people they would have left the twins with. She knows for a fact she didn’t have them, and she vaguely recalls an off-handed comment about Rio being busy this week, so that leaves only a couple of others who could have the boys.

Under normal circumstances, she wouldn’t have America’s number saved in her phone, but Wanda had all but begged her to when the twins were barely a week old as a precaution in case something happened and the young girl had all the answers. Agatha hates that it’s coming true now. 

She hits the button to call the teenager and then leans back further, running a hand through her hair as her eyes drift back to her friend. 

“Agatha?”

She lets out a breath. “America, hi. Sorry for calling so late.”

“It’s fine. I’m waiting for Wanda and Vision to get back anyway. Actually, do you…Do you know where they are? They said they’d be back by now, and they usually call if they’re gonna be late. I’ve tried calling both of them but neither was going through.”

Agatha swallows thickly. “They uh, they won’t be coming back tonight. They were in an accident, I’m at the hospital with them now.”

America gasps. “Oh no. Are they okay?”

“I’m not entirely sure. Wanda’s alive, but in critical condition. I haven’t heard about Vision yet. I…I can probably come and get the boys once I know everything’s okay here, but are you okay to stay a little longer until I know for sure? I can pay you for the extra time.”

“Don’t worry about it,” America says. “They pay me a flat fee, not by the hour, so I’m not worried about staying longer than usual.”

“Okay. Thank you. I’ll call you when I know more. Are the boys okay?”

“They’re fine, they’re sleeping. They went down without any trouble.”

Agatha lets out a breath. “Good, okay. I’m gonna go, but I’ll talk to you when I can.”

“Of course. Bye, Agatha.”

“Bye.” She ends the call and drops her phone into her lap, her eyes drifting back up to her friend. She smiles with tears in her eyes. “Try not to worry about the boys too much, okay? I’ll keep an eye on them until you’re out of here. And then I’ll be there every day while you’re recovering to help you out.” She smooths a hand over the blanket. “Everything's gonna be fine, Wands, everything’s gonna be just fine.”

But then reality comes crashing back down as her eyes focus on her best friend’s unmoving body. The pit in her stomach that had already been forming grows bigger and heavier, unrelenting dread setting in.

She sits up quickly, her eyes darting around the room, first to the monitor behind the bed and then to her friend’s face. “Wanda?” Her heart stutters in her chest. “Wanda!” She doesn’t bother with the button behind the bed and instead hurries toward the door. “I need somebody in here now!”

Multiple figures rush past her into the room, circling Wanda’s bed in a carefully controlled chaos. They’re all speaking quickly to each other, medical words and phrases that go straight over Agatha’s head; she only knows they’re important. Various tools and instruments are being passed around and either pressed against or into Wanda’s body at different places, piercing through her best friend’s typically flawless skin. It’s almost enough to make her vomit.

Several long, agonizing minutes pass while she stands in the doorway hoping and waiting for the pit in her stomach to shrink so she can sit by her friend’s side again. So she can hold her hand and tell her stories about their years of friendship to help her through the rest of this terrible time. So she can assure her over and over again that her boys are going to have all the support they need while their parents recover enough from their injuries to be able to take over. 

More minutes pass and the knot in Agatha’s gut grows more twisted the longer she doesn’t know what’s happening to her best friend. Her throat is tight, her eyes stinging, and a heavy weight is settling itself on her shoulders. Not knowing might just be the thing that destroys her. 

“Miss Harkness?”

She jolts upright, pushing back her hair that had fallen into her face. “What is it? Is she okay? Is she…is everything fine?”

The nurse in front of her frowns. “I’m so sorry.”

Agatha’s heart sinks, the weight on her increasing tenfold, her throat getting tighter. “No.”

“We did everything we could, but it seems her injuries were simply too much for her body to handle recovering from.”

Agatha wipes violently at her eyes. “No, you’re wrong. She’s not…she can’t be–”

“Agatha?”

She whirls around only to come face-to-face with the very last person she wants to see right now. Her eyes narrow at the other woman. “What are you doing here?”

Rio takes a hesitant step closer. “I’m here for Viz. I would have been here sooner, but I was buried in research at home and my phone was on silent. They said he was in an accident?” 

Agatha nods. “He and Wa–Wanda.” Her heart clenches at the unfair reminder of what just happened. “Car accident. Wanda just…she’s uh…she didn’t…”

Rio lets out a heavy breath. “Oh. Do you know if Viz is…”

Agatha shakes her head. “Wanda’s doctor didn’t work on him. Last I heard he was doing okay.”

“Okay, that’s good. I’m gonna see if I can find someone who worked on him to find out if he’s still doing okay. If the boys can’t grow up with their mother, then hopefully they’ll still have a father to look after them.”

Agatha nods silently, gesturing over her shoulder. “I’m gonna…”

“Oh, yeah, no, go. Go be with her. Do you know if the boys were in the car?”

“America was watching them tonight. I already called her, she said they’re sleeping. She doesn’t mind staying with them a little longer until one of us can get there.”

Rio nods. “Okay. That’s good.” She starts to walk away and then turns back, flashing a small, sad smile. “We’ll get through this, Agatha, okay? Whatever else happens.” And then she’s gone in search of a doctor.

Taking a shuddering breath in, Agatha steps back into the room where her best friend lies unmoving and forces herself to go to the bedside again. There are still a few tubes and wires attached to her body, but they’re no longer attached to the monitors near her. Evidence of the work that had been done to try and stabilize Wanda still lies scattered across the bed and her friend’s body. 

The worst part of it all, there’s no more steady beeping. It’s pure and utter silence. And it’s aggravating as hell.

Agatha chokes on a sob as she brushes a hand over Wanda’s head, pushing her hair back. “I…” She swallows thickly. “Your boys won’t forget you, okay? I promise. When they’re old enough, I’m gonna tell them about all the crazy stuff we did in college, and I promise I’m gonna be the best aunt. I’ll be around so much they get sick of me.” She smiles sadly, cupping her friend’s paling cheek in her hand. “I’m gonna miss you, Wanda. So much. But I’ll do my best to keep your memory alive for your boys. They’ll know how much you loved them. I promise.”

Leaning down, she presses a lingering kiss to her best friend’s forehead, grateful that there’s still some warmth there. She doesn’t want to leave, doesn’t want to walk away. She knows she needs to eventually, but she just can’t bring herself to do it. She never thought she’d have to. Not for a very, very long time. 

This isn’t how things were supposed to go for them. Not by a long shot. And now she doesn’t know what to do. She’s lost. Completely and utterly lost.

Rio is numb. That’s really the best way she can phrase it. She’s been numb since the moment she saw her best friend lying motionless in that damn hospital bed, the remnants of the doctors’ efforts to save him still surrounding his body. It’s haunting. It’s going to be the feature of her nightmares for a long time, she’s sure of that. She hates it. 

Stumbling slightly through the hallway, her legs unsteady given the news she’s just had dropped on her, she doesn’t even have a destination in mind. She just needs to be far away from that room. She can’t handle it right now.

“Ms. Vidal?”

Rio turns at the unfamiliar voice, brows furrowing when she spots a cop approaching her. “Yes?”

The cop offers her a small smile. “I’m Officer Woo. Do you mind if I speak with you for a moment?”

Rio swallows thickly. “Uh, now’s not really a good time. My friend just died, so I’m kind of…still processing that.”

“I understand. But it’s important that I—”

“Hey! She said she’s not up for it.” Agatha appears next to her, fuming, tear tracks visible through her makeup.

Officer Woo holds up his hands. “Ladies, I understand that this is a difficult time, but it’s—”

Agatha scoffs. “Difficult? Difficult? My best friend just died, leaving her children without a mother.”

Rio’s throat tightens. “Vision’s gone too.”

Agatha looks at her, eyes softening for a fraction of a moment before her fury turns back on the cop. “Both of our friends have died, they’re not even in the ground yet! And you think it’s an appropriate time to come and talk to us?”

“Actually, they are why I’m here. I was assigned to their case and—”

Rio’s entire body perks up. “Case?” A pit forms in her stomach. “Do…Are you saying this wasn’t an accident?”

Officer Woo shakes his head. “Not necessarily. It’s simply the standard protocol for situations like these. I’m trying to find out exactly what happened so I know how best to proceed. Do either of you know if they have any other family we would need to contact?”

Rio shakes her head. “No. Vision grew up in the foster care system, never got adopted.”

“Wanda’s family all passed away years ago,” Agatha says. “We’re their family. And their boys, of course. Billy and Tommy. They just turned one.”

Officer Woo nods. “Yes, we’re aware of their children. DCF is going to take them for the night while we figure out next steps.”

Rio frowns. “DCF? Can’t…can’t one of us take them? We’re their godmothers. Well, I’m Tommy’s, she’s Billy’s.”

“I understand your concern and wanting to take care of your friend’s children, but it’s procedure in situations like these for DCF to look after them until we can determine next steps.”

“That’s ridiculous,” Agatha growls out. “They lost their parents; they should be with people who love them and are going to actually look after them.”

“Miss Harkness, I can assure you that the boys are going to be very well taken care of.”

“Well, can we at least see them?”

“I’m afraid that's something you’ll have to talk to DCF about. They’re very strict about these kinds of things for the safety of the children in their care.”

Rio nods, stepping forward slightly before Agatha can go off on the man again. The last thing they need is to draw a crowd. “Thank you, Officer Woo. We’ll be sure to do that.”

“Of course. And I am sorry to hear about your friends.”

Rio throws the man a tight smile, her eyes fixed on him as he walks away. Once he’s out of view, she turns to Agatha, the other woman practically vibrating with anger. “It’s…it’s probably better that they’re with DCF right now anyway.”

Agatha glares daggers at her. “How can you even say that?”

“Can you honestly tell me that you’d be able to give the boys your full attention right now? That you wouldn’t break down randomly when you remembered that Wanda and Vision are dead?”

“That’s not fair.”

“None of this is. But it’s what's happening. So let’s call DCF to see if they’ll let us see the twins, and then we’ll figure out what to do from there.”

Agatha stares her down. “You’re too calm right now.”

Rio shrugs. “I’m still processing. Do you want to call or should I?”

Agatha’s already digging her phone out. “I can do it.” She frowns at her screen. 

“What is it?”

She shakes her head. “Nothing. Just America letting me know that she’s locked up the house, so neither of us needs to worry about it.”

“At least there’s that.”

“Yeah.”

The silence grows around them as Agatha fiddles with her phone, most likely finding the number for DCF. Rio could have just told her if she’d asked, but now is certainly not the time to bring that up. Something tells her that Agatha is one thread away from fully snapping. And she wants to avoid that. For as long as possible. Preferably forever.

Rio shifts on her feet and moves closer. “Did you find it?”

“I think so.” She taps on the phone screen and then holds it out between them. “Let’s hope this works.”

“Hello, thank you for calling the Westview Department of Children and Families, how can I help you?”

Rio breathes out a sigh of relief that it’s an actual person and not some automated response. “Hi, this is Rio Vidal, and I have Agatha Harkness with me too. We’re the godmothers for Billy and Tommy Maximoff and were told that they’re currently in your custody. We were hoping we could see them tonight.”

“Miss Vidal, we appreciate you calling, but unfortunately, it is late, and our facility isn’t open for visitors right now.”

“Not even for just five minutes?” Agatha asks. 

Just one glance tells Rio that Agatha’s hackles are rising again. “We just want to know they’re doing okay, see them for ourselves. We just lost their parents.”

“I am sorry for their, and your, loss, but if I bend the rules for you, I’ll have to bend them for someone else, and then what’s the point of even having rules?”

Rio sighs, pushing a hand through her hair. “No, that’s…we get it. When can we see them, though?”

“Our facility officially opens at eight am, so if you call back sometime after that, we can arrange a meeting for you and the boys.”

“Okay. Thank you so much.”

“Of course. Is there anything else I can help you with?”

“No,” Agatha says firmly and then ends the call, shoving her phone back into her pocket. She scoffs, turning away. “She has some nerve asking if she can help us with anything else as if she’d helped us at all before.”

Rio frowns. “She’s just doing her job, Agatha.”

“Well, it’s a pretty terrible job, if you ask me.”

Rio raises a brow. “So you’d rather she didn’t look after and care for orphaned and abandoned children?”

Agatha rolls her eyes. “When you say it like that, it makes me sound heartless.”

Rio sighs. “That wasn’t my intention. But there’s nothing else we can do tonight, okay? We’ll call them back in the morning so we can go see the boys, and then we’ll figure out what to do next.”

Agatha nods. “Right. I just…they’re gone, Rio. My best friend is gone.”

Rio’s heart clenches involuntarily at the utter devastation that paints Agatha’s face. She’s never seen her like this before and she almost doesn’t know how to handle it. She’s so much better with plants than people.

She hesitates, then steps forward to rest a gentle hand on Agatha’s upper arm. She doesn’t even get a chance to try and get a word out before Agatha is collapsing against her, one of the most gut-wrenching sobs Rio’s ever heard coming from the other woman. 

She moves quickly to wrap her arms around Agatha, pulling her as close as she can get her, closing her eyes against the crying being muffled by her shirt. This is certainly a first for them, and she only hopes that she’s not going to screw this up, because now she knows with a certainty that she and Agatha are going to be seeing a lot of each other over the next seventeen years as Billy and Tommy grow up. There’s no way they aren’t going to be around for the boys now that they no longer have their parents. Whatever happens, or whoever they end up with, Rio is going to do everything she can to be a consistent figure in their lives.

Several long moments later, Agatha shifts, so Rio lets her go. But just enough that she can lean back in and continue to cry if she needs to. She doesn’t want her feeling trapped, but she also doesn’t want her to think Rio doesn’t care. Because she does. Because while Rio has lost her best friend, Agatha has as well. And she may not know all the details of their friendship, but she has a sneaking suspicion that it’s going to affect Agatha a lot harder than she’d ever expect it to.

Agatha takes a deep breath and steps back a bit, wiping at her eyes. “Sorry about that.”

Rio shakes her head, finally dropping her arms back to her sides. “It’s fine. It seemed like you needed that.” 

“I think it just hit me all at once. So, I suppose I’ll see you in the morning, then? To call DCF?”

Rio nods. “Uh, yeah. I can…come by your place? Or we can meet at…Wanda and Vision’s place?”

Agatha winces, but it’s subtle. “Um, why don’t you come by mine? I’m only a few houses down from theirs. To the right when you’re facing the house.” 

“Okay.” She hesitates for only a moment. “Are…are you okay to drive?”

Agatha nods, sniffling. “I’ll be fine. I’ve driven in worse conditions.”

Rio frowns. “That’s not a good thing, Agatha. You’re clearly in too emotional of a state to drive. Where are your keys?” 

Agatha’s face screws up. “Too emotional? Of course I’m emotional, Rio! My best friend just died and she’s not coming back. Sorry I’m not some emotionless robot like you.”

Rio fights back the urge to snap, to argue, to egg on the potential brawl. Now is not the time for any of that, neither of them are exactly thinking clearly. She takes a deep breath to steady herself. “I’m sorry, that was a poor choice of words. I just meant that there’s a chance you’ll start crying again while driving, and that’s dangerous.”

“And you’re saying you won’t start crying while driving?”

“Yes.”

Agatha eyes her up and down for a moment, still clearly irritated. Then she digs into her jacket pocket, pulls out her keys, and tosses them at Rio. “Fine. Let’s go.” Agatha flips her hair over her shoulder and turns to walk out of the hospital.

Rio takes a deep breath and then follows after her, jogging quickly to catch up so they’re walking side by side. Her hands curl and uncurl a few times at her sides until she eventually shoves them into the pockets of her jacket so she doesn’t do something crazy like try to grab Agatha’s hand. They certainly aren’t there with their acquaintanceship. Despite the number of times they’ve been around each other, she’s never felt like she could call Agatha a friend and actually mean it.

Once they step out of the hospital, the cool evening breeze brushing past them, they stop just outside the door. There’s a soft whooshing as the glass doors slide closed behind them.

Rio rocks back on her heels. “So…you’re that way?” she asks, gesturing with her head toward the parking lot. 

“Yeah.” 

She gestures out with one arm. “Lead the way.” And again, she follows dutifully behind Agatha to the other woman’s car, eager to get her home so that Rio can get home herself. As soon as she figures out how she’s going to get home. She would take an Uber if her phone wasn’t moments away from being completely dead. She could probably walk, since she knows exactly how far Vision and Wanda’s place is from hers, which means that Agatha’s isn’t too far either. 

“Do I need to give you directions?” Agatha asks as soon as they’re both in the car, doors closed tightly behind them. 

Rio shakes her head, slipping the key into the ignition and then reaching for the seatbelt. “No. I’m good.”

“Okay.” Agatha leans back in her seat and turns her head to stare out the window. 

Rio takes a deep breath and starts the car, shifting the gear into reverse and slowly backing out of the space. She almost flips the radio on, like she does when she’s driving her own car, but thinks better of it right as her hand twitches against the steering wheel. She has no idea if that’ll agitate Agatha more and she’d rather not risk it. So the entire drive is spent in complete silence. 

By the time Rio pulls into Agatha’s driveway, she isn’t doing much better than she was at the hospital. The silence had allowed her mind the chance to wander, something she tries to avoid most of the time. In this instance, she couldn’t stop her mind from imagining all the ways this whole situation could have been prevented, and all the things that are drastically going to change now that their friends are gone. 

It almost might be too much change.

Shutting off the engine, she turns slightly in her seat to face Agatha, the other woman still staring out the passenger side window. “Uh, we’re here.”

Agatha jolts, blinking, and then looks over at her. It’s clear she’s been crying again. “Oh. Um, thanks.” She glances around and then frowns. “I didn’t think about how you’re getting home. Your car is still back at the hospital, and I just let you drive me when you probably–”

“Agatha, stop,” she says gently. “My car’s in the shop, I took an Uber to the hospital. I’m probably just gonna walk since I don’t think my phone will make it while waiting for another Uber.” She pulls the keys from the ignition and hands them over. “Here. I know it’s probably gonna be difficult, but at least try and get some sleep, okay? I’ll see you in the morning.” 

She’s out of the car quickly, tugging her jacket a little tighter around her as she slowly makes her way down the driveway to head home. It shouldn’t be too long of a walk, but it is November so it’s getting cooler in the evenings now, which means it’s probably going to be an uncomfortable walk home.

“Rio!”

She freezes and turns, finding Agatha standing next to her car, watching her. “Yeah?”

Hesitation passes over Agatha’s face. “You can stay. If you want. It’s late, we’re both exhausted. I have an extra charger you can borrow for your phone.”

“You’re sure?”

Agatha hesitates again and then nods. “Yes. Plus, this way I won’t have to wait for you before calling DCF in the morning.”

Rio nods. “Okay. Thanks.” She makes her way back up the driveway and follows Agatha to the front door of her home. She’s never been inside Agatha’s house in all the time she’s known her. Every time they’ve met up or interacted, it was always out in public or at Wanda and Vision’s place. She’s not entirely sure what to expect.

It almost feels wrong to be entering Agatha’s home. From what she knows of the woman, she hardly ever has people over, claiming that she likes her space and doesn’t like or want people trampling all over her stuff. Rio can’t say she blames her for that one. Rio doesn’t like crowds of people invading her space either, but she doesn’t mind having people over now and then. It helps that she lives with a roommate. She certainly wouldn’t mind such spacious accommodations like Agatha’s house, though.

“You can use a spare room or the couch,” Agatha tells her, pushing the front door closed and walking further inside, kicking off her shoes as she goes. “I don’t really care either way.”

“Uh, the couch is fine. Bathroom?”

“Down the hall, first door on your left. There should be spare toiletries in the cabinet under the sink. I’ll grab you that charger.”

She offers Agatha a tight smile. “Thanks.”

Agatha nods silently and then disappears further into the house, leaving Rio standing alone in the front entryway.

She moves slowly into the living room, taking the opportunity to maybe finally learn more about Agatha by whatever she has lying around or hung up on the walls. Unfortunately, there aren’t any framed family photos lining the walls of the room, only some very expensive-looking pieces of art. There are a few books lying around of varying genres, but nothing that could really give her a peek into the inner workings of Agatha’s mind. It’s frustrating to still not know much about the woman.

The quiet creak of the floorboards pulls her away from her snooping (because really, that’s what it is) and she turns around right as Agatha enters the space again with a phone charger and a small stack of clothes.

“I uh, I realized you might not want to sleep in jeans,” she says, setting the clothes and charger down on the nearest surface. “I hope they fit. I’ll…I’ll see you in the morning, I guess.” She starts to turn before stepping back. “Just…if you wake up at any point to something nuzzling you, that’s just Scratchy. He’s got free rein of the house at night and he’s a cuddler.”

Rio blinks. “Um, okay. Thanks for the heads up.”

“You’re welcome.” She doesn’t say another word, simply turns and disappears further into the house once again.

Rio lets out a breath, takes one more glance around the room, and then moves. She plugs her phone in first, then grabs the clothes and heads toward the bathroom, faint noises drifting down from the upper level of the house. 

She goes through the motions of getting ready for bed almost on autopilot. She doesn’t have any of her usual things with her, so she makes do with what she does have available. The clothes certainly aren’t what she'd normally wear to bed, but again, she’s making do under the current circumstances.

Once she’s done what she needs to do, Rio makes her way back out into the living room, stopping short in the entryway when she finds a familiar ball of fluff sitting on the hardwood. She dips her head. “Señor.”

The bunny’s nose twitches.

“Okay,” Rio says to herself, taking a small step forward. “I’m just gonna…” She slowly creeps around the bunny’s form, eyeing him over her shoulder periodically as she moves toward the couch. A pillow is sitting on the arm and a folded blanket is draped over the back, both of which hadn’t been there before. Agatha must have brought them down while Rio was in the bathroom. 

Sitting on the couch, she lets out a breath and pulls the blanket down over her body as she lies back, adjusting the pillow so she can lie comfortably against it. The heaviness of the last couple of hours is finally hitting her, though not a single tear escapes her eyes. Nothing builds up, though there is that familiar pit still forming in her stomach.

The only things that have been touched are whatever Rio needed to use to make lunch and then dinner. She hasn’t been able to touch or move anything else. Why should she be allowed to?

This isn’t her home. She’s simply taking up residence in it to raise kids that are only hers by an unfortunate accident. And even then, it’s only on paper. She’s not their mother. She could never be their mother. Being their mother means replacing Wanda. And she would never dare to do that. It’s…unthinkable.

She’s startled out of her head by the ringing of her phone. A familiar ringtone, one she’d set years ago as a joke and never changed.

She immediately answers without really looking at the screen, holding the phone to her ear and slumping further against the counter. “Hey, Alice.”

A heavy sigh from the other end of the phone. “Finally, she answers. I’ve been texting and calling since yesterday, I was getting ready to report you as missing. Everything okay?”

Rio swallows thickly. “Uh, no, it’s not.”

“What happened?”

“Viz and Wanda were in a car accident the other day. They didn’t make it.”

Alice gasps. “Oh. Oh no. The boys?”

Rio shakes her head. “They weren’t in the car.”

“Thank goodness. How are you holding up?”

“As well as I can be. I was at the hospital when Vision…when he died. Agatha was there for Wanda. I think she might have been in the room when it happened.”

“Oh geez. How’s she doing?”

“Honestly? I don’t know. She’s cried a few times, like…full-on sobbing, which is completely understandable. But when she’s with the boys, it’s almost like she’s pretending that everything is okay.”

“Maybe that’s how she needs to cope.”

“Maybe. I don’t know how I’d even approach her about it. It’s probably not the right time, though, everything’s still so fresh for both of us.”

“Hey, you know I get it. Do you need anything from me? Are you coming back tonight?”

Rio shifts on her feet. “Actually, I wanted to talk to you about that. Agatha and I…we’ve been made Billy’s and Tommy’s legal guardians now. And their parents really wanted them raised in their own home, so we’re both sort of…moving in.”

“You’re moving in together?”

Rio rolls her eyes. “Not like that. We’re obviously gonna be in different rooms, and it’s not like either of us actually wants to be living in the same space, but it’s for the boys. They should be able to grow up in the house their parents made for them.”

“They absolutely should, you’re right. So I’m guessing you won’t be coming back tonight or anytime soon?”

“No, I won’t be. I’m so sorry to do this to you.”

“Hey, no, don’t apologize. You’re doing a really good thing here, Rio. Those boys are lucky to have you in their corner.”

“Thanks. And if you have a few minutes, could you bring me some stuff? Just some pajamas and a change of clothes. And some stuff from my bathroom. I can come by and grab more stuff tomorrow, then figure out what to do with the rest of it later.”

“Yeah, of course. Do you have a preference on clothing?”

“Not really.”

“Okay. I'll be over as soon as I can.”

She smiles into the phone despite how tired she feels. “Thanks, Alice, I really appreciate this.”

“You know I’d do anything for you.”

“I know. And I apologize ahead of time if I’m not as available as I used to be.”

Alice laughs. “Girl, you were never as available as I wanted you to be because of your job. So I think I’ll be okay on that front.”

“Right. And uh, you’ll come to the funeral, right?”

“Of course. When’s it gonna be?”

“I’m not sure yet. We haven’t discussed it.”

“Okay. Well, just let me know when you figure it out, and I’ll make sure I can be there for you.”

Rio nods, even though planning a funeral for her best friend is the very last thing she wants to be doing. “Yeah, sure. Thanks again, Alice.”

“No problem. I’ll see you soon.”

“Yeah, bye.”

Ending the call, Rio sets her phone back where it had been and stares at the tiled floor beneath her feet. She’d almost forgotten that they need to plan a funeral before too much longer. No one else is going to do it; Wanda and Vision don’t have any other family to take care of that kind of stuff. 

She can’t think about that right now, though. She can worry about it at a different time, but hopefully sooner rather than later. They both deserve to be laid to rest as soon as possible. And hopefully next to each other. That’s the one thing for sure she knows they’d want.

In the meantime, she’s going to finish washing the dishes from dinner and then help Agatha get the boys down for the night. It’s going to be the first time the boys get put down in their own beds by someone other than their parents, so she’s not really sure how it’s going to go. 

.

.

.

Rio has never wanted to pull her hair out more than she does in this moment. Not even when weeks of experiments with her plants continue to not give her the desired result. Those are frustrating, of course, but this…this is a whole other level.

Sure, Vision had complained to her once upon a time about it, but she never actually thought that trying to get two toddlers to go to sleep would be so…intense. Among the meltdowns, diaper changes, and wrestling them into pajamas, Rio is beyond exhausted. And it’s barely 8:30. It doesn’t take a genius to see that Agatha is also frazzled. 

Even as the other woman rocks a fussy Billy in her arms while she stands in the middle of the twins’ bedroom because the second she tries to sit down in the rocking chair Billy starts wailing. The fear and frustration over all of this is clear as day on her face. 

Tommy is thankfully a little calmer now, but still refusing to be put down in his crib, shrieking every time she gets remotely close to it. His little fists clutch tightly to her shirt as his face scrunches up in displeasure. It would be adorable if she weren’t so damn tired. 

“I don’t know how much longer I can do this.”

Rio frowns in Agatha’s direction. “You're not abandoning me with both of them while they’re like this.”

Agatha scoffs. “As if I could. Do you see the death grip Billy has on me? I know Wanda always said he has a hard time falling asleep, but this is ridiculous.”

“I think they know something’s different. But they’re still too young to understand what’s going on.”

“So what do you suggest we do, then?” Agatha practically snaps. “We can’t just let them cry all night.”

“Can’t we?” 

“What?”

Rio shrugs. “They’ll tire themselves out eventually, right? I’m pretty sure I’ve heard that somewhere.”

“Do you really want to stay here all night waiting for them to fall asleep?”

Rio sighs. “Not really. But what other choice do we have? They’re not sleeping, Agatha. And they scream if we try to put them in their cribs.”

“I know that, but I just—”

A sharp knock drifts up from the lower level of the house, interrupting the moment. 

Agatha’s brows furrow. “Who could possibly be showing up right now?”

“Uh, that’s probably Alice. She’s bringing me some of my things. I’ll be right back.” She adjusts her hold on Tommy and then makes her way out of the room and down the stairs. As soon as she gets the front door open, she lets out a breath, her body sagging slightly as she offers her friend a tiny smile. “Hi.”

Alice’s eyes dart between her and a still fussy Tommy. “Uh, everything okay?” 

“No, it’s not. The boys are refusing to go to sleep. I think Agatha and I are just about at our wits' end. But you’re amazing for bringing me my things.” 

“Yeah, of course.” She leans in and sets the bag down. “Do you want me to stay and try to help?” 

Rio shakes her head. “No, thanks. We might just try letting them tire themselves out from crying. I’m sure they’ll get there eventually.” 

“Okay. And hey, I know it’s gonna be rough for a while, and I already told you on the phone, but I’m here whenever you need an extra set of hands, okay? Just say the word.”

Rio smiles at her friend. “Thanks, Alice. I promise I will take you up on that at some point. I just don’t know when that’ll be.” 

“That’s okay. Just wanted to make sure you know that even if it feels like you’re alone now, you aren’t. You’ve still got me.”

Rio takes a deep breath to steady herself. “I know. And I’m so grateful that I do. But I should probably go try and get this guy to fall asleep,” she says, gently lifting Tommy for emphasis.

Alice’s eyes go slightly wide. “Right, yes, sorry. I’ll let you go. Uh, tell Agatha I say ‘hey’. I can’t imagine this is easy for her either.” 

“It’s really not. I’ll see you later, Alice. And thanks again for the stuff.” She glances down at the boy in her arms. “Tommy, can you say bye to Alice?” 

“Bye, kiddo,” Alice says with a small smile and a wave. 

Rio throws her friend one more smile, waiting as she moves down off the porch and to her car, then pushes the door closed and flips the lock. She smiles softly down at the boy who is no longer crying, but simply staring up at her, his grip still tight on her shirt. “Okay, let’s try this bedtime thing one more time, shall we?”

.

.

.

The next morning, Rio is nowhere near as rested as she would like to be. The twins had eventually fallen asleep, but woke up repeatedly throughout the night, and not always at the same time. And because there’s only one baby monitor, she and Agatha took turns with it so they could take turns waking up with the twins. It was certainly far from easy, and she would have loved to have gotten more sleep instead of what felt like mere seconds every few minutes. 

But she supposes this is her life now. Having to run on very little sleep nearly every day. At least until the twins can get into the habit of sleeping through the night and no longer take hours to fall asleep. And hopefully, that happens sooner rather than later. 

However, seeing as she has many tasks she needs to get done, she has no choice but to do them and pray that tonight she can get a decent amount of sleep. That’s all she really wants. 

“Where are you going?”

Rio turns slightly, slipping her foot into her shoe and then standing to her full height. “Out? I gotta run a couple of errands. I told you last night.”

Agatha raises a brow. “You’re gonna leave me here alone with the boys?”

Rio snickers. “They’re toddlers, Agatha, not wild animals.”

“I know that. But do you know how many times I’ve actually been on my own with them for more than a few minutes? Not many. And Wanda was…” She pauses, swallowing thickly. “I can’t watch them on my own.”

“Well, you’re gonna have to learn how to eventually, since I do plan on continuing my job at some point. Which I’m actually going to go to right now so that I can explain the situation when I tell them that I need some time off while we…adjust to this new situation.”

“Great. Then I’m coming with you.”

Rio blinks. “You’re what?”

“I’m coming with you. The boys are, too. We need to take those car seats back to DCF anyway.”

“I can do that by myself, you know.”

“Of course you can. But how were you going to transport whatever you need? Isn’t your car still in the shop?”

Rio shrugs. “I figured I could just take yours.”

“Not without my permission, you aren’t.”

“Okay. So can I take your car?”

“Yes. But the twins and I are coming with you.”

Rio breathes deeply through her nose, her hands curling into fists for the briefest of moments. “Fine.” She follows Agatha back through the house to get the boys dressed and the diaper bag packed. 

For a fleeting moment, she questions if they’ll actually need everything that they’re packing, but then the reality of the situation hits her—neither of them have done this before. She suddenly doesn’t care if they bring a whole chest of items with them if it’ll help with the boys while they’re out in public. She’d rather have way too much stuff and be vastly overprepared than not have an extra set of clothes or that one special toy or something else that she wouldn’t be thinking of under normal circumstances. 

But these are far from normal circumstances. 

It takes them a while, but not quite as long as the first time, to get the boys strapped into the car. There’s only a small struggle with Tommy wanting to just run around the front lawn instead of getting in the car, but they eventually manage to get both boys strapped into their normal carseats and the carseats they borrowed from DCF, thankfully, shoved into the trunk of Agatha’s car. The diaper bag ends up on the floor of the passenger seat, far away from little hands that like to grab anything they can reach. 

She is in no way under the assumption that she’s going to be driving, especially given that Agatha has a near-death grip on the keys and immediately climbs into the driver’s seat. So she settles for the passenger seat and prays that the rest of the day goes well enough. She’s not expecting it to be perfect; that’d be asking for way too much. 

Once they finish at the DCF office, Rio verbally directs Agatha to her lab that sits closer to the edge of town. It’s not a long drive, thankfully, and the boys are still calm enough in the backseat. 

She unhooks her seatbelt as soon as Agatha pulls into an empty space. “I’ll be as quick as I can,” she says, and then climbs out of the car and jogs lightly up to the front door of the building. She swipes her ID card to be let inside and immediately heads down the hall toward the main lab. This time of day, her boss is usually making the rounds to see how the various projects and experiments are going. 

She spots him almost instantly, hovering near a workstation and observing one of many projects. “Dale.”

The man in question shifts his gaze to her, a small, professional smile tugging at his lips. “Rio, I didn’t think you were coming in today.”

“Uh, I’m not. I just needed to speak with you for a moment.”

“Of course. Why don’t we go to my office?”

Rio nods silently and follows her boss toward his office, her hands wringing together in front of her the whole way. It’s not her first time in his office, though it’s the first time she’s this nervous. 

She remains standing when the door swings closed, even when a chair is offered to her. She’s bracing for a speedy exit, just in case. 

“Well,” Dale says, “I don’t think there’s any use in trying to beat around the bush here. What did you want to talk about?”

“Uh, right. If we’re getting straight to it, I need to take some time off. I’m not sure for exactly how long, but uh, there have been some big changes in my personal life, and I need to focus fully on them for a little while.”

Dale nods. “Okay. You’ve got plenty of PTO accrued if you want to use that. And then just keep me informed when you know when you’ll be coming back.”

Rio lets out a heavy breath. “That would be great. Thank you so much.”

Dale smiles. “Of course. You’re one of my best employees, Rio. One of my hardest workers, too. I know you wouldn’t ask for time off unless it was serious.” He holds his hands up. “You’re not obligated to tell me what’s going on, and I’m not gonna ask for details, but are you doing alright? Nothing that I need to be worried about?”

Rio shakes her head. “No. Nothing like that. Just some unexpected life changes.”

“Alright. Well, I’m here if you need anything, just let me know.”

“I will. Thanks.” She offers him a small smile and then makes her way out of the office and to the car.

She passes a few of her coworkers as she goes, tossing them quick smiles and waves, not bothering to stop and talk. She doesn’t have the time for that right now. She has way too much on her plate to worry about small talk. She still has to get more of her belongings from her apartment, which could take a while. 

She’s relieved to find the car still in the parking lot, a small part of her brain had told her that there was a high chance Agatha would leave her behind to go and do something else without telling her. She wouldn’t put it completely past the other woman.

“What did your boss say?” Agatha asks her as soon as the car door is open.

Rio lets out a breath and buckles herself in. “He’s fine with me taking some time off. I won’t be gone too long, of course, just long enough to figure out whatever needs to be figured out with all of this. And, I guess, until you feel you have a handle on dealing with the boys by yourself.”

Agatha snorts as she starts the car. “Guess you’re never going back to work, then.”

Rio narrows her eyes at the woman but doesn’t respond. Partially because she doesn’t know what to say, and partially because she can’t quite understand Agatha’s aversion to being left alone with the boys or really spending any one-on-one time with them. She knows Agatha loves the boys, though, which does ease her concerns quite a bit.

“Just drive,” she ends up saying, leaning back in her seat and pointing toward the main road. “My apartment’s only a few blocks from here.”

“Great. But I’m not staying in the car with the boys this time.”

The drive is thankfully short and uneventful, and someone is leaving through the front door by the time they get the twins out of the car, so Rio doesn’t have to try and fumble for her keys to let them inside. And she knows Alice is home, so that’s one more obstacle she won’t have to face.

She hikes Tommy up a little higher on her hip when they reach the door of the apartment, doing her best to keep him from trying to get down and escape while kicking the toe of her shoe against the door. “I know you want to get down, buddy,” she tells the boy in her arms when he starts to whine, “but you can’t run around in the hallway, okay?”

The door swings open a second later, Alice appearing in the doorway, eyes wide. “Uh, hey. Wasn’t expecting the whole squad.”

Rio lets out a huff. “Neither was I.” She steps across the threshold and drops the diaper bag onto the floor. “We saved some of our old moving boxes, right?”

“I think so. Check the closet.”

“You better have saved some,” Agatha remarks as she sets Billy down on the carpet and passes him one of his toys. “Because otherwise getting your stuff out of here is going to be very difficult.”

Rio smiles over at her. “It’ll be an adventure.” She smiles at Tommy and bounces him lightly, eyes drifting over to Alice still standing near the door in her pajamas. “A little adventure couldn’t hurt, right?”

.

.

.

The rest of the day is, in one word, chaos. There are a few more meltdowns, multiple failed negotiations, and even a diaper blowout from Billy that has both of them freaking out. It’s so much more than she ever thought she’d have to deal with at this point in her life.

But she’s figuring it out one thing at a time. Or, she’s doing her very best to figure everything out. She’s sure they’re both messing up way more than they’re getting things right. And they only have so much to go on for how Wanda and Vision want their boys raised. She never paid close enough attention to all the little things, and it’s not as if there were any serious instructions left for them. 

None of them ever actually entertained even for a moment that any of this would happen.

Putting the boys down for bed goes a little easier, though not completely drama-free. The boys manage to fall asleep within a decent amount of time, but there is still a bit of a struggle, which is to be expected.

But she is completely exhausted by the time both boys are sound asleep in their cribs with the door shut tightly to drown out the rest of the world. The baby monitor crackles softly from its place in her hand, some of the tension finally leaving her body. It’s been a long couple of days and all she wants is to go to sleep for as long as possible until one of the twins eventually wakes up again needing something. There is one more thing she needs to do, though, before she loses the nerve.

She follows after Agatha, the other woman already halfway down the stairs, and does her best to mentally prepare herself for whatever she’s about to be met with. She knows how she’d like it to go, but this is Agatha Harkness, of all people, and one thing she’s learned in the short time she’s known the woman is not to think she knows the woman’s next move.

“Hey, Agatha?” she calls out once they’ve both reached the living room, several boxes and bags of Rio’s belongings tucked away in the corner. “Uh, now that the boys are asleep, there’s something pretty important we need to talk about.”

“What?”

She takes a deep breath, her hands wringing together in front of her. “A funeral for our friends.”

Agatha’s entire body freezes, her face hardening. “No.”

Rio takes a small step forward. “Agatha…”

“I said no.”

“Why?”

“Because…because I can’t.”

Rio shifts on her feet. “Look, I get it, okay? I miss them too. A lot. But we need to at least plan something. There are plenty of people besides us who I’m sure would like to say goodbye to them.”

“I…I wouldn’t even know where to start.”

“So we get help however we can.”

Agatha takes a small step back and shakes her head again. “No. I’m not talking about this. Not tonight. I can’t.” She turns and quickly makes her way back up the stairs, muttering to herself as she goes.

Rio sighs heavily and falls down onto the couch, pushing a hand through her hair and eyeing the mess of toys still scattered across the carpet. Really, she shouldn’t be surprised that Agatha reacted so poorly. Planning a funeral for her best friend is the last thing she wants to be doing, but it needs to be done. Preferably sooner rather than later. And if Agatha won’t help her, then she’ll figure it out on her own. She’s going to give her friend and his wife the best damn funeral she can manage. If it doesn’t break her first.
