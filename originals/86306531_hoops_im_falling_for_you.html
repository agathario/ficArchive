It just meant he was saving extra room for dessert. 

When it was finally time for dessert, Nicky watched with rapt attention as the waiters set up the chocolate fountain. Hot, shiny, melted chocolate cascaded down the fountain in three waves. The fountain sat on a table that also had brownies, cookies, fruit, pretzels, and marshmallows to dip in the running chocolate. Nicky wanted to go over to the fountain as soon as it was ready, but Nonna said no one was allowed to have any dessert until after the cake was cut. So, Nicky waited for what felt like forever for Mama and Rio to cut their cake. Once they did, everybody was served a slice. Nicky took one even though he didn’t like cake, because it seemed like he was supposed to. After that, Nonna finally let Nicky go to the chocolate fountain. 

It wasn’t until Nicky had finished the spoils from his third trip to the chocolate fountain that he started feeling sleepy. He wasn’t embarrassed, though, because Lorna was already lying with her head resting on the table and taking a nap. She looked comfortable, so Nicky decided to copy her. 

“Hey!” Nonna said, shaking his shoulder gently as soon as his head hit the table. “You ready to go home, kiddo?” Nonna asked. 

Nicky looked up and blinked sleepily at Nonna. “Yeah.” 

“Oh no! You have chocolate all over your mouth!” Nonna gasped before starting to laugh, “We have to clean you up before you get in my car.” 

“Can you just wipe it off with a napkin?” Nicky asked. 

“I think we’re gonna need a sink, Nicolino. Nonna said as she took out her phone and snapped a picture of Nicky. Then, she showed him the photo. Nicky had so much chocolate on his chin that it looked like he had a beard. 

“You might be right,” Nicky said. 

“C’mon, let's get to the bathroom to clean you up, and then we’ll leave.”

Nicky followed Nonna to the bathroom. The door was unlocked, but someone must've been inside because Nonna yelped with surprise as soon as she opened it. Nonna shut the door immediately, but not before Nicky could peek and see who was dumb enough not to lock the door when they were doing their business. Nicky was surprised to see that it was Mama. But, Mama wasn’t using the toilet. She was standing against the sink, all red and breathing hard. He’d never seen Mama like this before, and he didn't like it. She looked like she was having a meltdown. 

“She needs help!” Nicky exclaimed, squeezing past Nonna and rushing into the bathroom. 

“Nicholas, no!” Nonna shouted, but Nicky was through the door before she could block him. 

“Mama! Are you okay?” Nicky asked as she ran over to her. Mama’s mouth had fallen open in shock, and her hand clutched at her chest. 

“Nicky, why didn’t you knock?!” Mama squeaked, her cheeks turning an even darker red. 

“Are you having a sensory meltdown?” Nicky asked, but before Mama could say anything, Nonna was coming in. 

“What the fuck?” Nonna yelled. “You couldn’t lock the door?” 

“Don’t yell at Mama!” Nicky turned to Nonna. “Yelling never helps!” 

Then, Rio climbed out from under Mama’s skirt. Had they been here the whole time? Nicky was feeling confused.

“I could’ve sworn I felt the lock click,” Rio mumbled. 

“Hi, Rio. Why are you on the ground?” Nicky asked. 

Rio rubbed the back of their neck. “I was just helping your Mama, uh, fix her stocking. But it’s back on now.”  Rio said and stood up. 

Mama put her hand over her face and groaned. Nicky was surprised that his mama was so upset about a stocking, but Rio was there, so he knew mama would be okay. 

"I think you overreacted, Nonna," Nicky said. 

Nonna cleared her throat and glared at Mama, “I was just a little startled. Old lady problems.” 

Nicky nodded because he understood that it was tough to have old lady problems. 

“I’m surprised you’re not already home in bed, baby,” Mama told him as she smoothed out her dress. "Are you guys headed out soon?" Nonna was having a sleepover with Nicky tonight. 

“We’re leaving after we get this one cleaned up,” Nonna said. “We’ll see you in the morning to drive you to the airport.”

Mama and Rio were spending five days in Puerto Rico for their honeymoon. Nicky was still a little bit upset that he wasn’t allowed to go, but Mama and Rio were going to take him to Disney for his birthday, so it was okay. 

“We’ll get out of your way. Goodnight, Ma. Goodnight, baby.” Mama said walking with Rio towards the bathroom door. 

“Bye, mama, bye Rio! See you in the morning.” Nicky said as Nonna led him to the sink. 

“We love you so much, Nicky,” Rio grinned. “Sleep tight.” 

“Love you both.” Nicky yawned. Weddings were exhausting.
