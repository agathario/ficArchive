Alice was off to the side along with a few pediatric nurses, diligently waiting for the hand off. Jen worked as quickly as she could, mindful that Agatha had already lost a substantial amount of blood on the way over here.

And yet-

Rio jumped up, fifteen minutes in and Jen hadn’t gotten Alex out, she banged on the glass. Jen peered up at her, barely shaking her head. She bashed the intercom button. “What the hell are you doing?”

“LUS is too thick, I can’t-”

“Jen, get Alex out. Agatha is losing too much blood, I can fucking see it.”

“Oh, thank you, I hadn’t thought of that!”

“Agatha’s going to hemorrhage if you don’t-”

“I am aware!” Jen hissed, “The fetal malformations, Alex is too sick and the low uterine segment is too thick. If I pull Alex out now, I risk losing them both.”

“If you keep Alex in, Agatha’s gonna bleed out.” Rio barked, because either way, Agatha wouldn’t fare well. The longer she was open, the worse the outcome. Her body was too weak for the surgery to begin with.

Alex wouldn’t have more than a day with how premature she was, those hours would be spent in unending pain. And shouldn’t a mother protect her child from that? From agony?

“I can’t let Alex die.”

“You can’t let my wife die!”

“Agatha said-”

“Jen!”

“Agatha said-”

Rio set her jaw, making the decision before she realized she had. “Agatha is unconscious and I am her next of kin. I am telling you to save her. Legally and medically, you know I am right. Viability and quality of life. You are under an obligation to listen to my wishes. You save Agatha. Now!”

“And there’s the hemorrhage." Jen looked up one more time, shoulders slumping. “Alice, get ready.”

Shit, Rio had to get down there. If Alex wasn’t going to make it for Agatha, Rio had to be there for their daughter. She took off, sprinting down a floor and into the O.R., Alex had to go knowing one of them. If she hadn’t respected Agatha’s first wish, she’d try like hell to respect this one. She grabbed a mask, a cap, and a gown, throwing them on, running through the doors.

She closed her eyes for a beat, opening them and still seeing her worst nightmare. Rio walked past Agatha on the table, Jen shouting too many orders all at once. Honestly, the whole world sounded like white noise. Going deeper and deeper into the room, approaching Alice standing over Alex. And it was already over. Rio couldn’t have been more than a minute or two, but Alice–

Her best friend, voice shaking, uttered “Time of death,” and peeked at the clock on the wall. “11:11.”

The irony in angel numbers.

The cruelty.

Rio dug the heels of her palms into her eyes, trying to stop the sobs. “Fuck, sorry.”

Melina handed her a box of tissues. “It is okay.”

Agatha’s face was streaked with tears, her teeth gnawing at her lower lip.

“I would’ve crawled on my hands and knees, begging to the gods to have another way.” Rio said, voice breaking. “But, I- I couldn’t- I couldn’t let Agatha die.”

“What happened after?”

“Uh, Agatha got some blood transfusions-”

“Not to Agatha. To you.”

“I held her.” Rio nearly whispered. “Hoped that she’d still feel it somehow.”

Agatha sniffled, silent.

Melina looked at her ex wife. “Have you ever heard Rio’s side before? From start to finish?”

“No.” Agatha swallowed. “I knew she held Alex but, I guess I filled in the blanks myself.”

“I also didn’t correct it.” Rio added.

The doctor bounced her gaze between the two. “So, Agatha blames herself for Alex’s illness, Rio blames herself for Alex’s death.” She tilted her head, eyes narrowing. “Do you blame each other for either of those things?”

“Of course not.” Rio answered.

“No.” Agatha responded.

“Agatha,” Melina zeroed in. “Do you think you might harbor a grudge over Rio’s denial of your autonomy?”

“It was a necessary surgery-”

“Medical or not, the body holds trauma.” Melina interrupted. “So do you?”

Agatha wiped her face, looking down at her lap. “Yes.”

“Rio, you mentioned feeling resentful of Agatha. Do you think you still feel that way? You were scared and angry that she was at peace with dying, did you feel left behind?”

“I understood where she was coming from-”

“Did. You.”

“Yes.” Rio croaked. “And I think I still do.”

Melina opened her mouth to speak, a blaring alarm sounded out from all three of their phones. Everyone scrambled for their devices, the national weather service declaring the blizzard unsafe and urging people to stay home, along with road closures being released. Rio groaned, Agatha’s own following.

The therapist sighed. “Okay, it looks like we have to cut this session short. Not ideal considering I’m sure you’re both feeling very vulnerable. I promise, I will schedule another one in a few days, even if I have to stay late. However, unless we all want to be snowed in together, I suggest we hightail it out of here.”

“Yeah.” Agatha’s voice clipped, throwing on her jacket. “Thanks.”

Rio steadied herself, standing and following her wife out. “Will you get home alright, Dr. Vostokoff?”

“Oh, I live around the corner. Why do you think I take patients on the weekend?”

“Goodnight.”

“Get home safe.”

Rio caught up with Agatha, looking out the double doors of that practice at the snow whipping around. “Fuck, that picked up fast.”

Agatha looked at her, palm out. “Give me your keys.”

“Why?”

“I’ll drive you home. Your car will do better on the roads anyways.”

“I- I can drive.”

“No, you can’t. You’re crying. Just,” She made a ‘gimme’ motion with her hand. “Let me take you home.”

“I’m fine, I promise.”

“Rio,” blue eyes like stained glass burrowed into her. “I don’t want you to be alone right now. I know how it feels, it sucks. The thoughts that come after reliving that day, they-” She sucked in a breath. “I don’t want to be alone. Just give me your damn keys.”

“How will you get home?”

“Guess I won’t.” Agatha shrugged. “There are worse places to be trapped. Plus, I hear there’s tequila awaiting me.”

Rio weakly smiled, finally giving her wife the keys. “Don’t kill us.”

“I’m a good driver!”

“No, baby. You’re not.”

“So you don’t want me to donuts in the parking lot? The adrenaline will make you forget your sadness.”

“My car is insured but that’s not a free pass to crash it.”

Agatha opened the door, wind howling. “No, I think it is.”

“Agathaaaaa-”

“I’m joking!” She grinned. “...A little.”
