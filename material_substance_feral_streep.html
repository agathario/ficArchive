Something about motherhood made her even more ruthless of a seller than she had been and the second she was back from a well-earned maternity leave, she climbed the corporate ladder faster than anyone Rio had seen before. Rio was so proud of her wife.

They had quietly married shortly after their son’s first birthday. Their ceremony was small and intimate—only the three of them, Lilia and the Maximoff clan in attendance—but it captured the depth and profundity of their love in a way that so few got to see outside their little bubble. Agatha cried and Rio cried harder, their perfect bundle of joy cooing and drooling happily between them as they said “I do.”

Now, five years later, things were just about perfect for the Harkness-Vidals. That was, until Agatha’s phone rang during their leadership WBR. She quickly flipped it over, silencing her ringer with a grimace and mouthing “I’m so sorry” to everyone in the room. Wanda eyed her curiously over the top of her laptop—she knows that no one calls Agatha without prior authorization. The VP shoots her best friend a shrug and turns back to the CFO who was presenting about god knows what, pretending not to be completely rattled.

And then Rio’s phone rang.

Chocolate eyes going wide, she looks down at the Caller ID and sees Westview Prep in bold letters. Her eyebrows shoot up into her hairline and she quickly gets up to leave the meeting, nodding her head at Agatha to come join her.

“I’m so sorry for the interruption, but it’s Nick’s school. They don’t call unless it’s an emergency. We have to take this,” Rio says politely, opening the door and ushering her wife out with a hand on the small of her back. The nice thing about working for the same company for the last 7 years is that everyone on the leadership team knows them both and understands how seriously they take their family. Plus, Wanda and Lilia were both still in the meeting and could relay any need-to-know information to the wives afterwards.

The second the door to the meeting room was closed, Rio slid open her phone and answered quickly.

“Rio Harkness-Vidal spe—”

“Is Nicky okay?” Agatha climbed over to worry into the phone, interrupting Rio altogether.

“Good, you’re both here. Yes, Nicholas is fine,” the school administrator answered. “However, there was an incident today and we’re going to need one or both of you to come down to the school as soon as possible.”

“Incident? What do you mean incident? What happened? Is he hurt?” Agatha had completely lost the plot and was freaking out loudly on the poor admin on the other line.

“Nicholas is completely fine, Agatha. Just try to get down here as soon as possible and we can discuss in person.”

Rio took her cellphone back from her panicking wife. “Thanks, we’ll be there in ten.”

Agatha turned to Rio, eyes wide with worry, unable to accept the admin’s words that their son was okay. “Why did you hang up? We don’t even know what happened. What if he’s in real trouble, Rio?”

Rio put a calming hand in the center of Agatha’s chest, lightly applying pressure in a gesture that she knew would soothe her wife and bring her a bit of comfort.

“Just breathe, mi vida. We’re going to head down there right now and figure out what’s up. The admin said he’s completely fine. If it were something more serious, they would have said so. It’s going to be okay.”

“I’m scared, Rio,” Agatha confesses, her breathing getting heavy. “He never gets in trouble at school. What if something really bad happened?”

“Then we’ll deal with it as a family,” Rio assured her calmingly. “Nick is the best kid. He’s smart and empathetic—I’m sure he’s completely fine. But let’s just get down there quick so we know what we’re dealing with, okay? I’ll drive.”

They quickly grabbed their keys and purses and made their way out to the Lezbaru 2.0—they really do make great family cars. The seven minute ride from their office to Nicky’s school was done in complete silence with Rio’s hand glued to Agatha’s knee, giving her comforting squeezes of reassurance as she drove. Agatha absentmindedly chewed on her thumbnail as she stared out the window, her mind going a million miles a minute at all of the possibilities of what they could be walking into.

What they never expected was their son sitting outside the principal’s office, wiping the back of his hand across his face to clear the snot and tears in an effort to hide the fact that he had been crying.

The moment Agatha saw the redness around his eyes and that he was otherwise unharmed, she flew off into a rage befitting the true mama bear that she had become. She stormed into the principal’s office without a knock or a single pleasantry, immediately exploding on the poor man who had no idea what he had unleashed.

“I swear to god, Fury, if there’s a single hair harmed on my kid’s head, I’m gonna have your balls in a vise. You understand me? We pay how much goddamned money a year to this stupid school only to get called out of work to find our kid crying outside your office? You better have a damn good explanation for what happened here or so help me I will sue every single teacher and admin in this place so fast, you’ll wish I was only threatening you.”

Rio stayed behind with Nicky for a second, doing a more comprehensive check-over of her own. She quickly scanned his arms and legs, making sure there were no scrapes or bruises before taking his small face in her hands.

“You okay, mijo?”

He closed his eyes and nodded, more tears slipping out.

“Are you hurt?” she asked gently.

He shook his head no, still unable to speak an answer.

“Are you scared?”

He paused before nodding softly, which completely broke her heart into a million pieces.

“Are you scared because you’re in danger or because you’re afraid of getting in trouble?”

“I don’t want you and mama to be mad at me,” he squeaked before fully breaking down in more tears.

“Oh amorcito, your mama and I could never be mad at you. Don’t worry about that right now. We’re just so glad you’re safe, mijo. That’s all that matters to us.”

“You—you promise?” he stuttered.

“Lo prometo,” she vowed, sticking up her pinky to link with her son’s. “Now, I gotta go talk to your principal. I’m gonna send mama out here to see you and you’re gonna go to the car with her. Make sure you give her a biiiiiig hug, okay? She was really worried about you, so squeeze her nice and tight for me,” she orders, scooping her son up and giving him an exaggerated squeeze causing him to erupt in giggles.

“Si, claro, mami,” he responded through his laughter.

“Sit tight, champ,” she said, putting him back down on the wooden bench before walking over to Principal Fury’s office. As she got close, she could hear Agatha’s muffled yelling and she knew that her wife would have been giving the poor man the business. She calmly opened the door and took in the scene.

Agatha was standing over Fury, both of her hands on his desk, as she loomed over where he sat. He was reclined back in his big leather desk chair, letting her wife tear into him with a poorly-veiled expression of distaste splashed across his visage.

“Mi amor, why don’t you take Nicky to the car and let me speak with Mr. Fury here,” Rio cut in, leaving no room for discussion.

“But I—“

“Now, Agatha,” she orders.  Rio sat down in the chair across from Fury making it very clear that the conversation was over and her wife was to do as she said. She loves Agatha, she really does, but her wife is not always the most level-headed when it comes to their son and Rio has a feeling that whatever got them called into the school today requires a delicate touch.

Agatha huffs and purses her lips, flicking her long ponytail over her shoulder and giving Fury the finger before stomping out of his office.

Rio watches her go with her tongue pressed into her cheek, proud of herself for how she’s still able to wrangle her unruly wife even after all of these years. Then she turns back to the principal with nothing but unimpressed nonchalance.

“So Nick, you wanna tell me what was so important that you had to pull both me and my wife out of our weekly leadership review?”

“Well, Mrs. Harkness-Vidal, it appears that your son got into a fight with another boy in his class during recess today.”

Rio laughs out loud. Surely they must have gotten the wrong kid. Nicky would never harm an ant, especially not another person.

“A fight? Really? My Nicholas?” she scoffed.

“According to the teacher, she had to pull your son off of another boy in the schoolyard.”

“And did she see what started the fight? Surely Nicholas was not the instigator,” she questioned.

“She did. She said she watched your son shove the other boy to the ground and then immediately jump on top of him to pin him down.”

Rio swallowed harshly. That didn’t sound like Nicholas at all. Something serious must have happened to make him react that way.

“Did you speak to the boys to get both sides of the story? I’m sorry, but I’m having a hard time believing Nicky would act aggressively without being provoked. He’s normally the sweetest boy.”

“The other boy claims the attack was unprovoked, but Nicholas claimed that he saw the boy pull a classmate’s pigtails.”

Rio sat up a little bit straighter in her chair at that admission. There it was—the crux of this whole situation.

“This other student who pulled on the girl’s braids, did a teacher see that or do anything?”

“She didn’t see it, no,” Fury confessed, looking down at his shoes sheepishly.

“And was the other student disciplined in any way for harming the poor girl?”

“Harm? Mrs. Harkness-Vidal, I assure you that the boy’s intentions were purely innocent. Just good old fashioned ‘boys will be boys.’ I’m sure he has a little crush on her or something.”

“Pulling someone’s hair is assault. That behavior is harmful, not ‘boys will be boys,’” Rio spit.

“That’s not—“ Fury starts.

“We’ve taught our son about consent and about what to do when someone says no to your boundaries. Ever since he was very little, we’ve made it explicitly clear that no one is allowed to touch you if you don’t want them to. Is this not a policy held by this school?”

“No, it is, it’s just—“

“Furthermore, this school has a zero-tolerance policy for unwanted touches of any kind. Why does that not apply to young girls?” As she continues drilling into him, Fury sinks further and further into his seat.

“Who was the girl who was harmed? If you don’t tell me, I’ll just ask Nicky. He’d be happy to let me know.”

“It was Miss Stark,” Fury admits, unwilling to make eye contact with Rio.

“That’s funny because last time I checked, Tony and Pepper are some of the biggest donors to this school, no? I’m sure they’d love to know that their child is being assaulted and then it’s being brushed under the rug. In fact, I have a meeting with Pepper later this week. She’s on the DRKHLD board, you know how it is with quarterly reporting calls. I think I’ll go right ahead and let her know about this situation myself if you don’t mind…”

“NO!” Fury shouts. “We’ll handle it from here, Mrs. Harkness-Vidal.”

“See to it that you do. Nicholas was clearly defending someone who was not being protected by the institution and the adults who were in charge of doing so. No six-year-old should be running that kind of interference, especially not for what I pay for this school. Agatha and I will speak to him about his actions and the escalation to physical violence, but I trust that for now we’re square here and we won’t have to have this conversation again?”

“Yes ma’am. Understood,” Fury replies.

“And make sure the other boy knows that behavior is unacceptable. It’s 2026, Fury. We’re not doing this shit anymore. Get with the times.”

“Yes ma’am,” he nods as Rio gets up from the chair to walk out of the room.

“Oh, and one last thing. I want a formal apology to my kid for making him cry and believe that he did something wrong. We both know he did the right thing here, so don’t punish him for failing to do what adults couldn’t. You can give that to him tomorrow before school. I’ll walk him to your office personally.”

Fury simply nods in shame as Rio walks out.

“See you tomorrow, Nick. Be better,” she tosses over her shoulder before pulling the door shut.

She gives the admins at the front desk a heartfelt thank you before strutting out of the school with her hands in her pockets. When she gets back to their car, she’s surprised to find Nick tucked up in the passenger seat, curled in his mother’s lap. He’s crying as he recounts the story to her in full detail while Agatha softly brushes the tears from his cheeks and places soft kisses to the top of his head. Rio doesn’t interrupt, letting Nicky get to the end of the story before opening up the driver’s side door and sliding in next to her whole world.

“How are you feeling, mijo?” she coos softly, reaching out to cup a red, tear-stained cheek.

“I was—I was just telling m-mama about what h-happened,” he got out through his cries.

“I know, baby,” Agatha soothed, pulling him even tighter to her chest. “And your mami and I are so proud of you.” 

“You are?” he asked incredulously.

“We’re are,” Rio responded, taking one of his small hands in both of hers. “Being brave for someone else isn’t easy, but we’re so proud of the decision you made to stick up for Morgan. You didn’t like seeing her get hurt, did you?”

“The teacher didn’t say anything!” he cried even louder, the injustice wracking his tiny body with frustrated sobs.

“We’ll always support you defending people who can’t defend themselves,” Agatha reassured the boy. “However, there are a lot of ways to do that without shoving, hitting or pushing. We want you to use your words whenever possible.”

“I know,” Nicky admitted, his little cheeks flaming in embarrassment. “I just got so mad.”

“It’s okay to get mad, Nick,” Rio replies. “What’s not okay is hitting or pushing people out of anger. When you’re feeling mad, there are a lot of things you can do to help calm your body down, like taking deeeeeeeep breaths.” Rio sucked in an exaggerated breath, blowing it out all over his face, making their son giggle and kick his feet to get away from her.

“Okay, okay, I will!” he promised through shrieks of laughter as Agatha continued the attack with tickles to his ribs.

“We’re so proud of you, baby. You’re the best little boy in the entire world and we love you so much,” Agatha said lovingly, her eyes misty with tears that she would not let fall.

“I love you, moms. Te quiero mucho.”

“Hey, what do you say the three of us get ice cream for lunch?” Rio asks. “I think this little family deserves a sweet treat, don’t you think?”

And they did.

Because their little family with their little home and their little life was perfect in every way and it always would be as long as they had each other.
