Wanna Take a Chance?

One of the vets at the sanctuary had received a call from a friend stating that they'd found an orphaned raccoon in their back yard, one that appeared to be in possession of a broken hind leg. Immediately, she'd taken off to retrieve it, arriving back at the center shortly after Rio had when she arrived after her shift at work.

She would say that Crème needed to be fed, or that she needed a little extra attention. They were never lies, but they were certainly excuses to let her say just a little while longer.

And Agatha?

No, Agatha just let Rio be, let her exist in the newness of what Agatha's determination had given her.

Their marriage had been amazing, their sex life was top-fucking-notch, and their social life wasn't hurting for much. They had their select friends and they lived a happy life. It was a good life.

Clearly, Agatha could see the misunderstanding all over her wife's face and knew that being vague and indicatory was not enough in such an instance. This was looking to be one of those times where directness was absolutely demanded.

Rio never would have let herself see the truth otherwise.

Agatha paced further inside, landing behind Rio so that she could peer over her shoulder to behold Crème cradled in the safety of her hold. A loose arm wrapped around her middle and Agatha offered a firm squeeze.

"You know how Zap loves us both, but I'm his person?" It was a soft question, but it was not direct enough. Rio took something else from it instead.

"I mean, yeah, but... Way to rub it in that he likes you more," Rio muttered, sighing softly. Her mind was only on one track, a track where she believed that their lives were already as complete as they could be.

Pressing her lips to the side of Rio's head, Agatha breathed the softest chuckle. "No, silly, that's not what I mean." She hesitated and then moved to lift the arm around Rio's middle so that she could lightly scratch at the top of Crème's head. "I'm saying that you are Crème's person. And that she should come home with us."

The moment the words left her lips, Rio could hardly believe them as they landed warmly in her ears.

No way.

No fucking way.

In what world was Agatha, the same woman who almost had a stroke at the sight of their little Zap, telling her that they were going to adopt another raccoon?

"Like... for the night?" Rio asked, seeking clarification. This time, she wasn't being dense or missing the point, she was trying to stop herself from getting her hopes up.

"No, my love," Agatha responded, kissing Rio's head again. "For good."

Rio hadn't wanted to move, mostly because Crème looked so content to be pet by Agatha so gingerly, but she spun in place a second later, the blind hope written all over her face.

"We're going to keep her? And... And raise her with Zap and Ace?" It was another push, but this time, she wanted to look her wife in the eyes if she said it out loud.

"She's ours. And we're to bring her in every few days to be checked over, but Brayden and I had a long conversation about the kind of care she needs. So... Maybe when you go into work Monday, see if you can snag a week or so off. That way you can stay home with her and help her adjust."

Agatha was saying it, all of it, like it was the easiest thing in the world. She was stating it as though it were fact and not a suggestion. She was offering Rio what she had once had all those months ago with Zap. And honestly, not only was it moving, it nearly made Rio burst into tears.

She felt, in that moment, so fucking loved.

She had never once questioned Agatha's devotion to her, but right then, it was blowing her away.

Agatha saw the things that made Rio happy and then she just... gave them to her.

Glancing down at Crème, Rio fought those voices in the back of her mind saying it was too good to be true with one simple fact: That phrase summed up her wife in everything she was.

And yet, she was true.

She was everything Rio could have ever asked for in the world and more.

Right then, she was convinced it had never been more of a fact than it was in those moments.

"You're sure?" she questioned quietly, finally lifting her head to find those homely blues she loved so much.

"I'm positive. You deserve this."

"That's what you said about this place, too." It was a weak argument, but Rio was trying to dance around the lengths Agatha would go to in order to make her happy.

"Rio, baby," Agatha soothed, reaching up to cup her cheek. "If I could hand you the entire world on a silver platter, I would stop at nothing to do it." The sincerity in her voice was thick, showcasing her pure and raw honesty. Rio had always known it, but hearing it say it aloud did something to that wildly fluttering heart in her chest.

After taking a soft kiss, Rio pulled away with a smile and glanced down one more time to the little blonde ball of fur she did not have to tuck away for sleep.

"She's ours," she breathed, reaching up to run a fingertip down her nose.

"She is," Agatha confirmed, leaning in to place a light kiss to Rio's cheek. "So let's take her to meet her siblings."

Siblings.

Zap and Ace.

Now it would be Zap and Ace and Crème.

And Rio and Agatha.

The Harkness-Vidals in every bit of their weirdness.

Their family.
