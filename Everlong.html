Chapter 1

Westview was an ordinary town, perched on the edge of the New Jersey Pine Barrens. It had it’s share of commuters to the city, but mostly it stood alone, with its own businesses, its own life. It was the hub for other nearby towns, alongside it’s close neighbour Eastview, and those who still lived within the forest, either in cabins, small rural stops along the hiking trails, or those who remained in the towns left the rot, returned to nature with some almost completely swallowed up by the forest again.

For Rio Vidal, Westview was home. It had been for a while, since she moved out of the city and set up the flower shop she had always dreamed of. Well, she bought it from the original owner, using the money her Abuela had left for her in her will. She had wanted something closer to NYC, closer to her friends and the life she built for herself when she’d moved from Delaware for college. Yet shop space in the city was expensive, along with everything else, and so she packed all her stuff and her pet tarantula into her car and made the move to New Jersey.

That had been just over a year ago, and the business was doing well. She’d made a few friends and set herself up in the apartment above the shop. She’d even gotten herself a van for deliveries and had started running to keep active. Still, she couldn’t help but feel a little…stuck. Like her life had started just rolling by. And, if she was truly honest with herself, it had been like that for years.

When she looked in the mirror, she just saw an older version of the nerdy queer girl with glasses who played saxophone and bust her knee playing basketball. And yes, she’d achieved her dream. She had her flower shop, but she had no one to share it with. No partner, no family. She just lived her life, the same day over and over. Most of which were pleasant enough, but she couldn’t help but wonder if this was it. If this was the rest of her life, and the thought made her mellow.

The day that all changed had seemed like yet another Groundhog Day. She got up, had her breakfast, got dressed, and opened the shop like she always did. She set the displays up outside and rearranged the stock, making sure every flower was fresh and vibrant. And as her customers arrived, she greeted them with smiles, offering them advice and making sure each arrangement was made to perfection. She had her regulars, like Sharon Davis, who had her own passion for flowers, and was always interested in purchasing Rio’s best cuttings.  Sometimes, however, she had surprises. And as it was approaching lunch time, one came barrelling through the doors in a frantic rush.

“I need flowers.”

Rio studied frantic woman before her. She seemed to be around her age, with a mane of thick brown hair and the most striking blue eyes she had ever seen. She was wearing worn washed jeans and a baggy shirt with a tiny Snoopy over the chest. Despite the woman’s frustrated expression, she was undoubtedly beautiful, and Rio almost found herself tongue-tied. Yet somehow, she even managed to crack a joke.

“Good job you came here then.” Rio stepped out from behind the counter. “What can I get you?”

“Like I said: flowers.”

Rio laughed. “Okay…what kind of flowers?”

“Any kind,” the woman replied. “My grand opening is in three hours and the table decorations I ordered came damaged. I’ve found some old glass bottles, so I want to put some flowers in them. It isn’t what I envisioned, but it will have to do.”

“Right.” Rio perused her vast inventory. “What kind of place is it?”

“A coffee shop,” the woman answered. “Just across the street.”

“Ah, so we’re neighbours,” Rio commented. “The Witch’s Brew, right?”

“That’s it,” the woman confirmed, and Rio knew exactly what to go for.

“I’m thinking wildflowers,” she said as she perused her collection. “Let me see…Wild Daisies signal New beginnings, cosmos for harmony, some baby’s breath, and some aster for a splash of purple.”

The woman accepted the flowers and the sachets of flower feed Rio handed over with them. “Thanks, how much do I owe you?”

Rio waved her off. “Just bring me a coffee when things calm down.”

The woman smiled, and Rio swore the world grew brighter. “Well, thanks…Sorry, what was your name.”

“My name? Oh, it’s, erm, Rio.”

The woman smirked. “Are you sure about that?”

Rio nodded, internally kicking herself. Why was she such a loser in front of beautiful women? “Yeah, pretty sure.”

“Well I’m Agatha,” the woman said as she moved for the door. “Nice to meet you, neighbour.”

“Nice to meet you too,” Rio replied. “Good luck for the opening.”

Agatha winked before walked out the door. Rio watched her walk across the street, to the new business in town, before getting back to work, their interaction playing over and over in her head.

 

After closing, she decided to go for a run. The October nights were drawing in quick, so she wanted to take advantage of the remaining light of dusk. And so she quickly changed into her running clothes, put her airpods in, and set off jogging down Mainstreet.

She made sure to run by the Witch’s Brew and was pleased to see it was busy. Though that did mean she couldn’t catch sight of Agatha, but perhaps she could stop by on her way back. Step in and ask how it was going, offer to help clear up at closing. Walk Agatha home, ask her on a date…

Rio shook the thoughts away. She had no chance with a woman like that. Even if she was gay, which was a small probability, why would she want t go out with a scrawny nerdy florist?

She made her way to the edge of town, running through the suburban neighbourhoods, heading towards towering treeline of pine trees. There was a small trail that jutted off into the woods, cutting across the other side of town. It was a part of her usual route, and like always she headed through the trees without hesitation.

As she followed the woodland path, music blasting in her ears, her mind went back to Agatha. She wondered why she’d moved to Westview of all places. Was she fulfilling a dream in the most cost-effective way like she was or was she there for a reason. Family? Friends? A partner? Most likely the latter, she thought sadly. People didn’t move to Westview without good reason.

As “Celebrity Skin” roared in her ears, she couldn’t hear much around her. Just the usual sights of the trees, sharpened by the shadows that grew closer every moment. Yet as the track came to an end, and the guitar intro for “Everlong” began to play, she swore she could hear something.

A crackling of twigs.

She pulled one airpod out as she slowed down to glance around her, looking for the source of the noise. Yet she saw nothing but woodland.

With a shrug, she carried on.

The path began to rear back towards town. Soon she would see the yellow of the streetlights, freshly switched on, and the warm glow of homes and the sharp light of car headlights. Yet in the woods, the only light was the rising moon as the sun slid behind the horizon.

The heavy guitar still blared in her ears as the chorus came round again. Then, it cut to quiet again. That same low guitar.

Another sound met her ears, and Rio pulled her airpod out again. She heard nothing at first, just the sound of the surrounding nature.

Then she heard it.

A subtle sound at first, only to get louder and louder.

Closer and closer.

The sound of something running, and it was heading her way.

Rio took off again, dropping her airpod as she went. “Everlong” still blasted in her other ear as she ran, but her brain barely registered it. Instead, it was fixed on the other sound. As the fast thumps got louder and louder, and was soon accompanied by frantic breaths. Sounds that got closer and closer and she tried to run faster and faster, but it was no use.

Before she could make it to the streetlights and the houses, something collided with her, knocking her to the ground before jumping on top of her, pinning her against the woodland floor.

“Get off!” Rio gasped. “Please! I don’t have anything! Let me go!”

She tried to push up, but her attacker kept her down. Rio felt something tickle her neck and heard a soft sniffing sound. Like an animal picking up a scent. But whatever it was didn’t seem like an animal. It felt human.

And if it was human, it meant she could fight.

Gathering all her strength, she managed to swing her elbow to strike against it, enough so that she could turn and face her attacker. Yet the move made it frantic, and soon Rio was batting away swiping claws as white teeth snarled in the darkness.

She couldn’t make out the rest of it, just a dark mess of black hair, as flashes of orange caught in the light. Whatever it was, it was unlike anything Rio had ever known. A monster if she didn’t know better, and like hell she was going to become its prey.

“Fuck you!” Rio went to shove them off her, using her forearm to add more force. It managed it enough for her to roll free, but she didn’t move her arm in time.

With a strangled raw, her attacker lunged and sharp teeth pierced into her arm.

The pain was sharp and hot, and she let out a pained cry.

She used her foot to kick it back, its teeth ripping out of her arm, before it retreated back into the trees.

Rio clutched her arm, blood wetting her palm as she tried to apply pressure.

Her other airpod had fallen from her ear in the chaos, but she didn’t waste time looking for it. Instead, she moved as fast as she could down the trail, tears wetting her cheeks as blood weaved through her fingers. She could feel herself getting weaker, her entire body burning as the pain began to spread up her arm. Yet she kept going. If she kept going, she could get help. If she kept going, the pain would stop. If she kept going, she would get home.

Soon she saw the light. Saw the road. Saw civilisation, and her legs used the last of her energy to propel her forward, out of the trees and onto the road.

Relief washed over her. She’d done it.

Suddenly a bright light burned her eyes, and she turned her face away. There was a loud metallic thunk, and she looked up to see that the light belonged to a car.

The driver’s door opened and a figure climbed out. A small woman with grey hair tied up in a bun.

“Oh you gave me a fright!” The woman rushed to her side. “What’s happened? Who did this to you?”

Rio just blinked at her before dropping to the ground. The last thing she saw was the woman’s startled face, and the distant glow of the moon before she was descended into darkness.
