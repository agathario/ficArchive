Agatha Harkness hadn’t really ever seen herself becoming a mother. Her own childhood had been pretty awful, her mother a sadistic narcissist who had made the first seventeen years of Agatha’s life a living hell until she was able to escape to college a full year early. From that day, she had cut off all contact with her mother, moving across state lines just to hide from her.

She didn’t really want to do that to a child.

It had nothing to do with the protesters outside, whom she’d given the middle finger when they cheered as she walked back to her car.

She donated the money she would have spent directly to the clinic the second she got home. Then she immediately booked her first prenatal visit.

Even after cancelling the termination, Agatha took six months to decide she actually wanted to keep her baby. She had looked into private adoption companies and had even gotten as far as reading through a contract, but there were stipulations. Like if the child were born with certain conditions or spent too long in the NICU, it would not be considered for adoption. Agatha’s heart had ached at that, unable to fathom leaving a sick infant without anybody to love or care for them.

She’d vetoed adoption at nearly eight months pregnant. There was nothing wrong with the child growing inside of her, as far as she knew from her prenatal visits, but she still didn’t want to risk leaving him alone. She had learned he was a boy later than she probably should have, due to the stubborn little shit’s unwillingness to cooperate. This was definitely her son.

The day he was born, she had just finished and sent in her final thesis for grad school to be checked over by her advisor. Her due date was still a week away, but her baby had been kicking her ribs like a soccer ball player all morning, so she knew something was about to happen.
