Part One

Morning comes too soon.

She isn't getting any younger.

Agatha groans, sitting up in bed. There's a bottle of painkillers and a glass of water by her bedside table. Her phone lays on the floor, where it dropped after she failed to turn off the alarm.

This tour is killing her. But at least she is back home for the last part. She has missed her bed, sleeping next to her wife and waking up to the sounds of her family starting their days, always ahead of her.

She can just make out the sound of laughter coming from downstairs, the kids must be up already, eating breakfast and talking about their plans for the break.

She will join them as soon as she gets rid of her headache. They understand the life of a pop star is hard, but they know she would give it all up for them.

Motherhood has changed her.

Agatha had promised herself, when they adopted Billy, that she would make sure her kids would have every opportunity available to them, and most importantly they would always have her support.

Her time, lately has been harder to achieve.

But she is working on it. Family is worth putting in the time and the work. The one thing she learned from her mother, or despite her.

She gets out of bed and jumps in the shower, deciding to erase any and all thoughts of Evanora Harkness before she goes to join her family.

Billy and Nicky are finishing cleaning after breakfast when she walks in. Nicky comes barreling into her as soon as he notices her and she picks him up. At five years old he's beginning to be too big for this, so Agatha treasures every chance she gets.

Nicky begins to talk excitedly about the morning, from helping with breakfast to how excited he is for going to the music festival today. That's when Agatha notices Billy hasn't come say good morning yet, he keeps his back to her, almost purposefully.

Awfully suspicious.

“Billy,” she calls and he hums in acknowledgement but doesn't move. Agatha stifles a laugh and puts Nicky down. What did he do now, and also where is her wife? She's the usual suspect. “Teen,” she calls him, like every other time he's being specially teenager-y, which she finds greatly amusing.

Billy turns, slowly, an excuse already at the tips of his lips. He stops when she laughs at the sight of his face and motions for him to come closer so she can inspect him.

His usual heavy eyeliner look has been replaced by face paint in black and white, making him look like a lost member of Kiss. Agatha pats his cheek and chuckles. He's grown to be too much like his mama, terrible influence that she is.

“We need to get this fixed up. Where's your mama?”

Billy deflates, “Oh thank God. I used this paint I got from the store yesterday with Eddie, he told me to just ask you guys but you got home so late and mama-”

“Slow down, kiddo. Go wash your face and we can brainstorm ideas for the next face paint, okay?” If this is gonna be a thing, a thing she blames her wife for entirely, she's gonna make sure he looks good at least, and also that he's using stuff that won't ruin his face. He has big dreams of following her footsteps.

He nods, and leaves the room typing rapidly on his phone. Agatha watches him go and sits down on the table next to Nicky, who's been guarding her breakfast plate for her. He eyes the strawberries on her pancakes with a little too much attention.

“This is cute,” she comments, popping a strawberry into her mouth. The fruit had been arranged with the shape of a heart, “Did you help make this, bud?”

Nicky nods, and Agatha picks a second strawberry with her fork and passes it to him making plane noises.

He giggles, taking the strawberry with his hands, “Mommy, I'm too old for this. I'm a big boy now”

“Nooo,” she stretches her words dramatically, and he laughs, covering her face with sticky strawberry hands. “You're my baby,”

Nicky rolls his eyes, and Agatha will have a conversation with her wife about it. Speaking of which.

“Where's your mama?”

While he thinks over what should be a pretty straightforward answer, she cleans his sticky hands with a napkin.

“Nick”

“Yes, mommy?” He says, his big brown eyes stare at her with mischief. Just like his mama.

“Honey, I'm home,” her wife's voice comes from further in the house, like she was waiting in the shadows for the perfect moment to make her entrance. And in the second she looks away, trying to figure out where's she's coming from, Nicky has run out of the room.

Agatha turns her interrogation to her wife just as she enters the kitchen, not giving her the chance to speak, “Explain”

“Buenos días, hermosa,” Rio ignores her question and takes Nicky's vacated seat. She grabs Agatha's discarded fork and cuts her pancakes for her, then feeds it to her, “How did you sleep?”

“Rio,” Agatha swallows her pancakes. They're so good, but now is not the time to focus on her wife's culinary talents. She's got questions, and little answers, something is off, “What's going on?”

Her wife senses her discomfort, Agatha has never been one for surprises. Rio puts the fork down. “I would've told you yesterday, but you came home late and fell asleep halfway through the movie you insisted you wanted to watch with the kids”

“I did want to,” she offers lamely, and Rio's eyes soften. Agatha would think is sweet if she wasn't still in the dark about whatever the hell is going on, “Now explain”

Rio laughs, interlacing their fingers together over the table. Suspicious.

“So, the band got called to cover a show tonight”

Agatha groans, beginning to see where this is going. “I'm playing tonight”

Rio nods, “Yeah, the festival…me too?”

Why does she frame it like a question, when it's obviously not, Agatha will never know. But fuck, today is not going up to plan.

She can't wait till her band hears about it.

Her band, Sister of the Craft, is closing their tour playing on back to back nights at music festival. It had been a last minute addition, a toss up between playing home or in New York, and Agatha sided with the festival just to be closer to her family. She could do back to back shows if she got to sleep in her bed every night.

She had spoken to Rio about it, and then Billy and Nicky, and had gotten everyone tickets and VIP passes. Billy had been over the moon about it, he was going over a goth phase, for the past two years which Rio had pointed out made it not a phase, but some of his favorite bands were going to be playing at the festival too, so she had gotten tickets for his boyfriend, Eddie, too.

Everything had been ready for today for months. And now this.

Rio's band.

She loved her wife, she really did.

But she hated her band.

It was funny, really. It hadn't started out that way.

Their bands were different. Sisters of the Craft had been a household name since the 90s, a band Agatha had founded with her nemesis, Jen, Lillia, who didn't like her most days, and Alice for the talent. They each were the best in their craft and had come together joined by their mutual desire to break it in the industry, and occasionally to one up each other, although that was usually her and Jen. They had decided who they were twenty years ago, and had stuck to that, unafraid to try new sounds but keeping their essence the same.

Rio's band on the other hand was…where her wife went with all experimental ideas. A place to try new sounds and experiment, like she hadn't been able to work as a songwriter and producer for other artists, for Agatha. The sound she had come up with was…interesting. Loud, dark, and just a hint of whimsy. Agatha could recognize Rio's talent and still be surprised by her success. It helped that Rio had created her whole artistic persona just to antagonize her.

She had also poached Alice right from under her.

They had made a deal, when Rio decided to go on her little adventure and become a singer herself. Agatha was very private about her life, despite her very long career she barely spoke publicly about her family. Their marriage had been a secret for several years until she slipped up in an interview, which Rio found hilarious. So they had agreed they'd keep their professional lives separate from each other.

Rio had accepted gladly, too easily. Agatha had learned why three days later when Rio introduced her to Lady Death, and Agatha almost had a stroke.

Later, she would admit to herself the face make up was hot. Hot in a way only Rio could pull off.

She didn't tell Rio, nor did she have to. If the looks her wife always gave her when Lady Death came up in conversation were any indication.

No one knew Rio was the lead singer of The Court, it was an industry secret only few were privy to, and they were both happy with that. It had never been an issue. They didn't move around the same circles professionally, and they had only even been mentioned to one another publicly on a couple occasions.

Rio loved to antagonize her as Lady Death, and Agatha, as the lead singer of Sisters of the Craft, avoided questions about it with all the grace of someone who knows she's better than the freak in full face makeup.

A freak she loves, but people don't need to know that.

And now this.

For the first time both of them are playing in the same festival, on the same day.

Her stress over the whole secrecy surrounding her morning dissipates. She's excited at the prospect of going head to head with her wife and coming out on top.

Yes, this isn't a competition, it doesn't have to be. What are they gonna compete about? Who can get more people to sing along? Who will be the talk of social media later that night? They will figure something out.

She knows her wife.

Agatha looks up at Rio, her expression morphing from dread to a predatory smile. “You think you're ready for the big leagues, Vidal?”

And her wife knows her.

Rio's smug smile rivals her own. She picks up a strawberry from Agatha's forgotten pancakes and feeds it to her. “Baby, I've been bringing the big leagues to their knees every other day for years now, I think I've got this”

“Oh, every other day is it now?” She asks.

“Could be tonight,” Rio comments casually.

Agatha leans closer, her hands quickly find their way to cup Rio's face. Her wife's smirk is too annoying, even now.

“I'm gonna enjoy wiping your stupid face paint off your face”

Rio's smirk remains undeterred, she uses Agatha's hold on her face to bring them closer together, nose to nose. “You promise?” She asks, and kisses her before Agatha has the chance to answer.

Agatha can admit she's a competitive person. She likes winning. The satisfaction of knowing she's the best, even if it's at something dumb, she wants it. Craves it.

She's made it this far in her career out of spite, to prove everyone who doubted her she could do it.

That kind of fire never stops burning. She hopes it stays that way forever. But her kids, her wife have softened her edges. She's found a place to put her sword down, and she likes it.

She's not against some friendly fire, though. She likes Rio pushing her buttons even though she will never admit it.

She enjoys no one knowing Lady Death is all hers, knowing she alone can see what's beneath the makeup.

This is what power feels like, she thinks. And she loves it.

“OH MY GOD,” Billy's high pitched yell breaks them apart. Agatha pushes Rio off of her, slapping her hands as her wife laughs like this was somehow all part of her plan.

“I hate you,” she mutters, before turning the two boys, “You too, conspiring with your mama-”

“With whom you were being super gross with not five seconds ago,” Billy points out, “And she’s better than you at bribery”

“And proud of it,” Rio admits easily. She takes advantage of Agatha’s shock to give her a quick kiss and stands up, herding the boys outside. “C'mon kiddos, gotta get you ready to show your support to the better band. Billy, you're already ready. Nicky, how do you feel about mama painting your face?”

They're gone before Agatha can hear his response, but she can guess how that will go.

She trusts Rio though, she'll play Agatha's games, but she'll take care of her too, somewhere in the midst of it. When Agatha has gone too deep into win or die mode.

“Agatha!” Rio's voice carries through the house, and Agatha begins to follow it blindly. “Can you do my makeup? Your skills at drawing lifelike skulls are unparalleled, babe”

Agatha rolls her eyes, but follows her voice.

She has to make her wife look hot for when she loses.

“You're not technically new artists,” Jen says through gritted teeth. “So, shut up and smile.”

The photographers ask for a photo with just the band and Alice, and begrudgingly Agatha drops her hand on Rio’s waist.

“You'll be okay?” She asks, ignoring the murmurs from the band.

Rio rolls her eyes fondly, fixing Agatha’s tie like it's just an excuse to touch her. “Go, baby.”

The minutes it takes for everyone to get over their fucking selves feel eternal—first the photographers and then the reporters and their stupid camera crews. Agatha feels Rio’s eyes on her as she smiles for the camera and talks nonsense, because really, Agatha is not sure what she's saying. The only thing on her mind is for these interviews to be done so she can go back to her wife in that sinful dress, all alone on the red carpet.

"Rio, lovely to see you on the red carpet. You look stunning,” someone says out of nowhere and Agatha snaps her head to where she heard the voice.

Some man in a glittery suit with slicked-back hair is giving Rio his most charming smile. Don't they know her wife is gay?

"Thank you," Rio says to the man, finding Agatha’s eye over her head. "I always get pulled into these things, you guys just never notice. I like that."

Agatha has to hold back her laughter, and Jen steps on her foot.

"Well you're looking hard to miss today,” the glitter-suit guy says.

"Thanks, my wife picked it for me," Rio replies without missing a beat.

Jen elbows her, and Agatha returns the gesture while answering the next question with a forced smile.

When Agatha finds Rio again she's being accosted by another reporter.

"You're sporting an injury similar to Lady Death,” the woman says with a knowing look. “And showing up on Agatha Harkness’ arm– anything you wanna say to that?"

Rio shrugs, a smile tugging at her lips. "Not really."

"You think we'll ever find out who Lady Death is?" The woman tries again, like a dog with a bone.

"That's for her to decide,” Rio replies diplomatically, catching Agatha’s gaze and tilting her head subtly in a silent request for a rescue.

"And you don't know when or if she ever will?" The reporter insists, getting a little pushy.

Agatha makes a quick exit– she never did care to be polite after all. She has a reputation as a raging asshole to uphold.

"How's April 31st?" Rio answers, just as Agatha reaches her side.

The reporter looks taken aback, "Wait, are you—no because April—"

Ignoring the blabbering woman, Agatha wraps an arm around Rio’s waist, letting her drop some of her weight on her. She feels Rio relax against her, her smile a little less tense.

“We should go inside, hon.” Agatha doesn't wait for the reporter to cut in, focusing entirely on her wife.

“Lead the way, then, handsome." As they step away, Rio turns to the reporter with a grin. "Have a good night.”

The ceremony takes too long to start, and the host isn't even funny—just like every year. But at least she has Rio by her side this time around (Jen is to her right, and Agatha does her best to ignore her).

“Enjoy the moment, my love,” she whispers in Rio's ear when a member of the academy steps onto the stage for the achievement award. “Before you have to live knowing you're not the only one who's seen my butt.”

Rio snorts, her smile playful as the man starts talking on stage. “Me and how many models before?”

Agatha pretends to look shocked, just as the cameras approach the band. “Well, that was my twenty-year-old butt.”

“You've gotten hotter with age,” Rio says without missing a beat.

Agatha laughs. “You know there are people on TikTok who read artists' lips at events and stuff.”

“Well, I'm sure they won't be disappointed in us,” Rio says, just as the camera guy gets the group into focus.

Agatha rolls her eyes at the camera. She's not letting anyone think she's happy about this. Which is why, moments later, when she's on stage, she delivers her speech with just enough malice to pass for acceptable.

"I don't usually write speeches because that's bad luck, but then I was told this one wasn't optional,” she starts, holding the stupid award in her hand.

Her bandmates—and Alice (how did Alice get on the stage?)—roll their eyes, amused behind her, like they just knew Agatha would pull something like this.

Agatha smirks, loving the attention of a room waiting with bated breath. She catches her wife's eye across the stage. “So, I'd like to dedicate this win to my wife—the only real achievement worth celebrating. The Grammys can wait another thirty years before trying for special awards again. Thank you.”

When she makes it back to her seat, Rio grabs her hand but doesn't say anything. Agatha catches her smiling and lets out a breath she didn't know she was holding.

Before she can say anything, though, the not-funny host steps back on stage and announces their music video.

“Now, a surprise—Agatha Harkness and Lady Death's first collaboration: the ‘You Ruined Women for Me’ music video premiere.”

A cameraman approaches them again.

Agatha squeezes Rio's hand and smirks. This should be fun.

***

“Afterparty?” Agatha asks, wiggling her eyebrows as the car drives away.

Rio drops the award on her lap, moving her hands to Agatha's chest– over the lapels of her jacket– and leaves them there. “Are you planning any funny business?”

“Only freaky business,” Agatha says, mock serious. “After having defiled the grammys with my naked butt, I wanna defile this stupid award before Jen tries to steal it.”

“And how do you plan on doing that?” Rio asks curiously,  a little bit encouraging.

Agatha stares at the trophy for a long moment, “Overly ornate coffee mug?”

“Shot glass?” Rio counters, holding the award closer and inspecting it from side to side.

Agatha cackles, “This is why I married you.”

“I married you for your butt,” Rio deadpans. “I want a burger when this is all over. And fries.”

At the afterparty they're received with applause and whistles– and Jen, rolls her eyes, falling to hide her proud smile. Lilia raises her martini to Agatha in congratulations.

Agatha takes a dramatic bow, soaking in the attention before using her newfound power to cut through the line at the bar and order a beer.

When she returns to her wife's side, Rio raises an eyebrow at her. Agatha shrugs, passes the bottle to her, and offers her a hand.

They walk around for a while, chatting with other artists they rarely see. Agatha keeps her hand on Rio, enjoying the warmth of her wife's skin under her fingertips, the way Rio leans closer and doesn't shy away from being seen with her.

People here know, or assume and Rio doesn't seem to care. Agatha hasn't had a drink but she feels drunk. Every interaction feels charged somehow. People she's known and seen in passing over awards and events give them knowing smirks, and the more bold tease them.

No one says the quiet part out loud.

That Rio is Lady Death.

No one cares.

Agatha feels bold, she feels stupid. Like she's twenty-two and in love again, ready to burn the world to the ground to get the woman she loves.

“How are you feeling?” She asks Rio, tugging her to a secluded corner, beneath a pillar covered in golden drapery. Ridiculous.

Rio searches her face, her eyes soften. “I like this,” she murmurs, wrapping her free hand around Agatha's waist  under her suit jacket. “I could get used to this part of fame, at least.”

Agatha nods, her mind hazy, thinking of all the years of parties they’ve missed. She lets Rio pull her closer, hides her face in the crook of her shoulder, and inhales.

Oh, she's so far gone. She places a kiss on Rio's neck and prays to the heavens above that her wife is stronger than she is. Agatha is not God's strongest soldier.

Rio lets out a slow, measured breath. Her hold on Agatha’s waist loosens for a moment before tightening again.

“Kiss me,” she whispers against the shell of Agatha's ear.

Fuck .

Agatha throws caution to the wind. She's holding her Grammy and Rio's cane in one hand, but she hooks her free hand around Rio's waist and pulls her closer, bringing their mouths together.

One kiss never hurt anyone.
