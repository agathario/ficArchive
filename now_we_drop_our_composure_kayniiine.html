Summary

For the most part since the unexpected adoption of a baby raccoon, life for Rio and Agatha has gone back to normal.

Rio's return to work had been seamless, but when she buckles and does an atypical favor that goes against a very strict personal rule, things start to fall apart.

While having Zap in their lives has most assuredly brought with it an inherent happiness, life still goes on and it's never truly easy all of the time. The only way Rio knows to move forward it side-by-side with her wife, no matter what that entails.

Now We Drop Our Composure

Lunchtime had rolled around again in the middle of the work day. It was the one chance a day Rio Harkness-Vidal had when she was at work to talk to her wife. That day, the email she saw when she opened her phone immediately assured one thing:

Agatha was going to be pissed.

Having taken a couple of extra days off, Rio had finally returned to work about a week and a half after Zap had come into hers and Agatha's lives. She had known it was an obligatory duty as an adult, but she was jealous that Agatha had been able to secure a couple extra weeks of working from home so she could keep an eye on Zap.

The weekend after her return, Rio had gotten a text message from her cousin, asking for a favor. Rio had been cautious, obviously not agreeing until she was provided more information, but familial requests held a little more weight. That had come and in turn, it left Rio feeling uneasy. Her boyfriend's cousin had a Jaguar that was dangerously close to having the timing chain snap. Rio knew it was easy enough to do in a day off, but it meant breaking a rule she had kept in place for nearly a decade.

Rio didn't do side jobs for men.

There were a variety of reasons for this, but the primary was that they seemed to think they could take advantage of her in that their haggling knew no end. They also thought that somehow, even though they were the ones who had not been licensed and certified and spent hours upon hours learning all there was to know about the profession, they knew best and that even the impossibly fair price she was quoting them was still too high.

A couple of other reasons had helped her keep the rule intact, but her cousin's begging had worn on her and she had eventually caved.

As Rio had predicted it would be, it had been a mistake.

The job had gone smoothly; despite her employment, Rio had learned a lot of different things about a lot of different cars over the years. With a little more research in any given situation, she could fix most things on most cars (but would never touch a Tesla with a gun to her head). Still, she had worked with efficiency throughout the majority of that Saturday afternoon and had completed the job with minimal breaks in almost seven hours.

She had quoted him fairly low -- her cousin had informed her that the dealership had wanted eight grand -- at just over five thousand for both the chain replacement and an oil change, which she felt was more than reasonable. He had agreed and surprisingly enough, he had not tried to haggle or barter with her to get it any lower. He had even handed his card over with a smile, but it was one that had set her nerves on edge in spite of the fact that she could not explain why.

When she had returned home that night, she had informed Agatha that he had been polite enough, though he had made a couple of comments about her work that hadn't necessarily been rude, but he hadn't needed to make them. It had otherwise been a professional exchange and she had assumed that was that.

It was now Tuesday, Rio was on her lunch break, and she was staring down at the email telling her that someone had reported her for fraudulent charges and that the five thousand dollar deposit was tied up while it was disputed.

The moment she read it for the second time, it actually sank in.

That fucking dickhead had cancelled the charge. Seven hours of work and over three thousand dollars in parts and he had disputed the fucking charge.

Rio was upset. She was angry with herself because she had gone against a decade long rule for this very fucking reason and there she sat, five grand short and the knowledge that Agatha was going to lose her goddamn mind.

When Rio had agreed on that Saturday morning, Agatha had found more than enough to say about it.

"I really think you should give it a second thought."

"Are you sure it's a good idea?"

"Something about it just feels weird."

"Why would your cousin even ask? She knows how you feel about that. She says he's a good guy and so you believe her because you love her and then... What if he's not a 'good guy?'"

That was the most vivid one circling around Rio's head as she stared down at the screen, eyes stinging with her fury. 

Rio tried to be a good person. She tried to do the right thing, to help people and the environment. She gave a shit and in moments like that one, she found herself wishing that she could be more like her wife, more like the woman who would blatantly shove a middle finger in the face of someone who pissed her off. She wished that she had the gall to text her cousin and ask her what the fuck she thought this all was, but Rio just sat there and stared at that stupid email.

There was not enough time on her break to deal with it other than to click the button to appeal and she would have to stow her phone for the rest of her shift, but she knew one thing for certain: She could not tell Agatha over the phone.

Having that conversation before working four more hours was not going to end well for her and she knew it. She was not keeping things from Agatha but she also knew that timing could be everything, especially when it came to her own sanity. Rio would always love Agatha, but trying to work while thinking about Agatha being at home angry wouldn't serve her well and when it came to her job, she absolutely had to keep those feelings separated.

As she went to close her phone so she could prepare to return, a message bubble appeared at the top of the screen, notifying her that Agatha had sent her an image with a small text message attached that read: 'Had it express delivered. He loves it.'

Rio opened the message and despite the fact that her stomach was in nervous knots and her skin was flushed and hot with her anger, the image she found there was enough to knock her out of the moment she had been existing in to deposit her somewhere else, somewhere softer.

Agatha had taken the photo of herself, clearly holding the phone up high enough that she could get a shot of the majority of her torso. On it was a grey and white bjorn. Inside of said bjorn, Zap was safely nestled, playing with the necklace that was hanging around Agatha's neck.

The reality of it all was actually pretty silly, if not a little stupid. Her wife was at home, working with a raccoon held to her chest so that she could keep him out of trouble. Meanwhile, she was about to spend the next four hours elbow deep in the engine of a 911 while she thought about all of the different ways she could try to tell her wife that her own stupidity had, indeed, come back to bite her in the ass.

She stood and pushed her chair in, typing back a quick response.

"We love to see it. Already sending the picture to the locked folder and deleting the message. Be home later. I love you."

The response came as Rio was about to stow her phone in her locker.

"You know me too well. I love you too, get home to me safely. No cops."

Rio shook her head with a chuckle and tossed the phone inside, shutting the metal door with a loud clang before she secured her lock around the handle.

Once her coveralls were all the way back on, she'd headed back to work. She had tried to focus but instead, she had managed to isolate a specific portion of her brain where she could do little more than stew over the very difficult and very uncomfortable conversation she knew she would be having with her wife once she got home and showered.

All Rio wanted was to go home and relax, to play with the new laser pointer she'd bought that Zap toddled around following, loving it in the same way a cat might. All she wanted was to make a bowl of the potato soup Agatha had started that morning before Rio had departed for work. All she wanted was a nice calm evening where she didn't have to worry about the emotional fallout of someone else's selfish choices.

What Rio wanted didn't really matter. What she needed was to be honest, even knowing that it was going to set Agatha off and the night would likely be filled with shouting and slamming dishes and doors. Even though Rio knew that anger wouldn't be directed at her, she was still not certain that she was ready to be in the immediate vicinity of Hurricane Agatha.

The sliver of her mind that could not tear away from dreading the inevitable was sometimes quieted a bit as she focused on the task before her and made sure she was doing everything correctly. Rio was a perfectionist when it came to her craft and it was something she knew she would never deviate from, even when her personal life was on the verge of falling to shambles. She could compartmentalize, could box those thoughts away for the most part and operate as the cog in the machine she needed to be when she was within the walls of that dealership.

Finally, 5pm came and Rio clocked out, climbing into the Demon, though she did not immediately start the engine. Instead, she was still stuck in her uncertainty of what might happen when she got home. 

The only way to know was to get there and find out.

With a sad sigh, Rio turned the car on and put it into drive so she could begin the journey home, still not having made an actual decision on what she was going to say. The possibilities were endless, but it was likely she would simply just wing it and go from there. 

She considered stopping for chocolates or wine that might boost Agatha's mood, but she did not want Agatha to take it as any kind of bribe to not have very valid feelings toward the situation Rio was going to be presenting to her.

That was the thing. Rio overthought everything.

Even a kind gesture such as sweets or alcohol could be viewed as an attempt to soften a reasonable reaction. It was the small things that could have the biggest misperceptions tied to them and Rio knew better than to risk it, even if there was the tiniest chance that it could help. The possibility of it blowing up and making things worse was not one that Rio felt to be worth taking.

There was this tiny ball of dread that was rolling back and forth in Rio's stomach as she pulled into her garage and hit the button to shut the door behind her. She knew that choosing to sit out in her car would only prolong the inevitable and while she was nervous, she knew that it absolutely had to be done. The least she could do was buy herself a little bit more time with a shower.

Making her way inside, Rio had not expected to open the back door to find Zap immediately on the other side, as though he had known it was going to open inward and had dodged so that he could await Rio when she stepped inside. He was on his back legs, tiny arms reaching up for her in greeting.

For just a moment, all of that worry and anxiety that had been tangling her up since her lunch seemed to simply vacate her form as she leaned down and scooped the small raccoon into her arms, cradling him on his back in the crook of her elbow. Fingers on the opposing hand were quick to scratch at his round little belly, clearly having gotten his fill of eggs and grapes that day.

"Hello, Mr. Zap," she cooed at him, raising her hand so that she could drag a light finger between his eyes. "Have you behaved yourself today?"

In a reaction that had lost its surprise, Zap answered.

Ekekekekekekek.

The grin Rio wore happened in a flash and she felt herself soften somewhere in her middle. She had been looping around herself, over and over again, in an attempt to figure out a situation before it happened and it had only caused more stress and more anxiety. This was going to occur one way or another and there was no way out of it, but at the end of it?

Even if Agatha was angry, there was still Zap to tend to. He would keep her occupied, help her stay distracted. He could be the one thing that might actually keep Rio from blaming herself and her inability to keep firm on her boundaries. She'd gotten better over time, but it seemed fitting that the one time she chose to cave was the same instance that reminded her why she never did.

"My love?" came Agatha's call from the living room. 

With Zap still held in her arm, Rio paced forward into view, her lips curling despite the sinking feeling that suddenly formed in the bottom of her stomach. "Hey. I had a welcoming committee today. Just took a few minutes to rub his belly." Rio glanced down at Zap, her smile growing slightly before she looked back up at Agatha. 

She shuffled forward and bent over to carefully place a delicate kiss to Agatha's puckered lips, a ritual they practiced every night when Rio got home from work as to not stain anything before she could strip and wash off. "Gonna shower, but I'll be quick. I love you. And I missed you today. Made the picture extra exciting." She was grinning as she stood back up, taking half a step back from the couch in caution.

Agatha hands lifted, fingers flexing in demand to be handed the animal Rio was still cradling in her arm. Gently, Rio made the exchange, letting the warmth at the act win out over the bone chilling cold her anxiety had been bestowing upon her the majority of the afternoon. That gentle smile was still on her lips as she turned to depart.

"I love you," Agatha called after her. "And hurry up! Soup's ready!"

Rio chuckled softly to herself as she made the trek up to their room to strip down and shower off, spending a few extra minutes tending to her face in an attempt to make sure every bit of grease she could reach was removed to prevent any break-outs. She finished and toweled off, pulling on a t-shirt with the neck cut and a pair of joggers that were more for comfort than anything. Her damp hair hung over her shoulders and for a moment, she considered pulling it up but decided to wait.

The destination was downstairs, but before Rio reached the doorway, she hesitated and instead headed into the closet. A moment later, she returned with the ten pound blanket tossed over her shoulder and made her way down to the living room.

Upon seeing the comfort item, something in Agatha's face shifted and that bright blue dimmed to a shade nearer navy than cerulean. She pulled her legs up a little so that Rio could have a space to sit, though her interrogation came with little warning.

"What's wrong?" Agatha asked with no hesitation, already having gauged that something had upset Rio. Maybe she'd been able to hold up that facade upon her arrival home, but knowing she needed comfort that would be a visible sign of her distress was a chance she had taken anyway.

For a long moment, there was nothing but quiet until the soft sound of Zap's claws on Rio's blanket filled the air. She leaned forward and grabbed him up so that she could place him on her lap, though he was not ready to be still. He circled for a moment and then stretched up the front of her torso, his little hands meeting side by side directly over her sternum as he settled there. It looked rather uncomfortable, but his eyes slid shut and he seemed content.

Agatha lightly prodded at the side of Rio's thigh with socked toes, a silent willing for Rio to meet her gaze.

The very second Rio offered compliance, her lips turned down at the corners. "I got an email on my lunch today," she decided to start with, though her lips twisted with her nerves immediately after.

"And I take it this was not a good email," Agatha deduced, reaching forward to close her laptop. She set it atop the coffee table and turned back to Rio, sitting up a little more to show that Rio had her full attention.

"It was not," Rio answered, fighting the overwhelming urge to look at Zap in an attempt to find some form of courage. "It was from the bank. The five grand that I charged Riley's boyfriend's cousin for the work on the Jag was disputed and is likely going to be refunded. I replied that I wanted to appeal but I haven't gotten anything else."

Rio swore she could almost feel it. She could feel the singe of her arm hairs as Agatha's temper flared. If she couldn't feel it, she knew it was happening by the immediate tightening that happened in every muscle in Agatha's body. Not only that, but the breath Agatha sucked in was sharp and her facial expression was nothing but cold steel.

Unable to take the heat, Rio finally broke and diverted her gaze down to the animal flattened to her abdomen, asleep. She was quiet a moment, her anxiety mounting the longer Agatha was choosing not to say anything. It meant that Agatha was thinking, that she was trying to find a way to go about this that was not going to make it any harder on Rio, but her anger seemed to be winning out.

Another silent moment ticked by before Agatha found her voice, cold and hard as she requested information.

"What's his full name?" she asked, though there was an eerie calm settling over her words.

"I... Bryce something..." Rio mumbled, lifting a hand to drag her fingers down Zap's back, more to soothe herself than him considering he was already asleep.

"Last name?" Agatha pressed, voice still sharp though it was painfully obvious she was putting a valiant effort into not snapping at Rio.

"I don't know," Rio admitted, rubbing some of Zap's fur between her thumb and forefinger.

Agatha drew in a slow breath and although Rio was not looking at her, she could tell that she was so fucking angry.

"Can you please find out?" she asked and again, that lack of warmth caused Rio's stomach to twist anxiously. 

"I can text Riley," Rio voiced, though her tones were low and just a few decibels over a whisper.

"Please do," Agatha returned before she scooted forward. Rio could still feel the tension, even as Agatha leaned forward and placed a surly but light kiss to her cheek. 

"I'm going to change and go into the gym room to lift for a little bit. Please do come let me know when you find out, okay?"

This time when Agatha spoke, her words were a little softer around the edges and had lost the burning fury that had initially taken root in Agatha's chest. She was still close, close enough that Rio could feel every exhale she released on her cheek.

"I will," she answered, eyes still fixed on the slumbering Zap on her abdomen.

"Hey," Agatha whispered softly. "Can I have a kiss first?"

Rio's lips worried for a moment before she lifted her chin and turned to face her wife, offering the gentlest brushing of their lips, a communication that she was sorry this was even happening, an apology that she hadn't listened to Agatha in the first place and now... Well, now Agatha was stepping out of routine to work out because she was carrying a new aggression that had been instilled by Rio's foolish choices.

Voicing those thoughts would have been met with admonishment from Agatha, so Rio stayed quiet other than to tell Agatha the truth.

"I love you. I'll come in when I find out."

The corners of Agatha's mouth twitched upward just barely and she placed another, firmer kiss to Rio's lips.

"I love you too, handsome. Just let me know." In a movement too quick for Rio to process at first, Agatha diverted her face upward so that she could press a quick peck to Rio's forehead before she pushed herself off the couch and headed up the stairs to do as she had stated she would.

Rio listened to the twelve steps Agatha took to get to the top floor and exhaled a breath she had not realized she'd been holding. It all started to settle, the last several minutes replaying again and again on the screen at the forefront of her mind. 

What the fuck had actually just happened?

It had seemed simple enough to follow in the moment, but now that Rio could look back? Agatha's anger had been there, sure, but it had not presented itself in typical fashion. Agatha had not thrown anything, had not slammed anything. She had not yelled or even raised her voice much at all. Instead, she had made her calm demands for information and then, she had excused herself to isolate enough to process her own feelings.

Rio had never found an issue with communicating when she needed to step outside of a moment to regulate her own emotions, but for Agatha it was actually something big and noteworthy. Agatha never held much of a regard for how people saw her when she was in her anger, especially when it pertained to the manipulation and abuse that seemed to befall Rio as a result of her soft heart. Even when it came to Rio, she felt free to express that anger in front of her. 

That was why Rio was so confused. Agatha could have had her own meltdown, flashed those emotions in an extravagant way and Rio would never have judged her. But she didn't, and something so drastically different from the norm had left Rio, fingers gingerly stroking soft fur as she tried to understand the breakdown of what could possibly be going on in Agatha's head.

With the hand not petting Zap, she produced her phone from the pocket of her shorts and typed a short text message to her cousin.

"Hey. I need Mack's cousin's last name please." 

It was succinct and to the point, a direct request for information, but Rio should have known it would never be that easy.

"Why? Is everything okay?" came Riley's quick response.

Rio's lips pressed together firmly, but she glanced away from her phone screen to take in the sight of Zap as he shifted and rolled slightly, but did not actually wake up. Life had been so good since she had brought him home and he had been the perfect way to fill her time, but there was also a responsibility there, one to take care of him. While it wasn't as though they were hard up in any way, Rio worked to provide for what she cared about. That had primarily been Agatha, but now included Zap. 

Even if she was willing to shirk her personal worth, she was not willing to sacrifice the idea that the work she did, both on and off the clock, deserved compensation. Her abilities were uncommon and utilizing them to make a living was something she knew she was good at, but being used and taken advantage of was a pitfall that seemed to happen along the way.

Rio sighed and looked back to her phone, composing a reply. 

"Can you please tell me? I just need to look into something and I don't have the credit card receipt with me cause it's at the garage in town."

Three little dots appeared inside of the bubble at the bottom screen, but then they were away. There were there again, then disappeared once more. Rio was tired of the anticipation.

"Riley. Just tell me. It's not a big deal."

Rio didn't actually know if she was lying about that last sentence. She knew Agatha wanted the information, but she did not know why, exactly. Perhaps plausible deniability would suit her best right then.

"It's Lockhart."

Despite the fact that she had gotten what she wanted, this didn't feel like victory to Rio. It felt fragile and unpredictable. If Agatha was being so calm about the thing that made her angriest in the world, what was it that she was actually thinking? What could be going through her head that had her acting so outside of herself? 

Rio typed one last reply and then locked her phone. 

"Thank you. I appreciate it."

Zap had started to stir a little more, his legs kicking in a way that caused his feet to accidentally catch in the waist of Rio's shorts, drawing the softest chuckle from her. Although she had gotten what she had been seeking, there was something deeply seated in Rio's middle that kept her planted on the couch, eyes fixed on Zap as her began to rouse more and more. Finally, he had gotten to his feet and slid off onto the couch, indicating that he wanted down.

Rio placed him on the floor and was not at all surprised when he waddled his way over toward the kitchen, an obvious indication that he was hungry. Rio followed and pulled out the container of moon drop grapes, a special treat for herself but one she did not mind sharing with Zap. She took the time to slice them up and toss them into a small bowl that she placed beside his bed for him to munch on while she tended to her next unsettling conversation with Agatha.

Zap slid right up to the bowl, hunching over so he could pick up grape pieces to devour them. Once she was sure he was content and occupied for at least the next few minutes, Rio turned and headed up the stairs, her destination being the second doorway on the right hand side of the hallway, the door already open and soft rock music coming from inside.

Rio was cautious as she poked her head around the frame, trying to gauge where Agatha's mood might be after the twenty or so minutes she'd had alone. She had perched at the end of the weight bench and was working through a set of curls on her right arm when she noticed Rio's head in the doorway.

"Hi," Agatha greeted, a prompting for Rio to reveal more of herself. She stepped into the room and offered her wife a tight smile, raising a hand to rest on the back of her neck, pinching and pulling the hair there.

"I um... I got the full name. It's Bryce Lockhart, but Agatha --" Rio was trying to offer a dismissal, trying to convince Agatha that she could be a big girl and handle it on her own, but Agatha waved her free hand to indicate the need for her to desist.

"That was all I needed. I was just curious." Agatha was speaking so calmly, like she wasn't teeming with absolute boiling rage. This could have been believable, had she not looked Rio in the eyes. 

Rio could always find exactly what she needed to know in Agatha's eyes. She could put on a mask, she could throw up a front, but Rio knew simply by finding those blues and searching for the truth inside of them. Right then, she could see those irises shimmering with that unadulterated anger. Agatha might have been putting on an air that this was all fine and everything would go on as normal, but something in Rio knew better.

"So... That's it? You're... Going to let me handle it?" Rio questioned as she moved further into the room to seat herself on the Pelaton, though she had no intention of actually using it. 

"Define 'handle it,'" Agatha responded, though her tone lacked any sense of dryness. 

"Going through the proper channels, appealing the dispute. It'll take a while, but... It's the right way to go about it," she explained. She knew that wasn't going to be a satisfactory answer to Agatha and that she would likely push back. In fact, she was ready for it as Agatha's lips parted for her to speak.

When she did, it felt like Rio had been punched in the chest.

"Okay," Agatha stated. With nothing else said, she got to her feet and stowed the dumbbell she had been using. The way she moved from the weight rack back to the bench to swipe up her water bottle was almost unnerving in its placidity. 

"Agatha," Rio said, her eyes searching her wife's profile for some form of understanding despite finding nothing. Agatha lifted her head, making eye contact as her brows raised as if to encourage Rio to keep going. "You're... Being really calm about all of this. Too calm. What... What's going on?"

Instead of providing an answer immediately, Agatha brought the water bottle to her lips and tilted her head back, taking a too-long pull from it. As she lowered it, she turned and paced over to where Rio sat on the bike, leaning in to press a kiss to her cheek.

"Nothing's going on, love. I was simply curious, that's all." She straightened up but raised a hand, knuckles brushing lovingly against Rio's cheek. "I'm going to shower. Why don't you grab yourself something to eat, because I know you haven't yet, and then bring Zap up and we can get in bed. We can watch that documentary you like about the identical triplets."

This ripples of white hot anger were still in those beautiful blue eyes, but Rio knew that everything Agatha was saying and suggesting was genuine, and that she truly appeared to be trying to let this go.

That was the appearance.

Something at the very root of Rio's brain stem knew better.

"And we can let this go?" Rio posed, doing her best to keep her lips from turning down at the corners. She was trying to be hopeful, and even if Agatha was about to lie to her, she was going to choose to believe her anyway.

"We can let this go," she returned with a nod. "Now, go eat. I cut up some green onions for you and put them in a container in the fridge." She raised a finger to tap playfully at the tip of Rio's nose, punctuating her request before she moved to take her leave.

"Agatha?" Rio voiced, causing Agatha to pause so she could look over her shoulder in question. "I love you."

Rio could see from her spot perched on the Peleton as Agatha's lips curled upward but her features remained smooth and soft. 

"I love you too, Rio. Now serious, eat!"

Rio chuckled, dismounting from the bike to follow Agatha out of the room, sharing a quick kiss before splitting directions as they entered the hallway.

She had done as she had been told and prepared herself a bowl of the soup, adding in the onions with a large pinch of cheese as well. With her bowl in one hand and a bottle of water in the other, Rio perched on the edge of the couch with the bowl resting on the coffee table. Her dark eyes lifted, finding Zap as gave a valiant attempt at scaling the cat tree.

There had been the thought that Rio would have had to turn to Zap to help her process the aftermath of an emotional situation. Right then, all she could really think about was the fact that Agatha was in the shower upstairs and all she wanted to do was lay in bed with her wife and their pet raccoon.

Oh how weird they would forever be, those Harkness-Vidals and their ability to be extraordinary.

When she had been most of the way through her soup, Rio heard the sound of the pipes as the water shut off and the pressure was alleviated. It was her beckoning, a call for her to finish her meal and gather Zap to retire for the evening. After the last couple of bites, Rio did just that -- as well as picking up a few random odds and ends that needed put in their places -- and found herself climbing the stairs while she held Zap to her chest.

Once inside their room, Rio found Agatha already in bed, glasses on the end of her nose as she quickly typed something on her phone. Once she finished, she was quick to lock it and toss it on her nightstand, blue gaze lifting to Rio as she wound her way around her side of the bed, an unsure smile on her lips at the way Agatha surveyed her like that.

"What?" Rio asked bashfully as she folded into bed, fully aware that she had not placed Zap in his bed. If they were going to lay in bed and watch a film for a little while, then it stood to reason that there was time to let him snuggle with them, too.

"Our life was perfect," Agatha stated, lifting up her arm so that Rio could fit herself beneath it. Once she was safely secured, there, Agatha continued. "We both have professions we love, we have a home with everything we need, and most importantly, we have each other. So..." Agatha reached up with her far hand and gently ran her fingers over the fur on Zap's back. "How the hell did we have no clue that a silly little raccoon could make it even better?"

In a series of things Rio had not been expecting to happen, Rio was finding herself a bit verklempt at such a statement being so freely delivered from Agatha's lips. Rio knew that she had been softening -- as was evident by that fucking bjorn she had bought to keep Zap close -- but to hear her voice it so plainly actually made Rio wonder.

Surely, this tempered reaction had nothing to directly do with Zap, but what if it was his presence and the way Rio had behaved since that had caused Agatha to pause and search for a cleaner and quieter route to deal with the emotions she was feeling at seeing her wife's kindness abused yet again? What if it was the fact that Rio had truly proved yet again how gentle she would forever be and Agatha had felt she needed to exhibit a reaction that still honored that while paying attention to her own feelings too?

Rio did not want to ask those questions because it was obvious that the issue had been put to rest, but she couldn't help think them anyway.

Rio's head turned, her torso tilting just enough so that she could take a quick kiss before she settled against the pillows again, her eyes dropping to Zap.

"In our defense, 'adopting a wild animal' was not on the honey-do, list. It was a side quest that worked itself into the main mission." Rio chuckled, loosening her hold on the small raccoon. It was enough to cause him to stir, climbing down from Rio's chest to pass from the top of her thigh to the top of Agatha's where he began to toy with the silver chain on her wrist in interest.

If she conceded her own sense of justice and pride and agreed? Well, they could have a better night ahead of them.

"Yes," Agatha finally agreed, her voice soft. "Go get what you need."

"Thank you," Rio returned before she set about acquiring the things she would need to at least stabilize Agatha's hand until morning. Sure, it wasn't great that it had been over twenty-four hours already, but that could be an addendum to the argument they would have the next morning.

After a couple of moments, Rio returned with an Ace bandage, a wash rag and a magazine she had folded in half. It wasn't the best, but without going out to the garage and spending ten minutes to find a piece of wood, it was going to have to do.

She spent the next five minutes working in complete silence, aided only by the fact that Agatha had chosen not to speak a word. She knew that Rio was upset and yet, she was still doing what she could to make sure that Agatha was taken care of. 

That was the way Rio loved Agatha.

Agatha could drive Rio up a wall, fluster her, aggravate her, give her reasons to question her sanity and yet, every single time Agatha's well being was in question, Rio put it all aside to tend to her and take care of her.

She said the same thing almost every time.

"We can't argue about it later if you die first."

Every time, Agatha rolled her eyes and laughed before pushing her away by her touch across Rio's forehead.

Almost every time.

Not that one, though.

When the secured the bone-shaped clips to the wrap, Rio took a moment to survey her work before deciding that it would be enough to keep it a little more protected until morning. It wasn't ideal but she knew that she was never winning the fight of an emergency room visit that late at night.

"This should do," Rio announced decidedly, letting go of Agatha's hand so that she could move to turn the light off again.

"Rio, wait," Agatha managed first, reaching out with her good hand to lightly squeeze her wife by the forearm in an ask for compliance. Rio stilled and turned back around, her eyes falling directly into Agatha's. She tipped her head forward slightly, urging Agatha to continue. "I'm... Sorry. I know why what I said upset you. And I... I just made a choice that I likely shouldn't have. And I wanted to keep you from worrying about that choice because it accomplished what I needed it to accomplish."

"Agatha, I never would have asked you to be--"

Agatha's hand shot up from Rio's arm to quiet her, to keep her from continuing down a road she did not need to spiral down.

"I know you wouldn't have. But that is on the list of minor things I would do for you. Had he not complied with my request, a greater fate awaited him. You are such a pure fucking human, Rio, and I will not idly stand by as the people of this world try so desperately to leech it out of you." Agatha's unsplinted hand reached up, fingertips gracing so lightly over Rio's cheek. "There is nothing I would not do for you. By such a justification, I should know better than to deprive you of any opportunity to feel that way about me. So... I am sorry for the way I acted, it was hypocritical of me."

Rio did not often get emotional, and there had been quite a few times when Agatha had truly made Rio feel seen, but right then, she knew exactly why she had fallen in love with and married Agatha Harkness-Vidal. 

It was because in a world that had convinced Rio she could never know what love actually was, Agatha had turned up and proved to Rio that true love was accepting and embracing the flaws, leaning the quirks, and loving the journey of acquiring all the bits of knowledge there were to be had. Agatha had shown Rio time and time again that true love was little more than total acceptance, and Agatha would always accept Rio for all that she was, even if she had to show her ass first.

While Agatha never apologized to anyone wasn't Rio, ones of that magnitude did not come about often. Rio could both see and hear how much she meant it and while she was still concerned about the severity of the injury to Agatha's hand, with an apology like that, she knew she had to let it go for the night. 

With a gentle sigh that eased some of the tension from her muscles, Rio leaned forward and pressed her lips to Agatha's in a patient, undemanding kiss that lingered for a few moments. She pulled away, those dark but emotional eyes fluttering open to behold the woman she loved more than had ever made sense.

"I love you, Agatha, and that means that when you lose your mind occasionally, I am still going to want to tend to you and love you in spite of it."

Agatha's lips turned down at the corners slightly and for just the quickest second, Rio considered that she might shed at least one tear, but that shine in Agatha's eyes disappeared with only a couple of blinks and she bestowed a kiss of her own upon Rio's lips.

"I love you too, and I'm always going to fight for you. I just... promise to let you survey the damage after next time, sans the protesting." A soft chuckle passed through Agatha's lips as she tossed a glance toward the pillows in suggestion. "It's late, we should try to sleep."

Rio gave a gentle nod and shifted around so that she could take one last peek at Zap to ensure he was still fairing well as he slept before she turned the light back off and snuggled up with Agatha, deciding to take on the duty as big spoon this time, allowing for Agatha to rest her injured hand a little better.

The air around them had fallen still and just as Rio started to slip into sleep, Agatha's whisper nestled warmly in her ear.

"Meeting you was the best thing that's ever happened to me."

It was so honest, so wrought with the pure intention Agatha felt for Rio. Even though Rio felt suspended between states of consciousness, a small smile still curled up her lips and she murmured the softest response.

"Know the feelin', my love."

*****

When Rio's consciousness began to sleep back into her form, the first thing she noticed was pressure on her back in four different places. And then, it was moving. With a grumble, Rio reached up over her shoulder, lightly swatting at the air until her fingertips graced Zap's fur.

"Mmmm, good morning to you, furson," Rio muttered as she fought the grogginess that still felt so heavy in her mind. She moved slowly, rolling over in a way that did not completely dislodge the raccoon and toss him over the edge of the bed. She had thrown him around enough as it was and did not need to make it any worse. 

Her head lulled to the left, finding Agatha's side of the bed empty.

As she laid there for a moment, finding the bit of wherewithal she would need to greet a lazy Sunday morning, Zap set to work trying to burrow under the blanket that was around her abdomen. She chuckled softly and lifted it up, granting him the ability to slide inside.

"Only for a few minutes. We gotta go see what mom is up to," she informed him, as though he had somehow gained the ability to understand English. Unlikely, but it wouldn't deter Rio from doing it again.

In giving him time to roam around beneath the duvet, Rio had given herself time to ponder what was likely facing her when she got out of bed and went downstairs to find out whatever Agatha was doing to busy herself that morning.

She had given it a decent amount of thought by the time she pulled herself out of bed and left Zap in the warmth of the blankets a little longer while she brushed her teeth and washed her face. Once finished, she fetched him from the bed and made it to the best of her abilities with one hand, the other clutching him to her chest.

As she descended the stairs, Rio drew in a slow breath through her nose, reveling in the smell of the dark roast coffee that was brewing.

"Good morning, my love," Agatha greeted from her place at the dining room table, her iPad propped up and on in front of her.

"Good morning," Rio returned through a yawn. She placed Zap on the floor next to his food and water bowls and moved further into the kitchen to prepare herself a cup of coffee. In the process of doing so, she felt it best to simply cut to the chase.

"So... What time would you like to leave for the hospital?" she posed, pouring a splash of cream into her mug. She capped it and turned around to place it back into the fridge. Her gaze cut across to the dining room and she could see immediately that Agatha was preparing to argue.

"Maybe in three days if it's still an issue?" she shot back.

Alright, Rio thought, if this was how she wanted to play it, it was time to bring out the big guns.

"Agatha, my dear," Rio voiced sweetly as she picked up her coffee to take a sip, slowly gliding over to where Agatha sat. She placed herself in her designated spot and met Agatha head-on. "If you don't get it looked at and it heals wrong..." Rio hesitated, glancing down at the makeshift splint there. "Well, you may never be able to finger me the right way ever again. And that's your dominant hand, so that would be... Unfortunate."

Agatha's eyes widened slightly before they fell to her own hand and then lifted back to Rio again.

"You're making that up," Agatha pressed, eyes narrowing like it might make Rio crack.

Rio did not buckle. It was one of the biggest reasons Agatha loved her.

"I'm not. If the bones happen to not set right, it could cause a lot of problems. Clearly I'm not a doctor, but I've seen hand injuries in my time working with heavy machinery. So... Are you willing to learn how to be ambidextrous?"

Agatha considered this for less time than it took her to blink.

"Get the keys and put Zap in the mud room."
