Evanora, certainly, had never found a reason to celebrate her only daughter’s entrance into the world.

 

Something like this had never happened to her before, and Agatha had been woefully unprepared to just how deeply, unstoppably, unquestionably in love such a gesture would make her feel. Especially coming from Rio. Agatha takes stock of herself for a moment, digging into the depths of the squishy organ currently taking up far too much real estate in the middle of her ribcage. She asks it a question and waits for an answer.

 

When she finds it, Agatha doubles down.

 

“Will you marry me, Rio?”

 

For a long moment, Rio blinks at her in stunned silence. Then she reaches out and sticks the party hat on top of Agatha’s head. Rio’s face breaks into a ecstatic grin as Agatha obligingly leans forward in order to allow her to tie the ribbons under her chin.

 

“Yes, Agatha,” she finally replies, eyes twinkling merrily. “I’d love to marry you. Happy birthday, mi amor.”

 

 

They formally tie the knot in May, holding an outdoor handfasting ceremony attended by an intimate gathering of their closest friends and family.

 

Lilia officiates, and Señor Scratchy hops down the aisle with the rings in a pouch affixed to the back of his tiny custom tuxedo. In a flowing dress the color of violets, Agatha follows him to where Rio waits at the altar in a three-piece emerald suit, smiling so widely and for so long that her cheeks burn all evening after.

 

 

“This is all your fault,” Agatha huffs, glaring down at Rio’s kneeling figure.

 

A wry smile pulls at the corners of Rio’s mouth as she finishes lacing Agatha’s left shoe.

 

“Of course, my love,” she agrees uncomplainingly, leaning forward to give the curve of Agatha’s giant belly a kiss before reaching for Agatha’s socked but otherwise shoeless right foot. “And I’m very sorry about that.”

 

“I was a small baby. Scrawny. A runt, if you will. There are pictures. Lots and lots of photographic evidence exists to prove it.” Agatha sniffs, tossing her head and adjusting the fabric of her purple sundress to lie just right across her stomach. At this point, Agatha only bothers with pants when forced—which is never. “So this has got to be all you.”

 

“Mhm. I’m sure.” Finished with her task, Rio gives Agatha’s knees an indulgent pat. “You’re all set, mi vida. Let me help you up.”

 

Agatha’s shuffle from the mudroom to the driveway takes about a million years. She’s seen viral videos of sloths crossing roads at faster speeds than she can currently manage. By the time she’s settled into the passenger seat of the car, she’s panting a bit. And little Nicky’s gotten restless. Great. She rubs at her stomach, hoping to soothe him out of playing kickball with anything important. Lately, it feels more and more like she’s growing a future soccer player in there. That, or one hell of a future martial artist.

 

“We don’t have to stay long, right?”

 

In the rearview mirror, the corners of Rio’s eyes crinkle cutely as their gazes meet.

 

“Right. We can leave as soon as you want.” In a carefully cajoling tone, Rio adds, “It’s only your baby shower.”

 

Agatha rolls her eyes. Fine. Maybe she shouldn’t have pushed it back until this late in the game.

 

“One hour.”

 

“Make it two, and I’ll give you a foot rub as soon as we get home.”

 

Her biggest newfound weakness. Rio’s been playing so dirty as of late. Agatha decides to return the favor. She fully turns her face towards Rio, batting her lashes and pouting. Even though she personally can’t see it, Rio’s been swearing up and down that Agatha’s pregnancy has left her glowing like the sun. This particular development has been doing diabolical things to both of their already high libidos.

 

“An hour and a half,” Agatha bargains, baring her teeth in a coy, winning smile. “And maybe I won’t be too tired to ride you later tonight.”

 

Rio’s eyes lose almost every trace of their usual honeyed brown.

 

“Done.”

 

Agatha gloats.

 

“You are such a soft touch.”

 

With a laugh, Rio puts the car into reverse and starts backing down the driveway.

 

“For you, amor? Always.”
