Summary

Holy shit,” Rio says aloud. Agatha is somehow, miraculously, alive. Rio feels her own heart start beating faster in her chest with each distinct thump of Agatha’s heart through the warm skin in her neck.

Agatha Harkness, of all people, against all odds, is alive. Rio has to amend her earlier statement. It’s just us out here. Just Rio and her stubborn, narcissistic, cruel, and gorgeous boss, trapped together miles and miles away from any other human life form.

Rio clutches the arms of her chair as she watches Mrs. HART’s romance novel fly out of the gaping hole, along with Billy’s iPad and Rio’s laptop. Good riddance to all three, Rio briefly thinks before remembering that she is about to die.

If Billy was smarter, he would maybe be more worried about his own life than poor Sharon’s. “Billy, hold on,” Agatha scolds. She herself is gripping the arm of Lilia’s chair, which is luckily on the right side of the plane, along with Rio’s.

“I’m trying,” Billy gasps, even as his fingers start slipping.

“Try harder.” Agatha yells. “We’re not fucking dying while my mother’s body is still warm.”

“Agatha,” Lilia says, somehow serene amidst this metal hunk of death plummeting towards the ocean. “Take my seat.”

“What?” Agatha shouts. Rio has to agree.

“You’ve got more years in you than I,” Lilia says, like it’s simple. Why anyone would give up their life for Agatha Harkness is beyond baffling to Rio, but she’s mostly focusing on not vomiting and also not dying. “You deserve to live some life without her.”

What the fuck is wrong with this woman?

Agatha says nothing in response to Lilia’s bizarre sacrifice, just nods seriously. Then she frantically whips her body around to try to take Lilia’s seat. In the process, her foot smacks into the table that Billy and Alice are holding, shaking it enough so that Alice, with no warning whatsoever, flies off into the night.

“Alice!” Billy shouts as his own grip fails. His hands scramble in the air to find something to hold onto, which is inconveniently, the arm of Rio’s chair. Up close, she can see he’s weeping. She looks away.

“Agatha, how could you?” Billy cries.

“I didn’t mean to!” Agatha is shouting, as she still is trying to maneuver her way into Lilia’s seat. “It was an accident.”

“Alice is dead!”

They are all about to be dead, Rio thinks. If only her last moments alive weren’t listening to the most annoying people in the world yell at each other.

“It’s too late,” Lilia says. Her hands find her seatbelt. “Agatha, try your hardest to survive. You always do.”

Then she unbuckles her seatbelt and surrenders to the elements, whipping her out of the plane. It would be funny if it weren’t so terrifying. And also confusing. For Agatha?

Agatha, who wastes no time pushing herself up into Lilia’s seatbelt and fastening the seatbelt.

“Lilia!” Billy shouts, like all he can do is scream the names of women as they die and he somehow survives. Then, eyes still watering, but hardening, he looks up to Rio. “Give me your seat.”

“What?” Rio manages to get out. “No!”

“Lilia just did it,” Billy says, “please, Rio, I need to stay alive, I need it. My mom can’t lose another—”

A particularly rough bump in the air makes one of his hands fall off the arm of Rio’s chair. Good. Rio isn’t a fan of this kid, but the quicker the better. But Billy has other plans, it seems, his hand coming back up to grab ahold of, not the chair, but Rio’s wrist.

“What the fuck?” she yells.

“Rio please,” he begs. His hand grows tighter on her wrist. “I’m your boss now, you have to give it to me.”

The circulation is being cut off in Rio’s hand and the plane is maybe seconds away from plummeting into the Pacific and he’s citing his job title?

Rio tries to tear her hand away but it doesn’t work.

“Let go,” she screams.

“No!” He screams back. His other hand reaches for her seatbelt.

No. In a last ditch effort, Rio uses her other hand to try to find anything to deter him. Her fingers strike metal and she smiles. There it is. Without a second to spare, Rio takes her butter knife, still covered in jam, and plunges it into the back of Billy’s hand.

Blood spurts out of Billy’s hand and he lets out a piercing scream, but it works. His hand lets go of hers and then he’s off into the night air. Rio hears the sickening crunch of his body hitting the tail of the plane.

“Billy!” Agatha screeches, but then the screaming turns more visceral as the remaining fraction of the plane rips in two, and Agatha and the front half of the plane fall into the ocean.

Rio barely has time to reflect on the fact that it’s just her now, she’s all alone and she also essentially just killed a man (in self defense!), before her own half of the plane hits the water and it’s all silence. After the sounds of screaming and bones hitting metal and the wind funneling into the plane, the quiet is almost pleasant. Then Rio remembers she’s underwater and has maybe two minutes of consciousness left in her before it’s all over.

That Agatha, whether she knows it or not, is changing on this island, becoming a real person, a better and more interesting version of her former self.

Rio’s thoughts are (thankfully) cut short by the unmistakable sound of running water. Rio’s feet take her further into the jungle before her mind can catch up and then she sees it: a mile or so into the forest on the East side of the island is a pool of clear blue water, with a waterfall flowing into it that feels like something out of a movie, something too good to be true.

But it’s here. It’s real. Rio rushes over to reach her hand into the cool flowing water and feel it on her skin. It’s funny, all the things humankind invents in search of some form of manufactured happiness, but nothing beats the simple joys of the natural world. Rio opens her mouth to the flow of water and literally drinks it in, lets it splash on her face.

She thinks she’s laughing. She’s not sure, as all of her brain power has been overtaken by fhe innate pleasure of clean fresh water in such large quantities. God, Agatha is going to love this. She’ll pretend that she doesn’t, but she’ll take down a sip of this stuff and her face will go kind of slack, like it did with the bacon this morning, and then she’ll get her real rare smile on her face.

Rio finds herself smiling too, as she looks down into the pool and sees her own face reflected in it. Rio sees the woman grinning up at her from her reflection and almost doesn’t recognize her. She looks… good. She kind of can’t believe how good she looks. Rio has never been too concerned with physical appearances, just wore what fit and was practical, but looking at herself out here... Her arms are toned and tan and her face is starting to freckle a little, hair wavy from the saltwater. She looks hot. Maybe Agatha is actually flirting with her.

Not that that matters.

Just as Rio is about to fall into her reflection, Narcissus-style, she hears a sound she hasn’t heard in two weeks: the unmistakable whirring of an engine.

“Holy shit,” Rio says aloud.

Her body takes her from the pool toward the sea, running through the trees toward a cliff where she can now see the outline of a large motorized boat coming ashore.

“Hey!” she yells, “over here, we’re—”

Rio cuts herself short as she almost steps over the cliff metaphorically and literally. What the fuck is she doing? That boat will come, rescue them, and then what? Then Agatha goes back to being Rio’s boss, disrespecting her, laughing at her, and Rio goes back to fluorescent lighting and sunken in cheeks. These past 14 days will have been for nothing.

Rio covers her mouth, ducks behind a tree so the boat can’t see her. And she watches. She needs to see what this boat is, what it’s purpose is, and if there will be another one that could take them home.

But not yet.

Rio still has work to do.
