Agatha loved Jen. She really did. Sure, they could annoy each other into next week, and nobody was able to rile them up like each other, but at the end of the day, Jennifer Kale was Agathas non biological sister. They had been best friends for decades. In fifth grade, their teacher had decided that it wasn't helpful for the development of their social skills if they sat alone, and had put them next to each other. It took a whole week for them to get over their saltiness about the arrangement - neither of them gave a fuck about the development of their social skills, they were fine by themselves, thank you very much -  but then they realized that while they did dislike every kid in their class, they actually found each other quite tolerable, and when they discovered that disliking their classmates was even more fun when it became a two person activity, the deal was sealed. Agatha and Jennifer truly were a match made in heaven. All throughout highschool, as well as college, all the way to graduation, to their first jobs, their bond remained undeterred. They basically grew up together, being there for each other during challenging times, witnessing each others triumphs and heartbreaks. 

 

☆

 

When they were both 18, after just having started their first semester at college, one night Jen had stumbled into their shared dorm, drunk and in tears. Agatha had sat her down, given her a glass of water and a box of tissues, and was being really selfless about the fact that she had a raging headache, an exam in the morning, and it was almost four am. After Jen had drunk half of the water, accidentally spilled the other half when her drunken ass was having a hard time with spacial awareness and actually grabbing the glass, and gone through almost the entire tissue box, she had looked at Agatha, her face red and tear-stained, and told her that she got kissed at a party by another girl, and that she had liked it. 

“Agatha”, she had whispered, as if sharing an unspeakable secret, “I think I might be a lesbian.” 

Agatha had looked at her with raised brows for about three seconds. 

“You don't say,” she deadpanned, stifling a yawn. “Please tell me she got you pregnant and that's why you're freaking out and you didn't actually drag me out of bed acting like there's an emergency like, idk, the fucking building is on fire , just to tell me that you're gay.”

Jen had looked at her with wide eyes and Agatha realized that maybe her reaction, while genuine (she really could not care less who Jen was attracted to, that didn't change the way she saw her in any way, why would it?), might look a little bit like dismissal.

“I'm sorry you feel overwhelmed by this, Jen, but I promise you, everything is gonna be okay. She stopped herself. She was steering away from the actual topic. Also, what was she even saying? “I don't understand how anyone could be attracted to them”? For god's sake, she was straight, she was attracted to them. Did she have to remind herself of that every now and then, sure, but it's not like that wasn't normal. Every straight woman probably did. “Jen, you are a wonderful human being and I love you and so will whatever girl ends up being worth dating you, okay.” 

Her friend sniffled, but the tears had dried. 

“Thank you, Agatha,” she said, her voice sincere. Agatha gave her a kind smile and nodded.

Another nod.

“I know you're straight. And don't worry, so does Alice, and her friend knows too. I told you not to see it as an actual date. It's more like… a date for Alice and me, and our friends are like, well, there, too, and under different circumstances, this might look like a double date, but if you think about it, it's really not, and-”

The other woman squealed and immediately picked up her phone from the coffee table, presumably to text Alice.

Agatha let a few more seconds pass, before she made a decision. Like she said, there was no reason to overthink this. It was just a casual dinner with her best friend, the woman she liked, and some woman that was kinda Agathas date, but wasn't really, because, once again, Agatha didn't date. Oh, and she was not interested in women. At all. 

“Absolutely no reason to overthink this.” She repeated it like a mantra as she got up and moved back to her closet. A few minutes later, she was wearing her favorite outfit- rich purple slacks and a white blouse tucked neatly into the waistband. She was gonna pair it with her signature blue coat, and obviously she couldn't forget her locket. 

After a glance in the mirror, she turned to her rabbit. 

“What do you say, Scratchy? Not too bad, hmm?”

As always, the pet exercised his right to not provide any comments, but Agatha was sure he was agreeing with her. Duh.

When it was time to leave the house and get in the uber she had ordered, Agatha felt good. She got this. She was Agatha Harkness, for god's sake. Everything was gonna be just fine. She was gonna attend this dinner, let Jen and Alice have their fun, engage in some polite small talk with Alices friend, and then go home and pat herself on the shoulder for being such a good friend. That was how this was gonna go. Everything was just fine.
