Chapter 1: Pilot, Part One: Arrival

For as bright as the sun happened to be on a rather perfect Saturday afternoon, the wind carried with it a bite all its own. It was mild in some moments and fierce in others, somehow appropriate for the brewing storm.

It was not a storm caused by barometric pressure, or one that loomed overhead. The churning storm came from a different pressure.

A pressure felt by sixteen different people, all gathered for the same purpose:

A chance to lie, cheat, and win a whole hell of a lot of money.

In the hills of the Scottish Highlands, trouble was well on its way. Or, at least, it was reaching its last stop. It just hadn’t quite made it. After all, six people meeting on a train platform made for good airtime, didn’t it?

As the footsteps of an older woman with her wild hair tied into a low bun, wearing a tucked in, cream colored blouse and purple trousers echoed off the pavement, she was rather dismayed to realize she was the first arrival. She should have known, but production could have at least given her a heads up.

“Very funny, guys,” she called over her shoulder to no one visible – the cameramen and whatever producer happened to be listening.

“Agatha, you cannot talk to the crew. That’s your first warning.” The voice might have been disembodied, but it was authoritative enough to prevent her from being able to resist a challenge.

“Are you the cops?” she asked, unable to hide her smile.

“Agatha!”

While they got shots of her waiting, she was certain they would lay over the intro she filmed in that tiny little studio near the hotel she’d been put up in somewhere outside of Glasgow.

She wore a striped blouse that day, which wouldn’t have been terrible on its own, but when accompanied with the shawl she chose and the flat brimmed hat, it looked pretty atrocious.

Okay, perhaps it was just loud. In your face. Intentional.

Because every single fucking thing Agatha Harkness did was intentional.

“My name is Agatha, I’m forty-six, and I am a crisis manager for a liability firm. Which…” she laughed, staring straight ahead at the camera with a wicked grin. “I handle the really bad shit when the bad shit gets too bad.” Okay, that wouldn’t have been in the edit. They told her as much when they made her redo it without all the swearing. “When the bad things get worse than bad, I’m the one who picks up the phone.”

“Much better, thank you,” the younger producer offered with an encouraging thumbs up.

“Whatever you say, Marijuana,” Agatha responded, dealing a facetious thumbs up right back to her.

“It’s Mary Jane,” the woman grumbled as she flagged Agatha’s attention and pointed to the cameras. “And what is it that made you want to join this season of The Traitors?”

What brought her?

The chance to blatantly lie to a bunch of strangers to swindle them out of money and fuck off to the middle of nowhere. But she knew she couldn’t say that out loud.

“I was meant to play this game. I was born to be a traitor and the moment Claudia touches my shoulder, I’m going to prove it.”

Waiting for the next arrival, Agatha couldn’t help but chuckle to herself as she reflected on the entrance interview. It was probably for the best that she didn’t mention her worries about completely coming on the spot the moment her eyes met Claudia Winkelman’s for the first time.

What? She was hot, okay? And even hotter was the idea of meeting with her in a secret space high above the rest of the plebs she would be playing with. Well, not all of them. Two of them did have to be her partners.

Then again, she sure was hinging her entire experience on what was essentially a random draw, wasn’t she? There was no way to actually know until she either felt the touch or took her blindfold off. If the latter happened without the former, she was going to be fucking pissed.

Just as she was about to heckle the crew for taking their sweet time in loading someone else onto the platform, slightly muffled voices carried from around the corner.

Ah!

New friends!

Striding out from around the edge of the building came a pair, completely mismatched in their optics. On the left side, tall and wiry, was a young man that could have been no older than twenty. His hair was a curly mop atop his head and his sweater and jeans looked like they’d been run through a wood chipper. His feet bore sneakers that looked to be half a size too small, but his most noticeable accessory was the bright and beaming grin he wore across his lips.

“Hi! I’m Billy. I’m twenty one and I work as a visual effects specialist in the film industry.” His grin bordered on maniacal, but the lights reflected in his eyes were soft, borne from excitement.

His hands were restless in his lap, the nerves getting the best of him, but he maintained focus as the one sided interview rolled over his arrival, showing interspersed clips of his time on the platform.

“I guess I wanted to do The Traitors because…” His smile returned, but it was somehow a little sadder. “I lost my brother a couple years ago and we were huge fans of all versions of the show. We always talked about trying to get on, but life got in the way. So I guess I’m here for him. And to win, obviously.”

To his left, a woman a good half a head taller than him practically marched in her pink, buckled boots at his side. She, too, was thin but looked rather agile, especially given the lack of resistance with her bald ass head. She probably regretted that on the windier days. Her suit screamed business but at the same time, it was obnoxiously pink.

Agatha already hated her.

“Hi!” she greeted, her tone faker than a five hundred dollar bill. “I’m Agatha! It’s so great to meet you.” As she said it, it wasn’t a lie, but neither of them had spoken at that point so it felt like a safe bet.

“Oh my gosh! Hi, Agatha! It’s so nice to meet you!” the other woman gushed, giving Agatha’s energy right back to her. “I’m Jen!”

“I’m Jen, thirty-eight, and I own my own skincare line. And I am honestly thrilled to be here.” Jen’s features were sharp but not pointed as she practically preened for the camera. Her half buttoned blouse and camisole, her dangly earrings glinting in the path of the ring light, she exuded nothing short of unadulterated confidence.

“I came here to win,” she announced, staring straight ahead. There was a determination in her expression that the camera captured with pristine accuracy, putting it on full display. “And I mean that in any way possible. Faithful? I’m going to win. Traitor? I’m going to win and have the time of my life while doing it.”

After she separated herself from the stiffest hug she’d ever offered someone, Agatha turned to the other new arrival, holding her hand out. She only hugged people to figure out how easy it would be to stab them in the back.

“Agatha,” she offered by means of introduction, giving the boy none of the manufactured, disingenuous bullshit she delivered so easily to Jen.

“It’s really nice to meet you, Agatha,” he greeted, shaking her hand. “I’m Billy.”

His enthusiasm reeked of Gothic Golden Retriever type, but Agatha let it pass. At the very least, he was a little more agreeable than miss priss over there, tapping her toes as they awaited the three other arrivals. It wasn’t a long wait, but Agatha could almost smell the newest person to join them at least a half a mile away.

The cologne stench was rancid, if you asked her (or Billy, judging by the way he wrinkled his nose, and even Jen who had half her face covered) and somehow, it was a perfect match for the smug looking, goateed, well-suited… man that came around the corner.

Agatha knew who he was.

Everyone knew who he was.

Billionaire and Fuckass Playboy Extraordinaire, Tony Stark.

So what the fuck was he doing playing a gameshow where the total prize fund was as much money as he made in twenty minutes? Seemed rather prickish, in her opinion. Then again, a man like that often did anything for ego. He was probably just of the mind that he wanted to waltz right in, get picked as a traitor, and swindle fifteen people out of more money. With men like that, men who only cared about power and wealth, nothing was off limits. Men like that, they took what they wanted.

Which was why if Agatha got her wish, he would be her first murder as a traitor and she would eat up every powerful second.

“Oh, I’m sure you know me,” Tony greeted the camera, a smarmy little grin on his face. “But for those of you who live under a rock, I’m Tony and I’m a weapons developer. And a philanthropist. And owner of the biggest Bio Green initiative to date!”

Tony was a man of great, immaculate aura, and he made sure everyone knew it. Perhaps he could have been a humble, sensible man beneath all of the bravado, but no one in the public eye would ever know.

“I wanted to play the game because… It’s an experience. It’s not just a game, not just money. It’s a challenge, a chance to prove that I really am the smartest man in the room.” The way he said it, it didn’t come across as arrogant, but rather confident, even if it was both.

“Welcome to my game!” he joked as he let go of his suitcase and flourished his hands out.

Agatha rolled her eyes.

“Your game?” Jen snorted, turning her head to look down the way, hopeful someone else would show to detract from the sheer arrogance of the well-dressed buffoon in front of her. She could admire the fashion choice. It was the ego that was clearly putting her off.

“Yeah, your game and you’re probably the first one banished because everyone knows who you are,” Agatha offered, following Jen’s line of sight. Tony started into some explanatory elaboration of what good he had done for the world, but neither of them were listening even if Billy appeared to be attentive.

Thankfully, a moment later, a foot appeared around the corner and an average height blonde woman strutted out, her large suitcase in tow.

“Hi! My name’s Yelena, I’m twenty-four and I’m a recovery counsellor.” It was the standard introduction, but Yelena sits up a little straighter as she says it, clearly filled with pride to be sitting in a mockup that looks like the study in the castle. “I’ve applied for the last three years, but this is my time. And I think… I think I can win it.”

There was something on her face, perhaps cunning determination, that made her look almost powerful. It was a good look on her, one of outstanding confidence.

“I have wanted to do this for so long because I need to be a traitor. It’s not even a want at this point, really. I want to prove that despite my good nature, I can still be relentless and do what needs to be done to reach whatever goal I’m aiming for. I think it would be fitting for who I know I can be, but never get the chance to be because life never really asks for it.”

Admittedly, the new arrival was pretty fashionably dressed in a pair of dark skinny jeans and a black turtleneck, her hair short enough to sit just above her shoulders. She grinned broadly the moment she took in the sight of the four others, happy to see she was not alone. As she bounded up, she offered Jen her hand first.

“Hi! It’s nice to meet you! I’m Yelena!” There was the softest Slavic accent over her words, but it seemed to only add to her charm. Then again, the overt and too-cheery introduction gave away she was a little nervous, at least to Agatha. Then again, they were all probably a little nervous about spending two weeks with fifteen other strangers, locked up in a castle somewhere far away from home.

It was reasonable.

But not Agatha.

She had a job to go back to, but not much else. She had a couple of conquests she could call to for a night of fun, but no partner and no children to speak of. Just the way she liked it. She wasn’t a lonely person by any means, but she also would never normally and willingly subject herself to the attention of so many strangers for such a prolonged period of time.

If it was for five hundred thousand dollars, she figured she could manage.

Because that five hundred grand was hers, whether anyone else wanted to admit it or not.

“Hi,” she greeted, giving the young woman’s hand a shake. “I’m Agatha. Nice to meet you.” The woman turned to the younger boy, the two of them seeming to share far more energy than Agatha was capable of.

Somewhere in the background, Tony was still talking

No one was listening to him.

Agatha listened hard, hoping to hear the rumbling of the train, but instead another sound caught her completely off guard.

“Hey, hey! Looks like I found the party!” It came from down the way, but when Agatha lifted her head, she should have known someone who looked like that would announce a cutthroat gameshow as a party.

He was probably a couple of inches shorter than Agatha, with slightly spiked dark hair and was dressed like a complete and total dad. Adam Sandler kind of vibe, but replace basketball shorts with cargo ones and a baggy t-shirt with one that actually fit to his torso. Not the worst look in the world, but definitely screamed his status as a parent.

Oh, she had a feeling he was going to be telling dad jokes the whole fucking time. Maybe he would have to be murdered first, if she had her say.

“Hey! My name’s Scott and I work at an audio shop. Like… Car audio, carriage lights, the whole shebang. Now what I want to be doing for the rest of my life, but it’s a good… bassline.” Scott was the kind of guy who laughed at his own jokes, just as he did right then for an interview that millions of people were likely going to watch.

He seemed rather carefree as he beamed at the camera, looking like he’d won the lottery simply because he was sitting in front of it. Maybe the game was a lottery, but it wasn’t one he’d already won.

“I wanted to do this for my kid. I mean, she’s not a kid anymore, but… I guess I wanted her to see that you can be who you need to be when you need to be that person. You don’t always have to play by all the rules, because who made them anyway?” He waved a hand dismissively, rolling his eyes. “Rules are made by cops. These rules? They let us murder and lie and cheat and swindle. And… That sounds like a good time to me.”

“You’ve uh, got something on your suit there,” the new arrival voiced, pointing toward Tony’s lower chest. When the billionaire’s head dropped to find nothing, Scott laughed. “Got ya!”

Oh, that man was going to be fucking insufferable.

The chatter began as everyone seemed to devolve into conversation. Well, everyone except for two. On either sides of the group, both Agatha and Jen stood like bookends, simply observing the social foray between them. Tony and Scott were seemingly hitting it off, though Agatha wasn’t sure what Golf Day Adam Sandler and a fucking billionare could have in common. At the same time, the conversation happening between Billy and Yelena looked like two high school kids trying to make friends for the first time.

When she met Jen’s eyes across the space, she realized something.

If there was going to be any competition out of the five people standing in front of her, it was going to be the woman she was looking dead in the eyes.

Before she could ruminate on it longer than she needed to, she finally heard what it was she was waiting for. It was the softest rumble, one that vibrated from about a mile away. With the seconds ticking on, the rumbling grew louder and louder still until it was loud and forceful enough to shake the stones surrounding the track.

At long fucking last, their ride was finally there. She stepped outward, a little closer to the tracks and craned out her neck, seeing the black steam engine screaming toward them. A moment later, the screech of its breaks hit the air and it began to slow. The chatter died down and fell completely silent by the time it rolled to a stop and ten other faces appeared in windows to greet them.

It was finally go time, finally time to actually get her head on right and look at things like an actual game player and not just someone showing up for a fun time. Sure, that was part of it, but like everything in life, Agatha showed up to win, to take home the power and the glory and she was going to do just that, even if it killed her (or got her murdered, which she would be very upset about).

As the train doors pulled open, Agatha grabbed hold of her bag and lifted her head, chin pointed up as she shook out her hair and squared her shoulders, heading inside.

And so, the game began.
