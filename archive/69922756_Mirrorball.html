Chapter 1: And They Were Roommates

Rio sat cross legged at her tiny dorm desk, hunched over a mess of highlighted notes and textbooks. The soft hum of her desk lamp filled the silence, accompanied only by the faint rustle of paper as she scribbled down bullet points. She was deep in her world of botanical classifications and photosynthesis pathways when a sudden, sharp knock at the door jolted her upright.

Her pen slipped from her hand.

Who on earth-

Oh.

Her stomach dropped as realization dawned.

The roommate.

She shot a glance at the clock, she hadn’t expected her to arrive this soon. Shoving her chair back, Rio smoothed her hair down in a nervous, pointless gesture and crossed the room to open the door.

The moment it swung open, she was greeted by… boxes. Arms carrying them. A stack so precarious she was amazed it hadn’t toppled yet.

And then, from behind the cardboard fortress, a face appeared, peeking out with a flash of ocean blue eyes and loose waves of brown hair tumbling nearly to her waist.

For a second, Rio forgot how breathing worked.

“Oh- uh-”

“You must be Rio,” the stranger huffed, adjusting her grip on the box. “Do you mind? This is heavy.”

Right. Words. She needed those.

“Oh, sorry! Yes- I mean- I’m Rio.” She scrambled to step aside as the woman- Agatha, her brain supplied hazily, remembering the name from the housing email- strode past her like she already owned the room. Agatha set the box down by the empty bed, shaking out her arms before glancing around like she was casually assessing the entire place.

Her gaze landed on Rio’s side of the room, the overflowing greenery of hanging plants, succulents lining the windowsill, and a small monstera proudly dominating her nightstand.

“You like plants, huh?” Agatha asked, tone flat but her eyes sharp, scanning every detail like she was cataloging Rio herself.

Rio shifted, heat creeping up the back of her neck under the weight of that look.

Agatha suddenly sat up and stretched lazily, heading toward the door.

Her new roommate. Agatha Harkness. Achingly beautiful. Sharp tongued. Confident. Possibly the most distracting person Rio had ever met.

This was going to be a very, very long year.
