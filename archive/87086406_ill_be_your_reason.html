For the time being, it’s nice to go in and see everyone and keep things as close to normal for as long as she can.

She might be pregnant, but that isn’t going to slow her down any.

Then again, Agatha isn’t slowing down either. In fact, it seems like the need to build and create and nest in some ways is prevalent for her in a paramount way, almost like a compulsion.

Given what she came from, Rio gets it.

She wants to give their baby what she was given by the women who adopted her, wants their child to have a good life and to know that no mountain would remain unmoved if that is what it takes to make them happy.

Before they even take a breath in the world, they’re already so fucking loved.

As Agatha unloads the last sheet of plywood to take it around the side of the house, Rio appears on the porch with a glass of sweet tea in her hand and a smile on her lips.

“That’s the last trip?” she calls in question, both about the hardware store and the wood itself.

“Sure is!” Agatha calls from around the corner. She disappears for a few minutes, likely as she makes sure everything’s covered in the event of rain. While she waits, Rio takes a seat in one of the deck chairs, keeping hold of Agatha’s tea. A moment later, Agatha returns with a grin, happy to see her wife and the ice cold beverage in her hand.

“Hello, wife,” she greets as she climbs the steps to the porch and cuts right to Rio, leaning over to take a soft kiss as she lightly plucks the glass from her fingers. “Thank you for this. It’s been hot today.”

“And you left your water bottle here,” Rio playfully scolds. She leans up enough to take another kiss just as Agatha pulls away to sit in the chair next to her.

“I forgot my water bottle here,” Agatha corrects with a light laugh, hearty but not quite boisterous. She sips at her tea while her free hand crosses to the arm and Rio’s chair, picking up her hand to give it a light squeeze.

“I’ll forgive it,” she teases, casting her gaze out toward the neighboring field.

They’re quiet for a moment while Rio thinks and Agatha enjoys her tea, but they go to speak at the same time.

“I know what you’re doing.”

“I know you know what I’m doing.”

Their heads turn and gazes meet, a sad, soft laughter falling from them both. It’s not sad that they have the same thought at the same time, but the thought itself is a little sad.

“You’re keeping busy until Friday,” Rio observes, her voice gentle. Her eyes drop to watch as she brushes the back of Agatha’s hand with her thumb.

Friday.

Ultrasound day.

The day they see for themselves that their dreams are going to come true.

“I am,” Agatha agrees.

“And now you’re worrying that I feel like you’re pulling away.”

“I am,” she says again.

Rio chuckles softly and shakes her head, squeezing Agatha’s hand at the same time.

“Waiting for appointments is always going to be rough because we want the answers now, but it’s okay to stay busy in the meantime, my love. I don’t take it personally.”

Agatha sighs quietly beside her and sips her tea, her eyes breaking their stare to glance out toward the same fields Rio was studying not moments before. It’s nothing more than miles of farmland as far as they eye can see, but it’s kissed by pinks and oranges as the sun sets on the other side of the house. Where it does not illuminate, the shadows of the night begin to break through, aching to breathe.

It’s something they’re fond of, even if it does not belong to them. More than that, it’s something Rio has come to find so much beauty in. A year before, she would have hated every bit of the fresh air and the mosquitos and the lack of noise. As she sits there with Agatha and ponders what the week ahead of them holds, she knows she wouldn’t trade it in for the world.

She has a life she never thought she wanted and now, she’d fight to the death for it. Somehow, she knows it’s only going to get better.

And all because of Agatha.

She made a choice that changed both of their lives in ways unimaginable.

And Rio couldn’t love her more for it.

“It’s going to go great, you know?” Rio offers softly, squeezing Agatha’s hand between them. It’s a gentle reminder that they are only capable of controlling the things they control, a lesson she knows her wife is all too familiar with.

“I know… I just want to see them. And hear their little heartbeat. Because then…” She pauses and rubs her lips together, clearly fighting off unwanted emotions. “Because then we’ll know for sure that we aren’t in a collective fever dream.”

Despite how sentimental Agatha is being, Rio can’t help but breathe a gentle laugh at her words.

“If we are, we’ve been in it for well over a year, my love,” Rio assures her, picking up her hand to gingerly kiss the back of it.

“Then I hope we never wake up,” Agatha states. This time, her voice sounds a little stronger and a little less sentimental. Which, perfectly fine with Rio. She knows how Agatha feels when she gets a little too raw for too long. If she needs to steel up again, that’s perfectly fine.

Unexpectedly a moment later, she actually laughs.

Before Rio can question what seems to be so hilarious, Agatha fills her in.

“I saw Jen at the hardware store on my third trip. She stuck out like a sore thumb,” she divulged, her voice still teeming with her laughter. “She was looking for a leveler for an Ikea bookshelf she says she keeps trying to put together but won’t stay straight. I told her she’d be better off building one from scratch and she gave me the middle finger. So I gave her two back.”

“Oh, wow,” Rio marvels. “Like two teenagers. Fantastic,” she pokes, unable to curb her smile.

“If she wants shitty furniture, that’s on her,” Agatha says with a shrug and drinks more of her tea, almost draining the glass.

Rio actually thinks it’s kind of sweet, the way they bicker and fight sometimes despite always going to bat for one another. After all, Jen’s the one person who actually knows what the hell she’s doing when it comes to the future of Rio’s care and the prospective health and wellness of the child they hope to bring into the world.

“Agatha,” Rio tuts, rolling her eyes. “Not everyone is handy and can build whole rooms, much less home furniture. Maybe if you were super nice, you might build her a custom bookshelf when you finish the bedroom.”

She says it teasingly, but the way Agatha perks beside her lets her know she’s onto something. When Agatha speaks, she knows it even more.

“With what we’re paying her? She’d be lucky if I made her a little figurine.” She’s grinning as she says it, telling Rio she’s already picturing the exact shelf she’s going to build as a thank you gift to her friend.

“Yeah, a figurine. Right,” Rio snorts, shaking her head. Still holding onto Agatha’s hand, she gets to her feet and gives it a tug, urging her wife to do the same. “While you’re not planning the intricate accents on your friend’s new bookshelf in your head, why don’t you come in and eat? I made salmon and broccoli with the little crispy potatoes.”

Instead of following the instruction right away, Agatha uses Rio’s hold on her hand to pull her in and wrap her other arm around her strongly. When they’re still and steady, she places a light kiss to Rio’s lips.

“You are the woman of my dreams,” she compliments, causing a warmth to wash up Rio’s spine.

“Why? Is it because I make you dinner and run you baths and am going to be the mother of your child?” she murmurs against her wife’s lips, her own pulled into a grin.

“If I say all of the above, do I get brownie points?”

Rio’s face falls slightly in confusion, her brow furrowing given the fact that she’s a little caught off guard.

“What is it?” Agatha asks, also becoming confused.

“How the fuck did you know I made brownies?”

The following week is no less hectic as the world continues to spin around them, but things appear to be going well.

Samara, Thomas, and Lizzie, the three new hires for the work around the farm, seem to be fitting right in and picking up on their to-do lists with complete ease. It’s because, Agatha informs her, they’ve all done similar work for neighboring farms because it’s doable and the cash in hand is always nice to have. Is Agatha possibly running the risk of some slightly sideways tax issues?

Only if someone talks.

With how much she’s decided to pay them, she’s quite certain that won’t be happening.

She’s even confident enough that when she checks in with them Friday morning before they drive to Jen’s office, they’re okay to leave by themselves. She, of course, checks three times to make sure the house is locked up and any valuables are stowed, but she has faith in them.

Maybe Agatha can be a little stern and can give out orders, but she’s not cruel or unjust. She just has a particular way she likes having things done. It won’t be perfect every time, but the three of them seem rather eager to please. More than that, the money is also a motivator to not fuck them over. Any Google search would have told them who they were, but Rio hopes they’re not quite so curious.

She likes just being the lady that runs the bee farm.

And Agatha likes being just the woman who owns the sugar cane farm.

It’s just who they are, at their very cores.

Simple.

Uncomplicated.

Happy.

And… Nervous, if only on that morning.

“Shit,” Agatha hisses as they go to get in the truck, patting the pockets of her jeans.

Rio already knows what she’s looking for.

She holds her keys up in answer. “You probably left them on the end table by the front door,” she offers with a giggle, leaning over to place a kiss to Agatha’s cheek as she grabs the keys to head back inside. “I love you, Agatha. Take a couple of deep breaths. It’s okay.”

“But now we’re going to be late,” she grumbles, kicking her door open to slide out and double back inside for her keys.

Rio shoots Jen a quick ‘5 min late’ text and watches after her wife as she jogs in and out of the house, huffing a little when she hops back in the truck and holds up her keys triumphantly and gives Rio back her own.

“I texted Jen while you ran in,” she informs her with a laugh. “Said we needed an extra five minutes.”

“And what did she have to say about that?”

On cue, Rio’s phone buzzes in her hand.

When she looks at the message, she bursts out laughing.

“Oh god,” Agatha groans, already so far from amused.

“She’s closing early and we’re rescheduled to nine months from now.”

Agatha rolls her eyes and starts the truck, pulling out onto the road to head into the city. “Just for that, I’m leaving six screws out of her bookshelf.”

The drive there is its typical forty-ish minutes, but Rio can tell by how quiet Agatha is most of the way there that she’s still a little nervous. It’s highly unlikely that anything will go wrong, but Rio understands it.

Agatha needs everything to go right.

When they get to Jen’s office, she greets them warmly with hugs and has one of her nurses take them back to a room to get them situated. Finding herself on the table with her hand in Agatha’s, suddenly Rio feels nervous, too.

“It’s okay,” Agatha says this time, realizing Rio needs to hear it.

“I know… I just… What if…”

“No, no,” Agatha stops her, wrapping Rio’s hand in both of hers. “It’s going to be perfectly okay. We aren’t ‘what if-ing.’ That’ll be the death of us.” Her voice is so strong and steady that Rio has no other choice than to believe what she’s saying.

She’s right.

Everything is going to be fine.

A few minutes later, Jen and another woman come through the door with the little ultrasound machine. “I’m not a tech, but Dakota here is, so she’s going to put a little bit of cold gel right about here,” she explains, indicating Rio’s lower stomach.

Understanding, she unfastens her jeans and pushes them down just enough until Jen nods.

“Then she’s going to take the wand and run it along your stomach. When she does, we’re going to see what the wand sees on this screen.”

“You mean our baby,” Agatha interjects and while she’s being funny, Rio can hear the hope in her voice, too.

“Yes. We should see your baby,” Jen agrees, offering her friend a true, heartfelt smile.

“Then what are you waiting for?” Agatha retorts, causing the other woman to snap to attention.

“Please ignore her,” Rio offers, shooting her wife a playful glare as if to say ‘stop scaring the staff.’

Just as Jen explained, the tech places a few dollops of the gel on Rio’s stomach and presses the plastic to her skin, carefully moving it along from one side to the other as she looks at the screen.

They hear it first.

Thump thump thump thump thump thump.

It’s so fast but so soft.

Their baby’s heartbeat.

“Is that…?” Agatha starts, her voice already cracking. Jen nods.

“Yes. And…” She raises a pointed finger and indicates a little white blob that sits near the bottom of a blackened space. “That is your baby, and looking right on track for almost seven weeks.”

Rio wants to cheer, to dance and sing and rejoice.

She doesn’t do that, though.

No, instead, Rio cries.

Holy fucking shit. That’s their baby. Right there. Right on that screen. Right inside her body.

Their baby.

Immediately, Agatha is on her feet and leaning over to hug her wife, but it takes a moment for Rio to realize she’s shaking too. It takes her even longer to realize Agatha’s tears are soaking the top of her head.

Finally, when she lets go, the tech hands her a couple of paper towels and offers them a soft ‘congratulations’ before making her exit.

“All your labs from last visit looked good, so… I’ll see you in a couple of weeks for your next check up?” Jen asks, taking the paper towels from her to throw them away.

“Yes. And you should come over tomorrow,” Rio adds with a smile. “Let us make you dinner.”

Jen rolls her eyes. “A small price to pay, I suppose.”

“Oh shut up and come eat with us,” Agatha goads. She holds her hand out for Rio and helps her off the table, meeting Jen by the door.

“What time?” Jen asks, letting them out of the room. When she passes the reception area, Dakota hands her a small envelope.

“Four should be good,” Rio answers before Agatha has a chance to get sassy again.

“Four it is,” Jen agrees. She holds up the envelope, handing it directly to Agatha. “Don’t forget these.” Her smile is wide and sincere and she offers a nod of her head before heading back around to the back of the clinic. “Have a good night you two!”

“Bye!” they both call in unison, laughing softly as they make their exit.

When they make it to the car, Rio can’t help but peer over as Agatha starts the car and opens the envelope, pulling out the three still shots of their little pea sized bundle of joy. Before she can even speak, Rio can see the tears rolling down her cheek.

“There she is,” Agatha marvels, causing Rio to breathe a laugh through her own tears.

“‘She,’ huh?” Rio muses, leaning over to place a kiss to her wife’s wet cheek.

“I’m hopeful. Sue me.”

Rio giggles again, peppering a couple more kisses to Agatha’s cheek.

“It would just come out of our joint bank account.”

They both laugh.

“Shit. You’re right.”

“Aren’t I always?”

Agatha pauses for a moment, looking down at the photos again.

“About this? Yeah.”
