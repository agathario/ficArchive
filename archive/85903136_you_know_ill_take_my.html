It's like being blasted with cold water at high speeds. Fuck, he's dead. She's not even sure how Agatha will respond to the news, knowing that he's no longer alive to hurt her but also not able to go down and suffer for the crimes he committed. There is no justice brought. At least, not to Rio.

Happiness, at knowing that the man who caused so much harm can't harm her or anyone else again. Disappointment, maybe, at knowing that she'll never be able to get closure for what this man did to her and many others like her. Rio isn't sure how she feels about this.

Easier, less grotesque ways that would have taken him out but wouldn't have given me the satisfaction I crave. And then the ways I think about now that I'm out, and safe, and away from him. Ripping his throat out with my teeth and dangling him in front of his cronies to show that they're next."

Feral. That's the only accurate word to be used here. She knows Agatha has a temper, although it's never been directed at her. She knows from her file she has a tendency to violence, especially where men are involved. But she hadn't known how deep her desire to murder the men who hurt her went.

She can't find it disturbing, she can't recoil back in disgust, at hearing Agatha talk about her fantasies and how frequently she has them about murdering the men who hurt her.

Their very first kiss since Rio had helped Agatha through her heat. The kiss Rio will consider as their first real kiss, because both of them are in their right mind and there's nothing impeding them.

It's an odd time for a kiss, Rio admits, but she's not about to look a gift horse in the mouth. And it doesn't last long, anyway. After a couple of moments Agatha pulls back before diving in for a hug. Rio feels hot tears in her neck and has to choke back her own, because she can't be emotional right now. She needs to be here for Agatha. She needs to be the strong rock that Agatha needs to lean on.

"Okay," Agatha agrees.

Getting dressed and in the car is a quiet affair. There's nothing really to say, there's nothing Rio has been able to come up with to prepare Agatha for the information Fury is about to give her, and there's no reason Rio would expect Agatha to fill the silence.

So they sit in it, throughout the entire drive to the headquarters and when they're inside. They get ushered to Fury's office quickly and Rio tries not to notice that Agatha's tail has wrapped itself around her wrist, clenching it quickly, almost needing Rio's presence to keep her grounded.

Rio will gladly do that for her, allowing her hand to brush over the fur gently. It seems to work because almost immediately, Agatha begins to soften.

Or, at least, she looks a little less murderous. That's a start.

"Thank you for coming, Vidal. And Agatha." Fury motions for them to sit, but they don't. He doesn't pay that any mind. "I'm going to get right into it. I trust that you've given Agatha the news?" Rio nods emotionlessly. "Agatha, what do you know about Valeria Richards?"

While her parents, Reed and Sue, had cut of all contact with Doom, Valeria had kept in touch with him. Many say she likened his daughter more than a niece, since she cut off her own parents, too." Very minimal, as they usually are, since they want the least amount of liability possible." Fury leans back in his own chair, showing a detailed report atop a manila folder with Valeria's name on it.

"She sold him out," Agatha guesses.

"Bingo," Fury tells her. "In exchange for protection and for a plea bargain, of sorts, she told us all the information she knew. She told us where Doom was located, and where his partner, Lucia von Bardas, was also located. When our teams got there, they were both deceased." Agatha's face grows whiter, her lips flattening into a tight line at the name. She must be more than familiar with her as well.

Agatha clears her throat. "Did they kill themselves?"

"No," Fury says. The answer surprises both Agatha and Rio. Rio tries to hide her surprise but Agatha doesn't, her eyebrows rising and her mouth dropping open. Before she can question, however, Fury continues, "they were murdered. An intentional fire was set to the compound they were found in, along with a bunch of evidence that was not salvageable."

"How do you know they didn't set the fire?" Rio can't help but ask.

"Our team did a thorough investigation on the fire and how it was started. The doors were locked from the outside, all alarms were deactivated, and there was a gasoline trail leading away from the building. Which, consequentially, is where the projected fire was started."

Agatha still looks shellshocked. "Okay, but who? Valeria?"

"No, she's in our custody." Fury grabs another folder. "Agatha, does the name Wanda Maximoff mean anything to you?"

Rio's eyes snap to Agatha's face, watching very carefully. She can tell that Agatha is trying to school her features but she doesn't quite manage it. It doesn't seem like she wants to, apparently, because she tells Fury, "we were in captivity together. We got close." Her eyes narrow. "What does she have to do with this? Was she not found when I was? Where is she?"

"We did find her during our original operation. Along with other Hybrids, like yourself, but she didn't want to be part of the FBI investigation. She'd met with us and given us all the information she knew, but she refused to stay." Fury opens the file and shows a photo of a pretty redhead and an older man. "Do you recognize him?"

"Yeah," Agatha says slowly. "He was a guard. Well, guard—" Agatha makes air quotes around the word. "He was more like a gangster than security." It takes a moment, then two, then three, before Agatha says, "oh my God. He helped her do it."

Rio feels a little lost, her brain taking a second to catch up. She's a biologist, for fucks sake, she doesn't know how to deal with these situations. But clearly, Agatha had caught herself up and she understands whatever it is that Fury is putting down for her.

"Correct. Vision is the name he goes by. It seems him and Wanda Maximoff created some sort of bond when she was in captivity. He didn't like how any of you were treated and he decided to put his life on the line to feed information to Wanda so she could kill Doom and Bardas. He's the one who deactivated all the alarms and locked them in the compound. She's the one who set the fire."

Okay, so he's pretty much hand-feeding the information to Rio, but she's grateful, so she doesn't have to break up the momentum to ask her questions.

"How do you know all of this?"

Fury closes the file. "She turned herself in."

"What?" Agatha's eyes are so wide Rio is honestly afraid they might bug out of her head. "Why would she do that?"

"She wanted all of you to be at peace knowing he's dead and not coming back." Rio might be crazy but she swears that she hears a note of softness in Fury's voice. And maybe something else—something like respect. Rio can't help but feel the same.

"She's so stupid," Agatha sulks. "Can I see her?"

Fury and Rio make eye contact. Rio doesn't have anything against Agatha seeing Wanda, especially if she can, especially knowing that they've had some kind of bond and that Agatha had been thinking of her while she's been in Rio's care.

"You can visit her."

Agatha's ears twitch. "What about Jennifer Kale?"

Fury's eyebrows raise. "Jennifer Kale is in the care of Agent Wu-Gulliver. Similar to your arrangement with Doctor Vidal, I'd assume." There's a knowing look in Fury's eye that Rio finds she doesn't like, it feels too much like your principal knowing you've done something, even if it's not wrong.

"I want to see her, too," Agatha demands.

"That can be arranged. I will pass along Agent Wu-Gulliver's information to Doctor Vidal and they can communicate how best to get you and Jennifer together."

Agatha almost sags in relief. "Where's Wanda now?"

Fury stands. "Come with me. I'll take you to her. She's not in a cell. We have her in a room and she is accepting visitors."

Rio and Agatha look at each other briefly before they follow Fury. There is very little said as he leads them back through to the barracks where they have a bunch of single rooms, where Agatha was before she'd been put into Rio's care. It's a little eerie and Rio honestly feels nerves clawing at her, and she can't imagine how Agatha feels.

Fury knocks once with one of his knuckles before opening the door, which is unlocked from the outside. The door opens and reveals the redhead in question laying on her stomach on the bed, her very fluffy red ears perking up in question. It takes a moment of recognition before Wanda sees Agatha behind Fury and she flings herself off the bed and into Agatha's arms.

They hug tightly, whispering to each other, similarly to two people who have been through hell together and have been separated for an extended period of time. Their tails entwine and their noses rub together. Rio almost feels like she shouldn't be allowed to watch this. It feels too intimate.

When she looks at Fury, he's not looking at them either.

Rio and Fury stand there with their hands in their pocket, awkwardly making eye contact, for about two minutes before Agatha and Wanda separate. They're both crying, and their tails are still wrapped together.

"I can't believe you killed him," Agatha mutters, a little sulky.

"I know you wanted to be the one to do it, but I had the in. I had to, Agatha."

"I know. Thank you."

They hug again and Fury nods his head toward the door. Rio follows him, giving them a tiny bit of space even with the door still open.

"I've gotten notes from Monica and Doctor Calderu. It seems she's taken a real shine to you." He leans against the wall. "Now that the case is essentially closed, outside of any additional information the Hybrids may want to give us regarding the underground ring. We no longer need to monitor Agatha and she is free to do as she pleases." The implication causes Rio's heart to stammer unflatteringly. "If you two wish to keep your relationship going, we will not be involved. You both are free to do as you please without the impending doom of a trial and the impact that could come from that." Rio wants to chuckle at the pun but she just can't. "I don't think she'll be going anywhere. And I think you did a great job with her."

Rio feels her pride swell brightly. "Thank you, sir."

"We made a good choice with you. You care a lot about everyone around you, and you helped somebody who thought she'd never know how it feels to be cared for." Rio had never known Fury to talk this way with anyone. Okay, she'd heard rumours about Phil Coulson and Maria Hill, who were his favourites. "Your bond goes beyond whatever was needed for the bureau."

He goes back into the room after a moment and leaves Rio with those words. Another couple of beats later and Agatha comes bounding out of the room, the tear tracks on her cheeks dried down. She still looks so beautiful.

"I'm ready to go."

"Are you sure?"

Agatha just looks at her for a moment, looking so tired but so relieved. "Yes. Please get me out of here."

Rio feels like she could burst out into tears and thank her lucky stars and maybe send Monica a fruit basket, but she doesn't. Instead, she allows Agatha to take her hand and lead her out of the bureau. Somewhere, hopefully, Agatha will never have to step foot inside of again.

With all of the prior hesitation Rio had about seeing Monica at the beginning, she's now sure that she's going to miss their sessions.

Currently, in their last one, she's sitting across from Monica as Monica scribbles in her notebook. Her session with Agatha had taken doubly long so Rio had been forced to sit in the waiting area for two hours. Her ass is sore, she feels emotional, she might have a crash out. Who knows.

"So," Monica starts, almost goading. Her smile is wide. "I hear that you and Agatha are remaining together even after the case has shut itself."

Monica merely shrugs, letting the dig roll right off of her. "You're not my patient anymore after this. I think I have been given the right to gloat." She softens, just the slightest bit. Oh, no. Rio can't cry in therapy. She didn't even want therapy, she can't let this insufferable woman know that she'd actually helped her. "I'm happy that I was able to be apart of your journey with Agatha."

Rio feels tears spring to her eyes and she tries to hold them back, coughing and looking up at the ceiling. Anywhere but the genuine openness on Monica's face. "I'm just happy that Agatha is happy," Rio says, begrudgingly. Because she is. The number one thing she'd wanted out of this entire arrangement was for Agatha to know happiness.

And now she gets to keep it, and keep Agatha, and she's not sure what she's done to deserve it. But she'll be grateful that she has it anyway.

"She is happy," Monica confirms, not allowing any room for argument. Rio finally looks at her again. "She feels so safe with you, Rio. You really are good for her. And I think she's good for you, even if she might give you a headache."

Rio doesn't acknowledge the gooey-ness of it all, instead she mumbles, "grey hair."

"What?"

"She gives me grey hair. Since she's started living with me I've found at least two new grey hairs that were not there before I met her."

Monica laughs. An actual genuine, hearty laugh that Rio has never heard from her before. She's chuckled politely at some of the jokes she's cracked or at Agatha's attitude when she gets to see her with the mask lowered a little, but nothing like this.

"She is quite a handful. Are you prepared for that?"

It's obviously a rhetorical question, but Rio snaps, "yes, of course I'm prepared for that." She immediately feels guilty when she sees the shock on Monica's face at Rio's tone and how seriously she took an obvious joke. "Sorry, I'm sorry. I guess I'm so overwhelmed with everything that has happened."

Being charged with taking care of a traumatized kitty Hybrid who turns out to be the brattiest cat known to human kind, having to do therapy sessions, having to report to Fury… and then the case falling through because Agatha's best friend is homicidal and killed him before he could stand trial."

Of course I feel a certain way about the situation I was put in by my superiors, but that's nothing in comparison to how Agatha must feel about it. "You're such a brat," Rio mumbles. She feels her cheeks heat up and she takes another spoonful of ice cream. The idea of eating Agatha out on this table is getting more and more favourable.

"Agent Wu Gulliver—Alice—said that Jennifer spoke about you. She said she called you a bitch and she can't wait to hug you again."

"You deser—" Agatha's words catch up to her and she cuts herself off. "Hold on, wait, service top?"

Rio leans forward and kisses her again, mostly to shut her up, because the easiest way to quiet a brat is to keep her mouth busy. Which is something else Rio also hopes to get to explore with Agatha. If she's lucky.

"Let's go home," Agatha whispers.

"Home," Rio agrees.
