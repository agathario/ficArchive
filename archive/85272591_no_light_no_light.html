Summary

Agatha Harkness is the rising star of the international choir community. According to the rumor mill, a secret love affair with the conductor of the Scarlet Singers forced Agatha to quit and part ways with the choir she first found her voice in. Not keeping a bad bitch down, Agatha decides to follow her lifelong dreams to make a name for herself as the conductor of her own choir, the Coven Chorus.

Rio Vidal, a child prodigy classical music performer, finishing her doctorate under Dr Lilia Calderu. For her final assignment she is challenged to explore other aspects of classical music performance and ends up having to audition for the Coven Chorus, the new trailblazing chamber choir on campus.

Agatha, who has no time to inflate other’s egos, sees straight through Rio at the audition when the overconfident singer arrives. Something about Rio, be it her voice, confidence or just the way she stared down the great Professor Agatha Harkness, lets Agatha hesitate on taking a chance on Rio.

An AU based in the competitive choir world where reputation isn’t the only thing on the line for Agatha Harkness.

If Rio ever did imagine a world in which she would be at her happiest, she would probably never have imagined the life she was currently living in now. She always thought it would be a somewhat solitary life where she’d be content with her only love of her life being her music and the stage. 



To Rio, it had always been standing in the center of a large concert stage, perfectly performing a musical masterpiece with a full symphony orchestra backing her in front of a packed concert hall of people appreciating the art just as much as she always had. 



No, her imagined perfect life never included a special someone waiting in the wings for her with a specially made up bouquet of her favorite flowers. Never someone to come home to after a hard day or even having someone to celebrate with after a good day.



Yet now, with Agatha in her small off campus apartment, in her kitchen, making them coffee dressed in only one of Rio’s oversized t-shirts the morning after another night spent together, Rio felt her fantasies starting to look a little different. This thing with Agatha was still very very new, and not to mention something completely foreign to Rio to begin with. 



Sure, she’s had flings before, and even periods where she went on a few dates with the same person for a couple of weeks in a row, and granted, with whatever was brewing between herself and Agatha has only been a few days, this felt like something materially different. 



Though it was all still spent deep in secret and behind closed doors with coded messages and meaningful looks. The time they’ve spent together talking and laughing and yes, fucking like they were trying to set some kind of world record, was starting to alter a part of her brain. Specifically the part she had always known to be content with her living a blissfully solitary life. 



The somewhat unexpected phone call from her conductor the evening after she escaped unseen from Agatha’s townhouse the morning after their first… encounter, led to another night of Rio ending up in Agatha’s bed that very same night. That then led to another eventful evening of them together, after she invited Agatha over for dinner after another choir rehearsal where Agatha’s usual powerful and in-control mask hid the knowing looks Rio would get every so often. Jen and Alice were too busy in their own bubble to notice what was brewing between their friends. With the two obvious love birds finally admitting that they were in fact already in a romantic relationship, they spent even more time together in secret. Their usual invitations to get drinks after rehearsals or breakfast dates were left forgotten, giving Rio and Agatha more opportunities to sneak off together.



And really, Rio felt high off it. High off of the feeling of knowing that after holding onto the power and control in her role as Professor Agatha Harkness, Rio got to be the one giving her the safety to relinquish that control just a few hours later, in turn for leaving the conductor happily spent after making her cry out in ecstasy. 



Her eyes caught the time on the digital clock of the microwave reading 12:26. Under normal circumstances, Rio would have already spent the morning of her day off in way more productive ways than having scraps for brunch, no, lunch with coffee expertly prepared by her scantily dressed conductor. But they had just woken up from another marathon night and a lazy morning spent under the covers. Rio’s normally rigid schedule was now long forgotten and replaced by this. And something even more terrifying, hope.



“I forgot, did you want sugar?” Agatha asked out of seemingly nowhere.

“Sugar? I couldn’t remember if you wanted it?” Agatha said from behind her own steaming cup of coffee.



“Oh, no. Thanks.” Rio smiled. “You’re sweet enough for me.”

“So you’re telling me coffee paired with these stale biscuits and old grapes are not up to scratch for the great Professor Agatha Harkness? Do I need to be able to flip an egg if I want to see you naked in my bed again?” Rio smirked.



“It could help,” Agatha smiled warmly, reaching for Rio’s hand across the table. 



And there it was again. Hope. Something Rio never imagined having with Agatha after that first meeting in the audition room.



Rio hated the dread that accompanied hope. The dread of suddenly wanting more than she would be allowed to have. Or the hope of expecting that any of these feelings she started feeling for the first time in her life would be reciprocated in even the smallest way.

Lilia’s tea getting cold, forgotten on her desk. How the same spot on the large leather sofa in Lilia’s office gave way when Agatha flung herself at it dramatically. Even the fear of losing everything she had felt too familiar for comfort.



The only difference this time? This time she wasn’t just afraid of jeopardizing or losing her reputation. This time she was afraid that she could, or already had lost Rio.



Since Wanda’s confrontation at the Cauldron, Agatha had fought every instinct inside herself to  from reaching out to Rio. She wanted to see her again, to feel the safety of her arms and apologize, but somewhere between staring at her phone and dreaming about the singer, Agatha found herself longing to have the courage to get up and fight for Rio. To fight for herself. That was if she wasn’t so afraid of what that might mean for her and everything she’s built for herself. So she didn’t fight, she avoided.



“Agatha, there are no clear rules about this, she is not your student and you have no authority over her.” Lilia said plainly. “But you of all people should have considered how this would look when it all comes out. Now all you did was give Wanda exactly what she wanted. Power.”



“Well, I’ve ended it. Wanda will have nothing but rumors this time.” Agatha spat. “That scarlet witch will have nothing on me because there is nothing.”



For a moment Agatha swore Lilia was looking right through her. She subconsciously pulled at the lapels of her shirt, covering herself up more. 



“Does she know this?” Lilia asked softly.



“Rio?” Agatha asked.



Lilia nodded.



“I’ve made it clear from the start. She knows.” Agatha lied. Ghosting wasn’t saying it out loud, but in Agatha’s experience it sent the message clear enough.  



If Lilia didn’t believe her, she never showed it. She simply drank her cold tea and that was it. No more “I warned you” or disapproving looks, she just changed the topic and let Agatha sit with her own lies and disappointment.



“I will handle Stark, that man has always been a stingy bastard. The performance proved your worth and the donors did too. Everyone had the Coven on their lips at the Gala.” Lilia said confidently. 



Agatha got up from the sofa, gathering her things to leave. 



“Agatha,” Lilia called out, stopping her from leaving, “As the Dean, I have to tell you that Salem University will not be much more forgiving about another scandal coming out of this department, but with your reputation and what you have accomplished under the University’s name, they will have to be dumb to drop you from the roster, just… don’t give them any more reasons to do so.”



Agatha knew what that meant, “Stop this before it takes you down with it.”



“But as your friend,” Lilia continued and smiled warmly, “You deserve happiness too.”

Rio’s Apartment

 

Rio was nearing the end of the semester and her final assignment was waiting in her mentor’s mailbox. Her time singing in the Coven Chorus had given her an interesting angle to base her dissertation on, and after all the effort and time she put in, she finally had something worthy to present to earn her doctorate.



The Pedagogical Role of Tone in Classical Music Performance vs Choral Music Performance. Lilia was extremely impressed and Rio felt accomplished and proud of the academic chapter of her life coming to a grand end.



Since Agatha decided to ignore her entire existence in and outside of the Coven rehearsals and performances, Rio accepted it for what it was and moved on, choosing to focus on finishing her degree and lining up auditions or setting up meetings with prospective music companies for when she finally decides to leave Salem behind. That was the original plan after all. To be the best, and to get to perform all over the world for the best.



In the beginning it was hard. Or at least, Agatha made it hard. Rio tried everything to get the conductor to speak to her, to just tell her what went wrong, but after weeks of trying and an embarrassing amount of unanswered texts, Rio finally got the message. It was over.



It still hurt to think about it and Rio hated herself for believing it wouldn’t. For hoping things would not turn out this way. But this was what they agreed. To keep things “casual” even though Agatha preferred not to use the word to describe their arrangement. But both Rio and Agatha knew that what they had was nowhere near just being casual. 



Since Rio’s final assignment was submitted, she made the decision to resign from the Coven. In the end, like Agatha predicted, she got what she came for. Her little project was finally done. But if she was honest with herself, Rio could no longer stand being in the same space as Agatha without wanting to scream. 



“You are a coward!” she wanted to scream. “Why don’t you want me?” she would have yelled if Agatha had given her the time of day, but Rio went from receiving a black heart over text to being just another voice in the Coven. 



She was done, and that was exactly what she wrote in her resignation email to the conductor:

 

“Professor Harkness, 

 

After much deliberation, I have decided to submit my resignation from the Coven Chorus. 

 

I would like to thank you for the opportunity to learn from you and for being a part of something you loved. 

 

May you find what you want at the end of the road.

 

Yours, Rio Vidal.”



After hitting send, Rio felt a sense of relief and somewhere deep inside of her, regret. But this was closure, she decided. Closure and time to move on.



It was just late that night when Rio couldn’t sleep. Too many thoughts were keeping her up, so instead Rio scrolled through her phone, watching the endless amount of cat videos her algorithm had lined up for her. Out of nowhere her phone buzzed in her hand. After months of trying and getting nothing, the small notification bar at the top of her screen finally displayed the name she had almost finally given up on. Agatha.



The bar disappeared as soon as it came and the video of the small kitten stumbling over a stuffed toy replayed over and over while she stared at her phone, considering opening her messenger app or tossing the phone over to the other end of the room.



Rio, a bit hesitant, swapped apps and opened the message from the conductor.



Agatha: I guess you got what you wanted. 



Rio read and reread the message. Every time she did, the message stayed the same.



Rio laughed bitterly. What she wanted? If this was what she wanted, she would have never had to join the Coven in the first place. If this was what she wanted, she would have avoided getting to know Agatha. Avoided falling in love and getting her heart torn out.



Rio angrily typed a message to reply but when she rewrote and deleted the message for the third time she groaned in frustration and finally tossed her phone to the far side of her bedroom. Rio flinched when it almost hit the wall, but instead it ended up landing safely on a pile of laundry she had been putting off folding up.



In what would not go down as one of her proudest moments, Rio finally gave in. She had managed to hold them off for the last few months, but it was just too much. I guess you got what you wanted.

 

 

Rio knew the signs, she felt the static in the air change around her. “The Fourth Trial” was starting and Rio had nothing but hurt left to get her through to the other side.

 


Rio felt the tears falling from her eyes forming a large wet spot on her pillow as she let go and gave into the heartbreak of actually not getting what she wanted, and eventually it all surrendered her to sleep.

Salem University, Coven Chorus Rehearsal Auditorium

 

Agatha was annoyed. The Coven seemed to get every phrase wrong and some time between their last performance and tonight’s rehearsal, they managed to forget all the hard work she’s put in with them.



“Again!” Agatha called. “Alice, start them from bar twenty six.”



The piano led them in and the Coven followed their conductor’s hands as they started with the seventh repetition of the same phrase, and just like before, Agatha stopped them only a few seconds later. 



She frustratedly pulled her hands through her hair, “I guess if you’re all happy with sounding like a mediocre school choir, then by all means, carry on.” 



The tension was palpable in the auditorium. The conductor’s mood and frustration was reflecting directly off of her choir members' faces. 



“Alto’s, I need more sound, more support from you. And Sopranos, can you not hear yourselves? Nowhere in the subscripts does it say like banshees.” Agatha was pacing and barking. The Coven members were just waiting for her to explode, “Tenors, I don’t even know if you know the difference between your ass and your mouths at this point. And bases, welcome to your first ever choir rehearsal. This is a piano and these are called notes!” she snapped and banged on the keys, startling Alice.



“Agatha,” Alice whispered, “What’s going on?”



Agatha stopped and stared bewildered at Alice. The auditorium was dead silent, but Agatha heard her voice still echoing in the wings, she was losing it and very publicly too. 



She turned to the Coven. “Dismissed.” And waved them off.



The room erupted in a scurry of Coven members moving as fast as they could to get out of the auditorium and away from their fuming conductor. When everyone was away, Agatha flopped onto the chair with her hands in her hair, “How am I supposed to take this mess to the competition?”



Alice carefully stood up from behind the piano and walked over to where Agatha sat mumbling to herself.



“Agatha,” Alice started.



“Don’t Alice,” Agatha stopped her, “Just… don’t.”



For a moment Agatha thought Alice might leave it, but her assistant turned friend took the seat next to her, “You know they are good enough. They’re amazing… This isn’t about the competition, is it?”



Agatha jerked her head up and stared Alice down, “Of course it is! Didn’t you hear them? They aren’t ready… I’m not...” she trailed off with a gentle realization, and softened her posture in retreat, “I’m going to make a fool of myself.”



Alice shifted in her chair, “Are we still talking about the Coven, or does all of this have more to do with the open seat in the alto section?”



Agatha blinked. Agatha hated that Alice was right again. No matter how well Agatha thought she was doing with hiding her feelings from the outside world, some people could always tell, and one of those people had always been Alice. 



Since they started working together, Agatha’s cold and controlled facade seemed to bother anyone else but Alice. She saw Agatha for what she was behind the mask, and in times like these, she was not afraid of confronting her about it. That was how they became and stayed friends after all. 



“I know I’m not the main character here, but I notice things, Agatha. And I see changes and behaviors people may think they have under wraps.” Alice said into the space between them. “And with you… There was the Agatha after Wanda, then the Agatha with and the Agatha without.”



“Without what?” Agatha asked.



“Rio…” Alice answered plainly.



“It’s not R…” Agatha tried to defend.



“Please. She’s already told me.” Alice rolled her eyes. “We all noticed the change when ‘Agatha with’ changed to ‘Agatha without’. You seemed to be really happy for once in a long time. And now… Agatha, this has to end. You are bulldozing your own happiness and I can’t decide if you even know if you are doing it for the right reasons.” Alice spoke frankly.



“I can’t lose everything again.” Agatha admitted.



“You won’t lose the Coven.” Alice smirked, “We’re too good. Plus, I heard from one of the Scarlets that they’ve been having really bad rehearsals and might have to pull out of some performances.”



“This fucking place is the epicenter of the gossip mill, I swear.” Agatha snorted, somewhat amused.



“I know. No one is safe.” Alice smiled. “But Ags, if you’re not careful, what you will be losing is a chance at being happy.”



“What if I am not meant to be happy?” Agatha asked with an unfamiliar vulnerability in her voice. “What if I am too late?”



“She’s talking to New York.” Alice revealed after a few silent minutes.



Agatha slowly breathed out a shaky breath and pulled her lips between her teeth wistfully. Her eyes were welling up and her chest hurt where her heart was beating sadly. Agatha forced a teary smile. “And she’ll be amazing.”

It was already very late when Agatha was pacing the narrow hallway a few feet away from the staircase leading up to Rio’s front door. The neighbor who let her in, thinking she was the delivery guy, will have to wait a bit longer for his midnight sushi order to arrive.



It was not that she was afraid. It was that she was terrified. What if Rio slammed the door in her face or had someone else over. Or what if she was already gone.



The answer to her questions came sooner than she anticipated when Rio’s front door swung open when Alice and Jen, leaving hand in hand, greeted Rio goodbye, hugging her and wishing her luck, while Agatha hid behind the mailboxes out of sight around the corner.



When they turned the other corner Agatha realized she was acting like a crazy person, for hiding away in the corridors of her former lover’s apartment block. “Get it together, Harkness.” Agatha chastised herself with a low whisper. She couldn’t back out now, and heavens help her, she needed to make this right.



“Agatha?” she heard Rio’s voice call out from around the corner.



Busted. “Fuck.” she whispered to herself and with nowhere to escape, Agatha pushed herself off the wall and turned the corner to see Rio at the top of the staircase, looking down at her, and probably getting ready to call the police.



“Sorry, I…” Agatha started backing away. 



“Why are you here?” Rio asked, keeping her eyes on the conductor and her hand firmly around the railing of the staircase.



Agatha could have apologized or made up an excuse, she was usually good with those things, but now, all she could come up with was, “Congratulations.”



“What?” Rio asked, confused.



“New York.” Agatha explained. “You got in.”



“How do you know about New York?” Rio asked, still not moving from the top of the staircase.



“Alice… earlier.” Agatha sighed, letting her head drop. How was she messing up this apology? “I had to cancel the performance tonight because of you.” That wasn’t much better, Agatha thought.



“Because of me?” Rio rebuked. “What did I do?”



Agatha stared at Rio for a long time. What did she do? She gave her safety. She gave her hope. She made her happy. Until she didn’t. “You weren’t there, Rio!” Agatha cried, staring up at Rio. This was all going terribly. Hesitating slightly, but deciding to move forward she slowly started climbing the steps up to where Rio was standing with her arms angrily crossed.



“I messed up, I know.” Agatha said, taking a step higher. “And I pushed you away.” Another step. “But now, it's all wrong. Nothing sounds right.” Another few steps. “I can’t think because without your voice there is this huge hole in my head, and I can’t sleep without you being there anymore either.” 



Agatha was halfway up the stairs and Rio remained frozen in place.



“Even being alone irritates me, because without you it is too quiet.” Agatha admitted, now only a few steps away from Rio.



“So you are here because I am loud and won't let you sleep.” Rio peered at her.



“No, Rio.” Agatha said, finally meeting Rio at the top of the staircase. “I am here because I miss you. I miss having you around and I miss hearing you sing and waking up with you and knowing that if I needed it, you were the face in the crowd that would make it all feel worth it.”



Rio was holding her breath, and Agatha took another step closer. 



“Not out here.” Rio said softly, and turned to disappear back into her apartment.



“Right”, Agatha thought. She wouldn’t have given herself another chance either.



But just as Agatha was about to descend the stairs to go drown her sorrows in whatever bottle she could find in her liquor cabinet, Rio interrupted her thoughts. “What are you doing? Get inside.”



Agatha wasn’t waiting to be invited twice, hell, she was lucky to be asked in the first place, so she quickly stepped through the apartment door and waited as Rio closed it behind them.



Rio’s things were almost exactly as she remembered them. The books all stacked neatly on the shelves and tables around the small apartment living room. There were no boxes, no packed bags. Even the plant in the window sill looked brand new, like it had just found its new permanent home.



“I thought you were leaving?” Agatha asked, spinning around once more to make sure that she was seeing correctly.



“What?” Rio asked, “Why would I…”



“New York.” Agatha answered again, repeating her earlier answer. “And Alice and Jen… they said good luck. Like you need good luck because you were leaving for New York. But all your stuff… You haven’t packed?”



Rio was laughing and Agatha was even more confused than before. Either she had this all wrong, or Rio was really laughing because Agatha was making a real ass of herself.



“Why would you even care, Agatha?” Rio asked when she finally stopped laughing. “You ghosted me, and proceeded to ignore my entire existence without any explanation." Rio was mad. “And you think I got what I wanted… You obviously got what you wanted, because you ran away as soon as you did. Coward.”



The pain in Rio’s voice matched the hurt in Agatha’s chest. “And just so you know, I didn’t get what I wanted, because I still want you.” Rio confessed quietly.



Agatha’s insides were screaming and shouting, “Say it back Agatha!” but Agatha’s head was still reeling from Rio’s confession, that Rio still wanted her.



Time felt like it stood still and Agatha wasn’t moving or saying anything either, and Rio had just thrown her heart out on the floor between them, waiting desperately for Agatha to move to claim it or destroy it for good.



When another silent moment passed, Rio let her arms hang to her sides. “I’m not going to tell you what to say, so…”



Agatha knew this was it. This was the moment she came for. “I want you too.” She said it so quickly, so loud, she almost shouted it to stop Rio from thinking anything other than just that, that she also wanted Rio, maybe even more than Rio realized could be possible. “I want you, Rio. I have not stopped wanting you since I first met you. But you are… amazing, and so talented and Salem is too small for what you deserve. I’d do anything to make you stay, but that wouldn’t be fair.”



Rio took a step closer and reached out to Agatha. “Why do you keep saying that I am leaving, Agatha?”



Agatha, trying her hardest to understand, looked puzzled at Rio. Her eyes were just as intensely dark as their first night together, and her hands were just as warm, holding onto Agatha’s. 



“They said goodbye and good luck, and New York. Rio?” Agatha tried to explain.



“I was talking to New York, yes, but only for a one time performance with the Philharmonic. And Alice and Jen… I was going to call you to talk, to do this I suppose, and they wished me luck because they want this, us, to work out almost just as much...” Rio explained. “Agatha, I told them I loved you.”



“Oh.” Agatha answered, shocked and confused, but relieved and embarrassed, and Rio was looking at her with the warmest smile she’s ever seen. “You love me?” 



“And from what I am hearing is that you…” Rio was smiling and moving closer.



“...I love you too.” Agatha quickly finished Rio’s sentence.



Rio’s smile widened and she cupped Agatha’s cheek with the palm of her hand. “...love me too.”



Agatha gripped Rio’s shirt and pulled her in, connecting their mouths for the first time in what felt like years. Everything inside of her was on fire, and Rio kissed back with the same desperation and relief of finally admitting their feelings and having it be returned in an achingly beautiful moment of truth. Agatha grabbed, trying to get closer and Rio’s hands and arms pulled and held onto her in fear of letting Agatha get away ever again.  



It was when Rio felt the wet line of tears on her love’s face, when she slowly pulled away and rested her forehead against Agatha’s.



“I’m not going anywhere.” Rio whispered.



Agatha nodded against Rio. “Good, because I’m not letting you go without me.”
