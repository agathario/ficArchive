Chapter 1

 

 

Agatha pressed her ear to her cream-colored wall as she enjoyed the deep groans of the neighbor next door. She herself left the TV on in the living room, on a higher volume than was necessary. Her grin widened, served the woman right for putting up that fucking wind chime on her balcony that she now heard at all hours of the night. It had been driving her crazy for weeks.

 

She leaned away from the wall as she moved into her kitchen to grab a drink, a snort of triumph escaping her as she heard her neighbor’s door slam shut. She was leaving for a bit, possibly for a run if the sweaty nature she had found her in the last time they had a nighttime rift had been any clue.

 

She remembered that at the time Rio had been in long black basketball shorts and a tight fitted black sports tee, showing off her toned arms and legs as Agatha tried to pretend she had no idea who she was–as she gripped her paper bags in her arms from a quick grocery run for dear life. Rio had of course offered to carry her bags, but she had glared at her as if she were a stranger.

 

Agatha moved to her balcony, sliding the glass door aside as she took her wooden bamboo pole that she normally used for taking down spider webs (which Rio had chastised her for, with a passive aggressive rant about their importance to the environment). Well, now she used her stick to fish and knock down Rio’s windchime… and if it got lost in the bushes a floor down that really wasn’t her problem.

 

She tapped at it a few times before it finally fell from its little hook. She was about ready to celebrate when she felt a pull to look below her and saw Rio Vidal with her arms crossed, eyes narrowowing in on her.

 

Agatha’s own mouth dropped open as she looked back at the scene of her crime then back at Rio. “You know the rental agreement has a clause about nuisance items!” She swept her hand casually in the general area. “You know I get migraines.”

 

Rio rolled her eyes, as she stomped back into the building. Agatha’s heart was racing from catching her eye. Well, so much for pretending she didn’t remember her now. A moment later she heard a knock at her door, which she promptly ignored until Rio started to ring her doorbell incessantly. 

 

Rolling her eyes, she made her way to her door and ripped it open. Her eyes staring down the woman loitering on her purple door mat shaped like a planchet. “I said what I said.” 

 

Rio placed her hand on the wall beside Agatha’s doorframe. “It’s common courtesy to ask me to remove it first. I could have taken it down at night.”

 

Agatha rolled her eyes. “Like you’ve listened to any of my other requests before.”

 

Rio’s hand collided with her own face and slid down slowly, already tired of Agatha’s shit if her body language was any clue. “Look. I have tried. I do take them seriously–it’s you who never listens to mine, before trying to get even…”

 

Agatha scoffed. “Please, I would never put in that kind of effort for someone like you.”

 

Rio took a step back, lifting her hands in defeat. “You know what… have a good night.” She turned on her heels and moved over one doormat to her own. A blue rectangle with little green dinosaurs that disappeared beneath her feet as she tried to quickly open her door.

 

Aggravated with her own staring, Agatha let her door slam before she turned the lock.

 

Was it too much to ask for a little peace and quiet? She had been working like crazy the nights she did work. Which unfortunately were slowing down with the crowd dwindling at the strip club due to the new one opening up just down the block. 

 

She pressed her face to her white wooden door and let out an exhale. She really didn’t want to look for a new job so soon, it felt like she had just found the last one. She heard her stomach rumble and sighed. She was running out of ramen packets and would soon need to find another food source, maybe she could splurge after her next paycheck and get an actual dinner. The apartment was safe and in a good neighborhood, but that came with a hefty price tag, one she could barely afford.

 

One that would be even tougher to afford now with her dipping income. She tucked her long wavy brunette hair behind her ear as she swayed a little to the kitchen, already dizzy with hunger. She pulled out a bowl and dipped the contents in before placing it in the microwave. It was good enough. She moved to sit on her blue worn out couch to wait the three minutes when she heard a sharp whimper. 

 

Her brow furrowed as she grabbed her remote and turned her TV on mute. Her head tilted toward the glass doors to the patio as if straining to hear the sound again. It came quickly and she was up and out the front door after grabbing her keys and her shoes. What if something was hurt?

 

Her arms crossed to stave off the chilly night air. She moved around the side of the building to check things out and in the bushes rooting around was fricken Rio Vidal. She rolled her eyes and was about to go back inside when she heard the high pitched sound again. It was definitely an animal. 

 

She took an unconscious step toward the shrubs when she stepped on a stick and the sound of it cracking had her neighbor’s head shooting up. Agatha expected Rio to have some smart remark about her being out in the cold without a jacket on, but she waved her right over. 

 

“I think it’s coming from over here?”

 

Agatha looked around just to make sure Rio was talking to her, and when there was no one else in sight, she figured she would go take a look. She joined Rio between the hedges and started peering through the little trees. It didn’t take long for Rio to flip on her phone light and scan the area. A tiny black thing soon illuminated in the light, shaking in the corner. But as Rio stepped closer, it darted the other way.

 

Rio froze. “Okay. We need to work together. Can you go back to the sidewalk? It’ll get trapped near the front doors if I can scare it your way.”

 

Agatha wanted to let her know that she shouldn’t be scaring it at all, but if they could catch it and find out if it needed help, they could both get back to their respective evenings. She pushed back through the bushes and prepared herself for the tiny fluffy thing to come her way. It didn’t take long for a loud rustling noise to start and the little black thing to frantically scamper her way. 

 

Quickly crouching, and without much thought, Agatha extended her hands to try and get a hold of the small thing. It ran right into her shins and screeched. Her eyes squinted at the sound. Grabbing at a wiggly puppy, it shrieked its head off at being caught, most likely assuming she was going to eat it.

 

Rio appeared before her a moment later, wrapping her gray jacket around it as it continued to yelp. Swaddling it as quickly as she could, it started to settle, realizing that further struggle was useless. Rio scoped it up in her arms and smiled triumphantly. “We did it!” She smiled brightly at Agatha and she swore her stomach did a little flip. Rio had maybe only smiled at her once, the week she moved in, before their games of who could be the most annoying neighbor had begun.

 

Taking in the sight of the little puppy, Agatha’s chest melted. Its face was scratched as if it had been caught on a rose bush. It’s little whines letting her know the thing surely didn’t feel well. She had always had a soft spot for animals, one that never extended toward people, given how they treated her her whole life.

 

She extended a finger and started to scratch its little head, shushing it quietly. “We should see if it’s got any more injuries. I think it’s screaming a bit too much for it to be just the scrapes to her face…”

 

Rio stilted her hip toward Agatha. “Can you grab my keys? They’re in that pocket. We should take it to the vet. I know one that’s still open till twelve.” 

 

Agatha awkwardly paused before grabbing Rio’s keys slowly, figuring the woman meant for her to hand them to her so she could be on her way, but she turned and started walking toward the garage below their building.

 

“One of us is going to need to hold it. Do you mind driving?”

 

Agatha’s fingers nervously played with the keys as she followed. Rio’s car was one of the nice ones, clearly she took pride in its upkeep and Agatha didn’t want to be the one that ruined it with her clumsiness. But it wasn’t like she was about to tell Rio about her less than stellar driving record. She cleared her throat and muttered a sure as they made it into the garage, Agatha moving to open Rio’s side door for her, before climbing in the driver's seat and starting the engine.

 

The dog cried out at the sound, but Rio was quick to soothe it. “It’s alright. You’re okay.” Rio set her down in her lap for a moment and put on her seatbelt. “Take Mathews to Harrington street. My friend runs a clinic there on the corner.”

 

Agatha turned and pulled out of the spot carefully. As if she knew she couldn’t afford the damage she might do to the car. Rio pointed out each turn and soon they were sitting in a little waiting room, Rio handing the puppy over to Agatha as she rattled off information to the nurse about where they had found the puppy. 

 

Agatha held on to the warm bundle in her arms and the puppy moved to lay its head against her chest, clearly exhausted by the whole ordeal. She rocked it gently, hoping that would at least help a little as Rio talked her way into a room. Returning to guide Agatha with a hand on her lower back. “They said the overflow room is open, Alice will fit us in when she can.”

 

As they settled into the room the air grew thick between them. Clearly they were both animal lovers, something Agatha had never assumed about Rio because despite the woman’s plant covered balcony, she seemed to live alone.

 

Agatha herself would have had a pet if she could afford one. She couldn’t even afford herself. The silence was stifling at the best of times. She had run away from home at sixteen and never looked back and somehow her twenties were almost behind her. Time had marched on and she often felt she didn’t have much to show for it aside from surviving.

 

She felt her chest warm as the puppy slept, she felt the anxiety building about what would happen to the little thing. She surely couldn’t take it. Maybe Rio could, she knew next to nothing about the woman aside from her plants, her wind chimes, and the music in Spanish she often would play too loudly during the day.

 

She felt her breath hitch as Rio leaned closer to check out the puppy. “Okay, this little thing is really freaking cute. One of us has to keep this baby.”

 

Agatha swallowed dryly. “I… don’t really have the money–” She felt her voice trail off as her skin prickled under the admission.

 

Rio nodded, her face softening in understanding. “I have a lot of random meetings and really don’t have time for a puppy.” A few thoughts seemed to pass across the other woman’s face before she looked up suddenly. “Wait. You work nights, right? Maybe you can help me with her during the day?”

 

Agatha’s eyes widened. “You want to pass this thing back and forth? Like divorced parents?”

 

Rio shrugged, her excitement growing. “That could totally work!” Her eyes softened as they located the black fluffball. “Come on, she’s so cute. Clearly she needs somewhere warm to sleep.”

 

“She?”

 

Rio nodded. “I’d place money on it.”

 

Agatha let her guard fall a little. “A dog is a lot of responsibility, Rio. I really can’t commit to anything and have you fall through.” Her stomach soured at the fact that not only might she be homeless and looking for a shelter soon, or better yet not around at all if things went to plan, but she might give the little thing a home only to drag her back into the same fate.

 

“I knew you knew my name.” 


Agatha’s eyes lifted. Of course she knew Rio’s name. She was about to snark back when she noticed a red stain coming through Rio’s jacket. “Oh fuck, Rio. I think she’s bleeding!” She frantically tried to point out the red splotch and closed her eyes tight when the puppy whined.

 

Rio’s hands quickly moved to unwrap the puppy just enough. “Okay, it’s alright.”

 

Feeling her head grow dizzy, Agatha agonized over if the puppy was about to pass away in her arms when it wiggled, trying to get away from whatever Rio was doing. Agatha’s eyes snapped open.

 

“You’re making it worse!”

 

Rio ignored her and shifted a little foot once more before setting it down. “It’s not that bad, she might lose that toe though, looks like it snagged on something. She’s barely bleeding now, maybe you were keeping pressure on it.”

 

Agatha unconsciously snuggled the puppy closer. “Are you s-sure she’s going to be okay?”

 

Rio’s eyes landed on hers, nodding. “I’m sure. She’s alright.”

 

Agatha gulped, she didn’t need more heartache in her time on earth.

 

Just as she was about to try and find something to say to Rio, the vet arrived. Rio’s friend Alice swept in and took the puppy from her arms. Rio grabbed Agatha’s hand and squeezed when she leaned forward to follow the puppy.

 

The vet and tech moved around the little thing quickly, its dark eyes trained on Agatha the whole time, begging for help.

 

“Well, It’s a girl, congratulations!” Alice smirked and Rio practically beamed as if saying see?

 

“We’ll need to take that toe, but we will numb her up and stitch it real good. We’ll send you home with some antibiotics and some other pain meds and she’ll be good as new in a few days. We’ll take her to the back and get her fixed up for you. Shouldn’t take too long.” 

 

Agatha’s mouth opened then closed at another squeeze from Rio. Allowing the care team to leave with the dog as she frantically gripped Rio’s hand back. “Are we doing the right thing?”

 

Rio blew out a breath. “I mean it’s pretty mangled, and who would miss just one toe, right? I’m sure she has a long life ahead of her. We got her.”

 

Agatha chewed on her lip as she contemplated if that were true, or even good. Spiraling for a few minutes before realizing she still gripped Rio’s fingers. Turning her head away suddenly she cleared her throat and it took Rio a few moments to release her hand. She was silent as she continued to ponder the commitment laid before them. “I really don’t have the money for her… this appointment alone would bankrupt me.” She felt nauseous at her admission.

 

“I told you I got it. Just… help me watch her during the day, I can’t raise a puppy alone. My job gets kind of busy.”

 

Agatha bit her lip as she wondered if her taking care of anything was really the best idea. But if it meant that Rio would keep her, and not drop her off at some shelter, then maybe she could at least try.

 

She rolled her shoulders and sighed. “Okay. I… I can do days.” She felt her chest practically swell with the way Rio lit up.

 

“Awesome! I’ve always wanted a puppy! I had a dog and cat growing up and like ten lizards but nothing as an adult. I don’t know how people do it alone, you know? A puppy is worse than a baby… well, so I’ve heard.” She blushed and crossed her arms as she seemed prepared to rattle on. 

 

Agatha slumped down in her chair. “I’m so exhausted, I was hoping for an early night, and these chairs are killing me.”

 

Rio snorted and Agatha glared. “What?”

 

“Well, my first thought was to offer you my lap but I doubt that would be appreciated.”

 

Agatha opened her mouth to protest… finding that… she kinda liked the idea, but there was no way in hell she was about to tell her neighbor that when the woman waved her off.

 

“Anyways, hopefully we won’t be here too long and if we are, you can take my keys and go sit in the car.”

 

Agatha laughed. “What if I steal your car?”

 

Rio rolled her eyes. “I know where you live, don’t I?”

 

Agatha shimmied down further. “Unfortunately.” Her head leaning against the back of the seat as her eyes stared at the ceiling.

 

Rio tilted her head. “An early night, huh? That documentary was going to take a while.”

 

Agatha rolled her head to the side to stare blankly at her companion. “What documentary?”

 

Rio deflated a bit and Agatha found that she did not like how that made her feel. “Oh, I thought you were actually watching that. It’s about the Salem Witch Trials, I like that sort of stuff… I thought… maybe you did too. Was it really only to piss me off?”

 

Agatha huffed. “You… you play guitar at like nine AM on Saturdays. You’re one to talk about my noise levels.”

 

Rio narrowed her eyes. “Who’s not awake by nine on the weekends?”

 

Agatha took her foot and kicked at Rio in retaliation and the girl only smiled. “Hey! Stop that. I try to keep it down, I don’t even use my amp.”

 

Agatha’s eyes rolled. “You sing too.”

 

Rio seemed to forget what she was saying for a moment. “I… didn’t realize you could hear that part. I really thought I was quiet.”

 

Agatha shrugged. “The singing… isn’t terrible, I guess. But the guitar is annoying that early.”

 

Rio nodded. “I can wait longer before I practice. Maybe do my run first thing? Well, depending on when my mornings start with the puppy–when will you want to do handoffs if you sleep in on the weekends?”

 

“I’m not up by then during the week either.”

 

Dark eyes narrowed. “Then how will you potty her?”

 

She did not like her tone. “I don’t know. I’ll teach her to pee in the tub or something.”

 

Rio’s eyes fluttered shut. “We have to have consistency and I am not training her to pee in my tub. How about potty trays?”

 

Agatha snorted. “So my house can smell like urine?”

 

“We have balconies…”

 

“So she can fall to her death? We’re on the second floor, Rio, use your head.” She couldn’t believe this woman, it was as if she didn’t think things through at all. Agatha was always thinking through ten scenarios at once before making a decision, but she sort of had to for survival. She didn’t survive this long without learning how to play every single interaction… Well, maybe her guard was down a little for this one.

 

Rio opened her mouth to say something then refrained, taking longer to find what she needed. Expanding on her previous thought as if it were obvious. “She will be in a harness. I won’t let her out alone. Or yeah, we could fit a tray in the bathroom, maybe just to start?”

 

That sounded reasonable at least. She pushed her hair back from her face as she conceded. “Inside to start… but I don’t like it.”

 

Rio perked back up. “Deal.” She got out her phone. “I’ll order trays now, should come in tomorrow, we can grab some potty pads on the way home…”

 

So they were doing this. She didn’t have a lot in her home to begin with, an old ragged couch, a coffee table, a TV when one of the girls moved away and gave her an apartment full of essentials. Before that she had been sleeping in a sleeping bag on her hard linoleum floor. She now had a decent enough foam mattress, and sheets, but that had taken a while to acquire.

 

Her mind started to wander and she worried about where the dog would sleep. Her house being too cold to have her simply sleep on the floor on a towel. “The dog sleeps in the bed.”

 

Rio paused her scrolling and shook her head. “Absolutely not. What if I have a girl over? I am not starting that now. She will have a perfectly cozy donut bed. Next to me, on the floor. Maybe one in each room…”

 

Agatha pouted. “That’s horrible. I’m letting her on my couch.” She added and in my bed in her head where Rio couldn’t hear it.

 

Rio sighed. “I’ll allow the couch with a throw just for her. I don’t want her getting the couch a mess, but I can compromise.” She stuck out her hand for Agatha to shake, and she did so reluctantly. Not liking how trapped this was all making her feel already.

 

A knock at the door interrupted them from their planning as a barely awake puppy was placed back in Agatha’s arms. Alice patted Rio on the shoulder. “She’s a little champ! Didn’t even freak out about the shot. We will follow up with her other results tomorrow. I’ll text you, but she looks good. Her BP and heart rate were good, a little elevated but she was in pain. They are much better now.”

 

Rio stood. “Alright, thanks, Al, I guess we settle this at the desk?”


The tech smiled at her and Agatha wasn’t sure why but she was about to jump the woman for looking at her neighbor with a little too much interest. They had a puppy to take care of, Rio didn’t need to be distracting herself with other women.

 

Agatha stood and moved between them, staring the woman down. “I would like to get out of here at some point tonight.” Rio’s hand found her lower back and she quieted at the gentle touch.

 

“She’s just tired.”

 

The tech seemed unbothered, though her face fell slightly. She must have assumed they were together at this point. Good. Rio had a child with her now. She led them out and rang Rio up, Agatha wanting to vomit all over again with the fifteen hundred dollar charge, and that was after her friend’s discount for the amputation. 

 

Climbing back into the car she was silent as Rio stopped and rushed into a 24 hour pharmacy to grab essentials before hopping back in. Agatha’s voice was rough as she struggled to get her words out.

 

“I’ll find some money.” She cringed at the fact that “finding some money” would mean she needed to entertain some men in the champagne room, not like she even did that to feed herself, only when she was about to lose the apartment due to late rent payments and she was reluctantly still alive.

 

She didn’t feel much better when a moment later Rio scratched the idea. “I told you I got it, I just need you to watch her during the day. You know, train her, play with her, potty her. Feed her breakfast. Oh, we will need to get on socializing too… I wonder if anyone else has a puppy her age in the building…”

 

Agatha gripped the puppy tighter. Her voice wavering at the thought that the dog currently occupying her lap might actually be someone’s dog. Some negligent person’s dog. “Rio… what if she belongs to someone? What if they come take her away? What if someone is looking for her and they’ll just let her wander off again…” She didn’t need this kind of anxiety hanging over her head, she had already made up her mind on what she thought about the puppy’s current owners and she would flee the country if she were told to give her back at this point. No one, not even a puppy, deserved that sort of neglect.

 

Rio sat a bit lower in her seat as well. “Okay, so we take her to a class. That’s not too hard, there must be one nearby. We’ll figure it out.” She glanced at the now sleeping puppy in Agatha’s lap. 

 

She smirked and Agatha grumbled. “What?”

 

Rio shook her head. “Oh nothing, I just never thought I was going to knock a woman up is all… I know this is sudden… but we have a pretty cute kid.”

 

Agatha rolled her eyes. “You better come through with the child support.”

 

“I’m good for it. I work plenty. Don’t you worry.”

Agatha stood awkwardly in Rio’s place with the puppy still snug in her arms. Taking in the art on the walls, paintings of beautiful women with patterns that shimmered in the low light she felt both a sense of calm and a reminder that she shouldn’t be here. That this space was too nice for her to darken its doorstep.

 

Noticing a giant print on one wall across from the TV, she was unable to help herself as she stepped closer, jumping slightly when Rio appeared by her side.

 

“Oh, I loved this artist and just had to have it. It has a second layer that glows in the dark. Do you want to see?”

 

Agatha shook her head, smiling softly at the offer. “Maybe another time? It is beautiful though.” Her eyes took in the details. So Rio did have at least some money–she had figured if her expensive workout clothing was anything to go by. Turning fully to Rio, she approached the puppy topic once more. Worried that perhaps her neighbor had gotten caught up in the moment and wasn’t really ready for this. “Are you sure you have her for tonight? I don’t work till Thursday so I could take her–temporarily.”

 

Rio reached a hand out and pet the soft fur on the puppy’s head. “Is it sad that I really wanted to cuddle up with her tonight already?”

 

Agatha rolled her eyes. “I suppose not.” She looked down at the bundle in her arms and the little thing was looking up at her with wide brown eyes, as if begging her not to leave. She needed to get out of there before she did something embarrassing like asking to stay longer. “Fine. I’ll retrieve her in the morning.” She handed over the small bundle and felt like a piece of her heart was going with it. That was a new feeling for her. “I’ll be by around ten, does that work for you?”

 

Rio nodded, her eyes not leaving the little thing now in her arms. 

 

As Agatha turned she heard Rio hesitantly call out. “Uhm, Agatha? Do you think we could keep the TV down at night for her? I can move her once I get a crate to the kitchen or play her music or something but… I’m a little tired to figure that all out tonight.”

 

Agatha nodded. “Sure.” She closed Rio’s door softly and assumed her neighbor would lock up as she took out her keys and let herself into her own door. Somehow when she entered and turned her TV off it wasn’t so suffocating, knowing that right next door Rio and the puppy were waiting for her in the morning. It seemed silly, but no one would know, so she cuddled up on her beat up couch, and pulled a thick blanket over herself, just to feel a little closer to them.

Chapter 2

Agatha knocked at ten a.m. sharp and the door was frantically pulled open. The first thing she saw was a puppy in Rio’s arms, its mouth around her bicep and Rio’s headset starting to fall off the back of her head. Agatha’s eyes widened at the panic in dark eyes, Rio looked a mess. She hadn’t even done her hair and looked like she had just rolled out of bed… or never slept at all. She motioned for Agatha to come in, setting the puppy down and quickly shoving a toy in her mouth.

 

“She’s teething. I have a wet towel in the freezer, but it needs fifteen minutes and I have a call in ten. Besides that, her stuff is in the bathroom and kitchen. Anything you need we can figure out after this call, I'm presenting.” There was a loud ping from her computer, one Agatha recognized from the other side of the wall that drove her nuts when she was trying to sleep during the day. 

 

Agatha tucked her hair behind her ear feeling a bit awkward as she knelt to grab the puppy’s toy, starting a little game of tug of war while the little thing attempted to growl and tug the toy back playfully. Keeping a sharp eye on her paw, realizing she shouldn’t be playing too much.

 

Rio rushed to the kitchen and got a mug and before she could race back to her office, she was turning and her words tumbled out. “I medicated her, she should get sleepy soon. I tried to keep her calm, but she’s a menace. She’s lucky she’s so cute.” Another ping seemed to set Rio off. “I’ll be back. If you’re not here I’ll pop over to yours. Help yourself to anything in the pantry or the fridge.”

 

She disappeared and Agatha heard her voice change as she entered work mode, the sound muted by the shut door.

 

Agatha stared at the puppy when the toy was successfully ripped from her hands. Her stomach grumbled as her eyes drifted to the kitchen. She was hungry. Maybe she could take something small… something the woman wouldn’t miss. She watched the door and heard Rio continue to ramble and figured she was safe from her reappearing anytime soon. She took quick steps toward the cabinets and the puppy followed, knocking the toy into her legs repeatedly as she opened doors and found the snacks. 

 

Her eyes crinkled when she found many things she would expect to find in the children's aisle. Little muffins, fruit snacks, crackers, all things that she wished she could afford. She took a bag of muffins as the box was already open and some fruit snacks and moved to the coffee maker, recognizing the same one she had access to at work. She found a mug that didn’t look special with its plain white glaze, and started a brew.

 

She squatted and held the toy while the puppy jumped around, starting to slow down. She wondered if the temperature she kept her place at would be warm enough, she really did try and cut costs everywhere she could. Rio’s place was comfortable.

If Agatha got too cold at night she would just go shower and hang out in the warm bathroom until it went cold, she would definitely be allowing the puppy to sleep in her bed for warmth, it wouldn’t hurt anything. Rio could deal with it.

 

She opened the package and ate her food quickly before throwing it in the trash, noting Rio had already done the same with a similar bag. She moved to the coffee and found there was milk in the fridge and sugar beside the coffee maker, helpfully labeled as such. It took her a moment but she found the spoons and finished making her drink before she moved to the coffee table by the couch, noting one of the blankets for the puppy Rio had grabbed from the store was already spread out.

 

She set the mug on a coaster and picked up the puppy and its toy, settling it in its blanket as she bunched it around the little thing. She was about to grab her drink when she felt a little head bump into her before little paws were pressing into her lap as the thing curled up and promptly passed out. 

 

So she wouldn’t be leaving anytime soon.

 

She grabbed her drink and sat back slowly. Taking more time to look around the room. Her hand settled on the puppy in her lap, just to know she was still breathing. Agatha listened to the sounds of Rio talking excitedly for a while, almost nodding off. Her eyes were starting to close when the click of a door had her bolting upright. She was still in Rio’s home.

 

Rio groaned loudly as she came out of the room.
