“Rio, I’m sorry, but it’s set. She’s expecting you.”

When Dr. Kale drew a hard line, Rio knew better than to cross it, but damn, this was a tough one to swallow.

As of moments ago, Rio was the newly-minted choir master for Agatha Harkness’ degree recital. Lord help her. She had tried to talk her way out of it, but Dr. Kale clearly wasn’t going to budge. Had Rio’s relationship with Dr. Kale been anything other than the respectful balance that it was, she would have told her that in fact there was no way her job wouldn’t be that difficult because working with Agatha Harkness was inherently unbearable. She was musical, creative, well-read, and a natural-born leader. Many of the instrumentalists at the school loved her for all of those reasons. “Agatha, I…thank you for telling me that - it’s…it’s a big door.”

“Well, Dr. Calderu read my mind, as she always does, and had a little chat with me about taking down my walls before I die alone in here,” she chuckled to herself. “I like you, Vidal, I want you to know things.”

Rio never thought she’d be ready to skip to Dr. Calderu’s door and hug her.

She wanted to say the right thing.

“I’m honored, and I’d tell you I like you too, but you already know that.”

She refocused on the part of this that was important.

“Agatha, he’s lucky to have your blood in his veins.”

Agatha smiled gently in response.

“Okay, I’m done talking,” Agatha’s tone shifted back to where it was before Rio mentioned the piece.

“Fine,” Rio smiled softly and shifted more of her weight back onto Agatha. She was in awe of the woman before her more and more as she came to know her. “I think you’ve served your time.”

Agatha grabbed the back of Rio’s neck and kissed her, and kissed her, and kissed her.

 

At Agatha’s recital, Rio cried watching Agatha conduct the Brahms from the audience. She was a vision, and it felt like the greatest privilege of Rio’s life to know her story. When she was brought on stage for her bow, the excuse to hold Agatha’s hand was a lifeline. 

They were keeping it subtle, so Rio didn’t give her the bouquet she had brought until they were tucked away in Rio’s car. It was that night that Dr. Calderu grabbed Rio’s shoulder and whispered “take care of her.” Rio had every intention of doing just that. In fact, she couldn’t be stopped.

Agatha’s coat found a favorite hook by Rio’s front door, and every time Rio walked by it that spring, she marvelled at the gift she’d been given, and at the hours she’d put in marveling at how hot that gift looked in her signature coat.

Agatha started to teach Rio to play violin on Scratchy, but Rio found herself practicing late into the night in a way she hadn’t for a long time.

At their graduation, Rio and Agatha snuck away from the crowds onto the back stone steps overlooking the lawn.

“Are you ever scared we won’t be able to make this work? Neither of us knows where we’re ending up.”

“No,” Agatha replied quickly.

Rio turned, hoping for more of an answer that could make her share that confidence, and got one Agatha had never given before.

“I love you, maestro, the rest isn’t for us to worry about.”

Rio felt herself suddenly beaming.

That sounded like a good enough plan to her.
