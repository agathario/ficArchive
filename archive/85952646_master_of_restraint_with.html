June 10th.

Rio can only remember being nervous on the job once. Her very first day she could hardly sleep the night before. It was that day she established her morning routine. And it’s been the same ever since. 

Today, she isn’t nervous. She feels unusually calm. 

She wakes up at her usual 4am. Agatha didn’t stay over, both for the visual aspect for the case and because she was busy with her own set up for the day. Rio takes the same route on her jog that she did that very first day she ran into Agatha on the sidewalk. Right on time at 4:45 she’s in the shower. She gets dressed, makes coffee and feeds her pets.

Billy gulps, his face looking mildly terrified. “Yes, ma’am.”

They have a 30 second window to make this happen.

30 seconds from the time the President departs his armoured vehicle "The Beast," to the time he crosses the threshold of the building for a planned public speech. 30 seconds to change the course of history. 

It was a last minute decision to use an outside location, but Rio thought it would be better than inside during his speech. Less eyes, less cell phone cameras. 

The initial explosion will be big. Or at least the smoke of it will be. It’s the distraction. The one that will send half the team running and the other half scrambling to save him. 

She knows the agents will charge for the President the second they feel a whiff of trouble. She has to be among them. She has to be close enough to touch him, close enough that her hands blend with the arms and hands of every other agent there, mingling together until no one can tell who’s is who’s. 

They will shove him to the ground. It’s protocol. Make him as small as possible, cover every inch of his body until it’s them taking the hit, not him. 

Or so they think.  

The entire plan runs through her head. Over and over again.

Thanos exits the vehicle. And Rio’s mental timer starts. 

30 seconds. 

Rio’s eyes start to check every corner, every window, every doorway. The same stoic face she always has across her face, shoulders back, poised and professional. This time she isn’t looking for the threat to stop it, she’s making sure it’s concealed. 

She sees no sign of Agatha. Perfect. 

15 seconds. 

They walk in a coordinated group - Rio flanking one side of him, a slew of agents next to him, in front of him, behind him. Rio can feel the weight of the vial in her pocket, suddenly feeling like it is a thousand pounds. 

10 seconds. 

Halfway to the door, Thanos hesitates for a second, his footsteps flattering as he turns to cough into his hand. 

Rio holds her breath. Because even one second difference could be the difference between a coordinated attack and a failed one. 

Thanos starts walking again, closer to the doorway, closer to the threshold where it will all change.

3 seconds.

2.

1.

Boom.

Rio fights the urge to cough as smoke fills the air. The white haze is so thick she can barely see. Everyone is yelling around her, shouting directions. 

She throws herself in the direction of Thanos, landing in a heap with the other agents. He hits the ground hard, cursing and thrashing as the agents swarm him. Rio lands beside him, her left hand pinning his shoulder. Her right digs into her suit pocket and finds the syringe. Thanos bucks beneath her. 

The smoke obscures any vision she has of the other agents. It’s the blind flailing of limbs as Rio pulls the cap free with her teeth and stuffs it back in her pocket. She feels along Thanos’ shoulder with her left hand until she finds where it connects to his neck.

Then she plunges the needle down.

With her eyes closed, her mind only drifts to Agatha in this moment. If she’s completed her portion of it, if she’s safe, what it will feel like to walk away from it all. 

The shouting continues and she feels Thanos getting pulled away from her, away from the madness. He’s still grunting, yelling, but Rio can hear the slight slur in his voice now. She hears gurgling like he’s struggling to breathe.

Then she hears it. 

“MAD TITAN is down!”

Rio isn’t prepared for the aftermath. 

It isn’t the media frenzy surrounding Thanos’ death or the slew of conspiracy theories about how he was assassinated that surprise her. 

It isn’t even the military burial held for a body that isn’t hers. Or the induction to the Wall of Honor for her not dead self. 

It is the freedom.

For the first time in what felt like forever, Rio feels free. There is no gun on her hip, no earpiece, no being commanded what to do and wear and say every moment of everyday. 

She no longer belongs to the US government. She belongs to herself. And more importantly, to Agatha. 

It is like taking a full breath after being trapped underwater. 

Agatha and her board a private jet that same night. While the rest of the country devolves into chaos, they quietly assume their new identities and leave the rest of it behind. 

Somewhere over the Pacific, headed toward a remote island, Rio curls against Agatha’s shoulder and realizes she doesn’t mourning her old life at all. 

The plane lands hours later on a remote island. Agatha explains they were in the Marquesa Islands, 900 miles from the nearest continent. It’s more touristy than Rio expected with a few scattered hotels, restaurants and local stores. 

Rio hadn’t asked many questions about where they were going or what awaited them there. She’d trusted Agatha (and Lilia) to figure it out. 

But now there are people mingling about, tourists from other countries and Rio wonders if they thought this through enough. She ducks her head, letting her hair fall over her face. 

Agatha produces a large hat from her bag, plopping it on Rio’s head. “It’s going to be fine,” she reassures her. She takes her hand, leading her down a side street and onto the beach. 

“A boat?" Rio asks as they head toward a small dock. An island hopper is tied to the far end, bobbling gently with the waves. 

“It’s ours,” Agatha smiles. “We can use it to return to this main island to get supplies.”  

“And you know how to drive it?”

Agatha steps aboard and holds out a hard. “I watched a Youtube video. How hard can it be?” 

Rio freezes midstep. 

“I’m kidding,” Agatha adds. “One of my many talents.” She winks.

They drive out into the ocean, appearing headed toward nowhere. The sun beats down on Rio’s shoulders, the wind blowing through her hair. The air feels fresher, salty and crisp against her skin. 

After fifteen minutes, a small island comes into view. A village is nestled among the trees that line the shore. Once they dock, Rio helps tie off the boat via Agatha's instruction before following Agatha up the beach. The village consists of little more than a local bar and a small store stocked with necessities. More importantly, there are no hotels and only a small group of locals that give exactly zero shits about American politics. 

Agatha nods to someone near the bar, pointing toward their boat. They step onto the beach, grabbing their suitcases for them. 

They leave the village to continue down the beach. Tiny crabs scuttle along the shoreline. Seaweed tangles in their feet in the shallow waves. Rio picks up seashells, dropping them in her pocket as they walk. 

At first she doesn’t see the house in the distance. It’s tucked behind the trees, hidden beneath the canopy of broad green leaves. 

As they close the distance, the entire thing comes into view. The house sits just beyond the sand, sheltered by a cluster of mango trees heavy with fruit. A wide porch wraps around the front, the weathered wood facing the ocean. The trees provide enough cover that the house isn’t seen from the village, but not so much that it blocks the view of the water. 

“This is it,” Agath smiles. “Not bad, right?” 

Rio looks up at the wicker table and chairs sitting just off the porch.

“It’s perfect.” 

Agatha slips her hand into hers.

“Come on,” she tugs at her. “Let’s go see the inside.”

In the weeks that follow, Rio starts doing things simply because she can. The concept is a bit new to her, to just get up and do whatever she feels like.

Today, she decides she wants to learn how to drive the boat. She has a destination in mind and she wants to be able to go without asking Agatha to drive her. 

The boat has other opinions.

“Back so soon?” 

Rio glares at her. 

“You flooded the engine, didn’t you?” Agatha asks, but her words are softer than usual.  

Rio doesn’t reply, she simply throws the keys in her direction. She expects a lecture or teasing comment about how Rio only learned skills that the government deemed necessary, but there is none. Instead, Agatha stands and follows her down the beach, offering to clear the fuel line for her. Then she shows her how to properly untie it from the dock. Before long, Rio is jumping waves without a care in the world. 

Learning to drive the boat gives her the opportunity to take it over to the main island and ask around until she finds someone willing to tattoo her. Then she starts on the sleeve tattoo she’s wanted since she was 23, but never got given the Secret Service’s regulations against visible tattoos.

“Are you sure you want to?” Agatha asks her as Rio watches the screen.

Agatha nods, her face barely softening. 

The newscaster releases Thanos’ official cause of death - cardiac arrest due to smoke inhalation and blunt force trauma.  

It’s all bullshit. 

Agatha smiles.

Rio feels the tension drain from your shoulders and the muscles of her back relax.

Rio barely turns,  throwing her arms over the side of Agatha’s shoulders in a sideways hug.

The world quietly ticks on without them.

“Please.”

Agatha hums appreciatively as she chews. 

“It’s good, right?” Rio rumbles, her voice laced with arousal. 

Agatha’s eyes flicker up to meet her. Then she slowly licks a trace of mango juice from her lower lip. 

“So good,” Agatha mutters, her eyes still fixated on Rio.

It’s perfect, the life they’ve built here, away from everything else. She’ll be content to live out her days just like this - with the sand and the beach and Agatha.

She should tell her that. Tell her how happy she is. How, for the first time in her life, she’s stopped waiting for the other shoe to drop. 

Rio tilts her head up and opens her eyes. 

Agatha is kneeling before her. The oversized purple shirt nearly swallows her whole like this. Between trembling fingers is a small wooden ring. Even from here, Rio can see the mango wood, tiny vines etched into its surface. 

“I had this whole plan,” Agatha starts, swallowing hard. “Dinner on the beach and rose petals. I had a speech planned.” 

Agatha actually looks nervous. Rio’s heart stutters at the sight.

Rio’s heart leaps to her throat, her breath catching immediately. Tears flood her vision so fast, she has to blink multiple times to try to clear them.

But as the months went on, every future she allowed herself to picture ended the same way: her and Agatha growing old on this beach, surrounded by giant fruit trees and too many chickens. 

She’s thought about a small ceremony, their bare feet in the sand on the beach. Of sealing themselves together in more than just blood and bodies, but in vows.

“I know the moment isn’t perfect,” Agatha says softly. “But you are.” 

“Agatha,” Rio breathes. “It is perfect,” she says quietly, her voice wavering.  

“You are the best thing that’s ever happened to me, Rio. You make every day feel worth waking up for.” 

Agatha lifts the ring slightly. 

“So I’m done waiting.” Agatha’s smile is shaky and wet with tears. “Will you marry me?” 

Rio leans down to kiss one damp cheek, then the other. 

Agatha is opening crying now. 

Then she presses their foreheads together.

“Always."
