Chapter 1: Assumptions

Today’s the day.

It’s the day Rio Vidal is going to prove to her family that she’s more than just some debutante with a silver spoon in her mouth who sits in her ivory tower. It’s the day she’s going to prove to them that she can follow in her mothers’ footsteps and do something to be proud of in her life.

It’s just a negotiation.

Okay, maybe that’s underselling it.

It’s the negotiation, the only one that matters to all four of the Vidal women.

For Marisol and Xiomara, Rio’s mothers, it’s the negotiation that means they get to keep their livelihood, the one thing they’ve built from the ground up.

For Camila, Rio’s sister, it’s a promise that her inheritance remains intact and guarantees her succession in the family business.

The family business?

The Vidal Honey Farm.

It’s an expansive setup, totaling in at over sixteen thousand square feet with almost twenty-five thousand hives and over one hundred fifty million bees on a good summer. It’s something her mothers have curated for nearly forty years, their pride and joy even over their two children.

But there’s only one problem with the farm, in Rio’s opinion.

It sits on land the Vidal family does not own.

For decades, it’s been leased to them by an L. Calderu. Rio hasn’t done much research and certainly not enough to know what the L stands for, but one thing she does know is that the farm recently changed hands.

To one A. Harkness.

Rio wonders what the A stands for.

Alfred maybe.

Or Adam.

What about Alex?

She doesn’t know, and it doesn’t actually matter. What matters is that she needs to sit down with Mr. A. Harkness and plead her case as to why he should renew the lease for them. It’s been an ongoing tradition for as long as Rio can remember, but new ownership means new tactics.

And Rio needs to prove she’s up for the challenge.

She wakes up refreshed and well-rested, taking her time in getting ready. She showers, does her hair and make up, and puts on a dark navy power suit that’s sure to impress. She has no plans or using her appeal on Mr. A. Harkness, mostly because she has no aim to be disingenuous (as she would have no actual attraction to him no matter what he looked like), but also because she wants to do things the right way. She wants to use her intelligence, her cunning. She wants to prove she can do this on nothing but character alone.

When she arrives at the office, only Camila is there to greet her, informing her that their parents would not be present and were dealing with another issue.

Okay, that is fine.

Maybe even better than fine; there will be less pressure. She won’t feel like they’re watching her, waiting for her to do the wrong thing.

But she won’t. She's going to get it right.

Or so she thinks.

The board members gather around an elongated, polished wood table in their suits and business attire like an audience there to watch her perform. They know as well as Rio does that this is her one shot to not fuck this up.

“Just… Be smart, okay. Be direct but not abrasive,” Camila tells her as they congregate in the corner with Rio trying to hype herself up.

“I know, Camila. I’ve got this.” She says it like she’s certain, but she’s not. If anything, she’s scared to death of letting down their parents.

Behind her, a door opens and pulls her attention; a woman who looks to be in her mid thirties enters looking completely out of place. She’s not wearing a business suit or any kind of formal wear. In fact, she’s in a tank top, a flannel, and jeans.

Who the fuck is this and what the fuck is she doing here?

“Um…” Rio begins, taking a step toward the woman, preparing to see her out. “You’re here on behalf of Mr. Harkness?”

The woman’s attention cuts to her, her lips pursed and her eyes narrowed. But she does not speak.

So, stupidly, Rio continues.

“I’m sorry, I don’t mean to waste your time, but –”

“Rio,” Camila pipes up from behind her. Rio raises a hand, waving her off.

“But I’m only going to deal with–”

“Rio.”

Rio doesn’t even waste her energy by waving her off again. She flat out ignores her.

Stupid move.

Stupid fucking move.

“I fear I can only deal with the owner.” Rio glances over her shoulder, shooting her sister a glare that lasts all of two seconds before her focus is back on the woman.

Everyone else in the room is dead quiet, barely breathing. It’s so quiet, Rio is almost positive she can hear someone sneeze six floors down.

Why is it so fucking quiet?

The woman steps forward, deliberately knocking her shoulder into Rio’s enough to throw her off balance. She doesn’t completely lose her footing, but does have to shift to remain upright.

Oh, the nerve!

Who does this woman think she is? Rio spins on her heel to ask that very question, but only to see the woman place herself in the chair at the end of the table.

What. The. Fuck?!

She’s losing control of this entire thing way too fast, and she knows she needs to reign it in. In fact, the entire fucking room knows she’s fucking this up.

Maybe being demanding is her only option.

“Miss…” Rio begins, hoping the woman will fill in the blank.

When she does, Rio wishes she hadn’t.

“Harkness,” the woman voices, tilting her head to look up at Rio, who is looming over her shoulder. “Agatha Harkness.”

She leans back in her chair and bends her arm at the elbow, offering a hand for Rio to shake. Rio eyes it reluctantly but meets the gesture. She regrets that, too, when Agatha squeezes her hand so hard her knuckles roll.

Oh. So A. Harkness likes to play ball.

Rio’s got terrible hand-eye coordination.

But she’s got sass, maybe that will save her.

(It won’t.)

“Right, of course. My apologies.” Rio’s voice is calm, but as she steps away from A. Harkness, rubbing at her suddenly sore hand, she is fucking panicking. It’s a loop she wasn’t ready to be thrown for and she needs to rework her angle. Maybe it was a little sexist (and stupid) of her to have assumed A. Harkness was a man, but the context clues weren’t exactly helpful.

Her mothers had said more than once that Calderu (who she now also realizes is probably a woman) could be strict, but her successor would be worse. The crippling thing there is that Rio made an assumption that she, too, had been a man. It was stupid not to have asked, especially as she feels like a completely misinformed idiot who looked like an absolute clown.

Rio slowly shifts over and takes her seat, ready to begin negotiations.

They spend about forty minutes going over previous terms, stipulations, and payments, but A. Harkness doesn’t seem to be listening. She’s half vested, half picking at her nails (or what little of them there are – and from what Rio can see, there’s dirt under some of them) like she’d rather be anywhere else in the world.

Given what she’s wearing, that’s rather believable.

Who shows up to a business meeting in fucking flannel?

When the Vidal lawyers finally finish, A. Harkness has moved on to picking at the sleeve of her shirt, still completely disinterested.

Why is she even there, then? Why not send someone who actually knows what’s going on and can contribute?

“Miss Harkness?” the lawyer asks, a short and thin woman by the name of Delia.

A. Harkness’ head lifts, her attention suddenly piqued. Her eyes flitter around the table, blue like diamond dust, until they land and hesitate on Rio. They linger a little too long for Rio’s liking, though it leaves her staring right back.

She has to admit, even if only in her own head, A. Harkness is beautiful, in a rough and tumble kind of way, maybe even more akin to handsome. She’s got a strong jaw, perfect cheekbones, and a mean, vicious smile that does something to the base of Rio’s skull, like a tingling or a flash.

And then, her eyes snap back to the lawyer.

“Are there any conditions you’re willing to offer that we might be willing to add and meet?” Delia asks, blinking expectantly.

A. Harkness’ lips purse again as she considers, her gaze beginning to flitter around the room again.

If Rio’s honest, she finds this woman completely annoying. She’s wasting all their time for the sake of wasting all their time and Rio’s ready to go. She doesn’t want to sit here for another two hours while this woman pisses it all away for the sake of… What? Bad business? Why is she even acting like this is some hassle or convenience? For so long, it’s been a working agreement between her successor and Rio’s parents.

So why is it so fucking hard now?

Who does this woman think she is?

“I will only agree to lease the land on one condition.” She says it strongly, with conviction. She sounds like a woman who has already made up her mind.

Another lawyer, a wiry older woman with greying hair named Theresa, speaks up.

“Only one condition? You don’t want anything from the past leases put in?” It’s a smart question, but A. Harkness shakes her head assuredly.

“Only one.”

Rio knows she should ask, but something in her feels cold and it stops her mid-breath. There’s a look in the other woman’s eye as she stares down Theresa, one Rio can sense is nothing but trouble. Camila picks up the lead, realizing her sister isn’t going to say anything.

“And what’s that?”

A. Harkness fucking smirks.

“A hand in marriage.”

Okay, what?

What the fuck is she talking about? This isn’t 1800’s matriarchy! She can’t just demand a hand in marriage as some kind of blackmail. Who the fuck would she even want to marry, anyway? There’s no men in the room.

Stupid Rio.

Stupid, stupid Rio.

“Um?” Delia questions, clearly needing the woman to elaborate.

A. Harkness leans back in her chair and links her fingers together, letting her hands rest on her stomach. She looks cocky and smug in a way Rio can’t stand. It’s pissing her off, but she knows it’s fucking idiotic of her to say anything about it out loud.

“I’ll lease the land for ten years if she marries me.”

She says it with an air of certainty, but Rio can hear the utter defiance in her tone. It’s unnerving. It’s unsettling. But not as much as what she said.

She said ‘she.’ And… Well, there are about six ‘she’s in the room.

So who the fuck is she talking about?

“And um…” Theresa cuts in, “Who might you be referring to?”

She already knows. So does Delia. And the two other women.

But nobody realizes it quite as quickly as Camila, even before the piercing blue eyes of A. Harkness begin to shift around the table.

Rio is stupidly looking at her sister, only to find Camila staring straight back at her. When she turns her attention to the farmer at the end of the table, they make direct eye contact.

“Her.”

Her? Her who? Who the fuck is she talking about because it most certainly can’t be Rio!

Of course, like always, Rio is always the last to catch on. She’s completely suckerpunched by the realization as A. Harkness’ lips wind into a smirk.

“Me?” Rio questions, eyebrows raising as a hand flies up to point at her own chest. “You want me to marry you?” She decides at the last minute to leave the inflection off of the last word; she’d been judgemental enough for the day, no?

“I do,” A. Harkness agrees with a smirk. “And it’s the only condition to the ten year renewal. Anything less is unacceptable.” She leans forward in her chair and slaps her hands down on the table, using them as leverage to push herself to her feet. She’s got a Cheshire-like grin on her face and her eyes barely leave Rio the entire time.

“So give it some thought. I’d like to hear back from you by the end of the week.”

With that, she raises two fingers and taps her forehead, like some kind of stupid salute, and departs the same way she came in, only with a little more swagger in her step.

Oh, that’s fucking annoying.

It means she’s serious. She made her offer, set a timeline, and then walked right out the fucking door.

Slowly and still stunned, Rio turns to Camila in her seat, her face fallen and slightly horrified.

“Did that really just happen?” she asks, blinking.

Camila does nothing but nod.

“It did. So it seems you have a proposal to consider.”

Fuck.

 

Chapter 2: Explanations

Fuck. Fuck fuck fuck fuck fuck.

It’s the only thing Rio can think on a repetitive loop, and the only thing she’s been able to think since that morning. She has double checked with her sister at least six times to make sure that all actually happened and much to her chagrin, Camila confirms every single time.

So now… The only thing left… Is to tell the parents.

Fuck.

Rio loves her mothers, dearly so, but she somehow knows already that they are not going to be thrilled with the course of events taken earlier in the day. It’s not that she’s the family fuck up and they are probably awaiting some stupid recantation of how Rio fucked things up royally, but this is way worse.

This is way way worse.

What will they even think? Will they try to talk her out of it, tell her that she doesn’t have to? Will they push her forward like some sacrificial lamb, ready to fork her over to the first woman that asks for her hand in marriage? Could they truly be so cruel? Then again, it wasn’t really cruel if Rio was actually considering it in the first place.

Yeah, that’s a fucking thought.

If a decade of her family’s prosperity hinged on whether or not she would marry that dirty, smug, cunning… beautiful… admirable…

Hey!

Stop that!

Rio leans back onto the lounger in her room, pressing a hand over her eyes as she sighs defeatedly. She knows that she might be a downright idiot for considering the farmer’s proposal, but…

The truth is, her life is rather dull. Sure, she has money and nice clothes and a nice car, but what’s the point in having it all if there’s no one around to show it to? Sure, she has her sister, but they were raised isolated and homeschooled, leaving her to be just a little lacking in the social department. Camila is the same, but she has more charm about her than Rio is ever able to muster.

She knows there are plenty of questions that need to be asked and things that need to be truly, truly considered, but she isn’t sure if any of it matters. It is clear that A. Harkness – Agatha – had laid out her conditions and is only willing to grant the lease if those conditions are met. It is terrifying mostly because Rio has never been wanted so badly by someone her entire life.

Yes, she has been wanted the older she got, when she frequents clubs and raves with her sister, but not the way Agatha wants her.


Agatha wants her as a wife.

A wife.

Rio doesn’t have a single fucking clue in the world as to what it means to be a wife. Moreover, what is Agatha actually hoping to gain by taking on Rio as a wife? Rio knows nothing about her and so really, it could be anything. Maybe she’s hoping for a hostile takeover of the honey farm. Well, good luck to her on that because Rio only owns seven percent of the shares and that’s not a whole lot to do about anything. Most of her money’s in a trust anyway.

So what does a farmer like Agatha want out of marrying someone like Rio?

No, not someone like her.

What does she want out of marrying Rio?

“Hermana…” It’s soft as it comes through the door, accompanied by a light tap.

“Come in,” she calls dramatically, still splaying out on the lounger like she’s been shot and is slowly exsanguinating.

The door opens silently and Camila comes through, shutting it just as quietly behind her. She turns and takes one look at Rio, in her shorts and her stupid crop top, and rolls her eyes.

“Are you going to wallow in here all day?” she asks, crossing her arms as she plants herself by the side of the lounger.

“I might as well,” Rio heaves in a sigh, throwing her arm over her eyes to shield her from the sudden weight of the world.

“The moms are home now,” Camila informs her. “They… They asked how the meeting went.”

Rio’s arm drops and she snaps upright, wild eyes sweeping upward to her big sister. She’s panicking immediately, but figures she should probably ask anyway, no matter how much she doesn’t want to hear the answer.

“And what did you tell them?!” It comes out a little shrill and high-pitched, but only because Rio still hasn’t figured out how to explain to them that she did such a poor job of handling the negotiation that their only in is apparently for her to marry a fucking stranger.

And what does marriage even entail when it comes to Agatha? Does she want Rio to move in with her? To have a real wedding? To do wife stuff? What even is ‘wife stuff?’

Rio might be in her late twenties, but she’s not fucking ready for ‘wife stuff.’

Because she sure as hell isn’t going to be sleeping with Agatha, no matter how attractive she is, how nice her body was beneath her open flannel, or how toned her forearms looked beneath those rolled up sleeves…

Fuck! No!

Rio does not want to marry Agatha! She doesn’t even know her!

And now, she has to tell her parents.

They are not going to be happy.

“I told them they needed to ask you.”

Of course she did. Rio can even picture it in her head, the way Camila probably shrugged her shoulders and waved her hand toward Rio’s room upstairs. She probably acted like she has no clue what happened or how things have gone so far off the rails, but it doesn’t really matter.

Because it’s Rio’s fault.

Rio sighs and gets up, eyeing her sister warily before asking: “Shall we rip off the bandaid?”

Camila laughs.

“You’re ripping off duct tape, baby sis.” Sometimes when she says shit like that, it makes Rio want to shove her head in a toilet and flush, but she knows she has to behave. If she starts a knock down, drag out fight with Camila, their parents are surely going to know something is wrong and it’s only going to get worse from there.

With her head hung and her shame apparent, Rio heads out of her room and meets her mothers in the dining room, where they both sit sharing a piece of grapefruit for a mid-afternoon snack.

“Hola, mija,” her mama (Xiomara) greets.

“Had a busy morning?” her mother (Marisol) asks.

Rio wants to freeze. She wants to just lock up so severely that they don’t even notice her there anymore. She simply wants nothing more than to just disappear, fade into the tapestries, and never be heard from again.

“You could say that,” she replies, stepping up behind her usual chair for meal times. Her hands raise toying with the top rail and decorative carved points and her eyes focus on them to avoid having to make eye contact with her mothers.

“Rio Valentina,” her mama commands, causing her to lift her head to meet her gaze.

“Yes?”

“Did you renew the lease?”

Pinned. Straight pinned by the question. She has no way out, can’t dance around it no matter how badly she wants to. She’s honestly worried about the disappointment she’s going to see on the face of her parents and she wants to prolong it as much as she can.

It’s not practical, though, and she knows she has to tell them the truth.

“Not yet,” she admits, clearly unhappy because her eyes have not left her fingers and the way they’re smoothing over the wood.

“Not yet?” Marisol asks, causing Rio’s blood to run cold. “And why not?”

Rio grimaces, but even with her head dropped, they still clearly see it.

“Because I…”

“She fucked up,” Camila cuts in, earning her a sharp and pointed glare from her little sister.

“Oh?” Xiomara exhales, leaning back in her chair. The end of her spoon is pinched between her thumb and her pointer finger, the spiked end swinging menacingly.

Would her mama actually stab her with a grapefruit spoon? If she did, it would hurt like hell, but maybe she might show some mercy before considering it.

“I… I misunderstood… And made some assumptions… And it upset Ms. A. Harkness.”

Suddenly, both of her parents are staring directly at – no, through – her. They look alarmed, to say the least, but her mother looks fucking pissed.

“Rio,” she begins, very obviously trying to keep her voice low and neutral despite the sudden sense of alarm that settles over her. “What did you do?”

Rio’s eyes drop again, her fingers beginning to ache with how hard she’s pinching at the wood. “I… No one told me she was a woman, now did you?!” She doesn’t mean to, but at the exact same moment she whines, she stomps her foot like an absolute toddler.

“Rio, you did not,” her mother voices. She sounds really fucking disgruntled and really fucking disappointed.

“Oh, she so did,” Camila offers with a snort. “It was like a train wreck. Horrifying, but I couldn’t look away.”

Okay, so fuck her honestly. She could have saved the whole fucking thing, but she decided it was far better to sit back and watch Rio completely crash and burn at the one thing she was not supposed to fuck up.

Rio picks her head up again, seeing the downright terror on both of her parents’ faces, causing her stomach to sink. They think she fucked it up badly enough that they’re going to lose the farm. It’s enough to make her feel like a complete and total piece of shit.

And it also tells her what she already knows.

She has to marry Agatha.

“There’s… There’s still a way to renew the lease,” she voices. Her lips worry together and she exhales a sigh through her nose. “A. Harkness… Agatha… She said she will give us a ten year renewal…”

“What?! Really?!” Marisol chimes, leaning forward on her elbows with a mildly surprised expression on her face.

“Yes, but –”

“She has to marry the farmer!” Camila butts in. Swiftly, Rio swings out a hand and hits her – hard – in the stomach.

“Fucking hell, Cam,” she snaps, scowling. When she turns back to her parents, they both look fucking mortified.

“Mija, what?” Xiomara asks, her mouth agape.

Rio sighs, shaking her head. “I… I know I fucked up. And I… She literally said we didn’t need to meet any other terms or conditions, so long as I… Married her.”

There are no words in the history of any language that can explain the way her mothers are looking at her, but it makes her stomach sink like a hot ball of lead through her body, the floor, the crust of the earth, right into the core to be at home in the mass of hot fucking magma. They look so confused, but perhaps even more, they look immensely concerned. It’s because they don’t want to force her hand, but they both know if they don’t, they’re going to lose everything that means anything to them.

And Rio cannot let them lose the farm. Even more than it being her inheritance, it’s something they hold near and dear, something they’ve worked their whole lives to grow and nourish. It’s their entire livelihoods and realizing that they hang in the balance because of Rio… Well, they’re probably feeling rather torn.

There is a tense silence laying between them, heavy and wrought with the unknown, but Marisol is the one to speak first.

“We can find another plot… Get everything mo–”

“Mi amor…” Xiomara cuts in, her brow knit together in a scowl. “Do you know what you’re saying?”

“I do, Xio, but…” They’re only looking at each other, but her mother waves a hand at her. “You don’t honestly expect her to marry that woman, do you?”

Rio’s head drops again, ashamed, as the two women hold a back and forth.

“What I expect is for her to get us all out of a mess she got us into!”

That’s it. Rio can’t listen to any more. It’s hard enough as it is to know she’s fucked up, but she cannot just stand there and listen to them bicker and fight about what her choice should be. With a huff, she spins on her heel and ignores the calls after her to grab her keys, slip her feet into her slides, and make her way out the door.

She climbs into her Porsche and turns over the engine, purposely revving it before she peels out of the driveway and speeds toward the coast. She knows she shouldn’t have just blown out of the house without a single word to be said, but she needs a moment. She needs time to think.

She finds it on the beach.

The sounds of the waves as they wash on the shore calm her, enough to let her have a thought or two at a time instead of twelve.

The ultimatum given to her isn’t an easy one, but… Seeing her mothers’ reactions, seeing Camila’s glee at watching her turn into a complete screw up, Rio’s completely torn in two directions.

She wants to make them happy, but she doesn’t know if she can do it by giving up her life and tying it to someone she doesn’t even know.

But the fate of the honey farm depends on it.

It depends on her.

After another twenty minutes of mist and waves and peace and solitude, Rio finally makes her decision. She texts Theresa, one of the lawyers, and asks for A. Harkness’ contact information. She logs it into her phone the moment she receives it and brings up an empty text box, sighing heavily as she types.

“Hi, Ms. Harkness. This is Rio. Rio Vidal. I was hoping you might come into the office tomorrow for a meeting with me. Just me. No lawyers. I’d like to discuss the terms of your proposal. Does 11 am sound alright?”

Rio sends the message and sets her phone in her lap, looking out at the water in the light of the late afternoon sun.

It’s beautiful, really.

Will she still come here when she’s married?

Will she be allowed?

Her phone dings in her lap.

“It’s Agatha. And I’ll see you at 11. Have a good rest of your day!”

Great. Now, to get through a night with little to no sleep to meet her future wife in the morning.

Sounds easy, right?

Chapter 6: Understandings

As Rio bumps her way down the moderately sized driveway that leads to Agatha’s little one story house, she’s asking herself one thing on repeat:

What the actual fuck are you doing?

Plus, if she’s being brutally honest with herself, she’s been kind of a dick to the other woman. The least she can do is accept her hospitality.

Is that what this is?

Hospitality?

Or is Agatha merely trying to get the upper hand on her?

Or does Rio only think that because she’s the one so desperately trying to get the upper hand over her?

Because Rio is looking at this like a power play. That’s all it’s been for Agatha, isn’t it? Agatha is doing all of this elaborate marriage bullshit for power.

…Right?

Maybe it feels like she’s living in the stone ages with a proposal such as an arranged marriage, but this is different. It wasn’t like Agatha went about it the right way and asked her parents. No, she was strong-arming herself into Rio’s life by making demands, and unreasonable ones, at that.

And then, a thought strikes Rio.

Is Agatha doing this for money? One quick look around at the farmland that’s splayed out around her and she knows that can’t be the case. If anything, that amount of cane should bring in enough money to let Agatha build herself a bigger house. And yet…

Clearly, Agatha cares about it.

As Rio puts her car in park, she lifts her head to see Agatha by the door, holding up a finger to ask for a moment. She disappears inside, leaving Rio alone as she climbs out of the car and looks around.

It’s just fucking sugar cane as far as the eye can see.

God, what a different sight from the one Rio is used to of a concrete and steel jungle that smells like a mixture between burnt rubber and the carts of street vendors. It’s something she’s grown used to over the decades of living there, but it has its limits.

As she takes in a deep breath to brace herself for whatever Agatha Harkness has to say, she realizes something.

The air feels lighter. It feels fresher, even. It’s crisp despite the humidity and it feels almost calming, no matter the temperature. It’s so hot out, it makes Rio thankful for her choice of sportswear over anything professional. Surely, Agatha will have something to say about that, too.

She cuts across the front of her car and heads for the deck just as Agatha reemerges through the front door with two fresh bottles of water in hand and a somewhat sly smile on her face. It doesn’t fade in the least when she unexpectedly tosses one to Rio as she’s coming up the steps.

Thankfully, years of softball taught her how to be quick with her hands.

“Thanks,” she says with a nod of gratitude. It doesn’t seem to matter as Agatha passes her, lightly brushing their shoulders with intention. It might have been on purpose, but Rio realizes something almost immediately:

It’s nothing like the time the same thing happened in the boardroom. That was forceful, a reality check, a show of defiance. This time, it feels almost… soft. It isn’t meant to shake her, or display power. It’s just contact, barely there, and maybe not even on purpose at all.

Yeah, it’s hot and it’s a good thing; Rio can blame that for the way her cheeks suddenly burn.

Whoaaaaaa, what the fuck is that?

No, no! No blushing! No being soft over Agatha’s probably accidental brush of their shoulders!

Unacceptable!

“Uh…” Rio fumbles, looking over her shoulder as Agatha struts her way into the yard and around the back corner of the house.

“Are you coming or not?” she calls, her voice half muted due to the house between them.

Shit, right, she should follow.

Rio clutches at her water bottle as she scrambles around to meet Agatha, a little struck by what she finds.

Agatha is standing on the patio by an iron table and chairs, re-tying her hair into a disaster of a bun which she covers with that ugly hat again. Behind her, on the corner of the concrete patio, sits a covered hot tub. Next to it, a grill.

Somehow, Rio thought when she finally saw where she would be spending a decade of her life, she was going to find it downtrodden and depressing. What lies before her is not that at all; Agatha is no beast hiding away in her tower. She’s just a regular woman who likes to grill food and soak in a hot tub after a long day of hard work.

And that’s the ticket.

It’s the one thing that finally makes Rio understand.

Whatever Agatha’s motives are for this whole elaborate scheme, they’re human ones, not borne from pure evil.

She’s not trying to cut Rio off from her life or make her live in a dungeon to never see the light of day. She’s just… seeking a solution to whatever problems she might have. Even if Rio doesn’t understand it, that doesn’t mean she can fault her for it.

“Figured we’d start with the outside first,” Agatha says as she snatches up her water and meets Rio halfway. She waves a hand in the direction of the yard, and by extension, the fields and barn. Rio nods and falls into step behind her.

“The yard’s a decent size, obviously, but the real magic happens back here.” Agatha leads her to a far corner, one completely covered with about five rows of wooden boxes.

Rio very quickly realizes what they are.

Planters.

Some spots have metal wires sticking out of them, which Rio is pretty sure are for tomatoes. However, as they get closer, she sees there’s nothing in them.

“Some magic. It’s just a bunch of dirt.”

Agatha laughs, nodding. “It is just a bunch of dirt, yes. But this is how we’ve always fed ourselves. Growing your own food is self-sufficiency at its peak form. Plus, it’s fun.”

There’s something in Agatha’s tone that strikes Rio as slightly off, but she pushes it aside and nods when Agatha waves her on.

Through rows of sugar cane, they follow a dirt path that winds around and spills into the barn, which already smells horrifying, even from a distance.

So much for that clean, fresh air feeling.

Rio fights herself on covering her face, figuring she doesn’t want to offend Agatha yet again. However, Agatha clocks her without even turning around.

“The smell can be harsh at first,” she voices, approaching a pen where a Shetland cow approaches and allows her to pet it. “But Billy and I muck the stalls several times a day. You’ll get used to it, eventually.”

Oh, Rio hated that.

She hated the idea that animal shit and dirty fur were smells she would get used to.

But better, for once, to not say that out loud.

She asks a question instead.

“How many animals do you have?”

Agatha’s head lifts and a true smile curls her lips. It’s not sharp or defensive. It’s… calm.

“This guy,” she answers, nodding toward the cow. “His name’s Gus. And then…” She glances over her shoulder where Rio realizes there is a chorus of soft bleating coming from the other end of the barn. “Six goats. Twelve chickens. And a dog who we call Dog and roams between here and Billy’s mom’s house.”

“What kind of dog?” Rio asks, maybe a bit too eager.

“A mutt. We don’t know much about him other than he must have some sheepdog in him, given the way he tends to the animals.”

Rio softens.

She always wanted a dog.

But her moms always said no.

“That’s sweet.”

“He also loves bell peppers. He waits til I pick ‘em, but he sits there and watches me as I do.”

Okay, so that is adorable, if Rio is allowed to admit it to herself.

Maybe she’s been a bit… cruel toward Agatha. It’s not that anyone would blame her, but…

Perhaps sitting up in her golden tower with a silver spoon in her mouth her whole life has led Rio to be a bit… disillusioned. It isn’t that her superiority complex has always stemmed from maliciousness, but she realizes how that her maliciousness toward Agatha has come from a place of mentally dubbing her as inferior.

Just because she lives on a farm and generates her own income by getting her hands dirty, it doesn’t mean she’s inferior.

Just different.

Completely different from all Rio has ever known.

But maybe that isn’t the worst thing in the world.

Agatha gives Gus a gentle pat on the head and steps away, nodding toward the barn doors in silent permission that they could leave. Rio takes it for what it is and makes a quick exit, thankful for the reprieve of fresher air, even if the stench of the barn still lingers.

“And all of it’s yours,” Rio observes, kind of feeling like they’re in that scene from The Lion King where Mufasa shows Simba all that will be his one day.

Not that that’s what’s happening here, but the thought crosses her mind all the same.

“It was easier when my parents were still around, but I get help when I need it.”

Oh. Parents. That was where she got the farm?

Agatha begins walking back toward the house and Rio follows, not too far behind.

“Calderu?” she squeaks, trying to tread the line between curious and sensitive.

“Yeah. Lilia. And her wife Lorna. They…” Agatha pauses as she reaches the iron table and pulls out a chair. Instead of sitting in it, she pulls another one out for herself and plops down. She flourishes a hand toward the other, an obvious invitation.

Rio takes it.

She places herself across from Agatha, her full attention on her, as she assumes she was not quite finished speaking.

“They adopted me as an infant. Something about an unwell mother and a house fire and all that,” she says like it's nothing, flipping a hand out as she looks over Rio’s shoulder at the fields. “But… They raised me here, taught me how to do everything, how to tend to the animals and cull the cane. It’s my life, and most of what I’ve known.”

Agatha doesn’t miss a beat, though.

Holy shit.

And it’s Agatha’s reality.

At this moment, Rio realizes something very quickly and very harshly.

She’s been an absolute cunt.

Agatha isn’t some vicious monster setting out to ruin her life. She’s just a grieving woman who spends most of her time alone.

“You’ve got ten years of it in front of you, don’t you?”

“I do, don’t I?”

Oh how Rio hated the way Agatha was so fucking comfortable calling her out.

Back on track!

Dishes!

Chapter 13: Acquaintances

Priorities, right?

$0.00

Empty.

Nothing.

They drained her fund.

Fuck.

Agatha.

Wanda.

Probably a bruise Rio should not consider pressing. Hm. That was an interesting tidbit of information. Agatha was into the supernatural.

Agatha is going to do everything she can.

Sandwiches.

Chapter 14: Hospitality

Who ever would have thought that Rio would ever find more comfort out in the middle of nowhere rather than the place she grew up her entire life?

Not Rio, that’s for sure.

Yet, it is her life. It is her reality. It is a dream she didn’t know she had that has somehow come true.

With one glass split between them, they head home.

Or… Rio thinks they’re going home.

It’s why she’s incredibly confused when Agatha turns down a road that Rio knows does not lead back to the farm. She’s driven it enough times now to know where she’s at and how to get there. So where the fuck is Agatha going?

“My love?” Rio questions, glancing over at her wife as she keeps driving.

“Yes?” Agatha says back sweetly, as if she doesn’t know exactly what she’s doing.

“Where are we going?” Rio asks, her tone a little sing-songy to try and pull it out of Agatha a little more.

It fails miserably.

“It’s a nice day,” she half-answers. “I thought it would be a good day for a drive.”

She’s full of shit and they both know it. She’s got something up her sleeve and she is very clearly trying to hide it. They are both fully aware that everything she does, she does with purpose and as a result, this can be no joy ride in the middle of the countryside.

“I think you’re not being honest,” Rio pokes back, lightness in her voice. She isn’t upset or distrusting of Agatha’s motives, but she knows she’s up to something.

Agatha is always up to something.

It’s just who she is, apparently.

And Rio loves her for it.

“And if I’m not, are you going to divorce me?” Her voice is light, too, and she wears a proud smirk on her face because she knows Rio won’t.

They love each other.

For a marriage that started out of necessity, Rio knows there’s no one else she would rather be bound to in that way.

It’s only Agatha.

It could only ever be Agatha.

Maybe she showed up out of nowhere and turned Rio’s life completely on its head, but Rio knows now that she would not have wanted her life to go any other way. Gone is the feeling of being insufficient and not enough. Gone are the days of wondering whether what she was doing was enough.

Now, she knows as long as she gives her all in any situation, she’s going to have Agatha on her side, backing her up.

It’s a nice feeling to have, really.

One Rio knows she never would have had any other way.

“You’re an ass,” Rio teases, rolling her eyes. Her gaze casts out the window, watching the endless fields roll by. Finally, Agatha slows the truck and turns into a long drive, one with a massive white barn at the end.

She misses the sign, though.

“Agatha? What is this?”

Instead of answering her, Agatha starts to laugh.

It confuses Rio.

“What’s so funny?”

Agatha grins, dropping one hand to pick up Rio’s from her lap, weaving their fingers together.

“I was right.”

“About?”

“You’ve never been here in your entire life.”

It’s at this moment that it dawns on Rio exactly where they are.

They’re at the honey farm.

Their honey farm.

Immediately, her eyes well with tears and her free hand covers her mouth. It’s a moving gesture, one Agatha did not have to indulge in and chose to all the same. It’s touching in all the right ways, something that means more to Rio than she knows how to say.

Her wife and their honey farm.

It truly was perfect.

Agatha drives to the close end of the barn and pulls onto some gravel, parking the truck. She gets out and rushes around to open Rio’s door, pulling her into her arms the moment she sets foot on steady ground and brushing her tears away.

“Don’t cry,” she urges. “I didn’t do all of this just for you to cry about it.”

Ah, there’s her Agatha. Quippy and full of snark even in the most delicate of situations.

“I know, I know. I just…” Rio looks over her shoulder, surveying the pristine barn and the bees flying in and out of the vents in the sides and roof. She turns back to Agatha and reaches up to cup her cheek. “I’ve never had anyone love me the way you love me. I’ve never had anyone step up for me, defend me, steal a multi-million dollar company for me…”

They both laugh softly, causing a couple more tears to roll down Rio’s cheeks.

“I know I’m a mess sometimes, and I know I’m never going to do things right all the time, but… You know that, too, and you still love me. You love me through my flaws, my faults, and my insecurities. I don’t know why, but I am thankful you do.”

Agatha leans in and brushes their lips together, a light kiss that says enough but when she pulls away, she uses her words, too.

“My love… I… When I knew I wanted to do this, I had a Rule Number Six, too. Only mine was Rule Number One. It was a business arrangement, something I knew I had to do to protect what was left of what I cared about. And… You just…” Agatha shakes her head, her lips pressing for a moment as she searches for the right way to say things.

“You just showed up like a kicked puppy, clearly needing something no one else has ever really given you. And for as angry as I was, and as annoying as you can sometimes be… I mean it when I say there’s no one I’d rather be married to.”

Again, the tears begin to pour down Rio’s cheek but she pays them no mind as she takes hold of Agatha by the face and kisses her warm and slow. It’s a kiss of love and devotion, a kiss that says just how fucking far Rio would go to care for her wife.

When she pulls away, both stars and tears fill her eyes as she beholds the woman before her. Agatha reaches for one of her hands, holding it down by their sides, causing Rio to give her fingers a soft squeeze.

“I’ve been thinking…” Rio whispers, glancing toward the barn.

“Yeah? For how long?” Agatha teases, kissing her on the cheek.

“Since we left the office.”

“About?”

“I think we’re due for a name change, don’t you?” She turns back to her wife, a grin on her face.

Agatha tilts her head curiously in question.

“I think I like Harkness Honey.”

Agatha grins so fast and so wide it’s almost stunning, but she doesn’t let Rio see it long before she pulls her in for a strong hug. When she lets go, she takes Rio by the hand again and they turn to face the barn.

“Harkness Honey,” Agatha states, giving Rio’s hand a light squeeze. “I like it, too.”

And between them, one lone honeybee lands on the back of Agatha’s hand.
