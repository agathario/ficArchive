Throwing two balls of purple rage from her palms, Agatha laughed when Rio turned sideways in order to avoid the hits. “If you truly kept with the times, you’d know no one asks anything anymore.”

“Oh but I’d love to see you,” With a clap, Rio conjured a thunderbolt that hit the recently reconstructed greenhouse and to shield herself from the glass shards, Agatha lowered. “On your knees.” In a swift motion, Agatha’s outfit morphed into her new combat clothing and Rio shook her head. “Aren’t you a little old for a thigh gap?”

“Shouldn’t you be acting your age?” Agatha threw a purple attack that Rio smoothly deflected.

“I’m the natural order of all things, baby,” A green projectile flew out of Rio’s palms, clearly meant to miss Agatha by flying over her left shoulder. “And you love me.”

“BUT SHE WILL NEVER ADMIT IT.” Tommy spoke from his seat on the stairs, causing the two battling witches to laugh while Billy and Jen gave him a more concerned look. “What? I like it when they fight, free fireworks.”

“Is that right, Agatha?”

“Make me!”

Their back and forth didn’t last very long since their presence was required elsewhere. Without really saying goodbye other than a quick wave, Rio and Agatha entered a portal that would take them directly to where they needed to be. Truth be told, that had been the first time Agatha had travelled through Rio’s realm and while it was a faster, more direct, path, she didn’t arrive unscathed. A little dizziness or some motion sickness was to be expected, as per Rio’s explanation, and both side effects were experienced by Agatha.

Suddenly they were standing by Nicholas' resting place, precisely under the same tree where they had been a full year ago. The sunlight was hitting the leaves in a similar way and yet Agatha felt beyond changed. The past twelve months had taught Agatha a variety of lessons, some more painful than others, but all of them forced her into a stage where she hadn’t allowed herself to be before: acceptance. Of her circumstances, of Rio’s existence, of Nicky’s limitations. Everything had become abundantly clear through the prism of her translucent spiritual journey.

Still, as she watched Rio magically announce their presence to the trifoliate orange hedge guarding their son, Agatha was afraid. Less about Nicky’s reaction and more so about her worthiness of being in his presence. For centuries, she’d done everything he wouldn’t have wanted her to and while, yes, she had already dried a handful of witches since returning to the realm of the living, Agatha had made a point to only do that to those who deserved it. Rio said it truly didn’t bother the balance of the universe one way or another however she helped Agatha select those who would make less of a difference should they simply… disappear.

In fact, Rio was the happiest she had been in a very long time. It didn’t bring her any pleasure or personal gain to see Agatha take down witches but she was fully aware it was needed for Agatha to stay alive. To feel alive. If doing it exclusively to those who warranted a more cruel demise, so be it. Besides, it seemed to make Billy feel more ethical in his own choices, which in turn allowed Agatha to believe her actions were justified and, as a snowball effect, it made Rio happier. The system was working for everyone involved and for as long as that was possible, Rio would play her part.

Contrary to what had happened over the many years Rio had been the sole visitor of that sacred ground, the hedge produced a large number of fruits. Exchanging a smile, Agatha and Rio left unspoken that it was a clear sign they should’ve been doing this together all along. Recreating the crown she had crafted the previous year, Rio handed it to Agatha and, instead of taking it, Agatha lowered her head for Rio to be the one crowning her.

“How do I look?”

“Like my wife.” Rio’s tone was tender, wrapped with the emotion that almost clouded her eyes with a mist of tears. “Beautiful.”

Trying to hold her own tears back, Agatha smiled sheepishly. “So we are together, after all?”

“For as long as you’ll have me.”

“That will be a long, long, long,” Agatha lingered on the pronunciation of the forth “looooooooong” and held Rio’s hands. “Time.”

“Are you sure?”

“Never been more certain.” Pressing her lips to those belonging to the love of her life, Agatha quickly pulled back to add. “Or ready.”

“I love you.” The words flew out of Rio’s lips like the breeze on a warm summer day, freely and comforting.

“I love you too.” Agatha smiled, her chest filled with love and a beating heart. “Shall we?”

“Lead the way.”

From one of her pockets, Agatha retrieved the bell that Nicholas had stolen in Salem and that she used as the starting point for every chant. With a bang that echoed through the forest, from tree to tree, Agatha took a deep breath, reassured in the loving way Rio was looking at her.

“Seekest thou the road to all that's foul and fair

Gather sisters fire, water, earth and air

Darkest hour, wake thy power, earthly and divine

Burn and brew with coven true and glory shall be thine.”

Closing her eyes, Agatha handed the bell to Rio, who safely stored it inside her cloak before joining Agatha in her singing, their voices merging together in the same unison of their heartbeats.

“Down, down, down the road

Down the witches' road

Down, down, down the road

Down the witches' road

Down, down, down the road

Down the witches' road

Circle sewn with fate

Unlock thy hidden gate.”

Not too far from where they stood, Nicholas' portal began forming yet Rio was the only one who witnessed it since Agatha’s eyes remained shut.

“Marching ever forward 'neath the wooded shrine

I stray not from the path, I hold death's hand in mine.”

Rio held Agatha’s right hand, which made her present forth the left one as well, believing Rio wanted to close the circle. However, Rio kept her left hand away from Agatha’s reach.


“Primal night, giveth sight familiar by thy side

If onе be gone, we carry on, spirit as our guidе.”



“I still don't understand how Agatha is alive again.” Since they’d been left with dish washing duty, Jen handed Billy a wet plate for him to dry.

Despite having been there, Billy wasn’t entirely certain how it was possible either. “She just is.”

“And she's technically immortal now.”

“WHAT?!”

“Oh no.” Tommy looked between the two witches staring at him as if he’d confessed a heinous crime. “Oh no I shouldn't have said that.”

“Spill the rest or I'll read your mind!” It was mostly an empty threat since he would never force information out of his brother’s head without permission, that was a line Billy refused to cross. However… He simply needed to know.

“She is going to kill me…”

“We'll find you a protection witch,” Jen smacked both hands on the kitchen counter that physically separated her from Tommy. “Now talk!”

“Ah…” With a pained look on his face, and seeing how his brother wasn’t going to help him out of this one either, Tommy conceded. “Agatha is... part Rio now... Sort of... Kind of…” Shaking his head, Tommy scratched the back of his neck. “I don't really understand how it works but basically Agatha is immortal until she decides otherwise and then…” A pause and a deep sigh. “Rio dies with her.”

“That doesn't make any sense.” Jen looked at Billy who seemed just as confused as she was. “Death can't die.”

“Not Death.” Tommy clarified. “Rio, as we know her, will die with Agatha. Death will just.. become someone else. Or something else.”

“So they're... one?”

“I guess?” With a shrug, Tommy exhaled dreamingly. “Man, I can't wait to fall in love.”

“Some role models you have…” Jen shivered despite there being no cold breeze inside the house. “Does anyone hear singing?”

“Blood and tears and bone

Maiden, Mother, Crone.”

At last, Agatha opened her eyes and met Rio’s gaze, their singing moving into a whole other level of intensity.

“The road is wild and wicked, winding through the wood

Where all that's wrong is right and all that's bad is good

Through many miles of tricks and trials, we'll wander high and low

Tame your fears, a door appears, the time has come to go.”

Gesturing to the side with her head, Agatha followed Rio’s cue and saw a portal standing over Nicholas' grave. The eminence of their encounter made Agatha’s heart beat faster and Rio gave her hand a gentle squeeze, reassuringly. Without stopping the song, Agatha watched as their son walked out of the portal, smiling.

“Down, down, down the road (Down, down, down, down)

Down the witches' road (Down the witches' road)

Down, down, down the road (Down the witches' road)

Down the witches' road (Down the witches' road)

Down, down, down the road (Down the witches' road)

Down the witches' road (Down the witches' road)”

“Follow me, my friend,” Singing on his own, Nicholas held Rio and Agatha’s hands, closing the circle the way it had always been meant to: as a family. “To glory at the end.”

Healing isn’t linear, and neither is grief. Some days the sun shines, the grass appears to be greener and the blood in your veins feels warmer. There’s hope, and it's palpable. However there are days where the sun seems to be further away, the grass wilts and your skin feels colder. There is no point in attempting to control such processes, it’s a futile war that will inevitably end with your loss, one way or the other. The only path to survival without letting your heart harden or sink down with the weight is to accept… And allow yourself to feel.
