Sensing that Rio needed to decompress, Agatha had remained quiet throughout her process, even as she turned everything off and plated their food. She was even soundless as she made her way to the living room, a plate in each hand, so they could eat while watching whatever it was Rio had turned on.

As she passed behind where Rio was sitting on the couch, her eyes dropped innocently to glance at whatever it was Rio was looking at on her phone.

Socked feet planted to hardwood in the most unnatural way as Agatha blinked, still not making a single sound. Rio kept scrolling, picture after picture, pose after pose.

Rio was scrolling through pictures of babies.

Agatha took three seconds to decide her next move. She chose to keep walking, as if she had never stopped. "Whatcha lookin' at, my love?" she posed innocently as she came around the end of the couch.

As if caught red-handed, Rio quickly locked her phone and pressed it to her chest. Her eyes flew up, realizing that her reaction looked suspicious in and of itself, and she was right, but not for the reasons she was thinking.

They were locked into the moment as Agatha stood above Rio, holding that plate out with a genuinely sweet look on her face despite her confusion over what she had witnessed in the span of the last minute.

And then, Rio looked guilty.

"Can we talk about it after we eat?" she asked gently, lips turning down at the corners. 

Despite her question, Rio had still not taken the plate from Agatha. 

In accordance, Agatha turned and sat next to her, passing the plate over until she actually took it that time.

"We can talk about it whenever you're ready," Agatha answered calmly, picking up the empanada on her plate to take a bite. To diffuse the tension, she hummed happily. "I think these might be my best yet."

She turned to look at Rio and while she could see the traces of panic still lingering, Agatha offered a grin that she hoped might chase them away, at least long enough for Rio to enjoy something she loved.

Rio offered her a tight smile and turned to her food, taking a bite before she released a pleased hum of her own. "I think you're right."

"Usually am," Agatha reminded her, taking another bite of her food.

"I learn it more and more every day," Rio responded.

What she didn't say was how much she appreciated it, and just how much she was going to need it once their meal was over. Whatever Agatha had to say would be the right answer, Rio just needed to hear it first.
