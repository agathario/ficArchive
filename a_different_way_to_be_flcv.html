Chapter 1: daisies and irises

Tuesdays are never the busiest—why would they be? Rio gets it. It’s the middle of the week, there’s nothing to do, no buying flowers for partners, and most graves are tended to on weekends and holidays. It's slow, and Rio would like slow, if it didn't mean an emptier wallet. Flower arranging isn't new, but it's new as a profession.

Sometimes, things don’t work out. And that's alright. She always dreamed of doing something calmer. That's what she tries to keep alive, while desperately trying to keep her head above water, too. Rio's dream had never been of making it, and maybe that's why she failed; it's always been something simpler. Of stability, of finding community, and while she doesn’t necessarily care for having many friends, she does like mingling with everyone else. Being a shop owner practicing one of her hobbies is the closest she can get, for now. It allows her to interact with people, and she sees all types of characters.

She's done enough simple rose bouquets where it doesn't take much brain power at all. Maybe it'd be better they did, keeping her busier and distracted. Either way, she nearly has five in front of her by the time the door chimes.

The woman walking in is imposing, long coat flowing behind her like a cape, long windswept brown hair. She’s a little… odd, Rio can tell, right away. But it’s more than alright; Rio’s always liked the odd ones.

"Hello," she says, and Rio plasters on the same smile she gives everyone walking through those doors, once she stops gawking at the woman.

"Hello," Rio mirrors, usually a good rule for customer service. "Welcome to The Marigold, how can I help you?"

“Uh, flowers?” The woman deadpans, just shy of rolling her eyes. Rio grins. She’s always loved a challenge. “I need a bouquet. Daisies, primarily.”

Rio raises an eyebrow, and takes in the woman’s outfit again. Polished shoes, pressed dress shirt, shining brooch on her collar. She’s ready for a date, and with the way she keeps checking her watch, she’s cutting it close. Now, Rio usually wouldn’t, but she firmly believes that people decide the kind of customer service they’ll receive within their first few words. If they’re kind, Rio is bored. If they’re not, she toys with them a little before letting them go.

She doesn’t know her, but she can tell enough through the details. In front of her is a woman who controls the big picture through the minutiae, who always needs to be right and severe to hide all that’s wrong. Rio’s favorite hobby, now that botany has turned into a profession, is setting people off.

“Custom bouquets take a little longer,” she warns, soft and cloying even when she’s anything but. “Do you care about the rest of the flowers at all? Or should I give it my own touch?”

Rio watches as the woman rolls her eyes, the blue striking from between dark lashes, and Rio is very glad that her newest toy is rather attractive, too. It always makes things a little more fun.

“Whatever,” she sighs.

Rio is the one to roll her eyes when the woman looks down at her watch again. It’s no fun if she isn’t engaging. “So… who’s the lucky date?”

“What does it matter to you?” she snaps, and Rio does roll her eyes again, right in front of her. The goal is to set her off, isn’t it? And the woman is getting on her nerves.

“Well, I know what daisies mean. It’s thoughtful,” Rio explains. “They’re… pure. They signify new beginnings. You’re dressed well, and you’re impatient. The date must mean enough to you. And you want this bouquet to be nice, don’t you? So tell me more.”

Rio grabs one of the rose bouquets she was just making, switching a few roses for bunches of daisies. It's always a safe bet, for a date. She can add more as Agatha speaks.

“They’re also weeds, and you don’t hear me waxing poetic over them.” The woman leans against the counter, and Rio raises an eyebrow. “But fine. She’s new, yes. We met last week. I wasn’t expecting to… like her. But now she’s coming over and I needed something nice.” She pauses, then adds, “And I’ll have you know, I always dress well.”

Rio laughs, shaking her head. “Sure, I can see that. Do you know her favorite color?”

Her eyebrows scrunch. “Why would I?”

Rio smiles, settling on yellow, then. It matches the eye of the daisies, and she’s got a preference for it. She looks over what she has available, and chooses more wildflowers: buttercups, dandelions and bellworts. She keeps enough foliage around the roses to hide some of the striking red, framing and softening them.

“You’ll have time to find out, today,” Rio reassures, taking just a little longer choosing the paper and twine. She makes sure everything is secure, double-checks, triple-checks even. All to watch the woman squirm a few moments longer.

“Sure.” She laughs, even. Rio gets the impression the date isn’t going to be all that… romantic, then. But bringing flowers to a hook-up isn’t the norm, either.

“Your name?” Rio questions, golden pen in hand. “For the card.”

“Agatha,” she responds easily. The way she says it sounds incomplete. As if it's rarely said without her last name accompanying it.

Rio hums, making sure there are plenty of loops on the A and the g. It’s nice to know the name of her most interesting customer of the day. Rio is proud of her work, once she finally sets the bouquet on the counter.

“Here. That’ll be seventy,” Rio tells her, already setting the card reader down.

Agatha raises an eyebrow, but she doesn’t complain. She taps her card and pays. It’s not the biggest bouquet, and the most expensive are the roses. Still, no discount for a customer like her.

Maybe, if she comes back, Rio will be a bit kinder to her wallet.

Agatha is out the door in seconds.

Rio calls after her, “I hope your date goes well!” And all she gets is the door slammed on her, the bell chiming loud and annoying. Just like Agatha.

Rio grows her own flowers. 

She’s always had a bit of a green thumb, and the store came with a modest but serviceable back area. Once, it used to be a coffee shop, but Rio doesn’t like people that much to have kept it. Much to the then-owner’s displeasure, Rio turned the terrace into a greenhouse. She’d been against it too, at first. She wondered how the plants must feel, subject to perfect conditions year-round. Maybe a bit coddled, but Rio didn’t like messing with their growth like that. Things live and then ultimately die, it’s the one cycle she knows she can count on. However, customers weren’t interested in half-dead plants—no matter how charming Rio found them—and dried flowers only sold so much. If she didn’t keep roses in stock, she might as well close her shop.

Today, she crouches on the flowerbeds and tries to find out why her orchids are being stubborn. The damned things are pickier than most, and the air feels sticky against her skin, heavy with humidity and heat. It’s Sunday, and while the door is open, she trusts she’ll hear the bell if anyone walks in. It’s another slow day. People stroll around after their family lunches and afternoons at the park, and Rio quickly learned that she was scaring away potential customers by staring at them through the window, hoping they’d come in. Once, she even made a kid cry.

No one interrupts her for hours, as expected. Only near closing, when Rio is setting her tools away, does the bell chime.

Rio wipes her hands on her overalls, making sure to wipe her feet on the mat so she doesn’t track dirt inside. The store is as she last left it, tidy and devoid of the chaos from the greenhouse outside. She hopes this doesn’t take long.

It’s Agatha, again.

Another similarly long coat, this time draped over her arm. The day had gotten hotter than expected that afternoon. It showcases another neatly pressed shirt, this time in a deep purple. It suits her. Whoever her date was needs better eyes. Agatha looks like a treat.

Rio does recognize that the woman has a… difficult personality, and she’s seen her only once before. Agatha has a temper, but Rio would be lying if she said she wasn’t drawn to it.

“Back so soon?” Rio quips. If her attitude hadn’t scared Agatha away last time, she isn’t scared to tease her, now. She wants to see if she can make Agatha break.

“Unfortunately,” Agatha replies fast, lips twitching with a smile. Jackpot. Rio is now sure that Agatha is into their back and forth.

“What will it be this time?” Because, after her adamant request for daisies of all flowers, Rio is expecting yet another inspired choice.

“Irises, I think. I read that they…” Agatha trails off, catching herself. Rio just smiles, indulgent. She knows her flower meanings, but she wants to hear what Agatha has to say. Rio called daisies pure, where Agatha called them weeds. “They symbolize strength. Courage.”

“The purple ones do,” Rio agrees. “But color plays a role, of course. For a date, pink and red are the usual. I’ve used plenty white ones for wedding bouquets. But they come in a rainbow of colors. It’s what their name means, actually.”

“Whichever, then. Just make it look pretty.” Agatha sighs, leaning against the counter again.

Rio hums, nodding. She doesn’t mind creative control, and Agatha didn’t seem upset with her choices last time. Although, she doesn’t seem too interested in flower meanings, at least not as in depth as Rio is willing to talk about it.

“Purple, then. It suits you.” Agatha looks like she knows how to command a room. It’s fitting, given the meaning most purple flowers tend to have: royalty. “So… What happened on the last date?”

“Wouldn’t you like to know,” Agatha snarks. Rio glares until Agatha scoffs. Two can play at this game. “Fine. She was… unimpressive.”

“And so you’re onto the next one?” Rio raises an eyebrow.

“Precisely.” And she doesn’t sound the least bit sorry about it.

Rio grins, remembering she has a face once Agatha looks seconds away from vaulting over the counter and strangling her for all the questions. She isn’t judging her. In fact, she’s thinking something along the lines of good for her. Agatha is a womanizer, and what about it? Unfortunately, it adds to the allure, when it should make her run for the hills. But Rio has always done messy and complicated.

“Maybe it was the flowers' fault,” Rio suggests, as a truce. “Maybe these will work better?”

Agatha laughs, and it’s a glorious sound. “Maybe. We will see.”

Rio gets the feeling Agatha will be coming back again, sometime next week. And she seems to revel in the fact. Like she almost expects the dates to go wrong, and she’s… happy about it. It’s a massive red flag, Rio has been through enough to see that, but she’s intrigued.

Rio grabs more purple: shades of salvia, softer lavender, asters and freesias. It’s maybe a little much, but Rio knows it’ll look nice against Agatha’s shirt when she holds the bouquet. She could be faster, this time, she can see the end result clearly, but… she wants Agatha to stay a while longer. Her answers have been clipped, and she wants to hear her talk more.

“What’s this woman like, then?” Rio asks. It could be a man, but… Rio doesn’t think so. The smile Agatha gives her all but confirms it for her.

“She’s got a flair for the dramatic,” she says, almost as if it’s a bad thing. Rio raises an eyebrow, because seriously? “Spare me the judgement. I’m not dramatic. I’m just… opinionated. And bold, and I know how to—”

Rio smiles, biting back a laugh. “I think I have a dictionary somewhere, if you want to look for more synonyms.”

Agatha glares. Rio rolls her eyes, and she likes it too much to stop. Agatha always reacts so beautifully.

“She’s dramatic, fine. What else?” Rio adds a few longer stems, leaning towards more of a fuschia.

“She’s got nice eyes,” Agatha says, after a pause. As if she’s revealing too much.

Rio traces the petals of an iris, grinning. Another inspired flower choice. No matter how much Agatha tries to be indifferent, Rio is starting to see behind those walls, just enough to know that there’s a romantic in there, somewhere. And those are often her best customers.

Rio decides she won’t say anything else. She’s gotten what she wanted from Agatha.

She wraps them in chiffon, also purple, presenting it to Agatha with a smile. There’s something in her eyes for a brief second, gone in the next. Rio catches it, as she does with every instant. Whatever Agatha sees in Rio, aside from the stained overalls and the dirt underneath her nails, catches her attention. Or maybe it’s the mess that she likes.

“How much do I owe you for these?” Agatha breaks the spell, taking the bouquet from her. It’s just as gorgeous in her arms as Rio had predicted.

She’s almost tempted to say nothing, but she’s been reminded once or twice that it doesn’t make for a good business model. She settles on the same amount from last time: “Seventy.”

Agatha tilts her head, a small smile playing on her lips. “That seems like too much of a coincidence. This one is bigger, too.”

Rio shrugs. “Do you want a discount or not? I ripped you off last time.”

Agatha laughs again. “Evening the scales, are we?”

“Always.” Rio grins. She types the number on the card reader and sets it down for Agatha. “Next time, there won’t be a discount, though.”

“I wouldn’t count on it,” Agatha reassures as she pays, that same smile still on her face. “See you next week, Rio.”

Rio is too dumbstruck hearing her name fall from Agatha’s lips to hand her the receipt, but Agatha doesn’t ask for it either.

The beginning of the next week passes in the usual blur. It’s slow, repetitive, and toils away at her soul bit by bit. Maybe she’s being a little dramatic, but none of her few but growing customers are as interesting as Agatha. She tries to focus on the growing part. As much as she’d like to frolic in the woods and tend to her flowers all day, that just isn’t reality. She needs to make a living, much to her displeasure.

Agatha promised she’d be back, and Rio, somehow, doesn’t doubt it. There’s something there, between them. She knows it. Or maybe, it’s just Rio being interested in an unattainable woman yet again, which is also very likely. She doesn’t know how it’d go, if Agatha actually gave her a chance. Because Rio knows her modus operandi: she clings. And Agatha? She seems to leave her suitors for dead. She wonders what her tally will be at the end of the month.

When the doorbell rings and Rio drags her eyes up to greet her new customer, she thinks it’s Agatha. Long coat, imposing. But the face she sees is entirely too kind, and her hair too red.

The woman asks for a simple bouquet, something about wanting to give her husband one for once, and Rio smiles. It’s a nice gesture. It’s very… bright, once Rio’s done with it, but she seems to love it.

Rio grabs a red pen this time, adding one too many turns to the W in the woman’s name, securing the tag with a similarly red ribbon. It’s not her personal taste, but she’s good at reading people. Wanda’s smile as she takes in the finished result is contagious.

“She told me you were good with these,” the woman says, and Rio is confused. She’s touching one of the rose petals, entirely too interested in the small talk. “And she didn’t lie. The flowers are gorgeous. You grow them yourself, you said?”

“Most of them,” Rio confirms, even though no, she hadn’t said anything of the sort. “Who is she?”

“Agatha. She’s a good friend,” the woman brushes it off. “I might just come back if this goes over well.”

“I hope your husband likes them,” Rio offers, even though she’s still stuck on the mention of Agatha.

“I’m sure he will.” Her voice promises more, and Rio is too disgusted with it to hold her friendly, customer appropriate smile on. She does not want those details. “It’s nice to put a face to the name, too.”

“Huh?”

“Agatha’s been talking about you. And she doesn’t talk about many people. I think I understand, now.” The woman sounds so sure of what she’s saying that Rio doesn’t know what to do with herself.

Somewhere, deep inside, she rejoices in the knowledge that Agatha is talking about her to this woman. But the sheer mortification of being… spied on? By her… friend? Nope. Rio doesn’t like that, at all. She’s interested in her privacy, in not being known, in scaring her customers away and teasing them without repercussion. This is a little too close for comfort. And what does she mean, she understands? Rio is unsettled, and it usually takes a lot for that to happen.

Another part of her is oddly touched. Agatha has been recommending her to other people, hasn’t she?

Wanda is the first she notices outright, and only because she said Agatha’s name. But as the woman leaves, Rio remembers the past few days. Sure, they were boring, repetitive, but she had customers. Mostly walk-ins, simple bouquets, some just bought a handful of flowers. She remembers one asking for precisely six poppies, and another sold her out of tulips for the week. A little odd, just like Agatha, but it’s business.

She pays for the bouquet, tucking it under her arm securely, and off she goes. Rio watches her, head tilted, trying to figure out what her life has turned into. Rio doesn’t make small talk with customers, and neither does she entertain it. Were it about anyone else, she wouldn’t have answered. Any other week before this one, she’d have to throw flowers out—she’s never sold anything out.

Rio has only seen Agatha twice, and she’s already changing things. There’s a certain gravitas about the woman. A storm wherever she passes; Rio is unsure if she wants to be caught in it, but she can’t deny the obvious: she is curious.

“Your shop, then? Billy told me you have a patio,” Agatha suggests, still fighting through giggles.

And for once Rio has some hope that things will turn out alright, as long as she can make Agatha laugh like that again, just by being herself.
