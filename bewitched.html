Hela didn’t answer, just shot Rio a glare as she moved towards the door. “Just remember what your real job is.” 

Once she was gone, Agatha looked to Rio questioningly. “What was that about?” 

“Nothing,” Rio assured. “You brought me coffee?” 

“I did.” Agatha handed the cup over, savouring Rio’s smile.  

“You’re literally the best.” 

Agatha smiled brightly, before remembering the true intention behind very visit. “I need to talk to you about something.” 

“Oh?” Rio took a sip of her drink. “What’s up?” 

“It’s…” She’d had the words planned and ready, but now the moment was here, and she couldn’t recall a single thing. “I don’t really know what to say it without sounding crazy, but…” 

“Ah.” Rio set their drinks down on the counter and took her hands in hers. “I get it.” 

“You do?” 

“Yeah, and I want to say it too. I really, really do. Because, well, I do, Agatha. I love you.” 

The whole world stopped. 

“Y-you do?” 

“I really, really, really do.” Rio smiled with relief, like a huge weight had been lifted from her shoulders. “I know it’s probably too soon, but I’ve never felt like this, well, ever.” 

“Me neither,” Agatha admitted. “And it’s terrifying because I want to do this right, but I have no idea and I love you so much, and…” 

Rio cupped her cheek. “Hey, hey, hey. You’re doing everything right, Agatha. We’re good, we’re happy. I love you, you love me. That’s all that matters.” 

Agatha hugged her tight, burying her face into her girlfriend’s shoulder. She hoped Rio truly believed that, then perhaps her admission wouldn’t be as disastrous.  

They finished their coffees and Agatha reluctantly headed back to her own shop. She allowed her employee to go for their own break, leaving her alone in the quiet store. Alone with her thoughts, which had once again circled back to Rio.

She was a coward. She couldn’t do it and ended up making Rio spill her heart out instead. Again. She was the worst girlfriend ever. 

The door to the shop opened and she looked to see a teenage boy walk in. He was tall with curly black hair and a boat load of eyeliner. All his clothes had rips in, the kind that were part of their manufacture, and he had a notebook clutched to his chest.

He looked around the shop before his gaze locked with hers. His eyes widened and he cautiously approached the desk.

Agatha put on a smile. “How may I help you?”

“I came to talk to you.”

“To me?”

The teen nodded. “You’re Agatha Harkness.” 

“I am,” she confirmed, folding her eyes. “Who’s asking?” 

“I’m Billy Kaplan, and I’m here to save you.”

“Save me?” Agatha cackled. “I can look after myself, thank you’ve much.”

“You don’t understand,” Billy insisted. “You’re in terrible danger.”

“I’m always in terrible danger,” Agatha dismissed. “So unless you’re going to buy something…”

“I know there’s some witches trying to kill you.”

“There’s always-“

“They call themselves the Salem Seven.”

Agatha froze. “Salem?”

Billy nodded. “I met them in a witchcraft chatroom. They’re looking for any information about you. I got chatting with one of them, and they said they wanted revenge. That they wanted to kill you because you killed their parents.”

Her heart dropped. She knew exactly who the Salem Seven were: the children of her coven, whose mothers died trying to kill her.

“What else did they say?” she questioned.

“That they knew you were somewhere in the city, and that they needed someone with my powerset to trap you.”

Agatha pursed her lips and looked the boy up and down. “And what powerset is that?”

“Telekinesis, manipulation of space. It’s chaos magic.”

Her eyes narrowed. “And why are you telling me? Did they not tell you want I did?”

“Yeah, but I did some digging of my own.” He reached into his notebook and pulled out a folded piece of paper. He opened it up and handed it over. Agatha frowned at it.

“How did you get my yearbook photo?”

“I’m good at online research. I found online articles about…what happened. They said it was a cult sacrifice, that neighbours said your mother was very religious. How they often…worried about you.”

Agatha laughed bitterly. “Yeah, so worried they let me stay with that evil bitch.”

“You were a scared kid.” Teen looked down at his feet. “I get that.”

She frowned. “Are your parents…”

“They’re wonderful,” he quickly clarified. “They just don’t know what I am. A warlock and, well, gay.”

Agatha nodded in understanding. “Well my mum knew I was a witch and hated I was a lesbian. That was why I tried to run away and I…” She cleared her throat, pushing the tears away. “So what’s the plan, Teen? You want me to go apologise and tell them their parents tried to kill me first?”

Teen shrugged. “It’s worth a try, or we run. Buy us some time.”

“I’m not running,” Agatha insisted. “I’ve built a life here. They can come find me, and if they try kill me, I’ll do the same to them as I did to their parents.”

The teen’s eyes widened. “Agatha, they’re just kids.”

“So are you,” she reminded him. “And so was I.”

His shoulder’s slumped. “Well, I guess we need to get ourselves a coven, then.”

 

Jen and her girlfriend, Alice soon arrived at the shop. Alice introduced herself to Billy politely, while Jen looked him up and down in judgement, before shifting the same look to Agatha.”

“What have you done now, Harkness?”

“Nothing,” she insisted. “Well, nothing new.”

They settled in the kid’s reading area, Alice and Jen on the beanbags while she and Teen remained standing. They recited the whole situation to them both, from the past horrors to the recent plot of revenge. Alice nodded along quietly, while Jen kept interrupting with comments and disapproving sounds. When the tale was done, Jen sighed and stood up.

“So, when are we leaving?”

“We’re not,” Agatha answered.

“We’re not?” Jen put her hands on her hips. “Do you have a death wish, Harkness?”

“I can kill them easily,” she said dismissively.

“And we’ll end up having to deal with seven bodies,” Alice argued. “We could go to prison.”

“No, she will go to prison.” Jen pointed at Agatha accusingly. “I’m not going to prison for you, Agatha.”

“Did I ask you to?” Agatha snapped. “Teen wanted you here. I can handle this myself.”

“Fine, if that’s what you want.” Jen went to leave, but Alice tugged her back down on the beanbag.

“We can’t just leave her,” Alice argued. “And we can’t make her leave.”

Jen looked at Agatha suspiciously. “This is all about Rio, isn’t it? You don’t want to leave her.”

“Great detective skills there, Jen. Olivia Benson has nothing on you.”

“Fuck off, Agatha. You’re a fucking idiot. You do realise staying puts Rio in danger, right? No, it puts everyone in danger.”

“I know that, but…”

“But?” Jen questioned and Agatha’s shoulders slumped in defeat.

“I was finally happy.”

As Alice and Teen’s expressions turned to sympathy, Jen rolled her eyes and got to her feet one more, yanking her best friend into a hug.

“I’m sorry. I should never have done that stupid spell.”

Agatha didn’t regret the spell. She loved Rio. Loved her so much it made her chest hurt. She wished she didn’t have a fucked up past, and an even more fucked up present. Wished she was a magicless who just owned a bookstore and fell in love with the local florist. Maybe in that life, she could have a happiness that lasts.

But not in this one.

She pulled out of Jen’s embrace and pulled out her phone.

AGATHA

Hey where are you?

RIO

Oh shit did we have plans?

AGATHA

No but I want to see you.

RIO

I’m at a bar with Lilia. Come join us if you want.

AGATHA

Send me the address and I’ll set off now.

As soon as she got it, Agatha went to leave.

“Agatha, we don’t have time,” Teen argued. “We need to go.”

“We will,” Agatha promised. “I-I just need to say goodbye.”

 

The coven entered the bar and found Rio sat with an older woman by the bar. Rio must have sensed her presence because she quickly looked over and smiled brighter than a million stars. Agatha smiled back, even though she wanted to cry.

“Wow, she’s prettier than I thought,” Jen commented, and Agatha nodded.

“She’s the most beautiful person in the world.”

Alice put a supportive hand on her shoulder. “Let’s go talk to her.”

They walked over to the table and Rio jumped out of her seat to give Agatha a sweet kiss. “I’m so happy to see you.”

“Me too,” Agatha said truthfully. Despite the circumstances, she would always be happy to see Rio. “These are my friends, Jen and Alice. My, erm, shop assistant was going to come too, but he got carded at the door.”

Rio shook Jen and Alice’s hands, before waving her hands at her companion. “This is Lilia. My awesome ex roommate and forever friend.”

Lilia raised her glass to all of them before giving Agatha a wink. “I’ve heard a lot about you, Agatha.”

“All good, I hope.”

Lilia laughed “Like this one will say a bad thing about you. She talks like you’re made of stardust.”

Rio bowed her head before reaching down to take her hand. “Fancy a dance before Lilia embarrasses me?”

Agatha chuckled and nodded. “Lead the way.”

Rio led them both to the dancefloor and they began to sway to the music. Agatha felt her girlfriend’s hands on her hips before sliding lower.

“Rio, we’re in public and our friends are right over there.”

Rio smirked mischievously, but her hands moved back upwards. “You know, you have a really cute ass. It’s kind of distracting.”

Agatha couldn’t help but laugh. She loved Rio’s ridiculousness.

“Well I need you to concentrate because I-I need to tell you something. The real something I came to talk to you about today.”

Rio looked at her with a frown. “Is everything okay?”

“No,” Agatha admitted. “I-I came to say goodbye.”

“Goodbye?” Rio’s frown deepened. “What do you mean? Are you breaking up with me?”

“No, well I guess, but I don’t want to. I just…I have to leave.”

“Leave where?” Rio questioned. “I’ll come with you.”

“No, Rio. It’s not safe. I’m in trouble, okay? And I don’t want you getting hurt.”

Rio pulled her closer, holding her protectively. “In trouble how?”

“I did something bad a long time ago,” Agatha admitted. “I didn’t have a choice, but there are people who want revenge for what I did. And…And I’ve never been honest with you. Not really. There’s something about me that you don’t know. Something big.”

“What?” Rio questioned. “Agatha, you know you can tell me anything, right?”

Agatha sucked in a deep breath. “Rio, I’m a…”

BANG.

Screams.

Shots.

“AGATHA HARKNESS.”

Agatha sighed. It seemed running away wasn’t an option anymore.

The Salem Seven had blasted a hole in the wall, emerging through the smoke and rubble in ridiculous matching outfits, seamlessly dodging the retreating magicless crowd trying to escape.  One of them dragged Teen along in a headlock, and he had a deep scratch across his cheek. Agatha glowered. They were just like their parents. 

Jen and Alice were at her side in an instant, Lilia along with them. 

“You can’t fight them alone,” Jen said, and Alice quickly agreed. Agatha, however, did not.

“Leave this to me. Just make sure Rio is okay.”

Lilia laughed. “Rio can handle herself.”

“No, she really can’t,” Agatha snapped. “You don’t understand, Magicless. Let me deal with this.”

One of the seven stepped up. “You killed our patents.”

“And your parents tried to kill me,” Agatha pointed out. “They were fair game.”

The seven holding Teen pulled him forward. “Surrender to us and we’ll spare the boy.”

Agatha shrugged. “I only me him this morning.”

“Agatha,” Alice scolded, and she rolled her eyes. 

“Just let the boy go. He has nothing to do with this.”

“He betrayed our trust,” another accused. “Found out our plan and told the enemy.”

“She’s not the enemy,” Teen insisted. “She was just a kid.”

“So were we,” Teen’s captor argued. “She killed our mothers due to her own selfishness. And now, she will pay.”

Agatha stepped forward, forming a ball of purple in her hands. “Let’s see if you make me break a sweat.”

One of the seven ran forward, magic in their palms ready to strike. Agatha smirked and waited. One taste of that magic and she’d suck the little fucker dry. Yet before they could reach her, before she could get a hold of that magic, they were thrown back through the air, falling at the feet of their comrades.

“ENOUGH.”

The whole room went still, and all eyes moved to Rio.

“Anyone who tries to hurt Agatha Harkness will spend the rest of eternity in a realm of constant torture.

Agatha frowned. “Rio?”

Rio stepped forward and her usual clothes disintegrated, replaced with a black jumpsuit that looked like it was made from twisting vines, with her, erm, toned abs on full display. A matching crown rested on her head, and a green dagger was held tight in her palm.

“What the fuck?” Jen gasped. “Rios a witch?!”

Lilia laughed again. “Oh she’s more than that.”

“What do you mean?” Alice questioned.

“Rio,” Lilia began. “She’s death.”

DEATH?!?!?

Agatha looked to Rio and saw her face had changed. Her mouth, her nose, the entire lower part of her face had become skeletal, and her eyes were darker than she knew possible. 

“Leave,” Rio commanded the Salem Seven. “And never think of Agatha Harkness again.”

The Salem Seven retreated without hesitation, dropping Teen to the floor as they made their escape. He sat up and looked around. “It was that easy?”

Rio’s face returned to normal, and she looked to Agatha sheepishly. 

“Guess we were both keeping things from each other.”

Agatha stared at her. Her entire world had been thrown upside down, and there was only one thing she could do. She strode forward and pulled Rio, Death, the love of her life, into a heated kiss. A kiss free of secrets and lies. One of pure acceptance.

She heard Jen laugh somewhere behind them. “I should have known. Of course Agatha’s true love would be Death.”

Agatha figured she had a point, but it wasn’t entirely true. Her true love wasn’t Death. It was Rio. The one who made her smile and laugh, just as Rio’s true love wasn’t the famous witch killed.

They just all happened to be one and the same.

 

“So Hela is your, like, understudy?”

“Yes,” Rio answered as they walked hand in hand along the river. They had left the others to deal with the clean up at the bar, insisting they need to talk. Which they did, but also Agatha hated clean-up duty. 

“And you’re not been death for like, what, ten years?”

“I bound my powers.” Rio pulled up the sleeve of her shirt and revealed the tattoo. “I can’t take away my powers altogether, but I stopped all the voices, the pull to lost souls.”

“But why?” Agatha questioned. “You’re a cosmic being? Why would you want to run a flower shop and get coffee?”

 “I wanted to experience what so many were so sad to leave behind. So, Hela agreed that she would be death for ten years so I could see what all the fuss it’s is about.”

“And what did you think?” 

“It was good.” Rio squeezed her hand. “Then it got really good. So good that I almost forgot the ten years was up, and when Hela came to swap back I…well I refused.”

“Can you refuse?”

Rio shrugged. “It’s worth a try, unless I can persuade Hela otherwise. I’ve only just found you, and if it were up to me, I’d never leave your side.”

“Really?” 

Rio nodded. “I love you, Agatha.”

“I love you too.” Agatha placed a quick kiss to her lips. “We’ll work things out. After all, I’m not just some magicless or young doe-eyed witch.”

“Oh I know,” Rio grinned. “You’re my perfect match.”

“You know, I used a soulmate spell before we met,” Agatha admitted.

Rio raised her eyebrows. “You did?” 

Agatha nodded. “And then you stumbled into my shop during a freak storm.”

Rio kissed her on the cheek. “Guess it’s official then. We’re in it for the long haul.”

“The long haul,” Agatha repeated. Which she knew would be eternity.
