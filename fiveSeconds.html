“Five seconds!” The official called, raising one hand in the air as they watched their stopwatch.

Agatha counted her breaths and squared her shoulders. She clenched her hands twice for good luck. 

 A few feet ahead of her, Rio caught her eye, giving her a cocky smile and a wink. 

As the jammer, Agatha had one job. Break through the pack, lap them, score four points. One for each sucker trying to stop her. All while trying to stop the other jammer from making it past her own team. Then do it again, and again, until time ran out.

Even though based on stats she was undoubtedly the best in the league, roller derby was a team sport. That technicality didn’t stop her from frequently teasing that The Coven would be nothing without their star player. She got away with it because her ego wasn’t unfounded. People would occasionally roll their eyes whenever she crowed about her prowess and victory but rarely did anyone challenge her or correct her. 

She was just that good, formidable, undeniable.

The only other player she didn’t dispute matched her skill was Rio. She played the pivot, alternating between being an extra defensive body and the only other player who could score points. If Agatha passed the star-printed cover on her helmet to her. 

Agatha wasn’t the picture of a perfect team player, but she couldn’t be called selfish either. What she wanted most was for her team to win, and every bout she fought to do her part to make it happen. For the team, of course.

But there was no denying victory always tasted sweeter when she dominated the score board. She never settled for just eeking out a win, she wanted total defeat of her opponents. She craved blood. And Rio was the only other person who matched that intensity.

They made a good team even if stylistically they operated differently. Agatha was all force, unafraid of shoulder checking opposing blockers, doing whatever it took to force her way through. 

She didn’t care that it cost her an obscene share of penalties. She was the only person in league history to set the record for both the most points scored and most minutes spent in the penalty box in a single season. Another achievement for her to boast about.

In contrast, Rio, was all about speed, getting low and darting around the opposition with a dancer’s grace. Agility that was not just feline, but almost ethereal, like she could melt her form and pass through bodies like they were nothing. An undeniable force of nature.

Her signature move was jumping the apex, coming in hot to turns, and jumping around the mass of bodies to cut corners and gain a few seconds of advantage. More than once she’d had close calls, but always managed to pull it off. And when she did, she never failed to throw a cocky grin Agatha’s way.

Flanking Rio were the three blockers on the team: Alice, Jen, and Wanda. Responsible for both helping clear a path for her and keeping the other team’s jammer at bay. 

The ref’s whistle sounded, their hand came down, and the jam was on.

The pack began their lap around the track, bodies slamming into each other as her team fought for any available opening.

“Nice try!” she taunted, grinning around her mouthguard.

Out of the corner of her eye, a flash of a purple jersey as Rio dodged a hit and circled towards the back of the pack. As usual, intently watching Agatha’s every move, ready to capitalize on any chance to help score.

She always played better when she felt her eyes on her. A good show was nothing without an audience.
